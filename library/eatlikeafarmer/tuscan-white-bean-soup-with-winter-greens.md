This **tuscan white bean soup** is like the coziest sweater you own, but in soup form. It’s hearty, colorful, warming and nourishing in all the best ways, and it’s filled with veggie goodness (think celery, carrots and winter greens, alongside aromatic tuscan herbs and creamy white beans).

It’s also a great example of how good cooking isn’t complicated – but it often requires a bit patience, as you gradually and intentionally build flavor throughout the cooking process. There aren’t any fancy ingredients in this recipe (and I bet you already have all of them in your fridge/pantry), but by the end, you’ll be amazed at the depth of flavor you’ve achieved from such humble beginnings.

But *please* don’t forget the splash of lemon juice at the end! It really helps brighten the soup and kicks all the other flavors up a notch.

### Tuscan White Bean Soup with Winter Greens

*Serves 6-8*

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0Cy0!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack.com%2Fimg%2Fattachment_icon.svg)

**INGREDIENTS**

-   1 tablespoon olive oil
    
-   4 strips of bacon, diced *(optional)*
    
-   1 large (or 2 small) yellow onion, cut to a small dice
    
-   4 celery stalks, sliced ¼” thick
    
-   4 medium carrots, sliced into ¼” rounds
    
-   2 teaspoons kosher salt
    
-   ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
    
-   2 tablespoons tomato paste
    
-   4 garlic cloves, minced
    
-   1 teaspoon dried oregano
    
-   1 teaspoon dried thyme
    
-   3 15-oz cans white beans (Great Northern or Cannellini), drained and rinsed
    
-   ½ cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese, rind reserved
    
-   4 cups water
    
-   1 bunch winter greens (kale, collards, chard, etc), stems removed and thinly sliced (5 cups greens)
    
-   2-3 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
    

**INSTRUCTIONS**

1.  Set a large dutch oven or stockpot over medium-high heat and add **1 tablespoon olive oil**. When the oil is hot, add **4 strips diced bacon** and cook, stirring for around 4 minutes, until it begins to brown. *NOTE: to make this soup vegetarian, just skip this step!*
    
2.  Add **1 large diced yellow onion, 4 sliced celery stalks** and **4 sliced carrots** to the pot and season with **2 teaspoons kosher salt** and **½ teaspoon ground pepper**. Reduce the heat to medium and cook, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and starting to brown, 8-10 minutes.
    
3.  Add **2 tablespoons tomato paste, 4 minced garlic cloves, 1 teaspoon dried oregano** and **1 teaspoon dried thyme** and cook, stirring frequently to toast the aromatics, for about 1 minute.
    
4.  Add **3 cans rinsed and drained white beans, 4 cups water,** and **½ cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese**. If you’re grating from a parmesan wedge with a rind (or if you have Parmesan rinds stashed away), cut off the rind and toss it into the soup… it adds amazing umami notes and depth of flavor.
    
5.  Bring the soup to a gentle simmer. Cook for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the carrots are tender and all the flavors have melded.
    
6.  Add **1 bunch sliced winter greens**. Simmer for another 5 minutes, until the greens are wilted and tender. Finish by stirring in **2-3 tablespoons fresh lemon juice** and remove the Parmesan rind.
    
7.  *Taste!* Add more salt and/or lemon juice if needed. If the soup is thicker than you’d like, just add a bit more water.
    
8.  Serve with a drizzle of olive oil and some additional grated Parmesan.
