I know I’m biased, but **I would \*happily\* eat this fall orzo salad situation for breakfast, lunch, and/or dinner.** It’s that good. You’ve got color, you’ve got crunch, you’ve got salty and sweet and tangy and honestly? It’s a dang smorgasbord of autumnal goodness.

This salad is super adaptable, so feel free to swap out any of the veggies / fruit / cheese / nuts with whatever you’ve got on hand. For example, if you don’t have access to delicata squash, any kind of winter squash (like butternut, kabocha, etc) will do. Need to make it gluten free? Sub out the orzo and use quinoa instead. Don’t have pumpkin seeds? Walnuts, pecans, or slivered almonds are all great. See what I mean? *You do you.*

This kind of hearty salad is my go-to dish to bring to a potluck or picnic, because you can serve it at any temperature, and it’s not going to wilt after sitting out for a few hours. If anything, it only gets more delicious with time.

### Fall Harvest Orzo Salad with Apple Cider Dijon Vinaigrette

*Serves 4-6*

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0Cy0!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack.com%2Fimg%2Fattachment_icon.svg)

**INGREDIENTS**

-   2 cups uncooked orzo
    
-   Kosher salt
    
-   2 medium delicata squash, halved lengthwise, seeds removed, and cut into ½” half moons
    
-   1 large (or 2 small) red onion, diced
    
-   2 tablespoons olive oil
    
-   ½ teaspoon paprika
    
-   ½ teaspoon cumin
    
-   1 bunch kale or swiss chard, de-stemmed and thinly sliced
    
-   1 cup toasted pumpkin seeds (also known as pepitas)
    
-   1 cup chopped aged white cheddar cheese (or sub with aged gouda or feta cheese)
    
-   ½ cup dried cranberries
    

*Apple Cider Dijon Vinaigrette*

-   ½ cup olive oil
    
-   ¼ cup apple cider vinegar
    
-   2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
    
-   1 tablespoons maple syrup (or honey)
    
-   1 clove minced garlic
    
-   1 teaspoon kosher salt, plus more to taste
    
-   ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
    

**PREPARATION**

1.  Preheat oven to 400F.
    
2.  Cut **2 delicata squash** in half lengthwise and use a spoon to scoop out the seeds. Chop the squash into ½” thick half-moons and place on a rimmed baking sheet. (Note: you do not need to peel delicata squash). Add **1 diced red onion, 2 tablespoons olive oil, 1 teaspoon kosher salt, ½ teaspoon paprika**, and **½ teaspoon cumin** to the baking sheet and use your hands to toss everything together until the squash is coated in the oil and spices. Roast in the oven until the delicata squash is very tender, about 30-35 minutes, tossing once or twice to ensure even roasting. Remove from the oven and set aside.
    
3.  While the squash is roasting, make the apple cider vinaigrette: in a mason jar, combine all the vinaigrette ingredients, fasten the lid and shake well until the vinaigrette is emulsified. Set aside.
    
4.  Also while the delicata squash is roasting, fill a 4-5 quart pot with water and bring to a boil and add **1 tablespoon kosher salt.** Once the water is boiling, add **2 cups orzo** to the pot and cook uncovered, stirring occasionally. Cook according to package instructions (different brands have varying cooking times, usually 10-12 minutes). Drain and transfer orzo to a large salad bowl.
    
5.  De-stem **1 bunch kale (or chard)**, thinly slice the leaves into ribbons, and add to the bowl on top of the orzo (but don’t mix with the orzo yet). Pour about **¼ cup of the vinaigrette** over the kale, then use your hands to massage the kale with the vinaigrette for 1 minute, until the leaves turn dark green and the pile of kale leaves breaks down considerably. *Don’t skip this step!*
    
6.  To the salad bowl add the **roasted delicata squash,** **1 cup pumpkin seeds, 1 cup chopped aged white cheddar cheese, ½ cup dried cranberries,** and **all of the remaining vinaigrette**, and stir to combine. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed. Enjoy warm or at room temperature.
