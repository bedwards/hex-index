This recipe took me a few tries to get \*just right\*, but now I can say it’s some of the best damn chili I’ve ever made. It’s a *double* beef (stew beef AND ground beef), butternut squash, black bean, and warming autumnal spiced chili situation, and it’s hands down my new go-to cozy fall + winter recipe.

Like with any chili, there’s some work to do on the front end – searing the meats, sauting onions/garlic, blooming fragrant aromatics, chopping the winter squash, and then combining everything together – but trust me when I say, don’t rush the process! Those steps in the beginning are creating layers of complex flavor that are very worth it in the end.

So as these days and nights keep getting colder and darker, do yourself a favor and warm everything up with this giant pot of goodness. And let me know what you think!

### Butternut & Black Bean Beef Chili

*Serves 6*

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!0Cy0!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack.com%2Fimg%2Fattachment_icon.svg)

**INGREDIENTS**

-   1 tablespoon canola/vegetable oil
    
-   1lb stew beef, cut into 1” cubes
    
-   1lb ground beef
    
-   1 large yellow onion, diced
    
-   4 cloves garlic, minced
    
-   1 to 2 tablespoons chili powder (use more or less depending on your heat preference)
    
-   1 tablespoon ground cumin
    
-   2 teaspoons smoked paprika
    
-   2 teaspoons kosher salt, plus more to taste
    
-   1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
    
-   2 tablespoons tomato paste
    
-   1 butternut squash (about 3lb), peeled and cut into ½” dice
    
-   2 14.5-oz cans diced fire-roasted tomatoes, undrained
    
-   3 15-ounce cans black beans, rinsed and drained
    
-   3 cups water or beef broth
    
-   2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
    
-   *Suggested garnishes:* shredded cheese, chopped green onions, sour cream/plain yogurt, avocado
    

**INSTRUCTIONS**

1.  *Prep and brown the meat* – Cut **1lb stew beef** into 1” cubes, pat dry with a paper towel, and season generously with kosher salt. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat **1 tablespoon canola/vegetable oil** over high heat. When the oil is hot, add the stew beef and cook until all sides are seared, 6-7 minutes. Transfer the browned stew beef to a plate lined with paper towels. In the same pot over high heat, brown **1lb ground beef**, seasoned generously with kosher salt, for 5-6 minutes, stirring occasionally until no longer pink. Transfer the browned ground beef to a plate lined with paper towels.
    
2.  *Sauté onion & garlic* – Leave any liquid fat in the pot and lower heat to medium-high. Add **1 large yellow onion** (diced) and cook until softened and beginning to brown, 6-7 minutes. Lower the heat to medium and add **4 cloves minced garlic** and cook for another minute, until fragrant.
    
3.  *Add aromatics* – Stir in **1 to 2 tablespoons chili powder** (use more or less depending on your heat preference), **1 tablespoon cumin, 2 teaspoons paprika, 2 teaspoons kosher salt,** and **½ teaspoon cinnamon.** Cook and stir frequently over medium heat for 1 minute to “bloom” the spices and release their flavor. Add **2 tablespoons tomato paste** and cook for 1 more minute, stirring frequently.
    
4.  *Combine ingredients* – Return the browned stew beef and ground beef to the pot. Stir in **1 peeled and diced butternut squash, 2 cans fire-roasted diced tomatoes** (undrained), **3 cans black beans** (drained and rinsed), and **3 cups of water** (or beef broth). Use a flat wooden spoon to scrape any browned bits from the bottom of the pot, and stir to incorporate with the chili.
    
5.  *Simmer* – Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Simmer uncovered for at least 1–2 hours, or until the stew beef is tender. The longer you simmer, the more the flavors meld. If the chili becomes too thick, add more water or beef. When the chili is finished simmering, stir in **2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar** and remove from heat. Taste (!) and adjust seasoning as needed.
    
6.  *Serve*! Ladle the finished chili into bowls. Garnish with your favorite toppings like shredded cheese, chopped green onions, sour cream/plain yogurt, or avocado.
