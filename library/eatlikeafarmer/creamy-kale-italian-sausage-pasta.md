As I’ve written about before, I’m taking this fall season “off” from our CSA program (where our CSA members get a box of fresh veggies every week), so I can focus most of my time and energy on being a present mom to my two toddlers.

Despite the farm being more sparse than usual, there are still plenty of fall veggies in the ground so we can feed ourselves and to sell occasional boxes when I have surplus. I’m already looking forward to being back in the full swing of things next year once both my kids are preschool, but honestly I’m so, so grateful for this time to take a step back to focus on our family, and *not* being constantly stressed about having enough veggies to fill our weekly CSA boxes.

**With all that in mind, here’s the latest from our farm (and helpful links for your fall garden)….**

-   **PLANTING:** Here in zone 8a, our first frost is usually around mid/end of October. So right now, we’re planting one last succession of quick-growing fall crops (like arugula, radishes, lettuces, Japanese turnips, etc) in our outdoor beds, and in the next few weeks we will start planting winter greens inside our caterpillar tunnels, where it stays much warmer. To dig deeper into fall veggie gardening, check out my super comprehensive fall gardening guide **[here](https://eatlikeafarmer.substack.com/p/fall-vegetable-garden-guide)**.
    
-   **HARVESTING NOW:** Asian greens (like bok choy), lettuces (looseleaf and head lettuce), turnips, radishes, swiss chard, kale. We’re also enjoying all the winter squash (butternut, delicata, acorn, etc) that we harvested back in August, now that they have cured for a few weeks. And we still have a few summer crops like peppers, eggplant, okra and the occasional tomato still hanging on. For a full tutorial on how to properly harvest fall greens (and to maximize your crops’ production), watch my video **[here](https://eatlikeafarmer.substack.com/p/video-how-to-harvest-spring-greens?utm_source=publication-search)**.
    
-   **COMING SOON:** Carrots, broccoli, cabbage, collard greens. These veggies can all handle light frosts without a problem, and if anything, they get sweeter as the weather turns colder.
    
-   **PESTS ISSUES:** Right now, the biggest pest issue we’re seeing is **cabbage worms on our brassicas** (broccoli, cabbage, collards). These bright green worms can cause a TON of damage if they aren’t caught in time, so look carefully at the photo below to know what to look for in your garden… you’ll want to train your eye to see the worms and their distinctive green poop (known as frass). If you have a small garden with only a few brassica plants, I suggest checking your plants twice a day for worms, and just picking them off. For larger gardens (or if you don’t have capacity to pick off the worms), the most effective *organic-approved* treatment is spraying Bt (aka Bacillus thuringiensis) once every 1-2 weeks, as needed. Here is a [Bt concentrate](https://amzn.to/4nhsJPe) and here is a [ready-to-use spray](https://amzn.to/46pBa5d).

Just keeping it real with y’all… it’s been a very full and stressful week, and basically all I want to eat are big, creamy, comforting bowls of pasta (with some fall veggies, obvi, and ideally eaten while curled up on the couch watching the latest episode of [The Great British Baking Show](https://www.netflix.com/title/80063224)… IYKYK).

That’s pretty much all the inspiration I needed for this week’s recipe… and let me just say, this **creamy kale & italian sausage pasta** situation DELIVERS. She is hearty and cheesy and salty and cozy and even my very picky 4-year old loves it. Like all of my recipes, this one is super straightforward and unfussy, and you can easily whip it up on a busy weeknight.

A note that you can totally leave out the sausage if you are vegetarian! Though I would recommend substituting with a hearty veg in place of the sausage… some roasted butternut would be *divine*.

So consider this recipe all the excuse you need to eat dinner on the couch in your pajamas tonight (if that’s your kind of thing). *Happy cooking!*
