Let’s get started:

-   A cycling association in Bruges, Belgium, successfully [lobbied for a city-wide cycling zone](https://www.openstreetmap.org/user/Pieter%20Vander%20Vennet/diary/407661) by using OpenStreetMap tools to measure and visualize street widths.
    
-   NYC is sprucing up its streets and stops: $40 million to add [benches to bus stops](https://www.nyc.gov/mayors-office/news/2025/10/mayor-adams-invests--40-million-for-universal-seating--installin), plus a bill opening [thousands of new vending licenses](https://www.cityandstateny.com/opinion/2025/11/opinion-fixing-nycs-street-vending-chaos-moral-obligation-and-simple-one/409253/).
    
-   A Calgary family reflects on [two years of using an electric cargo](https://gooutside.substack.com/p/two-years-on-a-cargo-bike) bike as a “very cheap minivan”, highlighting the joy, practicality and community-building benefits.
    
-   A new study on [adolescents in high-rise neighborhoods](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/shima-bakhshayeshi-614b10236_urbanization-urbanplanning-housing-activity-7391130791533117441-2Y50/) in Toronto and Kitchener-Waterloo found they often struggle with isolation and “anti-social” designs. The research highlights that healthy density must prioritize human connection and that cities should listen to youth voices.
    
-   NYC’s 24-hour economy generates $35 billion and 300,000 jobs. With 80 cities [introducing night mayors](https://initiatives.weforum.org/global-experience-alliance/24-hour-economy), urban planning must rethink daytime-focused strategies to unlock the nighttime economy.
    

Videos, podcasts, books, and socials:

-   MITx is offering a free online course, [Introduction to Pedestrian Mobility in Cities](https://mitxonline.mit.edu/courses/course-v1:MITxT+11.024x/), that equips learners with the tools to analyze, plan, and advocate for pedestrian-friendly environments. Starting **November 4**.
    
-   A new PBS documentary, “From Rails to Trails,” chronicles the grassroots movement to [convert abandoned railway lines into affordable hiking and biking trails across the U.S.](https://www.collegetowns.org/p/from-rails-to-trails-doc-blurs-nimby) The film shows how these projects connect communities, offer alternatives to car infrastructure and complicate the typical NIMBY-YIMBY narrative.
    
-   A Next City podcast episode explores how arts leaders in Minnesota, Toronto and Oakland are [pioneering alternative models to sustain artists](https://nextcity.org/podcast/alternative-models-for-funding-and-supporting-cultural-spaces-and-workers) and preserve cultural spaces.
