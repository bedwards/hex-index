Let’s get started:

-   Some UK councils are requiring “[bee bricks](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/anna-wysocki-3a6a4a79_in-the-uk-a-fascinating-architectural-innovation-activity-7377965320357031936-xS3u/)” in new construction projects to help combat pollinator decline. The bricks have built-in cavities designed to house solitary bees, embedding biodiversity directly into buildings.
    
-   Residents in Atlanta raised $10,000 and used volunteers to build a [pop-up protected bike lane](https://nextcity.org/urbanist-news/these-residents-built-a-10000-bike-lane-in-atlanta) connecting two schools as part of the city’s tactical urbanism program.
    
-   A feminist collective in Mexico City called [AmorrAs](https://www.instagram.com/amorrassomostodas/) provides a [ride-sharing service exclusively for women](https://lacaderadeeva.com/entrevistas/amorras-taxis-feministas-en-cdmx-que-acompanan-a-mujeres-segura/15219). The grassroots initiative was created in response to systemic violence against women in public and private transportation. English CNN article [here](https://edition.cnn.com/2025/09/23/americas/mexico-city-women-only-taxis-as-equals-intl-cmd).
    
-   The King County Council unanimously [banned landlords from using rent-setting software](https://www.seattletimes.com/business/real-estate/king-county-council-bans-rent-setting-software-like-realpage/) like RealPage, which is accused of helping landlords collude to artificially increase rents.
