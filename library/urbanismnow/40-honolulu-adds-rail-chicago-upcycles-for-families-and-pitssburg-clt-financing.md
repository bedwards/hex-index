Let’s get started:

-   Chicago’s Digs with Dignity fills a crucial gap that social services can’t address by designing and [furnishing homes with upcycled furniture](https://nextcity.org/urbanist-news/closing-the-gap-between-housing-and-home?utm_source=Next+City+Newsletter&utm_campaign=094dad82e2-DailyNL_2025_10_16&utm_medium=email&utm_term=0_fcee5bf7a0-094dad82e2-470490664) for families transitioning out of homelessness, creating spaces that feel comfortable, functional and dignified.
    
-   [Honolulu’s new 5.2-mile Skyline rail segment](https://www.staradvertiser.com/2025/10/20/breaking-news/new-rail-segment-sees-61k-passengers-in-first-4-days-of-opening/), which includes a stop at the airport, saw 61,968 rides in its first four days of operation.
    
-   Next City reveals how one [Pittsburgh community land trust](https://nextcity.org/urbanist-news/what-if-more-community-land-trusts-made-friends-with-banks?utm_source=Next+City+Newsletter&utm_campaign=f9c494530b-DailyNL_2025_10_06_COPY_01&utm_medium=email&utm_term=0_fcee5bf7a0-f9c494530b-470490664) cracked the construction financing puzzle by slowly building relationships with a regional bank, proving that patient relationship-building can unlock millions in affordable housing development.
    
-   A new academic article examines the legacy of [South Korea’s urban commons movement](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/hyun-bang-shin-2a816b5_new-publication-alert-im-delighted-activity-7383822977454354432-kvbd/) in the 1970s. The study highlights the “working-in-commons” practices, such as mutual aid and shared childcare, used by poor women in a Seoul shantytown to foster community and survive precarity.
    

Videos, podcasts, books, and socials:

-   [Everyday life in Costa Rica](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wxWfpBQvfLs) reveals an alternative way of understanding space and time, where people navigate without a centralized address system, using landmarks and shared memory instead of street numbers, offering a poetic rethinking of how we relate to place.
    
-   A new 5-part podcast series, “[A Tiny Plot](https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/a-tiny-plot/id1838823145),” follows residents of an encampment in Oakland as they fight for their own plot of land to build a community and set their own rules. The series explores scarcity, community, and creating something new from the margins of a broken system.
