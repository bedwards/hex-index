Let’s get started:

-   New research develops an [open-data method for assessing equity in X-minute cities](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/epub/10.1177/23998083251398660), finding that ethical frameworks significantly shape equity assessments and that car-dependent cities can surprisingly outperform walkable ones in proximity-based planning models.
    
-   URBACT explores [Vienna’s people-first mobility transformation](https://urbact.eu/whats-new/articles/vienna-calling-mobility-lessons-city-built-around-people), where half of households are car-free and 130 projects have created 100 kilometers of cycling infrastructure, turning former parking lots into climate-resilient community spaces.
    
-   [Iowa City’s fare-free bus program](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/11/18/climate/iowa-city-free-buses.html) has been extended for another year after a successful two-year pilot saw ridership surpass pre-pandemic levels by 18 percent. The program led to 1.8 million fewer miles driven and a drop in emissions equivalent to taking 5,200 cars off the road.
    
-   Here’s an analysis of how the activist group Safe Street Rebel used [traffic cones to immobilize autonomous vehicles](https://www.mediapolisjournal.com/2025/11/playful-resistance/) in San Francisco. The piece frames the protest as “playful resistance,” a form of tactical urbanism that challenges the corporate takeover of public space and exposes the vulnerabilities of sensor-based tech.
