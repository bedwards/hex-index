Let's get started:

-   A new comic explores Mexico City's 1989 "[Hoy No Circula](https://www.crucialcomix.com/comic/hoy-no-circula-mexico-city-car-law-pollution-history-comic/)" law, which limited the number of cars on the road in an effort to fight pollution, and questions its effectiveness. Mercedes Campos López does a beautiful job turning a dry topic into an engaging story.
    
-   Los Angeles has opened its long-awaited [LAX/Metro transit center](https://www.latimes.com/california/story/2025-06-06/metro-opens-lax-station-offers-free-weekend-rides-across-system), connecting the K and C rail lines to the airport. But without the automated “People Mover” connecting directly to the airport, [some folks are underwhelmed](https://www.collegetowns.org/p/reviewing-the-new-laxmetro-transit).
    
-   Meanwhile, Lu Dadao, a leading Chinese economic geographer, argues that [China has massively overbuilt its high-speed rail](https://www.pekingnology.com/p/china-massively-overbuilt-high-speed), creating huge financial losses, underused infrastructure, and absurdly located stations.
    
-   [Kitty’s Laundrette](https://citychangers.org/kittys-laundrette/) in Anfield, Liverpool, is reviving its high street with eco-friendly laundry services, community events, and living-wage jobs. This worker-owned model generates £43 social value per £1 invested while rebuilding community connections and local commerce.
    
-   Kittys in Amsterdam will be a little safer soon as plans to install “[wildlife exit sites](https://nltimes.nl/2025/07/22/amsterdam-install-kitty-steps-canals-save-cats-drowning)” in canals to help cats and other small creatures are in development.
    

Videos, podcasts, books, and socials:

-   Here’s a news clip about those [(unpermitted) wooden benches](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2d9-gJ40v7Y) volunteers are installing at bus stops in the Bay Area to provide seating where none exists. $100 goes a long way.
    
-   BBC released a new video, “[Could ‘degrowth’ save the world?](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=596dU6pDEU8)”, exploring the movement’s roots in Barcelona, its global rise, and what a post-growth economy might look like, from housing co-ops to ecological justice.
