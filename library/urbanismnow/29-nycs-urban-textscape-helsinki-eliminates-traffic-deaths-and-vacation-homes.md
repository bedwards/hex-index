Let's get started:

-   [Helsinki has gone a full year without a single traffic-related fatality](https://www.politico.eu/article/helsinki-no-traffic-death-roads-eu-accident-finland-driving-transport/), a remarkable feat achieved via new speed limits, speed cameras, and many infrastructure changes. (Submitted by Oriane)
    
-   A new visual essay from The Pudding explores [NYC’s “urban textscape”](https://pudding.cool/2025/07/street-view/) by recording text from signs, graffiti, and advertisements found in public spaces using Street View. A pretty amazing technical feat along with a great analysis.
    
-   An analysis from Construction Physics shows that over [half of America's 4.8 million vacation homes are packed into just 4% of its census tracts](https://www.construction-physics.com/p/where-are-vacation-homes-located). These homes are limited by the same low construction productivity and restrictive zoning that impacts housing in cities.
    
-   [Chronotrains](https://www.chronotrains.com/en?city=Phoenix%2CUS), lets you see how far you can travel by train from any European station in up to five hours, using clever isochrone mapping and real travel data. It’s a fun, practical tool for trip planning or exploring Europe’s best, and worst, connected cities.
