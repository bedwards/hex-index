Apologies for the late newsletter. This week we attended the final graduation and reunion for [4CITIES](https://www.4cities.eu/). It was sad to see such a great program end, but wonderful to reunite with old friends and meet many proud alumni. Over 130 joined!

Let’s get started:

-   Fast-growing ‘[tiny forests](https://www.bbc.com/future/article/20250910-the-tiny-forests-that-thrive-in-cities)‘ using the Japanese Miyawaki method are spreading across Scotland and the U.K., bringing biodiversity and climate resilience benefits to urban areas.
    
-   Five of [Vancouver’s popular pop-up plazas](https://vancouversun.com/news/local-news/neighbourhood-plazas-become-permanent), which transform streets into public gathering spaces, are being made permanent fixtures.
    
-   Montreal is experimenting with [three cargo bike-share initiatives](https://nextcity.org/urbanist-news/these-cargo-bike-share-experiments-are-taking-off-in-montreal), including a nonprofit, a community-based platform and an integration with the city’s Bixi bikeshare system. The programs make cargo bikes more accessible by lowering barriers like cost, storage and theft.
    
-   Officials in the Philippines’ Department of Transportation are now [required to take public transit](https://newsinfo.inquirer.net/2109831/fwd-palace-on-dotr-officials-required-to-use-public-transport) once a week to better understand the needs of commuters.
    
-   Rail travel is [booming in America](https://www.economist.com/united-states/2025/09/21/rail-travel-is-booming-in-america), with Amtrak launching new European-style Acela trains between Boston and Washington, D.C.
    
-   Dutch tech startup Hardt Hyperloop set a [new European record for hyperloop technology](https://metro.co.uk/2025/09/16/europes-ultra-fast-hyperloop-trains-set-reach-435mph-breaking-new-speed-record-24170424/) after a successful lane-switching test in the Netherlands. Submitted by Oriane.
