Let's get started:

-   Despacio and Light Projects explore how Bogotá's new Metro underpasses could become "[Urban Rooms of Luminous Colo](https://despacio.org/2025/08/12/illuminating-bogotas-metro/)r" through participatory lighting design, rather than sterile LED corridors that divide communities.
    
-   Next City examines how Philadelphia's [AI-powered bus cameras](https://nextcity.org/urbanist-news/philly-is-using-ai-cameras-to-keep-bus-lanes-clear-transparency-trust) detected 36,000 violations in just 70 days, but cautions that transparency and fairness are essential for public acceptance.
    
-   Sweden's innovative ReTuna mall, the [world's first shopping center selling only secondhand items](https://nextcity.org/urbanist-news/swedens-secondhand-only-shopping-mall-is-changing-retail), transforms circular retail into a stylish, municipality-supported experience.
    
-   Los Angeles is one step closer to [eliminating parking minimums](https://laist.com/news/housing-homelessness/los-angeles-parking-requirements-housing-city-council-planning-committee-vote) for new housing after a city council committee advanced a proposal to study the policy change citywide.
    

Videos, podcasts, books, and socials:
