Let's get started:

-   The Surveillance Technology Oversight Project released [a handbook exposing how New York City's extensive surveillance network](https://www.stopspying.org/peoples-handbook) threatens civil liberties and reinforces racial inequities. The report calls for urbanists to think carefully about data collection in public spaces.
    
-   A study involving [5,400 individuals relocating across 1,600 U.S.](https://news.stanford.edu/stories/2025/08/walkable-cities-walk-score-daily-movement-steps-research) cities shows that those moving to walkable areas take 1,100 more steps daily. Check your [city’s walkability score](https://www.walkscore.com/how-it-works/).
    
-   An MIT doctoral student's [research on land-value taxes](https://news.mit.edu/2025/impactful-graduate-work-big-cities-campus-nick-allen-0820) is influencing policy debates in Detroit, proposing a way to encourage city-wide investment and reduce foreclosures. 
    
-   ​​The evolution of [children's playground infrastructure in Montevideo](https://www-elobservador-com-uy.cdn.ampproject.org/c/s/www.elobservador.com.uy/nacional/la-evolucion-los-juegos-infantiles-las-plazas-montevideo-las-jaulas-mono-las-hamacas-accesibles-y-pisos-goma-n6012058/amp) reflects an increasing awareness and commitment to creating inclusive public spaces, though they still primarily focus on physical accessibility.
    

In mobility:

-   Berlin citizens propose the [world's largest car-free zone](https://www.tomorrow.city/berlin-car-free-area/?utm_source=organic&utm_medium=social_linkedin_image&utm_campaign=BerlinCarFreeArea_2025&utm_target=general&utm_term=&utm_content=), covering an area larger than Manhattan, aiming to enhance urban living and environmental health.
    
-   Denver's Regional Transportation District is funding a pilot program that makes the [first mile of scooter and e-bike rides to two light rail stations free](https://www.9news.com/article/traffic/rtd-scooter-rides/73-a1946600-2de2-475e-a67c-b333cce6e834). The program was conceived by a graduate student.
    
-   [Intercity buses](https://www.smartcitiesdive.com/news/intercity-bus-city-stations-terminals/757761/) in the US are having a moment, with eight major cities investing in bus terminals.
    

Videos, podcasts, books, and socials:

-   A video from City Beautiful explains why [most one-way streets in U.S. cities should be converted back to two-way systems](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eNslRSeP7Cw), arguing they make local trips longer, increase crash severity, and can depress property values.
    
-   A podcast with cultural anthropologist Dr. Manissa Maharawal explores how [ethnography can be a tool for activism](https://www.publicethnographylab.com/podcast-episodes/s1-e1-ethnography-activism-maharawal), drawing on her work with the Anti-Eviction Mapping Project to contest tech-led gentrification in San Francisco.
    
-   On the lighter side, check out this YouTuber who built [a stealth camping dumpster](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=exWKlZWo2kA) to spend a night on the Venice Boardwalk.
    
-   We also enjoyed this short critical comic: [When a contractor designs the city](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/anuj-kale-a9769221_shreya-khandekar-and-i-try-to-take-public-ugcPost-7365228993274408960--1-x/).
