Let’s get started:

-   Peter Füssy for the Urban Cycling Institute, explores how [children’s books reinforce car-oriented thinking](https://urbancyclinginstitute.substack.com/p/rethinking-urban-futures-through?r=ifks&triedRedirect=true) from an early age and argues that radical children’s literature can help reimagine urban futures by challenging motonormativity and opening space for alternative mobility imaginaries.
    
-   An article from the International [Journal of Urban Sustainable Development](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/19463138.2025.2594259#d1e927) finds that 29% of building area in Nairobi was replaced, renewed, or removed between 2010 and 2021, showing cities change internally through slum formalization, rezoning, and urban renewal, not just by expanding outward.
    
-   New York City is the epicenter of a boom in [office-to-residential conversions](https://www.wsj.com/real-estate/commercial/nyc-office-residential-conversions-housing-4723b702). Over the past two decades, developers have converted nearly 30 million square feet of office space into residential living.
    
-   [Jakarta is now the world’s largest megacity](https://www.axios.com/2025/11/24/jakarta-tokyo-worlds-biggest-city-population) with nearly 42 million residents, surpassing Tokyo, according to the U.N. “World Urbanization Prospects 2025” report.
