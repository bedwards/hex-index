Let’s get started:

-   Social media is fueling the decline of local [trick-or-treating by promoting “destination neighborhoods,”](https://www.collegetowns.org/p/where-have-all-the-trick-or-treaters) which consolidates the activity into a few, often affluent, areas and turns a walkable community tradition into a car-dependent event.
    

-   [Bangkok’s formalized moto-taxi system](https://medium.com/lubyanza/moto-taxis-in-thailand-aabdc1b5907e) uses expensive, tradable jackets to limit driver supply, creating high barriers to entry and a stark divide with the massive, informal app-based gig economy. This regulated system, once a political win, has struggled to adapt and compete with the onslaught of ride-hailing apps.
    
-   Helvetas is building more [resilient cities in Bolivia](https://www.helvetas.org/en/switzerland/how-you-can-help/follow-us/blog/urban-engagement/the-urban-experiment-how-bolivia-is-building-more-resilient-cities) by strengthening urban planning capacity, promoting citizen participation through initiatives like innovation labs, and implementing digital tools. The model, now applied in 13 cities, uses nature-based solutions and circular economy principles to address climate change and social inequality.
    
-   A new academic article explores [why 259 U.S. transit agencies adopted fare-free public transport](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/keblowski_no-fare-no-fear-exploring-the-rationales-activity-7386602991245746176--nzo/) during the COVID-19 pandemic. The study found that knowledge-sharing networks, the need for physical distancing, and providing financial relief to essential workers were key drivers.
    

Videos, podcasts, books, and socials:

-   A new e-course, “[Digital Tools for Participatory Planning](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/urban-living-lab-center_urbanmobility-participatoryplanning-digitaltools-activity-7386657642242277376-Au37/),” explores how digital innovation can make cities safer, more inclusive, and sustainable. The self-paced course is available in English and Spanish.
    
-   [Forrest City, a $100 billion urban development in Malaysia](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y6ZGkjdFAZs), has become a ghost town with only 9,000 of its planned one million residents.
