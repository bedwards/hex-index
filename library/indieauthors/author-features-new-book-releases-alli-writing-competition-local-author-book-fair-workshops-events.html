Happy September, Indie Authors, Book Lovers, and Curious Creatives

May used to be my favorite month of the year, but after this scorching summer, I’m looking forward to September. I’m not surprised at all the happenings this month as the weather becomes more tolerable to be outside and enjoying nature. Locally, we have the James Dean Festival, Ducktail Run, and Jonesboro River Rally, which takes over our community the last weekend of September.

I’m taking advantage of the traffic and having a yard sale on Saturday, September 27th after I sort through my late mother’s belongings to give the family what they want and sell the rest to get her a headstone. Mother was my biggest fan. She showed up to all my events and supported my crazy ideas. I miss her so much!

Mom being a good sport getting her face painted by my artist-grandson at the bookstore I had at the Centrum Mall.

I’m considering opening another brick and mortar bookstore with a group of local authors. We are having our second planning meeting this month. I’ll keep you posted on our ultimate decision and progress! In the meantime, we will keep promoting indie authors’ books online and at local events.

Author Feature: Marissa Malson

The Not So Average Life of Average Jane by Marissa Malson

Marissa Malson is a mystery and suspense author, book publishing coach and marketing specialist. She is passionate about entrepreneurship and following your own path! She owns Just A Good Book Publishing and recently published her debut novel, The Not So Average Life of Average Jane. Jane is the first book in the Jane Smith, PI series. Book two can be expected in late 2026. She also writes Plot Twist on Substack, which focuses on empowering indie authors with publishing, writing, and marketing tips.

Welcome, Marissa! Congratulations on your debut novel. What’s it about?

This light-hearted mystery follows the adventures of Jane Smith, PI. Being invisible used to be her greatest weakness, but now she’s turned it into her greatest strength. She’s watching you, but you’ll never notice her because she might be average, but solving murders isn’t!

How did you come up with the idea for this story?

I was never intending to write a book, although I do have a background in journalism and content marketing. But one day after my regular yoga class the idea hit me. I heard this voice in my head, which was the prologue of the book, and it would not leave me alone. I grabbed my phone and wrote the first three chapters that night! And then quickly moved to writing on my computer :)

What do you hope readers will take away from Average Jane?

I had set out to write just a good book, which also happens to be the name of my company Just A Good Book Publishing. I love mysteries, but oftentimes as a reader when I would search through my TBR I wouldn’t find exactly what I was looking for. I wanted just a good story that wasn’t too gory, wasn’t too romantic, wasn’t too much of anything—just a good book!

Many readers have said that they have enjoyed Jane’s story because it is a light-hearted mystery. Others have said it’s about celebrating the ordinary and being true to yourself, which I was not expecting readers to take from the book!

What was the most challenging aspect of getting your book published?

Self-publishing was always my first choice. I have a background in marketing and my sister is a designer and she designed my book cover, so I thought publishing myself was something I could take on. But the most challenging part was all of the learning involved. I spent hours researching where to find an editor, how to publish on KDP and IngramSpark, how to build an ARC team, where I should have author profiles, and the list goes on.

In addition, I did workshops such as the Erma Bombeck Writers’ Workshop and I began a certificate program in Editing and Publishing from UCLA Extension, which I will finish in September 2025. However, the research and learning paid off and now I have a book I’m pretty proud of! I’ve also taken all of this knowledge to create the Ultimate Guide to Publishing on Amazon KDP and IngramSpark, which walks indie authors step-by-step on how to publish on these platforms, with the hope that their path to publishing will be a little less stressful than mine.

What do you do when you get writer's block?

When I feel stuck: I move! I go for a walk, do yoga, or anything with movement to clear my mind and give me new focus. Or I do something mindless, like doing the dishes. I’ve solved a lot of plot points that way!

If you could do a book signing anywhere in the world, where would it be?

Alsace, France. Part of Jane’s story takes place there so I feel like that’s the only right answer. Her story was inspired by my trip there. I fell in love with Alsace and got to meet family members who live there and many of whom I had not met before!

What advice do you have for aspiring authors?

Whether you are pursuing traditional or indie publishing I would say it’s so important to build your own team. Writing is a solitary task and publishing is a tough world to navigate alone. So build up your network, reach out to other authors, editors, marketers and have that support system in place. Also, give yourself wayyyyy more time than you think you need. Especially with a debut book, you’ll be learning some things by trial and error, so you will want extra time.

What has helped you sell books?

Making one-on-one connections and developing new and old relationships. I’ve been overwhelmed at the support and enthusiasm people have shown when they learned I wrote a book. I’ve had people reach out who I haven’t talked to in more than a decade to let me know they read my book. And I’ve been able to form new connections in my community through doing interviews and local book signing events. I’m so grateful to every one of my readers and supporters.

What is the most valuable lesson you’ve learned as an indie author?

The most valuable lesson I’ve learned is the importance of having a trusted team around you. My editor was amazing and she helped not only make Jane’s story shine, but she also made me a better writer. I was lucky enough to have a talented sister as a designer because people do judge a book by its cover. It’s critical to have a cover that conveys the genre but also grabs the readers attention. For all the indie authors out there, I would say your editor and your designer are your most important teammates.

What is one of your favorite quotes?

“You can write!” These words were first said to renowned columnist and author Erma Bombeck at her alma mater and mine, the University of Dayton. When she came to Dayton she had been feeling discouraged about pursuing writing and after a professor read her work, he sat her down and said “You can write!” I think of these words often and I know they, along with Erma’s story, have inspired so many writers.

Where can we find you?

I’m on Substack at Plot Twist. You can also find me on Facebook and Instagram as marissamalsonauthor. I am on LinkedIn and so is my company Just A Good Book Publishing. And my website is justagoodbook.com.

Anything else you would like to share?

Indie publishing is hard, but it’s so rewarding! For all the authors out there, my advice is keep going and keep writing. The world needs your story!

Curious Leadership: 50 Powerful Questions for Leaders at Every Level by

Hi! I’m Mel Rosenthal. I’m a 56 yo leadership coach by day and newbie writer by night. I’m based in Melbourne, Australia with my partner and our 10 year old mini Goldendoodle (or groodle as they're known down here). I’m a keen runner and love travelling the world to discover new friends, food and wine. Curious Leadership: 50 Powerful Questions for Leaders at Every Level is my first book, released in August 2025. I self published it on Amazon and am in the midst of learning all about book marketing. All your tips are very welcome!

I’m working on my second book now, which is VERY different - I wish I could tell Mum. What it’s about, I hear you ask. The truth is, that’s hard to answer. The idea came to me after a conversation with my aunt. She was celebrating as she’d won a prestigious award. She wanted to share the news with her Mum, but that wasn’t possible. Her Mum has advanced-stage dementia. I was struck by the heartache of knowing that her Mum was still alive, yet not present or available. I was inspired to tell the stories of people like my aunt. To capture their emotions and experiences so we can all understand them better. So, I guess I wish I could tell Mum is a book about missing a parent who is still alive. At the moment it’s a series of essays and I’m publishing them on Substack in the hope that it helps more people sooner. I plan to publish a chapter each month. I’d love your feedback so I can keep learning and improving.

You can find me on Substack, my coaching website and Linkedin. And you can find Curious Leadership: 50 Powerful Questions for Leaders at Every Level on Amazon here. I’d love to hear from you.

Happy Habits by

In a world that never seems to slow down, it’s easy to fall into reactive patterns that leave us feeling scattered, stuck, or burned out. That’s where Gunnar’s new book, Happy Habits, comes in. Drawing on modern behavioral science and years of real-world experience, Gunnar offers a practical guide to building clarity, energy, and momentum one small habit at a time. Instead of striving for perfection, Gunnar helps you reset with simple, meaningful changes that align with your values and fit your real life, even when things get messy.

Whether you’re recovering from burnout, seeking a fresh start, or simply looking for more balance in your creative journey, this book is a supportive companion to help you pause, plan, and practice habits that truly matter.
