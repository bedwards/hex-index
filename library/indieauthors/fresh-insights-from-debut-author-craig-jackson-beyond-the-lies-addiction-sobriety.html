Featured Author: Craig Jackson

Craig “C.S.” Jackson is an 11-year sober man on a mission to help the loved ones of addicts and alcoholics. He lives in New Hampshire, loves being involved with his two “screenagers,” playing “old man” basketball, and surfing (even in the winter in NH!). Now he adds writing to his favorite hobbies. Welcome to the writer world, Craig. It’s addictive in the best way!

Craig is in the process of launching his debut book Beyond the Lies. The ebook is available now and the paperback will be available January 20th. He already has the manuscript written for the second and third book in this series!

Beyond the Lies is for loved ones of addicts and alcoholics, who are frequently left out when it comes to recovery resources. With this series Craig takes the loved ones of addicts and alcoholics on a journey of understanding addiction in a way most people don’t, learning about the ways addicts manipulate their loved ones (so that the reader may protect themself), and he gives them a map for the sometimes long and windy road to recovery (for both the addict/alcoholic and their loved ones).

Read on to learn more about Craig and Beyond the Lies…

What do you hope readers will take away from your book?

Readers will gain an understanding that addiction is much more than just using drugs and alcohol. I pull back the curtain on addict/alcoholic behavior, what goes on in the mind of an addict, and show the loved ones how they have been affected by chronic exposure to their behavior. I write about rarely discussed aspects of addiction and exemplify how these concepts play out in real life through parts of my addiction story, my wife’s story as a loved one, and the stories of many other people who are children, siblings, partners, and parents of addicts/alcoholics.

What was the most challenging aspect of getting your book published, and how did you overcome it?

Deciding what publishing path to pursue–traditional publishing, custom publishing, or self-publishing–was the toughest. They all have their pro’s and con’s. The traditional publishing path seemed daunting, and to be honest I wanted to maintain creative control over the content and how it is delivered…but traditional publishing seemed to really be the way to gain instant credibility as an author. Self-publishing was attractive because I would maintain creative control, but finding all the resources to publish on my own felt like a tall task. So that left me with custom publishing. This is what I chose. Since I have a day job I had the money to pay a project manager at a custom publishing house to connect me with all the resources I needed. The challenge then became who to choose. I ended up choosing one I had a personal reference for and that was Clovercroft Publishing. Overall, it’s been a great experience.

How did you come up with the idea for your book?

A few years after I got sober, my oldest brother was still in the grips of addiction. Every now and then his partner would call me and say things like, “Your brother claims he is sober; however I just know in my gut something is not right with him, but I don’t have proof.” I would then ask her to describe the situation, and I would instantly know exactly what was going on because I did the same things he was doing when I was active in my addiction. I would tell her what I thought was going on and I was pretty much right every time. After many calls over the course of a year, she eventually told me that I should write a book to be the “secret decoder ring” of addict behavior. And so that’s what I did!! (note, my brother is now 6+ years sober and they both are doing great)

What is your favorite genre?

I love reading thrillers/suspense at night, it helps me disengage my brain from the day’s activities. I also love reading spiritual books, namely the modern mystics (the likes of Eckhart Tolle, Michael Singer, Wayne Dyer as well as many others!) and spiritual fiction (books like “Breakfast With Buddha”).

What is your writing routine, and any specific habits or rituals that have helped you stay productive?

I usually write in the morning while my mind is clear from daily clutter. Once I get going in my day job the mental clutter accumulates quickly! Because I am writing to help people who are touched by addiction, my writing must be useful to accomplish my goal. So, when I sit down to write, I set my intention by saying, “Please bring me the message that needs to be heard and help me arrange the message in a way that it can be received.”

How do you handle writer’s block or moments when you feel creatively stuck?

I don’t force it, when it’s not there it’s not there. When I am “in the flow” my hands just type. Even during a writing session which is going well, if it has taken its course and I am starting to have trouble, I just stop. For me it’s really a feeling. That being said, sometimes when I am feeling stuck, I will go back and do some editing, especially on stuff I had written months before. I find that sometimes when I go back to things I had written months before, I get re-inspired because I’ll be like “Damn! That’s pretty good!”

What are you working on next?

This project has morphed so much since it started, and as planned now Beyond the Lies will be a three-book series. I participate in some help groups for loved ones of addiction, and what I came to realize is that the content I first started writing needed my readers to have some foundational knowledge for it to be effective. So, this first book Beyond the Lies was born. The original content I was working on at the beginning of the project is now “book 2” of the series, and the manuscript is probably 70% complete. I am hoping to have that released in about a year.

Where is the most exciting or unusual place you have promoted your book?

I am very early in this promotion process, so right now this interview for Indie Author’s Association is! I am very excited to see what people who don’t know me think of my work. When your friends and family say they like your writing, you can’t trust them! It’s like when they taste something you made, say it’s good and then discreetly spit out the food into a napkin LOLOL.

What advice do you have for aspiring authors?

It’s not like I am Stephen King or Eckhart Tolle here giving advice…but here goes: Just do it! Sit down and write. Create a routine, try to stick with it and if you need to take an extended break, then do so. Don’t feel bad about taking a vacation from your writing. There were times where I didn’t write for months, and then when I got back to it, boy did it flow. Another recommendation I have is to put your writing out there for people to read. Yes, there are people who won’t like it, but you can’t please everyone. The gift is in that one comment you get from someone who really connected with your writing.

What is your favorite quote?

I guess right now it’s something my dad used to say, not sure where he heard it: “Don’t believe everything you think.” So powerful because my ego tries to get me to buy a nearly constant (usually negative) narrative, speaks to me in my own voice, and can talk me out of good decisions and into bad ones.
