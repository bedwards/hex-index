After the earnings call, my high-level takeaway was that Lip Bu hit a single. Singles are good. They put runners in position to score. (*Apologies. World Series on the mind.)*

What else stood out? LBT’s 14A gambit worked!

Yet it’s not all sunshine and roses for Intel.

The widely quoted line “current demand is outpacing supply” line from the [press release](https://www.intc.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/1753/intel-reports-third-quarter-2025-financial-results) is missing an important word.

[

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NvRz!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F576e0b65-075f-4fb5-88ca-05e22f42727b_1180x228.png)



](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!NvRz!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F576e0b65-075f-4fb5-88ca-05e22f42727b_1180x228.png)
*[Source](https://x.com/DeItaone/status/1981451400706937231)*

It should have read “current demand is outpacing supply **mix**”. That single word changes the outlook. Raptor Lake is *not* Core Ultra.

Lastly, I’m still frustrated with the industry’s fixation on AI PCs. The vision is right, the timing is wrong. And it keeps hampering Intel.

That said, net-net it’s a solid single and another data point in favor of the executive-in-chief.

Now, into the nuance—the fun part. *This gets long.*
