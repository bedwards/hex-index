The mystery of the missing fingers begins in a French cave. In 1985, explorer Henri Costs crawled 130 meters through submerged tunnels to reach a main chamber in Costa Cave. What he found was astonishing: prehistoric paintings depicting horses, seabirds, even a lion cub. The art was extraordinary — scenes of ice age life rendered by people who lived roughly 22,000 years ago during the Gravettian period.", "But something else caught his attention. Among the cave paintings were handprints — human hand stencils pressed directly onto the walls. These are among the most evocative forms of prehistoric artwork left behind.
