# ‘The Greatest Economy Actually Ever’

[

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!gPyJ!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fba076c2e-e5d5-4926-9538-2eb87a98f813_6400x4264.jpeg)

Ever since Donald Trump beat Kamala Harris in the popular vote, one thing was entirely predictable: The Joe Biden economy would quickly become the Donald Trump economy – and if Trump got his way with his orgy of tariffs and other policies, he would make America’s capitalist economy even more brutal for working families.

*A lot* of savvy Republican operatives and Trumpland figures (read: smug sociopaths) who I spoke to in late 2024 seemed to be in denial about this, *insisting* that Biden’s baggage was Biden’s baggage and Trump had a golden opportunity to remake the country [and the culture](https://zeteo.com/p/first-draft-trump-loses-super-bowl). I would ask them each the same question, in the same way: “**Are you guys gonna fuck this up**?” I can count the number of times on one hand that I got what I deemed a clear-eyed, credible, self-reflective answer from any of these elite Republicans.

It’s been a year. [They’re fucking this up royally](https://zeteo.com/p/gop-freaks-out-at-trumps-awful-numbers). Poll, after poll, after [high-quality poll shows](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/show/tamara-keith-and-amy-walter-on-trumps-low-approval-ratings) that the American people really didn’t like the Biden economy – and that everything they really didn’t like about the Biden-Harris economy, they *reeeeeeeealllly* do not like about the Trump-Vance economy.

“**Et tu, Rasmussen**?” a White House official messaged me, regarding a survey released this week by the Trump-friendly pollster showing more Americans are starting to see a rosier picture of the Biden presidency compared to Trump’s. “**If an election were held TODAY between Trump and Biden, Biden would win**,” Rasmussen pollster Mark Mitchell bluntly [stated](https://www.nj.com/politics/2026/02/a-gop-friendly-pollster-is-delivering-dire-warnings-to-trump.html) on Tuesday.

There are plenty of reasons why Trump’s job performance is polling on par with the less glamorous STDs.

One reason is that the Trump administration is committed to its increasingly unpopular programs of [ethnic cleansing and murder](https://zeteo.com/p/murder-inc). Also: it’s the economy, you [stupid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/It%27s_the_economy,_stupid) fascist.

And yet, the president is aggressively claiming credit for an abjectly cruel economy he’s improving for nobody but [his rich pals](https://www.oxfam.org/en/press-releases/billionaire-wealth-jumps-three-times-faster-2025-highest-peak-ever-sparking). He’s going on cable news to tell you that this is **“[the greatest economy actually ever](https://www.ms.now/news/bernie-sanders-rips-trump-greatest-economy-billionaires-ai),”** and that you’re [Jailable Fake News](https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2026/jan/22/trump-defamation-suit-new-york-times-poll) if your poll finds Americans acknowledging the country is not better off with Trump leading it. Trump’s lieutenants, such as White House trade adviser Peter Navarro, spent the early end of this week begging the public to yell at the White House less for [tanking the economy](https://thehill.com/business/5731440-peter-navarro-jobs-report-warning/) – with Navarro explaining, **“We have to revise our expectations down significantly for what a monthly job number should look like.”**

But then on Wednesday morning, as **Zeteo**’s senior politics editor Andrew Perez notes, the Labor Department announced it revised down its jobs numbers for 2025 to show the US economy only added 181,000 jobs last year, a third of what the government had previously reported. It’s the worst figure since 2020, the first year of the COVID-19 pandemic, and means the US [was in](https://thehill.com/business/5734225-january-jobs-report-trump/) **“a hiring recession”** last year. (During Biden’s presidency, the jobs market [was](https://seidmaninstitute.com/job-growth/year/) much stronger, especially early on, than it was in 2025, though it steadily cooled over time.)

At the same time, the Labor Department announced Wednesday that US employers added 130,000 jobs in January – leading to oddly positive news reports everywhere about the labor market suddenly being **“stronger than expected.”** Within the day, the White House was claiming that Wednesday’s jobs report was a sign that happy days are here again – despite the fact that if you talk to enough senior administration officials, they will candidly tell you (albeit anonymously) how much Trump’s economy is biting them in the ass with the voters.

Early in his first presidency, when Trump was presented with predictions and charts about a potential future economic disaster, he would sometimes respond with: *Your egghead chart shows that “**[I won’t be here](https://www.thedailybeast.com/trump-on-coming-debt-crisis-i-wont-be-here-when-it-blows-up/)**” when catastrophe strikes, so the unpopular decisions can just be another president’s problem, not mine*.

But he’s here now. The voters know it. They know it’s *his* and JD Vance’s economy. And so the voters have spent months signaling to this administration that, given the chance, they plan on electorally kicking the White House’s teeth in.

-   **House votes down Canada tariffs:** The House [voted](http://thehill.com/homenews/house/5734340-trump-tariffs-canada-republicans-rebuke/) to repeal Trump’s tariffs on Canada, with six Republicans joining all but one Democrat to pass the resolution. The vote came just a day after the House [voted down](https://www.reuters.com/legal/government/us-house-rejects-ban-challenges-trump-tariffs-democrats-ready-vote-canada-2026-02-11/) a measure from Speaker Mike Johnson that sought to continue blocking legislative challenges to Trump’s tariffs.
    
-   **Fox Lite:** CBS Evening News producer Alicia Hastey quit the David Ellison-owned, Bari Weiss-run outlet, and sent around a [scathing farewell note](https://x.com/benmullin/status/2021786653623304277?s=46) in which she said stories at the Tiffany Network were now “evaluated not just on their journalistic merit but on whether they conform to a shifting set of ideological expectations.”
