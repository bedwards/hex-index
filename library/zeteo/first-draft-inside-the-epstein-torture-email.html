Tweeting Through the PedoFiles

Amid its confused efforts to protect at least six powerful men associated with Jeffrey Epstein, the Department of Justice may have just revealed the hidden identity of someone involved in one of the more disturbing exchanges revealed from the Epstein files. We say ‘may’ because the Trump administration flatly refuses to give Americans real transparency.

This week, after repeated insistence from Congress, the DOJ allowed lawmakers to see the Epstein files, unredacted.

Except, as Rep. Ro Khanna told me, much of the important information was, curiously, still redacted.

Khanna said Monday night that some such redactions covered survivor statements “implicating rich and powerful men who raped these underage girls.”

“And that’s the information that the American people want to know: who are these powerful people who committed these heinous acts? Who were these people who showed up on Epstein’s island? And the fact is that they are being protected, and it’s disgusting,” Khanna added.

He noted that some of those redactions “protected” six specific individuals – “prominent” men from both the US and other nations.

But it appears that as the DOJ defended its stonewalling, it may have walked into a rake, perhaps accidentally revealing the identity of an individual whose identity was previously redacted. Or were they playing games?

On Monday night, Khanna’s colleague, Thomas Massie, took to Twitter and wrote, “A Sultan seems to have sent this. DOJ should make this public.” He attached an email exchange between Epstein and a redacted recipient, in which Epstein wrote, “I loved the torture video,” and the recipient replied: “I am in china I will be in the US 2nd week of may.”

Deputy Attorney General Todd Blanche responded, “…you know that the Sultan’s name is available unredacted in the files. See EFTA00666117,” presenting this as some sort of own. “Be honest, and stop grandstanding.”

That other file, EFTA00666117, features the name of Sultan Ahmed bin Sulayem – the chief of Dubai-based global logistics empire DP World, and the chairman of Dubai’s Ports, Customs & Free Zone Corporation.

Whether intentional or not, Blanche gave us the near feeling of transparency regarding a disturbing email – but it very well could have just been out of an instinct coursing through this administration’s veins: when in doubt, tweet and attack.

The DOJ did not immediately respond to Zeteo’s request for clarification about what Blanche meant.

The possible horror abounds far beyond that.

Nine years old. That’s how young victims of Jeffrey Epstein may have been, says Rep. Jamie Raskin.

The top Democrat on the House Judiciary Committee shared Khanna’s concern of many “suspicious and baffling” redactions in the documents, including one related to billionaire businessman and Epstein client Les Wexner.

“What I saw today was that there were lots of examples of people’s names being redacted when they were not victims,” Raskin said, noting that Wexner’s name was among them. He said he didn’t know whether the redactions were the result of “sloppiness and negligence” or something else, adding that Trump’s name was also redacted “in a number of different places.”

Also Monday, Epstein’s partner-in-crime, Ghislaine Maxwell, declined to answer lawmakers’ questions in a closed-door congressional deposition. Her lawyer said she would testify only if Trump – a longtime friend of Epstein’s – granted her clemency.

“Only she can provide the complete account. Some may not like what they hear, but the truth matters,” said her lawyer, David Oscar Markus. “For example, both President Trump and President Clinton are innocent of any wrongdoing. Ms. Maxwell alone can explain why, and the public is entitled to that explanation.”

The refusal infuriated some lawmakers. “That’s simply her hiding information,” Khanna said. “And she’s using it as a bargaining chip. She’s saying, ‘If you give me clemency, I’ll spill the beans. I’ll tell you who these people were who raped young girls on Epstein’s Island.’ I said she needs to immediately be sent back to a maximum security prison.”

Word on the Streeting

“Israel is committing war crimes before our eyes. Their government talks the language of ethnic cleansing… This is rogue state behaviour. Let them pay the price as pariahs, with sanctions applied to the state, not just a few ministers.”

This is what UK Health Secretary Wes Streeting, and the UK’s possible next prime minister, was saying in private to now-disgraced UK Ambassador to the US Peter Mandelson – whose name appears all over the latest tranche of the Epstein files.

Streeting released his messages in an attempt to stamp down speculation about his relationship with Mandelson, who was fired last fall after files revealed his continued association with Epstein. This week, Mandelson resigned from the Labour Party and the House of Lords, after the release of additional files revealed he allegedly received payments from Epstein and passed sensitive government information to him.

Streeting’s comments – while made privately – are the strongest rebuke of Israel’s genocide in Gaza so far by a sitting British cabinet minister.

The remarkable move by the Health secretary to release his messages, perhaps betrays how keen he is to generate pressure on Keir Starmer – who controversially appointed Mandelson as ambassador and is now under pressure to resign – and to garner support for his own rise to become the next prime minister instead.

It is worth noting that Streeting also notes in these private texts with the former UK ambassador to the US that he has “never been a shrinking violet on Israel,” adding that he has supported Labour Friends of Israel (LFI) for over 20 years.
