Sarah’s latest, [Inside a Teamster Rebellion: This Is What Union Democracy Looks Like](https://workdaymagazine.org/inside-a-teamster-rebellion-this-is-what-union-democracy-looks-like/), profiles Tanner Fischer, the 26-year-old president of the International Brotherhood of Teamsters Local 90 in Des Moines, Iowa, who has been working for UPS since he was 18. The story is a look into a militant local gearing up for what is potentially going to be the largest strike against a single company in U.S. history. A contract between UPS and around 350,000 Teamsters is set to expire July 31.

**Labor news in Minnesota**

Minnesota workers have been [busy](https://www.house.mn.gov/SessionDaily/Story/18002) pushing the DFL trifecta to pass laws protecting workers’ rights and safety, including [warehouse worker protections](https://workdaymagazine.org/high-injury-rates-push-minnesotas-amazon-workers-to-organize-for-safety/) and banning [noncompete covenants](https://workdaymagazine.org/how-secret-bondage-fees-trap-contracted-workers-in-low-wage-jobs/) and captive [audience meetings](https://workdaymagazine.org/what-captive-audience-meetings-are-and-why-minnesotas-labor-movement-wants-to-ban-them/). *Minnesota Reformer* has a handy list of these [labor victories](https://minnesotareformer.com/2023/05/17/labor-victory-minnesota-lawmakers-approve-9-major-worker-friendly-changes/).

After voting to authorize a strike that would’ve started May 15, mental health care workers with SEIU Healthcare Minnesota & Iowa [reached](https://www.cbsnews.com/minnesota/news/allina-seiu-reach-tentative-agreement-union-calls-off-7-day-strike/) a tentative agreement with their employer Allina Health. Over 500 workers who unionized in late 2021 have been fighting over a year for their first contract.

After Hastings school food service workers went on strike for 6 weeks earlier this year and won an agreement, the district is now [seeking](https://www.cbsnews.com/minnesota/news/hastings-food-service-workers-union-expresses-concerns-as-school-district-seeks-outsourcing/) to privatize food service.

Nurses with Minnesota Nurses Association that have been demonstrating at the capitol in support of the Keeping Nurses at the Bedside Act have been up against nonprofit hospital executives at the Mayo Clinic threatening to take their investments out of state. Lawmakers seem to have [caved](https://www.mprnews.org/story/2023/05/15/mn-legislators-add-hospital-carveout-to-nurse-staffing-bill) into some of the demands from the Minnesota Hospital Association. Meanwhile, another group of Mayo Clinic workers, along with the National Right to Work Foundation, are trying to [decertify](https://minnesotareformer.com/briefs/second-group-of-workers-at-mayos-mankato-hospital-file-petition-to-get-rid-of-union/) their union with AFSCME Council 65.
