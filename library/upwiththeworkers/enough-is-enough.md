# **[Why Do We Let Our Bosses Control Our Health Care?](https://workdaymagazine.org/why-do-we-let-our-bosses-control-our-health-care/)**

[

![A brown haired white woman wearing a light blue shirt reading "well-behaved women rarely make history" stands in front of a red emergency hospital sign](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!aI_n!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F88c7b901-6565-492b-a92b-0656915c4bc0_1200x800.jpeg "A brown haired white woman wearing a light blue shirt reading "well-behaved women rarely make history" stands in front of a red emergency hospital sign")



](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!aI_n!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F88c7b901-6565-492b-a92b-0656915c4bc0_1200x800.jpeg)
*Rose Roach is the national coordinator for the Labor Campaign for Single Payer, advocating for U.S. health care reform on behalf of workers. Photo by Sarah Whiting.*

In the past few years, the rate of Minnesota’s uninsured population has been at a historic low. Most people get healthcare insurance through their employer. But many people still lack access to affordable care in the state and throughout the U.S. Workers and their unions are tasked with fighting against increases in their healthcare plans, like Hennepin County workers with AFSCME Local 34, who have held recent actions against increases in their plans. Hennepin County contracts with United Medical Resources, or UMR Inc., a subsidiary of UnitedHealth Group, which is currently being [sued](https://www.dol.gov/newsroom/releases/ebsa/ebsa20230804) by the Department of Labor for wrongfully denying claims. For a joint article with *[Minnesota Women’s Press](https://www.womenspress.com/)*, I talked with Rose Roach, the new national coordinator for the [Labor Campaign for Single Payer](https://www.laborforsinglepayer.org/home), which is growing support in the labor movement for taking healthcare off the bargaining table by establishing a single-payer health care system in the U.S.

**“Housing is built with our hands.”**

On Tuesday, construction workers with CTUL and allies [marched](https://ctul.net/day-6-of-woa-taking-the-streets/) through Minneapolis to demand that developers Solhem, Yellow Tree, and United Properties join the [Building Dignity and Respect Program](https://workdaymagazine.org/new-program-seeks-to-end-wage-theft-and-abuse-in-the-local-construction-industry/). They left plaster molds of their hands in front of HenCen, one of Solhem’s recent projects, and also printed out a version of Isa’s article on wage theft, intimidation, and delayed treatment of construction workers: [Delayed Care, Stolen Wages: The Human Cost of Worker Misclassification](https://workdaymagazine.org/delayed-care-stolen-wages-the-human-cost-of-worker-misclassification/).
