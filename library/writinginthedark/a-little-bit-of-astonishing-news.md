IT’S HAPPENING!!!!

I’ve been sitting with this news for about a week now, waiting for it to be officially announced, and, meanwhile, letting it settle into my bones just like a good line eventually does—first with a startle, then slowly, then unmistakably.

Today, finally, I get to say it aloud:

**My next book—*****ONE WORD AT A TIME*****—based on** ***Writing in the Dark*****, has found its publishing home with Harmony Rodale/Penguin Random House, thanks to my incredible agent, Laurie Abkemeier.**

**It’s real!**

Even now, typing those words feels fantastical. But also inevitable, because this book didn’t come from nowhere.

It came from *you.*

Every single writer who has ever signed up or shown up here or to a class or a workshop or retreat or CAMP or last year’s SCHOOL or next year’s **[CRAFT SCHOOL](https://www.craft-school.net/)** feeling nervous or hopeful or tired or even outright scared but still willing. Every person who let me hold their story with reverence and curiosity. Every single edit someone trusted me to make. Every comment left on the posts here—celebratory, vulnerable, thoughtful, or just quietly present. Every writer who took my workshops in the early days before *Writing in the Dark*, before *The Part That Burns*, before my MFA, when I knew what I believed about writing, when I knew exactly what broke my own work open, and wanted to share that with other writers, but didn’t have much “street cred.” This book belongs to every one of you who has chosen to believe in this human project of doing language together, and waking ourselves up in the process.

You helped create this book as surely as I typed its first sentence.

And that first sentence has been a long time in the making. It’s been more than twenty-five years since I first started teaching, and more than fifteen since I began teaching creative writing exclusively. That’s a lot of years watching sparks become sentences, sentences become voice, and voice become power. A lot of years of watching people grow—not just as artists, but as beautifully awake humans.

This book is a love letter to all of it.

To the drafts and the doubts.  
To the sentences that resist.  
To the readers who see us even before we see ourselves.  
To the way language feels like oxygen.

To the way language moves in us like life itself.

Mostly—it’s a love letter to all of you. Without you, there would be no *Writing in the Dark*. Because this place has never been just a newsletter—it’s been a living, breathing creative commons: a place for risk and wonder, grief and permission, striving and celebration, experimentation and hilarity, disappointment and encouragement, and, most of all, creative growth. A place where art and life blur so thoroughly we stop pretending they were ever separate.

And it is absolutely because of *Writing in the Dark* that this book is finding its way into the world.

So thank you—each of you, past and present and still-to-come. Thank you for every story you’ve trusted me with. Thank you for every moment you chose to return to the page, even when it felt impossible. Thank you for helping me believe this work matters enough to keep going—one workshop, one post, one conversation, one sentence, one word at a time.

**We did it! We’re doing it! Together.**

And I am so grateful I get to write this life with you.

### Love, love, love,

### Jeannine

### **Because it turns out life marches on even when books are sold …**

So, this is our **last week of the** ***Infinite Small*** **intensive on micro flas**h—it’s been so fun, and I’m excited to go out with a bang, and also excited because…

**Our Essay in 12 Steps intensive starts in January!** When we did this one two years ago, it was extraordinary. Many of you reached out to tell me about the essays you published, and the ones I’ve been able to read online are truly stunning. If you want to put your writing on the front burner in 2026, you should join us. All you need is a **[paid membership](https://writinginthedark.substack.com/subscribe?utm_source=menu&simple=true&next=https%3A%2F%2Fwritinginthedark.substack.com%2F)** and you’re all set—no additional fees, nothing to sign up for, just watch the Wednesday posts.

Finally, **you’ll hear more about [CRAFT SCHOOL](https://www.craft-school.net/) in the weeks ahead**, especially after the new year, but, I have to tell you how moved and overjoyed I am at how [CRAFT SCHOOL](https://craft-school.net/) is taking shape. Working with Gina and Emily has been a dream. These are writers and teachers of the highest caliber who know beyond any shadow of any doubt that it’s community that makes for a long, productive, joyful writing life. They know, because it’s how they’ve built their writing lives, through membership in various literary communities, one after the other and many concurrently, in order to find their path and stay on it—which Gina wrote about so so beautifully here in this essay, **[Monday Motivation](https://substack.com/home/post/p-181108546)**.

And **[CRAFT SCHOOL](https://www.craft-school.net/)** is going to make this possible for so many others.

Those of you who’ve signed up already, I see you! I am so excited! We’re well past our minimum threshold and the ship is already sailing. If you haven’t joined yet and have questions I can help you answer, please reach out. The more clear we have become (and continue to become) about all that **[CRAFT SCHOOL](https://www.craft-school.net/)** will offer and everything that will transpire there, the more I believe that there is no better way to prioritize your writing and creative life, to prioritize and nourish your creative spirit, than to take a chance on **[CRAFT SCHOOL](https://www.craft-school.net/)**.

And as for the **December and January calendar of open mics and write-ins**, I need one more minute to get caught up after my ulcerated cornea and Jon’s surgery and Frannie’s kennel cough. And now Christmas. But I’m getting there, I really am, and I appreciate your patience and your love.

You’re amazing. Thank you!
