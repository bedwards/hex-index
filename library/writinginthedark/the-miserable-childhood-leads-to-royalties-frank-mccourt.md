And now for today’s micro flash! Since it’s a busy week for most of us, I decided to go realllllly small today, with six-word memoirs.

Most of you have probably heard about six-word memoirs, and have maybe even tried your hand at them before.

The Six-Word Memoir project has an interesting back story. It was launched in 2006 by Larry Smith, founder of *SMITH Magazine*, **who challenged people to describe their lives in exactly six words**. Smith’s idea was, of course, inspired Ernest Hemingway’s iconic six-word short story:

> “For sale: baby shoes, never worn.”

### **The Challenge**

The central challenge of the six-word memoir is to distill a life story (can be your whole life story or a specific life experience) into a compelling six-word sentence.

The idea of a six-word constraint is, of course, to force us into greater creativity as we struggle to say something meaningful within such an infinitesimal container.

Many thousands of people have responded to Smith’s challenge, including many whose names you might recognize:

#### **Lin-Manuel Miranda: Immigrants, we get the job done.**

#### **Aimee Mann: Couldn’t cope so I wrote songs.**

#### Stephen Colbert: Well, I thought it was funny.

#### **Deepak Chopra: Danced in fields of infinite possibilities.**

#### **George Saunders: Started small, grew, peaked, shrunk, vanished.**

#### And one of my favorites …

#### Frank McCourt: **The miserable childhood leads to royalties.**
