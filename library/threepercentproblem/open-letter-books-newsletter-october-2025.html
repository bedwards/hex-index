Things move fast during awards season.

Just a couple weeks ago, the 2025 Queen Sofia Spanish Institute Translation Prize Longlist was announced and included one of our books: Of Beasts and Fowls by Pilar Adón & Katie Whittemore. This is a great honor for a great book, and I highly recommended listening to this podcast, recorded during Pilar’s U.S. tour last fall, to get a better sense of what makes this lush, wondrous book about a cult/non-cult, about signs and symbols, about finding yourself, and about water.

Well, I waited a week too long to write a celebratory post about why Of Beasts and Fowls deserves to win the QSSI Translation Prize, about Adón & Whittemore’s next book, The Mayflies, about the last time Whittemore was a finalist (for Wolfskin by Lara Moreno), about last year’s winner, Heather Cleary and her Sergio Chejfec translations for Open Letter, etc., because the shortlist was announced yesterday and our book isn’t on it.

[No regrets though! Last Tuesday was the official pub date for To Essay by Rusana Bardarska, Christopher Buxton & Zornitsa Hristova, which is also a fantastic, rich, engrossing book and one that I highly encourage you to check out.]

Congrats to all!

Last thing: I’ve teased this a million times, but I’m not actively in process of updating the Translation Database—and could use your help!

This was started in 2008 to provide a comprehensive list of all titles published in translation for the first time (no retranslations, no paperback editions of previous published hardcovers) in the United States (or at least distributed here). We began by tracking fiction and poetry, and when we moved the database to Publishers Weekly, we added both nonfiction and children’s books.

As the only source in the world collecting this information, it’s served as an invaluable tool for analyzing publishing trends, researching international literature, identifying who is publishing what, etc. During the pandemic . . . it all went awry. And due to changes in my relationship with Publishers Weekly the database has been very dormant the past couple years.

But it’s time to change that! The question as to where the Translation Database will be hosted in the future is an open one (anyone know how to design a website like the PW one? If so, hit me up), but for now, you can find ALL the data on this Google Sheet.

As you’ll see, there’s a lot missing. Children’s and Nonfiction are still a work in (backward) progress as I work from 2025 toward 2008 to fill in the gaps; and everything post-2021ish is pretty spotty.

But you can help! If you’re a publisher of translations who sends out hard copies of your books, please consider sending them my way (Chad W. Post, University of Rochester, Morey Hall 303, Rochester NY 14627) and I’ll log them by hand, include them on a “Books Received” post, and consider including them in monthly posts about findings from the Translation Database. (Ebooks are fine as well, although I can almost assure you I will lose these in my email, or bury them in some other way.)

Also, publishers, authors, translators, literature fans can fill out this form to the best of their ability, and, after vetting, I’ll be able to add these to the database. Don’t expect this to happen immediately, but the plan is to update the database once a week in whatever form it ends up taking.

Thanks in advance to anyone who helps out with this, and even if it takes six months or a year to get it all up to date and as complete as possible, I’m dedicated to making this happen.
