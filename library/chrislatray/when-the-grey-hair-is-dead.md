*Boozhoo, indinawemaaganidog! Aaniin! That is to say hello, all of my relatives! Welcome to another edition of* An Irritable Métis*. I’m not going to sugarcoat it: November has been a rough one to keep up with, as reflected by my absence in this space. The second-to-the-last week of the month served as culmination[1](#footnote-1) to an abundance of time spent on the road for about as long as I can remember, concluding with eleven speaking engagements spread over four days, with multi-hour drives separating most of them. It was a lot! And now, with that behind me, I’m slowly returning to myself (and catching up on correspondence)*[2](#footnote-2) *and, having nothing beyond a couple zoom events scheduled for December (see below, in fact!), you can expect to hear more from me these remaining weeks of 2025. In just a day or two will be the monthly sentences and shortly after that another post I’ve been making notes toward for a couple weeks now.*

*This post serves mostly – via convenient bullet points, a format I rarely employ – to share a couple things I think you might be interested in, a couple events you might want to participate in and, finally, a few things I think are important that might interest you in this holiday season. I hope you think they are too. As I mentioned, a proper essay will follow next week.*

*I’m grateful you are here. There’s been a lot of stressful tumult in the newsletter sphere these last few weeks that has been distracting and made me question to point of doing it at all*[3](#footnote-3)*. The constant and what makes it work for me has been the ongoing support of all of you. Whether you pay for your subscription or not, I’m happy to have you. Paid ones help, and I appreciate them more than you might realize, but they will never be required to be part of what’s going on here and there will never be stuff you have to pay to see. [As Sarah says](https://sarahkendzior.substack.com/), “I would never paywall in times of peril.” I agree. I also subscribe to the idea that people know what they can afford and what is valuable to them, and that – the ability to pay – shouldn’t give more opportunity for stuff like this than it does for those who can’t. Things are tight and I get it.*

# Random Updates

-   I hope your Thanksgiving was decent. Mine was! It consisted of a small gathering at my sister’s house of pretty much the entirety of the immediate family I have in the area, which meant there were seven of us. The food was excellent (a highlight was the homemade sourdough rolls my mom brought, which I slathered in copious butter) and there was no stress[4](#footnote-4). I don’t get caught up in the narrative of what Thanksgiving is supposed to mean and never really have. For a couple few decades now for me it’s just been a day to eat and chill. Still, if you want a reminder, or need a refresher, on the ongoing bullshit narrative, you won’t find a better primer than what [Rebecca Nagle](https://bookshop.org/p/books/by-the-fire-we-carry-the-generations-long-fight-for-justice-on-native-land-rebecca-nagle/288762f2ed30e05a?ean=9780063112056&next=t) (Cherokee) posted the other day, which you may view **[HERE](https://gohini.substack.com/p/the-myth-of-thanksgiving-is-as-american)**.
    
-   I usually watch *[The Last of the Mohicans](https://youtu.be/SAUDWVuOUq0?si=-yBVFOWr2tWVbQzO&t=20)* on Thanksgiving. This year I watched ***[Trudell](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dlcmTeCcbME)***. It’s a good one.
    
-   Speaking of Rebecca, she and I were on a panel together last summer at the Mississippi Book Festival in Jackson, MS. I forgot that that discussion – which was moderated by Tammy Greer (United Houma Nation) and also included Jay Wesley (Choctaw) – was filmed by CSPAN. I’ve recently learned the discussion has been posted online and may be viewed **[HERE](https://www.c-span.org/program/book-tv/native-americans-identity-and-cultural-preservation/666479)**. What I remember most about it is that the event was held in Jackson’s old supreme court chamber, and we were seated up on the bench where the judges would have been. It was very representative of the grandiose power dynamic of such a place and I found it unsettling and a little disturbing. I enjoyed the talk quite a bit though, and it ended far too quickly.
    
-   The great **[Semi-Rad](https://semi-rad.com/)** – aka Brendan Leonard – wrote a wonderful post the other day about how *One-Sentence Journal* inspired him to start writing short poems tied to his daily running escapades. I was very moved by the things he had to say and I think you might dig his post too, which you will find **[HERE](https://semirad.substack.com/p/i-forgot-to-stop-writing-bad-poems)**. Brendan and I both live in Missoula, have many mutual acquaintances, have both led multiple workshops for the **[Freeflow Institute](https://freeflowinstitute.com/)**, and have certainly been in the same room together in multiple instances, yet I don’t think have officially “met” in person[5](#footnote-5). Hopefully we can change that in 2026, as I’ve been an admirer of his work for a few years now.
    
-   I’ve been a regular listener and huge fan of **[The Red Nation](https://www.therednation.org/)** and their podcast for years now, so you can imagine my delight when [Nick Estes](https://bookshop.org/p/books/our-history-is-the-future-standing-rock-versus-the-dakota-access-pipeline-and-the-long-tradition-of-indigenous-resistance-nick-estes/1b93bb0c8e26ab8f?ean=9798888900826&next=t) invited me to join him in discussion of *Becoming Little Shell* when I was in Minneapolis back in October. It was a lot of fun, and you may check it out **[HERE](https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/becoming-little-shell-w-chris-la-tray/id1482834485?i=1000738078023)**. Thanks, Nick!
    
-   Speaking of Minneapolis, Nick and I recorded the podcast at the University of Minnesota, specifically in their American Indian Studies building. The [Ojibwe People’s Dictionary](https://ojibwe.lib.umn.edu/), which I use almost daily, is hosted there, and while there I was also introduced to the [Midwest Indigenous Immersion Network](https://www.miinojibwe.org/) (MIIN) and their excellent language **[app](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/miin-ojibwe/id6751417874)**. Great stuff! They gave me a sticker too!

# Seasonal Support via Commerce

Black Friday is behind us. Small Business Saturday is behind us. And now Cyber Monday looms on our immediate horizon; it’s possible you’re reading this on that very day, or even after it. The nice thing is when it comes to buying online, you’re still a couple weeks away from being too late!

My friend [Anna Brones](https://annabrones.bigcartel.com/) wrote **[THIS NEWSLETTER](https://creativefuel.substack.com/p/seasonal-deals-8be?publication_id=1323568&post_id=179646959&isFreemail=false&r=h5l6&triedRedirect=true)** about the complications between making and selling for artists. This quote in particular grabbed me:

> According to a [survey](https://www.americanexpress.com/en-us/newsroom/articles/shop-small/2025-american-express-shop-small-impact-study-results.html) done by American Express, small business owners estimate that Small Business Saturday sales will be responsible for 20% of their annual revenue. For a lot of artists I know, it’s way more than that. It’s a consequential, make-or-break time for most small businesses, artists, and creatives dependent on retail as a part of their livelihood.

20% is a lot and I don’t doubt that the number is accurate! Similarly, Brendan Leonard also wrote about this struggle **[HERE](https://semirad.substack.com/p/6-things-ive-learned-about-designing?publication_id=2771401&post_id=180295385&isFreemail=false&r=h5l6&triedRedirect=true)**. Both Brendan's and Anna’s posts are important reads, I believe, and I urge you to do so … and consider supporting them with a merch purchase as part of this year’s gift giving regimen.

### What About Me?

I’m often amazed by the frequency of people telling me they “can’t find” my books. They are referring mostly of course to my first two, which came out via small presses and yours truly is essentially the distributor. But they are, and have always been, linked to on my website right **[HERE](https://www.chrislatray.com/books)**. Signed copies of every one will always be available at my home bookstore – **[Fact & Fiction](https://factandfictionbooks.com/)** in Missoula. If you want them – and this goes for any book you order from any independent bookstore – please don’t wait too long. This is a busy season and the sooner you jump on your orders the better it is for everyone.

#### And remember: I’ll happily personalize any books you order from F&F! Just indicate what you’d like in the comments!

Finally, subscriptions to this newsletter are hugely beneficial to me. You can also gift subscriptions to anyone you think might like what happens here.

# Oh yeah, there are ROCK SHOWS too!

The other night, Friday the 28th, my kid’s ultra-heavy stonerdoom band **[SWAMP RITUAL](https://swampritual.bandcamp.com/album/vol-iii)** played a show opening for a couple touring bands. They had thirty minutes to play and decided to devote it all to KISS covers in honor of the late Ace Frehley. They asked if I’d join them onstage to sing the classic “God of Thunder” to close the set, and of course I said yes. It was a blast. Which is a long way to introduce you to the reality that my blistering rock band **American Falcon** has an unprecedented TWO SHOWS coming up in December! I can’t remember the last time that happened.

-   **December 12th** – [The Grange](https://www.rockymountaingrange.org/) in Hamilton, 7:00pm. Friends, I’m not sure we’re a good fit for this but we’ll play anywhere once. We aren’t a “bar band” in any sense of the word and we don’t do covers … though we intend to dust off six or eight of the few we’ve accumulated over the years. We are loud and unrelenting and the last time we played this room many, many years ago under completely different context the breakers couldn’t handle our raw power. So we’ll see how it goes. The show is a benefit for **[Montana Public Radio](https://www.mtpr.org/donate)** so there’s that too.
    
-   **December 19th:** Monk’s Bar in Missoula. I don’t know what time. Get there at 8:00 or 8:30? We’re playing with a band called **Places Called Home** and, of course, the mighty **Swamp Ritual**. It’s going to be a good time.
