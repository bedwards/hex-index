Cultivated chicken is real chicken meat grown directly from animal cells rather than from raising and slaughtering a bird. Scientists take a small sample of chicken cells and place them in a controlled environment, usually a stainless steel bioreactor, where the cells are fed nutrients like amino acids, vitamins, minerals, and energy sources that mimic what they'd receive inside a living animal.

Those cells multiply and mature into muscle and fat tissue. The same types of tissue that make up conventional chicken meat.
