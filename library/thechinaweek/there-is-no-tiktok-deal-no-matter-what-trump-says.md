U.S. Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent [said](https://www.thechinaweek.com/p/tiktok-deal-touted-as-chinas-snubs) after trade talks last weekend in Madrid, Spain that details of a TikTok deal with China “will be confirmed in a Friday call” between Donald Trump and Xi Jinping.

That call happened this morning, September 19 Washington D.C. time. A few hours later, Trump [posted to his personal social network](https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/115231649861246548) that “the call was a very good one, we will be speaking again by phone, *appreciate the TikTok approval*” (italics mine).

But the [Chinese readout of the call](https://www.pekingnology.com/p/chinese-readout-of-xi-trump-phone) shows no such thing. This is all the document says about TikTok:

> On the TikTok issue, Xi stated that China’s position was clear: the Chinese government respects the will of the businesses, …
