#### Controlling the means of production

# Rare leverage

This week has been full of bluster coming from Washington, D.C., and justified concern around the world about China’s new [export control rules on rare earths](https://www.thechinaweek.com/p/china-plays-rare-earths-card-hard), which are key to global manufacturing of cars, electronics, and military hardware.

China’s rare earths leverage appears to be driving the Trump administration to distraction. U.S. Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent [called](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-10-16/bessent-calls-china-trade-negotiator-an-unhinged-wolf-warrior) China’s senior negotiator Lǐ Chénggāng 李成钢 “unhinged,” and suggested without evidence that he may have “gone rogue.” Trump threatened a bajillion percent tariffs (OK, the number was 100%) but then softened—again—and talked—again—[about meeting Xi Jinping](https://www.scmp.com/news/world/united-states-canada/article/3329466/trump-confirms-xi-jinping-meeting-calls-100-china-tariffs-not-sustainable) and making a deal.
