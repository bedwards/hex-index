-   **[VitaDAO raises $5.1M USD in token sale](https://twitter.com/vita_dao/status/1408464661649952768?s=20)**[.](https://twitter.com/vita_dao/status/1408464661649952768?s=20) [VitaDAO](https://www.vitadao.com/) is a decentralized autonomous organization that funds longevity research and commercializes the resulting intellectual property -- all through the [Ethereum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethereum) blockchain. The genesis auction of the DAO’s governance tokens ([$VITA](https://www.livecoinwatch.com/price/VitaDAO-VITA)) raised a total of ~2,500 ETH = ~ $5.1M USD over six days (June 18 - June 23). The current VitaDAO market capitalization is ~$16M.
    
    VitaDAO’s successful token sale is a positive development in this experimental world of DAOs as internet-native collective funding vehicles for longevity or other frontier areas of technology. The potential benefits of funding longevity through a DAO compared to traditional VC funds are numerous:
    
    -   **Democratization** - not restricted to accredited investors.
        
    -   **Reduced friction** - obtaining $VITA governance tokens takes less than a minute using Uniswap and a [MetaMask wallet](https://metamask.io/).  
        
    -   **Decentralized participation** - Anyone, anywhere can be part of the organization, vote on proposals, earn $VITA tokens for doing tasks for the DAO, or be a recipient of the research funding. 
        
    
    There’s a vibrant community forming around VitaDAO and I encourage anyone who is interested to check out their [Discord channel](https://discord.com/invite/3S3ftnmZYD) to get involved (~1600 members and growing). Researchers and startups can also get in contact with the VitaDAO community in order to submit funding proposals. *Disclaimer: I participated in the VitaDAO token sale and am HODLing $VITA.*

-   **[Vita Therapeutics raises $34M Series A round led by Cambrian Biopharma.](https://endpts.com/can-a-cell-therapy-treat-muscular-dystrophy-a-german-billionaires-anti-aging-startup-is-trying-to-find-out/)** Vita Therapeutics is a biotech startup that builds on the work of [Gabsang Lee](https://www.hopkinsmedicine.org/profiles/details/gabsang-lee) and [Kathryn Wagner](https://treat-nmd.org/about/contact/kathryn-wagner/) at Johns Hopkins. The company is developing a stem cell therapy to treat muscular dystrophies (first in [limb-girdle muscular dystrophy](https://www.mda.org/disease/limb-girdle-muscular-dystrophy)). The Series A round was led by [Cambrian Biopharma](https://www.cambrianbio.com/), one of the big multi-company [“hub-and-spoke”](https://www.fiercebiotech.com/biotech/bio-bridgebio-ceo-kumar-its-hub-and-spoke-model-and-where-else-it-could-work) umbrella companies in the longevity space (Cambrian was founded by [Sebastian Brunemeier](https://ch.linkedin.com/in/sebastianaguiar), [Christian Angermeyer](https://twitter.com/c_angermayer?lang=en), and [James Peyer](https://www.linkedin.com/in/jamespeyer))  
      
    Vita is developing a stem cell therapy where blood cells are taken from a patient, turned into stem cells ([induced pluripotency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Induced_pluripotent_stem_cell)), genetically engineered by editing out disease-causing mutations, turned into [myosatellite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Myosatellite_cell) cells, and then engrafted back into the patient. Cambrian’s Peyer noted that experiments in mice showed good integration of the muscle stem cells and improved grip strength and treadmill performance.   
      
    The application of this myosatellite cell approach won’t work for the more well-known form of dystrophies like [Duchenne muscular dystrophy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duchenne_muscular_dystrophy) (no myosatellite cells in the heart). There is potential that Vita’s approach could reverse aging in skeletal muscle through regeneration. However, there is also a debate as to whether muscle stem cells can flourish in an aged microenvironment ([Novak et al. 2021](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1111/acel.13411?campaign=wolearlyview) argue the limitation for regeneration is the aged tissue microenvironment, not the age of the transplanted cells).

-   **[Wake Forest researchers win the NASA x Methuselah Foundation Tissue Vasculature Challenge prizes.](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/nasamethuselah-challenge-winners-make-history-by-engineering-sustaining-live-human-tissue-in-a-laboratory-301309212.html)** Two teams of researchers at Wake Forest, led by [James Yoo](https://school.wakehealth.edu/Faculty/Y/James-J-Yoo) and [Anthony Atala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthony_Atala), were awarded winners of the [Vascular Tissue Challenge](https://www.nasa.gov/directorates/spacetech/centennial_challenges/vascular_tissue/the-challenge.html) sponsored by NASA and the [Methuselah Foundation](https://www.mfoundation.org/). Initially conceived in 2013, the challenge required researchers to engineer an in vitro vascularized tissue at least 1 cm thick that could provide adequate blood flow for at least 30 days. Both groups used [3D bioprinting technologies](https://www.nasa.gov/press-release/teams-engineer-complex-human-tissues-win-top-prizes-in-nasa-challenge) to win the challenge.  
      
    Tissue engineering is a valid approach to reversing aging -- one that doesn’t require the understanding of the complex biochemical processes behind aging and metabolism. It may even be possible to [reverse aging in the brain](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3s31WctpHSM) by progressive replacement of neural tissue as [Jean Hebert](https://einsteinmed.org/faculty/9069/jean-hebert/) is attempting at [Albert Einstein College of Medicine](https://einsteinmed.org/). (See “[Replacing Aging](https://www.amazon.com/Replacing-Aging-Jean-H%C3%A9bert-ebook/dp/B08GJPWW3X)”)  
    
-   **[David Sinclair announces the development of a low-cost epigenetic age test.](https://www.doctorsinclair.com/)**  During our last [Longevity Panel discussion](https://longevitybiotechshow.com/017-longevity-panel-the-scientists-working-to-reverse-aging-june-15th-2021/) on June 15th, [David Sinclair](https://twitter.com/davidasinclair) announced that he was developing a low-cost epigenetic age test (likely for consumers). Epigenetic age tests measure [DNA methylation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DNA_methylation) at [CpG](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CpG_site) sites, the pattern of which can be used to [predict chronological age](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epigenetic_clock).   
      
    The usefulness of epigenetic clocks for [surrogate biomarkers](https://www.fda.gov/drugs/development-resources/surrogate-endpoint-resources-drug-and-biologic-development) of aging in something like a clinical trial is still up for debate; the causal mechanism is totally unclear. However, during a recent [Foresight Institute call](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YdCHBqcehHI), [Steve Horvath](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steve_Horvath) -- the godfather of epigenetic clocks and UCLA researcher who is owed an Illumina T-shirt -- made the prediction that the development of an aging biomarker would likely come from a commercial venture, driven by capitalist market forces.   
      
    One commercial example of this type could be the application of the [GrimAge clock](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30669119/) (which is correlated to mortality) at [FOXO Technologies](https://foxotechnologies.com/), a life insurance company. It is also possible that a DTC low-cost epigenetic age test that is used at scale in millions of people over long time periods (and linked to medical records) could lead to the development of a robust aging biomarker (lots of assumptions here).    
    
-   **[Heartseed raises $37M USD Series C](https://www.fiercebiotech.com/medtech/heartseed-collects-new-venture-round-following-598m-heart-failure-stem-cell-deal-novo).** [Heartseed](http://www.heartseed.jp/en/) is a stem cell therapy biotech company based in Japan. After signing a [~$598M USD deal](https://www.novonordisk.com/news-and-media/news-and-ir-materials/news-details.html?id=61691) with [Novo Nordisk](https://www.novonordisk.com/) on June 1st, the company went on to announce the completion of a $37M Series C funding round on June 11th. 
    
    Heartseed is developing an [allogeneic](https://en.wikipedia.org/?title=Allogeneic&redirect=no), [iPSC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Induced_pluripotent_stem_cell)\-derived [cardiomyocyte](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muscle_cell) cell therapy to repair heart muscles in patients with [ischemic heart failure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heart_failure) (usually caused by narrowing of coronary arteries, [atherosclerosis](https://www.webmd.com/heart-disease/what-is-atherosclerosis)). Their novel approach uses spherical clusters of cardiomyocytes implanted in the heart muscle wall versus the more commonly used cell sheets engrafted to the heart surface While the longevity biotech drug scene in Japan is limited ([Taisho](https://www.taisho.co.jp/global/)’s [senolytic partnership](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/insilico-partners-with-taisho-on-end-to-end-ai-powered-senolytic-drug-discovery-301153031.html) with [Insilico Medicine](https://insilico.com/) being one exception), there are many Japanese stem cell companies -- stem cell therapy regulations in Japan are [fairly relaxed](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-019-02847-3). 
    
-   **[Continuum Biosciences receives $2.2M in grant funding from the NIH.](https://www.eurekalert.org/pub_releases/2021-06/vt-drr061421.php)** [Continuum Biosciences](https://continuumbio.com/team) is a biotech company that develops mitochondrial uncouplers to treat [NASH](https://www.niddk.nih.gov/health-information/liver-disease/nafld-nash) (a type of fatty liver disease), obesity, and other metabolic diseases. The company announced on June 14th that it had received a $2.2M grant from the [NIH](https://www.nih.gov/).  
      
    [Mitochondrial uncouplers](https://astralcodexten.substack.com/p/shilling-for-big-mitochondria) are a class of drugs that intentionally make mitochondria less efficient at [oxidative phosphorylation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxidative_phosphorylation) (usually by allowing protons to leak across the inner membrane), allowing for more glucose to be used up to create the same amount of ATP. Simply: These drugs allow for the burning of more fat without exercising.  
      
    Continuum is developing a mitochondrial uncoupler molecule called BAM-15 (or analogs) that acts similarly to a now-banned dieting drug, [2,4 dinitrophenol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2,4-Dinitrophenol), but without all the negative side effects. [In preclinical studies](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-020-16298-2), researchers at Continuum were able to demonstrate that BAM-15 could reduce liver fat and prevent diet-induced obesity and **glucose intolerance** without altering food intake, lean body mass, or body temperature.  
      
    Continuum Biosciences is one of David Sinclair’s [Life Biosciences](https://www.lifebiosciences.com/) portfolio companies. [Equator Therapeutics](https://equatortherapeutics.com/), a [Y Combinator](https://www.ycombinator.com/) company is also developing similar mitochondrial uncoupler drugs.  
      
    For an overview of longevity companies developing mitochondrial therapeutics see my previous newsletters: [A Map of Mitochondria Longevity Companies Part 1](https://sub.longevitymarketcap.com/p/022-mitochondria-longevity-companies).  
    
-   **[Retrotope announces initiation of Phase 2 study of RT001 to treat Progressive Supranuclear Palsy (PSP).](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2021/06/30/2255495/0/en/Retrotope-Announces-Initiation-of-Phase-2-Study-of-RT001-in-Patients-with-Progressive-Supranuclear-Palsy-PSP.html)** [Retrotope](https://www.retrotope.com/) is a California-based biotech company that is using [deuterated polyunsaturated fatty acids (D-PUFA)](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC6546729/) to treat a number of diseases linked to lipid peroxidation and oxidative stress, including [PSP](https://www.ninds.nih.gov/Disorders/Patient-Caregiver-Education/Fact-Sheets/Progressive-Supranuclear-Palsy-Fact-Sheet). On June 30th, the company announced it was initiating a [Phase 2 clinical trial](https://clinicaltrials.gov/ct2/show/NCT04937530?term=retrotope&draw=2&rank=4) for [progressive supranuclear palsy (PSP)](https://www.ninds.nih.gov/Disorders/Patient-Caregiver-Education/Fact-Sheets/Progressive-Supranuclear-Palsy-Fact-Sheet), a rare neurodegenerative disease characterized by loss of nerve cells involved in locomotion, balance, and eye movement.  
      
    [Lipid peroxidation](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5684309/) of mitochondrial membranes is believed to be linked to mitochondrial dysfunction, one of the so-called “[Hallmarks of Aging](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3836174/)”. Retrotope’s D-PUFA therapy is believed to intervene in the lipid oxidation chain reaction through a trick of chemistry called the [kinetic isotope effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kinetic_isotope_effect).
    
-   **[MetroBiotech announces collaboration with US SOCOM military to trial NAD+ enhancing drug next year](https://breakingdefense.com/2021/06/socom-to-test-anti-aging-pill-next-year/)**[.](https://breakingdefense.com/2021/06/socom-to-test-anti-aging-pill-next-year/) [NAD+](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicotinamide_adenine_dinucleotide) boosting supplements are becoming extremely popular in the wellness/biohacking longevity circles -- though evidence of any anti-aging benefit still needs to be determined through clinical trials. Now the US military wants to get in on the action by teaming up [MetroBiotech](https://www.metrobiotech.com/), a biotech company co-founded by [David Sinclair](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_A._Sinclair) that develops NAD+ boosting drugs. A trial of the company's anti-aging, “performance-enhancing” drug is scheduled for next year. 
    
    NAD+ is an important coenzyme that is involved in metabolism in all living cells. NAD+ levels decline with age and it is believed that restoration of these levels could have some anti-aging benefit. For the most thorough review of NAD+ restoration therapy please see Forever Healthy’s [insanely comprehensive review](https://brain.forever-healthy.org/display/EN/NAD+Restoration+Therapy).
    
-   **[GenFlow Bioscience looking to publicly list on The London Stock Exchange.](https://genflowbio.com/)** [GenFlow Bioscience](https://genflowbio.com/) is a UK-based biotech company that is developing a gene therapy to treat aging in humans and dogs. According to my source (also confirmed by a detail on [LinkedIn](https://be.linkedin.com/in/steven-beckett-48baa856?trk=org-employees)), GenFlow is preparing to go public on the London Stock Exchange this year.  
      
    The company has a Phase 1/2 human trial planned for [Werner syndrome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Werner_syndrome), a progeroid “accelerated aging” disease (note that [progeria and aging are not the same](https://www.fightaging.org/archives/2021/06/thinking-of-progeria-as-accelerated-aging-only-produces-confusion/)). GenFlow’s therapy uses an adeno-associated virus vector to deliver the cDNA that encodes for a centenarian [SIRT6](https://www.genecards.org/cgi-bin/carddisp.pl?gene=SIRT6) gene. SIRT6 is a member of the highly-conserved [sirtuin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sirtuin) proteins that play a role in cell metabolism and DNA repair. [A recent study](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-021-23545-7) showed that overexpression of SIRT6 in mice resulted in a 15% - 27% increase in median lifespan and an 11% - 15% increase in maximum lifespan. 
    
    More longevity companies going public is generally a good thing -- for a list of publicly traded longevity biotech stocks check out [longevitymarketcap.com](https://longevitymarketcap.com/)

## Adventures in Longevity

I’ve been working full-time doing what I can to accelerate progress in longevity for just under a year now. 

In that time, I’ve started a [longevity investing newsletter](https://sub.longevitymarketcap.com/), [jobs board](https://longevitylist.com/), [industry database](https://longevitylist.com/longevity-industry-database/), [podcast](https://longevitybiotechshow.com/), [longevity biotech stock tracker](https://longevitymarketcap.com/), [clinical trial tracker](https://longevitymarketcap.com/longevity-trial-tracker/), [mega longevity panel discussion series](https://futureoflongevity.org/), [venture fund](https://healthspancapital.vc/), dabbled in longevity [streetwear](https://shop.longevitylist.com/), made my first angel investment in a longevity company, met (virtually) many of my heroes, made so many new friends in longevity who have helped me along the way, and taken on the responsibility of Program Director for the [Longevity Biotech Fellowship](https://www.beondeck.com/longevity-biotech/) at On Deck.  
  
And yes, some longevity memes were also made. (Exhibit [A](https://twitter.com/realnathancheng/status/1371884314032545793?s=21), [B](https://twitter.com/realnathancheng/status/1392209517476261895?s=21), [C](https://twitter.com/realnathancheng/status/1398717332415975427?s=21), [D](https://twitter.com/realnathancheng/status/1411405449778372608?s=21)).

My relatively short but surprising adventure in longevity thus far reminds me of something Marc Andreesen wrote: 

> “The world is a very malleable place. If you know what you want, and you go for it with maximum energy and drive and passion, the world will often reconfigure itself around you much more quickly and easily than you would think.”

I definitely believe this to be true — and in longevity especially, people are willing to help others who put in the effort.

There are two important keywords here, though:

**Passion**. How do people know if you are really passionate about something, like longevity? As readers of Kundera’s *[The Unbearable Lightness of Being](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Unbearable_Lightness_of_Being)[1](#footnote-1)* would know, the word *passion* comes from the Latin root for “pain”. I know founders in this space that faced rejection after rejection from investors. Those who watched as their peers climbed career ladders while struggling with their own startup. Be aware that the path is often very painful and failure is common.

**Go.** Aging is a problem of the highest urgency and highest unmet need. 100,000+ people die of age-related diseases every day. The manner of decline and suffering associated with aging is inhumane for many -- and unfortunately, it’s in the cards for everyone if nothing is done about it. We need those who recognize the scale of the problem of biological aging to **go take** **action today**.  
  
If you have been on the fence about getting involved in longevity consider taking the leap. There is [capital](https://www.longevity.technology/michael-greve-commits-e300m-for-rejuvenation-start-ups/) flowing into the space and **[a lot more](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=x9TSJK1widA&t=2005s)** is on the way. There are a number of [requests for startups](https://www.fightaging.org/archives/2021/01/request-for-startups-in-the-rejuvenation-biotechnology-space-2021-edition/) from knowledgeable leaders in the field. There are programs to support longevity founders and operators (like the one I am organizing at [On Deck](https://www.beondeck.com/longevity-biotech/)).  
  
All we need now is **passionate people to go out there** and meet the challenge of aging head-on. 

\-NATHAN

**Projects**

-   **[On Deck Longevity Biotech Fellowship (Program Director)](https://beondeck.com/longevity-biotech/)**\- On Deck Longevity Biotech (ODLB) is a continuous community for people to come together to build, join, or invest in revolutionary longevity biotechnology startups.
    
-   **[Healthspan Capital](https://healthspancapital.vc/) -** An early-stage longevity biotech venture fund.
    
-   **[Longevity List](https://longevitylist.com/)**  - Find jobs, companies, and investors in the longevity industry. Email me if you want to add your job or company to the list. **It’s free.**
    
    -   **[Longevity List Database](https://longevitylist.com/longevity-industry-database/)**\- A handy Airtable base covering people, companies, trials, academic labs, and a list of longevity companies that are presently raising capital.
        
-   **[LMC Clinical Trial Tracker](https://longevitymarketcap.com/longevity-trial-tracker/)**\- Longevity therapeutics in clinical trials. Completion calendar useful for investors who want to time data readout catalysts.
    
-   **[LongevityMarketcap.com](https://longevitymarketcap.com/)**\- The main page tracks prices of publicly traded longevity stocks.
    
-   **[The Future of Longevity](https://futureoflongevity.org/)**\- A monthly series of public Q&As in themes and trends with experts in longevity science and industry. Co-hosted by [Laura Minquini](https://www.linkedin.com/in/lauraminquini), [Avi Roy](https://about.me/agingroy), and me. 
    
-   **[Longevity Biotech Show](https://longevitybiotechshow.com/) -** Official website of the Clubhouse show / [podcast](https://open.spotify.com/show/6DGY45bpJMxT2I4x8FGDip?si=8eL9tqI_QpmnmyhppFMpWQ). We interview the people building, funding, and championing technologies that extend healthy human lifespan. On Clubhouse / [Spotify](https://open.spotify.com/show/6DGY45bpJMxT2I4x8FGDip?si=8eL9tqI_QpmnmyhppFMpWQ) podcasts. 
    
-   **[forum.longevitybase.org](https://forum.longevitybase.org/)** - An open question and answer system for aging biology and longevity biotechnology. 
    
-   **[The One Percent Bet for Longevity Pledge](http://onepercentbet.org/) -** An initiative to get individuals and corporations to pledge at least 1% of their resources to fund longevity companies and research.
    
-   **[LongevityBase.org](https://longevitybase.org/) -**  An open-source volunteer initiative to itemize every instance of modulation of biological aging in the scientific literature.
    
-   **[Humans for Longevity](https://humansforlongevity.com/)** - A literary art project featuring the stories and motivations behind the people working on solving the problem of aging.
