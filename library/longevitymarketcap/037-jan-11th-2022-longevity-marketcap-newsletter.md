### Industry:

#### **🧬[Coinbase CEO, Brian Armstrong, announces $105M funding to launch a new epigenetic reprogramming startup called NewLimit.](https://twitter.com/brian_armstrong/status/1469821720781484032)**

\* *errata: Original publication stated NewLimit’s focus was on radically extending human “lifespan.” This has been corrected to “healthspan”.  
*[  
](https://twitter.com/brian_armstrong/status/1469821720781484032)The crypto & longevity crossover continues. [NewLimit](https://www.newlimit.com/) is a newly announced [epigenetic reprogramming](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17638084/) startup founded by [Coinbase](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coinbase) founder and CEO, [Brian Armstrong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brian_Armstrong_\(businessman\)), along with [Blake Byers](https://twitter.com/byersblake?lang=en) (biotech investor formerly at [Google Ventures](https://www.gv.com/)). The company has raised $105M and is focussing on “*radically extending human healthspan*” by first going after indications in eye disease and immunosenescence using reprogramming before age reversal in all tissues.

With this announcement the cellular reprogramming race continues to heat up. It was only a few months ago that [Altos Labs was leaked](https://www.technologyreview.com/2021/09/04/1034364/altos-labs-silicon-valleys-jeff-bezos-milner-bet-living-forever/) – a new reprogramming mega-company backed by [Jeff Bezos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jeff_Bezos), [Yuri Milner](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yuri_Milner), and [Rick Klausner](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Klausner) to the tune of $270M USD (with a rumored $6B earmarked), hiring a large fraction of the top researchers in reprogramming/epigenetics. **In my opinion, Altos was the biggest development in LongBio in 2021**.   

Altos and NewLimit are not alone, however. [Retro Biosciences](https://retro.bio/), [Iduna Therapeutics](https://www.longevity.technology/life-biosciences-in-licenses-ip-from-new-studies/), [Turn Biotechnologies](https://www.turn.bio/), [Shift Bioscience](https://www.shiftbioscience.com/) (I’m an investor), [AgeX Therapeutics](https://www.agexinc.com/) (NYSE:AGE), and [YouthBio Therapeutics](https://youthbiotx.com/)  are also pursuing the reprogramming paradigm for aging.   

The basic idea behind epigenetic reprogramming is to reset the **gene expression** of old cells to that of a young cell **without** turning the “clock” all the way back to a pluripotent embryonic state (often referred to as “partial reprogramming”). This can be achieved using some combination of transcription factors like the [Yamanka factors](https://www.longevity.technology/yamanaka-factors/) (OSKM) or other factors / potentially small molecules. The reprogramming technique traces its roots back to [Shinya Yamanka’s Nobel Prize winning work](https://www.nobelprize.org/prizes/medicine/2012/press-release/) on [induced pluripotency (2006)](https://www.cell.com/fulltext/S0092-8674\(06\)00976-7). Development of partial reprogramming is more recent, some notable examples: [Ocampo](https://ch.linkedin.com/in/alejandro-ocampo-46678539)/[Belmonte’s](https://www.salk.edu/scientist/juan-carlos-izpisua-belmonte/) [in vivo partial reprogramming work in mice (2016)](https://www.cell.com/fulltext/S0092-8674\(16\)31664-6), and [David Sinclair](https://sinclair.hms.harvard.edu/people/david-sinclair)’s reprogramming work demonstrating the [regeneration of the optic nerve in mice (2019)](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-020-2975-4).  

Epigenetic reprogramming is one of the most promising and perhaps **overhyped** approaches to reversing aging. It promises “full” rejuvenation at the cellular level and potentially applicable to all cell types. However, it is still very early days – there **has not** yet been a demonstration of lifespan extension in wildtype mice using reprogramming. Reprogramming also would not likely be able to address aging damage in DNA mutations or crosslinks in the extracellular matrix. There’s also questions of safety regarding tumor formation. Reprogramming remains a high risk, high reward bet. 

#### **🧬 [Cambrian Biopharma raises $100M Series C.](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/cambrian-biopharma-closes-100-million-series-c-to-advance-healthspan-boosting-therapeutics-to-the-clinic-301408017.html)** 

  
[Cambrian Biopharma](https://www.cambrianbio.com/) is one of the big distributed drug development companies (“DisCo”) [hub-and-spoke](https://www.fiercebiotech.com/biotech/bio-bridgebio-ceo-kumar-its-hub-and-spoke-model-and-where-else-it-could-work) companies in the longevity space. At the end of October, Cambrian announced that it had raised $100M in a Series C round led by [Anthos Capital](https://anthoscapital.com/) and [SALT Fund](http://salt-fp.com/). The funds will enable Cambrian to advance their [existing programs](https://www.cambrianbio.com/pipeline) and work with more scientific collaborators. Cambrian has 14+ programs under its umbrella, including assets from [Sensei Biotherapeutics](https://senseibio.com/) (phage cancer vaccine), [Vita Therapeutics](https://www.vitatx.com/), and several others that have not been made public.  

The hub and spoke DisCo model consists of a parent holding company that holds stakes in daughter biotech companies. Shared centralized resources in the parent company can be deployed in a flexible manner to help develop the assets of the portfolio companies. DisCos are touted as being capital efficient and flexible (compared to traditional VC / venture studio), but are more suited for asset-based portfolio companies and not [platform-based](https://www.nature.com/articles/d43747-020-00184-3) or [founder-led](https://founderledbio.com/) portfolio companies.   

Cambrian Biopharma, based in New York, was founded in 2019 by James Peyer, Christian Angermeyer, and Sebastian Brunemeier.  
  
*For more info on Cambrian and their hub-and-spoke approach, I highly recommend you check out the wonderful [Translating Aging Podcast episode](https://open.spotify.com/episode/2lj2K6ZMQm8FwdEtKb49Bh?si=6q-y17m4QLauZWXcE6FyUg) with Cambrian CEO, James Peyer.*  

#### **🧬 [Jean Hebert’s Brain Aging Reversal Tissue Engineering receives a $1M grant from The Methuselah Foundation.](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/methuselah-contributes-1-million-h%C3%A9bert-lab-brain-tissue-colangelo)** 

In early December, [Jean Hebert](https://einsteinmed.org/faculty/9069/jean-hebert), professor of neuroscience and genetics at Albert Einstein College of Medicine announced receipt of a $1M USD grant from the [Methuselah Foundation](https://www.mfoundation.org/) to support his work on **[progressive](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29548515/)** [](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29548515/)**[replacement](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29548515/)** of neural tissue in the neocortex.   

[Tissue engineering](https://www.nibib.nih.gov/science-education/science-topics/tissue-engineering-and-regenerative-medicine) and cell therapy replacement strategies to treat disease or reverse aging is an exciting and fundamentally comprehensive approach that avoids having to tinker with the unfathomable [complexities of metabolism](http://www.sigmaaldrich.com/technical-documents/articles/biology/interactive-metabolic-pathways-map.html). Jean Hebert’s strategy to go after the most difficult organ to replace, the brain, is **one of** **the defining bioengineering projects** of our time. It’s a moonshot worthy of much more attention considering how little progress has been made on traditional single target [approaches to tackling neurodegenerative disease](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-018-05719-4).

#### **🧬 [Lineage Cell Therapeutics partners with Genentech for eye disease.](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20211220005028/en/Lineage-Establishes-Exclusive-Worldwide-Collaboration-With-Genentech-for-the-Development-and-Commercialization-of-OpRegen%C2%AE-RPE-Cell-Therapy-for-the-Treatment-of-Ocular-Disorders)**

  
[Lineage Cell Therapeutics](https://lineagecell.com/) ([NYSE:LCTX](https://longevitymarketcap.com/stock/lctx/)), an [embryonic stem cell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Embryonic_stem_cell) platform startup working on cell therapy for regenerative medicine, announced a partnership with biotech giant [Genentech](https://www.gene.com/) (part of the [Roche](https://www.roche.com/) group) to develop its [OpRegen program](https://lineagecell.com/products-pipeline/opregen/) for dry [age-related macular degeneration](https://www.webmd.com/eye-health/macular-degeneration/age-related-macular-degeneration-overview). The deal included $50M dollars up front with up to $670M in milestone payments ([biobucks](https://www.statnews.com/2016/11/28/biotech-biobuck-deals/)). Lineage’s share price was up 15% on the news.   

Lineage’s [OpRegen](https://lineagecell.com/products-pipeline/opregen/) is a treatment for dry age-related macular degeneration – the leading cause of vision loss for older individuals in wealthy nations. The OpRegen cell therapy consists of [allogeneic](https://www.mayoclinic.org/tests-procedures/allogeneic-stem-cell-transplant/pyc-20384863) embryonic stem cells that are differentiated into [retinal pigment epithelial cells](https://www.nature.com/articles/eye2001141) (RPE) and surgically transplanted into the retina. The therapy is currently in [Phase 2 trials](https://clinicaltrials.gov/ct2/show/NCT02286089), with some very promising [clinical data](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20210720005358/en/OpRegen%C2%AE-Clinical-Data-Continues-to-Demonstrate-Functional-and-Anatomical-Improvements-in-Patients-With-Dry-AMD-With-Geographic-Atrophy) showing sustained improvements in vision in treated patients.

#### **🧬 [Endogena announces $20M additional Series A funding](https://www.biospace.com/article/endogena-completes-series-a-with-total-funding-of-29-million-to-progress-treatments-for-degenerative-diseases-of-the-eye-/?s=80).**

  
[Endogena](http://endogena.com/) is a Swiss-based longevity biotech startup that is developing drugs that stimulate endogenous regeneration in tissues in the body. The company announced an additional $20M in Series A funding, bringing a total of $29M raised to date. The funding was led by [Rejuveron](https://rejuveron.com/en), one of the large [DisCo](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/life-sciences/our-insights/a-new-portfolio-model-for-biotech) / hub-and-spoke companies in the longevity space. 

  
The funding will allow Endogena to continue to advance its program, [EA-2353](http://endogena.com/odd.html), a drug that stimulates the regeneration of retinal photoreceptor cells to treat [retinitis pigmentosa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Retinitis_pigmentosa), a type of progressive eye disease that causes loss of vision. Clinical trials for EA-2353 are expected in 1H 2022.   

Some other companies developing drugs to stimulate endogenous regeneration include [Frequency Therapeutics](https://www.frequencytx.com/) (NASDAQ:FREQ), [Biosplice Therapeutics](https://www.biosplice.com/) (formerly Samumed), and [Morphoceuticals Inc](https://www.juvlabs.com/organization/partner/morphoceuticals-inc).

#### **🧬 [Alliance for Longevity Initiatives jumpstarts political lobbying for geroscience and longevity.](https://a4li.org/2021/11/creating-a-bipartisan-movement-to-increase-healthy-lifespan-in-the-us/)**

The [Alliance for Longevity Initiatives](https://a4li.org/) is a new 501(c)(4) non-profit organization founded with the goal of “*creating social and political action around the issues of combating age-related chronic conditions and increasing our number of healthy, disease-free years.*” 

Lobbying for longevity, essentially. 

The organization, helmed by [Dylan Livingston](https://www.linkedin.com/in/dylan-v-livingston), held their first bipartisan public panel in December to discuss how to involve Congress in longevity and how to grow a grassroots campaign in this space. ([recording here](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XOcJtxWsejY))

Panelists included former Speaker of the House of Representatives [Newt Gingrich](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newt_Gingrich), former Congressman [Steve Israel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steve_Israel), Director of the Institute for Aging Research at Albert Einstein College of Medicine [Nir Barzilai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nir_Barzilai), M.D., Partner at the SALT Fund [Dina Radenkovic](https://www.linkedin.com/in/dinaradenkovic) M.D., and A4LI Chair of the Board [Sonia Arrison](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sonia_Arrison).

**My take:** I cannot stress enough how important this organization is, and how important it is to get longevity on the political agenda. Currently, **less than 1%** of the NIH budget goes towards geroscience. Political campaigns were hard fought to bring cancer and HIV/AIDs research to the forefront of public support. Aging/geroscience will need to go through this same fight eventually. Might as well start now.  

To quote [Siddartha Mukherjee’s](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siddhartha_Mukherjee) biography of cancer, *[Emperor of All Maladies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Emperor_of_All_Maladies):* 

> “For any illness to rise to political prominence, it needed to be marketed, just as a political campaign needed marketing. A disease needed to be transformed politically before it could be transformed scientifically. ​​If Farber’s antifolates were his first discovery in oncology, then this critical truth was his second.”

*I’ve become a donating member of A4LI, and I highly recommend others to do so too. You can donate to A4LI or become a member [here](https://a4li.org/become-a-member/).*

*Roundup of 2021 - key stats in the Longevity Biotech industry. Data from [longevitylist.com](https://longevitylist.com).*

2021 was an incredible year for longevity biotech. 

There were SPACs, IPOs, DAOs, new $100M funds, new clinical trials, and new mega companies (specifically in epigenetic reprogramming). 

Let’s take a look back at the investment landscape and capital inflows into the LongBio industry in 2021.

### $3B USD total funding in 2021

\**Update March 17, 2022: Since publishing this post, [Altos Labs](https://altoslabs.com) publicly launched with $3B USD in funding, much larger than the reported $270M in the MIT Technology Review scoop.*

New capital flowed into the LongBio sector to the tune of $3B in 2021 (based on publicly announced financings).

To be fair, a large percentage of this funding was Calico and AbbVie’s renewed partnership ($1B in 2021). But outside of Calico, there were several 100M+ financing rounds, from SPACs, IPOs, or venture financing rounds.

### 36 Completed Capital Raises

2021 saw 36 publicly announced capital raises in longevity biotech, which included Altos Labs, New Limit, Loyal, and many others.

Here’s the breakdown by financing type

-   32 private venture deals / funding
    
-   2 IPOs
    
-   1 SPAC
    
-   1 acquisition

### 10+ New Longevity Biotechs Founded in 2021

Ten or more new longevity biotech startups were founded in 2021. This is definitely **an incomplete figure** as most biotech companies stay in stealth mode for some time before officially announcing. We might not know the true count for 2021 for a year or more.  
  
For reference, 2020 saw 14 new longevity companies founded (probably incomplete data, too). Not much can be said about trend until 2021 companies start coming out of stealth.  
  
Take away: **[We need more longevity founders](https://youtu.be/T41x36Ydq1k?t=578).**
