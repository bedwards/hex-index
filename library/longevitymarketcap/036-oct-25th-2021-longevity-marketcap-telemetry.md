*I have so much to tell you.*

-   **[Altos Labs, a new anti-aging mega-biotech by Jeff Bezos, Yuri Milner, Rick Klausner, and many others.  
      
    ](https://www.technologyreview.com/2021/09/04/1034364/altos-labs-silicon-valleys-jeff-bezos-milner-bet-living-forever/)**It’s finally here!  
      
    I had written about Jeff Bezos’s rumored involvement in a big longevity initiative since [May](https://sub.longevitymarketcap.com/p/032-may-18th-2021-longevity-marketcap). Earlier in September, an [MIT Technology Review](https://www.technologyreview.com/2021/09/04/1034364/altos-labs-silicon-valleys-jeff-bezos-milner-bet-living-forever/) scoop filled in some of the details of this new company, Altos Labs, and its roster of luminaries:
    
    -   Backed by Israeli-Russian billionaire [Yuri Milner](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yuri_Milner) (Breakthrough Prize) and [Jeff Bezos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jeff_Bezos). *Rumors are that [Bob Nelson](https://www.archventure.com/team/robert-t-nelsen/) at [ARCH](https://www.archventure.com/) is also involved (not surprising) as well as [Bill Maris’s](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bill_Maris) [Section 32](https://section32.com/) (Bill Maris co-founded [Google Calico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calico_\(company\)))*  
        
    -   Helmed by biotech titan, [Rick Klausner](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Klausner) (NCI, [Juno Therapeutics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Juno_Therapeutics)). [Shinya Yamanaka](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shinya_Yamanaka) will chair the scientific advisory board.
        
    -   At least **$270M raised already**. (*My sources indicate it is actually 2x this amount.* 
        
    -   [David Sinclair](https://sinclair.hms.harvard.edu/people/david-sinclair) indicated Altos **had** **$6B** during our interview last week at [On Deck](https://www.beondeck.com/longevity-biotech/). 
        
    -   Altos Labs appears to be focussing on **cellular reprogramming** -- a nascent technology tracing its roots to the Nobel Prize-winning work of Shinya Yamanaka that allows old cells to reset their state to one resembling a young embryonic stem cell. Modern approaches include partial or [transient reprogramming](https://www.cell.com/fulltext/S0092-8674\(16\)31664-6), with or without the OSKM [Yamanaka factors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Induced_pluripotent_stem_cell), in order to rejuvenate cells.
        
    -   Altos is poaching top scientific talent by offering $1M+ salaries and equity. [Manuel Serrano](https://www.irbbarcelona.org/en/research/manuel-serrano), [Wolf Reik](https://www.babraham.ac.uk/our-research/epigenetics/wolf-reik), [Steve Horvath](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steve_Horvath), [Juan Carlos Izpisúa Belmonte](https://www.salk.edu/scientist/juan-carlos-izpisua-belmonte/), and other top academic experts in cellular reprogramming have left their posts to join Altos. 
        
    -   Multi-site: USA, Europe, Japan, etc.
        
    -   The initial focus will be on doing “great science”.
        
    
    **My take:** Altos Labs is a welcomed development in longevity -- high-profile names will raise awareness, legitimacy, and capital for this space. However, the question on everyone’s mind within the community is how Altos Labs will avoid disappointing like [Google Calico](https://www.calicolabs.com/), which, contrary to [Time magazine’s clickbait](http://content.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,2152422,00.html), has **not** solved death yet -- much less produced an FDA-approved therapy for any indication.[1](#footnote-1)  
      
    Altos Labs, like Calico, seems to be focused on basic science, initially. But unlike Calico, Altos appears to be focused on a very specific therapeutic approach, cellular reprogramming -- which several other [LongBio](https://longevitylist.com/what-does-longbio-mean/) startups are hotly pursuing ([Shift Bioscience](https://www.shiftbioscience.com/), [Retro Bioscience](https://retro.bio/), [Turn Bio](https://www.turn.bio/), [Iduna Therapeutics](https://www.lifebiosciences.com/our-science), and [AgeX Therapeutics](https://www.agexinc.com/)).   
    
    The alleged $6B earmarked for Altos Labs is just one of the big announcements slated for the longevity industry this year. **Hevolution**, a yet-announced initiative based in the Middle East and helmed by ex-Life Biosciences CEO, [Mehmood Khan](https://www.linkedin.com/in/mehmood-khan-6648696), is rumored to have $20B committed towards longevity.

[Loyal](https://loyalfordogs.com/) is a dog healthspan extension biotech startup founded by [Celine Halioua](https://twitter.com/celinehalioua/). The project has raised $20M+ from individuals such as [Juan Benet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Juan_Benet_\(computer_scientist\)) (IPFS), [Jed McCaleb](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jed_McCaleb), and [Vitalik Buterin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vitalik_Buterin). -   **[Astera’s $70M Rejuvenome project partners with the Buck Institute](https://www.buckinstitute.org/news/astera-institute-and-buck-institute-announce-70-million-collaboration-to-redefine-the-field-of-research-on-aging/)**. The newly formed [Astera Institute](https://astera.org/longevity/) announced they would be working with the [Buck Institute](https://www.buckinstitute.org/) for their $70M Rejuvenome project -- a large-scale multi-omics study of anti-aging interventions and combinations of interventions in mice.
    
    While there are several interventions known to [extend maximal lifespan in mice](https://www.ldeming.com/longevityfaq), very few studies have been tried on combinations. There’s reason to believe that combining multiple interventions could increase lifespan in additive or even synergistic ways. For instance, [metformin combined with rapamycin](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5892694/) increases lifespan in mice additively.

Armstrong also came out in support of longevity in general. The Dog Aging Project is a study involving 10,000+ dogs to track changes in canine biomarkers during aging. A small [rapamycin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sirolimus) trial will also be conducted in a subset of dogs.

-   **[Elevian raises $40M Series A](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/elevian-raises-40-million-series-a-to-advance-treatments-for-stroke-recovery-and-age-related-diseases-301378696.html). [](https://www.elevian.com/)** [Elevian](https://www.elevian.com/) is a longevity biotech startup that is developing recombinant [growth differentiation factor 11 (GDF11)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GDF11), a “young blood” circulating factor believed to be involved in improved tissue regeneration. The proceeds from Elevian’s Series A round will enable them to file an IND with the FDA and initiate Phase 1 clinical trials using GDF11 to treat stroke. [Prime Mover Labs](https://www.primemoverslab.com/) led the round, with [Bold Capital](https://www.boldcapitalpartners.com/), and Leslie Ventures also participating.
    
    Elevian is based on the scientific work of [Amy Wagers](https://hsci.harvard.edu/people/amy-wagers-phd) and [Lee Rubin](https://hsci.harvard.edu/people/lee-l-rubin) at Harvard. The role of GDF11 in longevity and regeneration is a bit contentious. [](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5302888/)Some studies suggest that GDF11 could play a role in regeneration and [improve neurogenesis](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-018-35716-6) in mice. In contrast, a 2015 paper suggested that GDF11 [inhibited muscle regeneration](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26001423/). See this [review paper on GDF11](https://www.aging-us.com/article/202881/text) and this [review too](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31144559/).

Fauna’s latest investment round was led by [LifeForce Capital](https://www.lifeforcecap.com/).

[Disulfiram](https://medlineplus.gov/druginfo/meds/a682602.html), a drug approved to treat alcohol dependence, was identified by Spring’s machine learning platform to potentially modulate hyperinflammation. [Inflammaging](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41574-018-0059-4) and overactive/dysregulated immune systems are suspected to play a large role in aging and mortality in elderly COVD-19 patients.

The company is an [AGE1 accelerator](https://www.age1.com/about) alumni and was founded by [Ben Kamens](https://www.linkedin.com/in/ben-kamens) in 2017. 
    
-   **[Underdog Pharmaceuticals raises $10M Seed II](https://www.globenewswire.com/en/news-release/2021/09/23/2302036/0/en/Kizoo-Portfolio-Company-Underdog-Closes-10M-Financing-Round.html). [](https://underdogpharma.com/)** [Underdog Pharmaceuticals](https://underdogpharma.com/) is a [SENS Research Foundation](https://www.sens.org/) spinout that is developing novel [cyclodextrin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclodextrin) drugs to treat [atherosclerosis](https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/arteriosclerosis-atherosclerosis/symptoms-causes/syc-20350569). Last week the company announced the closing of a $10M Seed II round led by [Kizoo Technology Capital](https://www.kizoo.com/). Underdog plans to use the funds to enter the clinic by 2023.   
      
    [Atherosclerosis](https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/arteriosclerosis-atherosclerosis/symptoms-causes/syc-20350569) is an age-related disease characterized by the stiffening of arteries caused by the buildup of plaques in the inner lining of the artery. [Cyclodextrins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclodextrin) are molecules of glucose linked together in a ring. [Beta-cyclodextrin](https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/chemistry/beta-cyclodextrin) is known to have the potential to remove [7-ketocholesterol](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2213231719311759) (7KC), a product involved in arterial plaque buildup, but has safety issues as a drug. Underdog is attempting to create novel safe cyclodextrins to remove 7KC.
    
    In May, Kizoo and Web.de co-founder [Michael Greve](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Greve) announced [362M EUR earmarked for investment in rejuvenation biotech startups](https://longevitylist.com/michael-greve-announces-300m-eur-commitment-to-fund-longevity-biotech-companies/).
    

-   **[Unity Biotechnology announces positive Phase 1 clinical trial data for UBX1325. Begins Phase 2 study](https://www.ophthalmologytimes.com/view/unit-biotechnology-announces-positive-data-from-phase-1-trial-of-ubx1325).** [Unity Biotechnology](https://unitybiotechnology.com/) is a publicly-traded clinical stage [LongBio](https://longevitylist.com/what-does-longbio-mean/) company developing drugs that kill senescent cells. Earlier this summer, the company announced positive results for their Phase 1 safety trial for their drug UBX1325 (a [Bcl-xL](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bcl-xL) inhibitor) to treat [diabetic macular edema](https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/diabetic-retinopathy/multimedia/diabetic-macular-edema/img-20124558) (DME). There were no treatment related adverse events and further early efficacy data showed some improvements in visual acuity and macular thickness. Unity is currently conducting [Phase 2 trials for UBX1325](https://clinicaltrials.gov/ct2/show/NCT04857996) in DME (results expected in 2022) and planning trials for [macular degeneration](https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/wet-macular-degeneration/symptoms-causes/syc-20351107).  
      
    Unity is the most prominent company in the nascent [senolytics](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41587-020-00750-1) industry. The company raised hundreds of millions from investors including [ARCH Venture Partners](https://www.archventure.com/), [Longevity Fund](https://www.longevity.vc/), Peter Thiel, and Jeff Bezos and raised an IPO in 2017. Unfortunately, their first program UBX0101, a senolytic drug to treat osteoarthritis in the knee, failed in Phase 2 last year, which imploded their market capitalization. 
    

-   **[Minovia announces partnership deal with Astellas for mitochondrial therapeutics](https://www.fiercebiotech.com/biotech/astellas-to-pay-minovia-20m-upfront-up-to-420m-per-product-for-mitochondrial-cell-therapies)**. US and Israel-based mitochondrial augmentation startup [Minovia](https://minoviatx.com/) announced a partnership with Japanese pharma giant, [Astellas](https://www.astellas.com/en) in late July. The deal includes a $20M payment upfront with up to $420M per product. Minovia has tested their mitochondrial augmentation therapy in [Phase 2 clinical trials](https://clinicaltrials.gov/ct2/show/NCT03384420) for [Pearson syndrome](https://rarediseases.info.nih.gov/diseases/7343/pearson-syndrome) -- a rare mitochondrial disease.    
      
    [Mitochondrial transfer/augmentation](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41392-020-00440-z) is a therapeutic approach to correct mitochondrial dysfunction in rare or chronic age-related diseases through the addition of undamaged mitochondria into cells. [Minovia](https://minoviatx.com/), [Cellvie](https://cellvie.bio/), and [Mitrix](https://mitrix.bio/) are the only three startups I’m aware of pursuing this approach -- each with a different spin. Minovia infuses [hematopoietic stem cells](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hematopoietic_stem_cell) with undamaged mitochondria ex vivo before transfusion of the stem cells into the patient. [Cellvie](https://cellvie.bio/) uses direct transfusion or injection of autologous mitochondria into tissue. [Mitrix](https://mitrix.bio/) is leveraging so-called “[mitlets](https://www.longevity.technology/exclusive-mitlets-hold-potential-for-mitochondria-transfusion/)” mitochondria ejected from platelets in extracellular vesicles.

## Media is code.

[

![Twitter avatar for @realNathanCheng](https://substackcdn.com/image/twitter_name/w_96/realNathanCheng.jpg)

Nathan S. Cheng is a longevity maximalist @realNathanCheng

Aging is malleable But so are regulations DNA codes for cells Media codes for people

I’ve personally donated $1,000 to fund Tim Maupin’s full feature film “[The Last Generation to Die](http://www.lastgenmovie.com)”. 

Here’s why:

Longevity biotechnology is not solely a scientific or commercial enterprise: It is also a political enterprise. DNA and RNA coding of cells will play an important role in increasing human longevity. But so will the coding of collective human effort towards longevity. And [media scripts for human beings](https://twitter.com/balajis/status/1411655484801622017?lang=en).

### Storytelling.

Siddhartha Mukherjee writes in his book “The Emperor of All Maladies”

> *The campaign against cancer, Farber learned, was much like a political campaign: it needed icons, mascots, images, slogans—the strategies of advertising as much as the tools of science. For any illness to rise to political prominence, it needed to be marketed, just as a political campaign needed marketing. A disease needed to be transformed politically before it could be transformed scientifically.*

Aging is a disease[3](#footnote-3) in dire need of a political transformation. Less than one percent of the NIH budget today goes towards geroscience proper -- even though most major diseases are driven by aging. How can we align the public on the problem of aging?  
  
We need common stories: Pro-longevity media and storytelling are the vectors for propagating a shared vision for a future devoid of all age-related diseases. 

Media modulates regulations downstream, too. 

The speed at which we can test and commercialize anti-aging interventions is currently constrained by the FDA. But the FDA is made of people and reflects the collective desires of society. COVID-19 demonstrated how quickly a new intervention can be approved if people want it badly enough. Shared desire. Shared stories. Shared vision. Media codes for these.

Since my last newsletter, I’ve noticed a promising wave of pro-longevity media initiatives being formed -- some through the On Deck Longevity Biotech Fellowship as well. We’ll need more media efforts like these to counter misleading clickbait journalist narratives of longevity, those centered on blood boy-draining immortality-seeking billionaires, overpopulation, etc. These are tired tropes.

So if you share the same optimistic vision for the future as me, consider funding those who endeavor to tell the longevity story.

\-NATHAN
