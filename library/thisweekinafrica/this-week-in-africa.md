**Quote of the week**

“Food has the ability to evolve in all the ways that life does, going beyond dreams, ideas, thoughts, intent, hope and good will. It lets us experience liberty from trauma.” – [Yewande Komolafe](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/12/01/dining/obe-oniru-ofada-stew-nigeria.html?smid=tw-share), on Ọbẹ̀ onírù

**DR Congo’s war**

[M23 advances to the city of Uvira](https://cddrl.fsi.stanford.edu/publication/can-deliberation-cure-ills-democracy), a strategic city that borders Burundi in South Kivu. Some [analysts say](https://x.com/L4ingstone/status/1998571709511750110) the M23 now controls the city. More than [200,000 people were forced to flee](https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/articles/cly59283d7ko?utm_social_handle_id=36670025&at_ptr_name=twitter&utm_social_post_id=619345387&at_medium=social&at_format=image&at_link_type=web_link&at_link_id=97427674-D5B4-11F0-8028-A3E08EAAE485&at_link_origin=BBCAfrica&at_campaign_type=owned&at_campaign=Social_Flow&at_bbc_team=editorial). A new investigation finds that [M23 is using rape and sexual violence](https://www.reuters.com/investigates/special-report/congo-security-rapes/) as a weapon of war. The advance comes despite the recent [peace agreement brokered by President Trump](https://www.npr.org/2025/12/10/nx-s1-5638922/fighting-between-rwanda-and-congo-continues-despite-trump-brokered-peace-deal). Clearly, Trump [has not ended the war](https://www.economist.com/middle-east-and-africa/2025/12/11/donald-trump-has-not-ended-conflict-between-congo-and-rwanda), and the [security strategy remains “myopic.”](https://www.semafor.com/article/12/10/2025/a-myopic-approach-to-africa-in-new-us-security-strategy) Paul Kagame [praises Trump](https://www.aljazeera.com/video/the-bottom-line/2025/12/7/kagame-never-seen-this-much-attention-to-ending-africa-war) for de-emphasizing democracy, freedom, and human rights.

**Sudan’s civil war**

The Rapid Support Forces have [taken control of crucial oil fields](https://www.middleeasteye.net/news/sudans-heglig-why-oil-field-taken-rsf-matters) in the strategic, southern oil-rich regions. The advance could [contribute to a new major refugee exodus](https://www.reuters.com/world/africa/sudans-militia-advances-could-trigger-new-refugee-exodus-unhcr-chief-warns-2025-12-09/?utm_source=Facebook&utm_social_post_id=618840229&utm_medium=Social&utm_social_handle_id=114050161948682&fbclid=IwdGRzaAOlRHdjbGNrA6VEKWV4dG4DYWVtAjExAHNydGMGYXBwX2lkDDM1MDY4NTUzMTcyOAABHt3Yol8Or2IuYw3q9nq9ajHTmvrhJBCZRuqhDJE-BngCIJs0wtCdx2wfMK1d_aem_dgSP-0LIFIRbEIgwA5x96w). [Mass atrocities should not be a new normal](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2025/12/8/after-el-fasher-we-must-refuse-a-new-normal-of-mass-atrocities-in-darfur). The UAE’s funding of the RSF is [beginning to threaten their relationship to the West](https://www.telegraph.co.uk/world-news/2025/12/09/uae-royal-brothers-ties-west-threatened-sudan-massacre/). Sudan’s civil war is [worsening a major humanitarian crisis](https://www.nbcnews.com/world/africa/sudans-bloody-civil-war-worsening-major-humanitarian-crisis-rcna247815). Here are [some thoughts](https://nationalinterest.org/feature/how-to-stop-sudans-collapse) about how to stop Sudan’s collapse. One way to move forward is [to support](https://www.insidephilanthropy.com/home/why-philanthropy-should-fund-sudans-brave-volunteers) Sudan’s [Emergency Response Rooms](https://www.mutualaidsudan.org/mutual-aid-in-sudan). The groups won the [2025 Chatham House Prize](https://www.chathamhouse.org/2025/11/sudan-grassroots-aid-groups-awarded-2025-chatham-house-prize?utm_source=twitter.com&utm_medium=organic-social&utm_campaign=prize&utm_content=sudan).

Lina Abushouk [reflects on Khartoum](https://africasacountry.com/2025/12/khartom-the-most-selfish-city-if-we-let-it-be), and argues that it must move beyond selfishness and think about the entire country.
