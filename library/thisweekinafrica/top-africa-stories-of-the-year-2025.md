**Here are our top stories of the year 2025:**

> **1\. Sudan’s civil war goes on**

[Two years on](https://foreignpolicy.com/2025/04/20/sudan-civil-war-two-years-conflict-international-aid-response/), Sudan’s civil war has created the [largest displacement crisis in the world](https://www.reuters.com/graphics/FAMINE-RESPONSE/SUDAN/zdvxqkqxnpx/). The [country faces erasure, again](https://news.sky.com/story/pyramids-relics-warrior-queens-sudans-history-took-centuries-to-be-recognised-and-faces-erasure-once-again-13283642?dcmp=snt-sf-twitter). A [millennia of history has vanished](https://www.al-monitor.com/originals/2025/08/how-millennia-history-vanished-sudans-war) in the war. The war [fuels a new regional conflict economy](https://issafrica.org/iss-today/sudan-s-civil-war-fuels-a-new-regional-conflict-economy), and the conflict has become a [world war](https://newleftreview.org/sidecar/posts/sudans-world-war). Mai Hassan and Ahmed Kodouda discuss Sudan’s protracted war on [this podcast](https://www.foreignaffairs.com/podcasts/sudans-intractable-war). Khalid Mustafa Medani provides this [helpful primer](https://www.merip.org/the-struggle-for-sudan/) on the war. Eiad Husham [outlines](https://alexthurston.substack.com/p/seven-questions-on-sudans-conflict?r=4ugz&triedRedirect=true) the current political actors and institutions worth paying attention to. Eisa Dafallah [argues](https://continent.substack.com/p/foreign-interests-drive-local-dynamics?r=4ugz&triedRedirect=true) that foreign interests now drive local dynamics, while Rahiem Shadad [outlines](https://continent.substack.com/p/sudan-us-vs-them-in-the-bad-way?r=4ugz&utm_campaign=post&utm_medium=email&triedRedirect=true) the issues of identity that are at stake in the war.

The [Gulf States’ proxy war](https://nai.uu.se/stories-and-events/news/2025-02-07-gulf-states-proxy-war-exacerbates-conflict-in-sudan.html) continues to destabilize the country. This [report](https://thesentry.org/reports/sudan-rsf-business-network-in-the-uae/) details the RSF’s business networks in the UAE. This is why [Middle Eastern monarchies](https://democracyinafrica.org/middle-eastern-monarchies-in-sudans-war-whats-driving-their-interests/) involve themselves in the conflict. Outside forces contribute to the [unraveling of Sudan](https://www.foreignaffairs.com/sudan/sudan-unraveling). The Crisis Group offers its reflections on how to [end Sudan’s atrocious war](https://www.crisisgroup.org/africa/sudan/after-el-fasher-ending-sudans-atrocious-war).

Pro-democracy groups have been instrumental in [providing aid during the war in Sudan](https://newint.org/resistance/2025/revolutionary-aid-sudan), as [this community kitchen](https://www.okayafrica.com/sudanese-sururab-community-kitchen-fundraiser/) demonstrates. Lovise Aalen and Mai Azzam [discuss the role of civil society organizations](https://theconversation.com/sudans-protesters-built-networks-to-fight-a-tyrant-today-they-save-lives-in-a-war-270176) who are helping save lives. Michelle D’Arcy [argues](https://sudantransparency.org/a-broad-civilian-front-is-fighting-to-end-the-war-in-sudan/) that there is a united civilian front that is fighting to end the war in Sudan, represented by coalitions like [Somoud](https://www.facebook.com/somoud.sud?_rdc=1&_rdr). Mark Leon Goldberg [argues](https://www.globaldispatches.org/p/sudans-emergency-response-rooms-should?r=4ugz&utm_medium=ios&triedRedirect=true&hide_intro_popup=true) that Sudan’s Emergency Response Rooms should win the Nobel Peace Prize. Somoud and other pro-democracy forces [should be at the negotiating table](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/09/21/opinion/sudan-military-humanitarian-crisis.html?smid=tw-share) during peace negotiations.

> **2\. The siege of El Fasher and mass atrocities in Darfur**

Sudan’s Rapid Support Forces [now control the city of el-Fasher in Darfur](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/10/28/world/africa/sudan-el-fasher-burhan-rsf.html?unlocked_article_code=1.w08.vzuR.HLxTXCxtMDvP&smid=tw-share). The [seizure of the city](https://www.wsj.com/world/africa/sudanese-rebels-accused-of-genocide-seize-key-city-1204f867) [divides the country](https://alexthurston.substack.com/p/the-rsfs-capture-of-el-fasher-sudan?r=4ugz&utm_medium=ios&triedRedirect=true). The World Health Organization [accused the group of killing 460 people at el-Fasher hospital](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c364jgkge46o) in a particularly brazen attack. Satellite imagery shows [mass burials](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/12/16/yale-report-unveils-rsf-attempt-to-cover-up-sudan-atrocities-mass-burials) in the city. This [report](https://www.info-res.org/sudan-witness/reports/displacement-detentions-and-killings-following-the-rsf-takeover-of-el-fasher/) documents killings, kidnappings, and detentions following the RSF takeover of El Fasher. The [world has failed](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/10/30/opinion/rsf-el-fasher-darfur.html?unlocked_article_code=1.xU8.Xs0c.EW9y7HZWPcq6&smid=url-share). Mo Ibrahim [says the world ignores the conflict](https://x.com/BBCAfrica/status/1930937831083454825) because of the skin colour of its people.

The siege has [redrawn Sudan’s map](https://www.geopoliticalmonitor.com/gold-gulf-and-the-fall-of-el-fasher-how-the-rsf-redrew-the-sudan-war-map/). This is how it [turned into a genocidal slaughter](https://www.thenewhumanitarian.org/analysis/2025/10/30/atrocity-foretold-how-rsf-siege-el-fasher-turned-genocidal-slaughter), [20 years after the first Darfur Genocide](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/10/31/world/africa/darfur-sudan-atrocities.html?smid=tw-share). Watch this [video report](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8SXGwWxX4Es&t=1s) for more. The [Sudan War Monitor](https://sudanwarmonitor.com/p/rsf-ethnic-based-killings-in-fasher), [Centre for Information Resilience](https://x.com/Cen4infoRes/status/1985394599381065748), and the [Yale Humanitarian Research Lab](https://medicine.yale.edu/lab/khoshnood/) investigate. This is a [thorough multimedia investigation](https://edition.cnn.com/interactive/2025/12/africa/sudan-army-saf-ethnic-killings-canals-intl-invs-vis/) into the massacre. The [fall of the city](https://continent.substack.com/p/sudans-split-in-two-again-is-now?r=4ugz&utm_medium=ios&triedRedirect=true) marked a [bloody new chapter in the war](https://www.crisisgroup.org/africa/sudan/el-fasher-bloody-new-chapter-sudans-ruinous-war).

> **3\. Trump 2.0**

President Trump tries to reshape Africa. He has [slashed foreign aid](https://www.newyorker.com/news/the-financial-page/elon-musk-and-donald-trump-are-not-fixing-us-foreign-aid-but-destroying-it), [promoted trade](https://foreignpolicy.com/2025/02/11/us-africa-policy-aid-trade-trump/), [implemented tariffs](https://blogs.lse.ac.uk/businessreview/2025/04/08/what-us-tariffs-mean-for-africa-and-the-global-south/), and chased minerals deals. His approach is [very transactional](https://www.economist.com/middle-east-and-africa/2025/07/08/donald-trumps-approach-to-africa-is-very-well-african), a [“carrot and stick” approach](https://www.csis.org/analysis/president-trumps-carrot-and-stick-approach-africa) to the continent. [Experts outline the challenges and opportunities](https://www.csis.org/analysis/experts-react-challenges-and-opportunities-trump-administration-africa#Hudson) for the Trump administration in Africa. Zainab Usman [outlines six of Trump’s executive orders](https://carnegieendowment.org/research/2025/02/the-six-areas-in-trumps-executive-orders-that-countries-in-africa-and-the-global-south-should-pay-attention-to?lang=en) that African countries should pay attention to. While the continent is [“relatively” immune](https://cnbcafrica.com/2025/the-african-continent-is-relatively-immune-to-trumps-tariffs/?utm_source=theafricabrief.beehiiv.com&utm_medium=newsletter&utm_campaign=africa-in-brief-april-04-2025&_bhlid=3254bba92ac4670550998e11b5a7f895738a6fbd) to the tariffs, they signal an [end to trade deal that meant to boost development](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/steep-us-tariffs-africa-signal-110514531.html?guccounter=1&guce_referrer=aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuZ29vZ2xlLmNvbS8&guce_referrer_sig=AQAAAHNgMLYXNnRqtFERj9h4R8LOyxmgunT8pcfvV3oSKohuKx9Lwl4HzL5FJVPUkYlU2b96Uz_kY2p8LS-iyXwnmRlbL6N_I1pLiG7PpxaHg0HPoBbKFAZpVfGXFNd4WSxfDI0nmJl_kBm-bUatJt1WMDzdA67blCSJ980DKe36iIsc). It has been quite the year [for Africa and Trump](https://www.theafricareport.com/402860/africas-year-of-donald-trump/?utm_source=newsletter-tar-daily&utm_campaign=tar_nl_daily_news_2025-12-23).

[Unserious men for unserious times](https://continent.substack.com/p/unserious-men-for-serious-times?utm_source=post-email-title&publication_id=1620194&post_id=179044607&utm_campaign=email-post-title&isFreemail=true&r=4ugz&triedRedirect=true&utm_medium=email).

> **4\. A different future for foreign aid**

The [US Government](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/06/22/us/politics/usaid-cuts-doge.html) effectively [shut down USAID](https://www.globaldispatches.org/p/the-death-of-usaid?r=4ugz&utm_campaign=post&utm_medium=email&triedRedirect=true). Anne Pitcher explains [what happened](https://cpsblog.isr.umich.edu/?p=3450). The [cost of the executive order](https://continent.substack.com/p/the-cost-of-trumps-executive-disorder?r=4ugz&utm_campaign=post&utm_medium=email&triedRedirect=true) measured in lives. Aid cuts are [taking their toll on the continent](https://futures.issafrica.org/blog/2025/The-toll-of-USAID-cuts-on-Africa) and [imperils the lives of millions](https://www.nybooks.com/online/2025/03/12/usaid-goodbye-to-all-that/?fbclid=IwY2xjawI_LWBleHRuA2FlbQIxMAABHcoR0rKXtZWTVIPw5ctVM9BV4pJmlivQG7tZ4aXKcDH2hRp8XwBP9ILCwA_aem_cEThC75PVcYGk6dHleIMCA) across the globe. The decision created a [man-made hunger crisis](https://www.propublica.org/article/kenya-trump-usaid-world-food-program-starvation-children-deaths). The UN [sketches a global map of mayhem](https://www.devex.com/news/scoop-un-sketches-global-map-of-mayhem-from-us-aid-freeze-109268?utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=devex_social_icons) from US aid freeze. Chris Ogunmodede [provides these 16 thoughts](https://coogunmodede.substack.com/p/16-thoughts-on-what-the-trump-aid?r=4ugz&utm_campaign=post&utm_medium=email&triedRedirect=true) on what the Trump aid cuts mean for Africa. What is the [role of donors in a post-aid world](https://odi.org/en/insights/donors-in-a-post-aid-world-january-2025-update/)? What does a [world without foreign aid](https://www.vox.com/future-perfect/406022/usaid-foreign-aid-trump-rubio-cuts-gavi-vaccines) look like? Listen to [This American Life episode](https://www.thisamericanlife.org/862/some-things-we-dont-do-anymore) for more.

The shutdown is [not just a humanitarian issue](https://www.newyorker.com/news/the-lede/behind-the-chaotic-attempt-to-freeze-federal-assistance), but also [threatens American interests](https://theconversation.com/usaid-shutdown-isnt-just-a-humanitarian-issue-its-a-threat-to-american-interests-248939?utm_source=twitter&utm_medium=bylinetwitterbutton). It [creates an opening for China](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-02-03/trump-and-musk-s-usaid-shut-down-threat-opens-door-for-china?cmpid%3D=socialflow-twitter-africa&utm_source=twitter&utm_campaign=socialflow-organic&utm_medium=social&utm_content=africa). But it also [presents an opportunity to African countries](https://www.semafor.com/article/02/24/2025/why-ending-aid-dependency-is-an-opportunity-for-africa). CGD [outlines these four ways](https://www.cgdev.org/blog/changing-course-good-faith-aid-review-how-four-principles-could-advance-american-interest-and) to improve aid. Gregory Rockson [explains](https://www.semafor.com/article/02/03/2025/how-to-make-us-foreign-aid-work-for-africa) how to make foreign aid work for Africa. Ken Opalo [argues](https://www.africanistperspective.com/p/how-reflexive-catastrophizing-inhibits?r=4ugz&utm_campaign=post&utm_medium=email&triedRedirect=true) that reflexive catastrophizing holds back policy imagination in African states. His posts on [the aid cuts](https://www.africanistperspective.com/p/american-aid-cutsdisruptions), a [new aid paradigm](https://www.africanistperspective.com/p/what-will-become-of-international), and [ending aid dependency](https://www.africanistperspective.com/p/african-countries-must-start-the) are worth reading. Laura Robson [discusses](https://thebaffler.com/latest/the-end-of-aid-robson?utm_source=Baffler+Readers&utm_campaign=74cbd03706-03192025_weekly&utm_medium=email&utm_term=0_-7b264bf4e9-481353591) the end of aid. Read these alongside W. Guyde Moore’s [post](https://gyudemoore.substack.com/p/africa-united-a-turbulent-world) on the need for a united Africa and [a world without the US at the center](https://gyudemoore.substack.com/p/essay-imagining-a-world-without-the?r=4ugz&utm_campaign=post&utm_medium=email&triedRedirect=true), and Grieve Chelwa’s [post](https://gchelwa.substack.com/p/ending-aid-dependency-the-tax-edition) on the importance of taxes. Duncan Green [discusses](https://blogs.lse.ac.uk/internationaldevelopment/2025/03/10/the-future-of-aid-and-what-nextgen-aid-jobs-might-look-like/) what the future of aid might look like. *Foreign Policy’s* [Fall Issue](https://foreignpolicy.com/2025/09/08/introducing-the-fall-2025-print-issue/) assesses international development after foreign aid. Adam Tooze’s lead essay situates the issue within a context of “[the end of development](https://foreignpolicy.com/2025/09/08/adam-tooze-un-sustainable-development-goals-us-aid-finance-economy/).” Clearly, [Africa seeks a post-West plan](https://www.theafricareport.com/378756/africa-seeks-a-post-west-plan/?utm_source=Twitter&utm_campaign=Twitter&utm_medium=Social%20media#=).

> **5\. Authoritarianism and resistance in Tanzania**

Samia Suluhu Hassan and the CCM [deepen their control of Tanzania](https://theconversation.com/tanzanias-samia-hassan-has-ushered-in-a-new-era-of-authoritarianism-heres-how-266598?utm_source=twitter&utm_medium=bylinetwitterbutton), but opposition is growing. There were “elections” this year, but [opposition figures were barred from running](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/10/29/world/africa/tanzania-presidential-election.html?smid=tw-share). The [ruling party](https://theconversation.com/tanzanias-ruling-party-has-crushed-the-opposition-the-elections-are-a-mere-formality-265771) has [dismantled the opposition](https://www.theafricareport.com/392951/tanzania-election-how-samia-has-dismantled-the-opposition-ahead-of-polls/). The public is [getting fed up](https://apnews.com/article/tanzania-election-president-samia-suluhu-hassan-9a9ecbce98769709ad8fd5cd6c5254ee) with the government’s [increasing opposition crackdowns and forced disappearances](https://www.crisisgroup.org/africa/tanzania/enforced-disappearances-and-crackdown-opposition-darken-outlook-tanzanias-elections). Hassan is engaged in “[reform-washing](https://www.dropbox.com/scl/fi/d7lgpjq1gpaky5hp3hs4x/Paget-and-Kwayu-2025.pdf?rlkey=hcc7c0q1puaaw0hb6qr17rrw3&st=2chtmonp&dl=0)” while repressing political opponents.

In resistance to the sham elections, youth [took to the streets in protest](https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/2025/10/29/tanzania-election-president-samia-suluhu-hassan-opposition/). Security forces [fired tear gas at protesters](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cwy7124p544o?xtor=AL-71-%5Bpartner%5D-%5Bbbc.news.twitter%5D-%5Bheadline%5D-%5Bnews%5D-%5Bbizdev%5D-%5Bisapi%5D&at_campaign=Social_Flow&at_link_id=466DC12C-B4BC-11F0-92B4-DF2674382520&at_bbc_team=editorial&at_campaign_type=owned&at_format=link&at_medium=social&at_link_type=web_link&at_ptr_name=twitter&at_link_origin=BBCWorld). Post-election violence has [threatened Tanzania’s reputation](https://www.thecitizen.co.tz/tanzania/news/national/unforgettable-moment-how-chaos-damaged-tanzania-s-reputation-as-island-of-peace--5253492) as a peaceful country. The protests illustrated [years of bottled-up anger](https://www.theafricareport.com/396809/tanzania-releasing-years-of-bottled-up-anger-amnesty/?utm_source=Twitter&utm_campaign=Twitter&utm_medium=Social%20media#=) finally [being let out](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/11/01/world/africa/tanzania-president-election-protest-violence.html?smid=tw-share). [Hidden graves in Dar es Salaam](https://www.theafricareport.com/399625/morgues-overflow-hidden-graves-in-dar-es-salaam-tanzania-accused-of-mass-killings/) provide evidence for mass killings after elections.

Michaela Collord [explains](https://africasacountry.com/2025/11/after-the-coronation/) what happens now after Hassan’s coronation. Journalist Erick Kabendera’s memoir tells the story of [Tanzania’s fall from Nyerere’s idealism to Magufili’s tyranny](https://www.theafricareport.com/394722/in-the-name-of-the-president-tanzania-memoir-packs-a-punch/). Dan Paget [argues](https://theconversation.com/tanzania-president-samia-hassans-grip-on-power-has-been-shaken-by-unprecedented-protests-268849) that Hassan’s grip in power has been shaken and wonders if the country has [reached its breaking point](https://www.journalofdemocracy.org/online-exclusive/has-tanzania-reached-its-breaking-point/) and if [it will never be the same](https://www.journalofdemocracy.org/online-exclusive/tanzania-will-never-be-the-same/). Read this [report](https://www.cfr.org/blog/protest-and-crackdown-tanzania?utm_source=blognotification&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=Blog%20Post%20Notification%20Africa%20in%20Transition&utm_term=AfricaInTransition) on protest and crackdown in Tanzania. Nick Westcott asks if the [CCM losing Tanzania](https://africanarguments.org/2025/12/is-the-ccm-losing-tanzania/). Yonatan Morse [argues](https://theconversation.com/autocracies-in-transition-in-2025-cameroon-and-tanzania-rulers-clung-to-power-but-look-more-vulnerable-than-ever-270718) that the CCM looks more vulnerable than ever.

> **6\. Kenyans exploited in Saudi Arabia**

Abdi Latif Dahir, Vivian Nereim, and Justin Scheck spent months documenting the struggles of Kenyan mothers and children who are trapped in Saudi Arabia. This [story of a mother](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/12/11/world/africa/saudi-arabia-unmarried-mothers.html?unlocked_article_code=1.708.GOYp.LiuTjBabCssg&smid=url-share) who is fighting to free her daughter from the country is especially harrowing. Read this [excellent article](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/11/10/world/middleeast/mothers-trapped-in-saudi-arabia-told-us-their-stories.html) where mothers tell their stories. Kenya is [betting on its women abroad](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/12/14/world/africa/kenya-saudi-arabia-worker-abuse.html), while politicians and businesses exploit the practice.

> **7\. Gold mining threatens environment but provides opportunities**

This is an [important article](https://www.dropbox.com/scl/fi/0scj1hsdrp2uy526y24d2/Girard-Molina-Millan-and-Vic-2025.pdf?rlkey=ew6js7md6oubmrwixjcb4m1p6&st=8d1yc4xn&dl=0) on the economic scale and environmental impact of artisanal mining across Africa. The practice, called galamsey in Ghana, is [wreaking environmental devastation](https://time.com/7325561/ghana-gold-mahama-galamsey/?fbclid=IwY2xjawNjAZVleHRuA2FlbQIxMQABHjoHNRXd3ART8mFSUWVBHcZ_v9BV-hothnEGL-WC8iwdzHIjyKs6NMmZxcu7_aem_jfHx5L2xMldPgC5F0ni_Ng). This is how [Ghana’s gold rush](https://www.dw.com/en/how-ghanas-gold-rush-threatens-to-fuel-illicit-trade/a-74516647?maca=en-Twitter-sharing) threatens to fuel illicit trade. As gold prices soar, [Uganda’s artisanal miners get edged out](https://continent.substack.com/p/as-gold-prices-soar-ugandas-artisanal?r=4ugz&utm_campaign=post&utm_medium=web&triedRedirect=true). This [article](https://www.dropbox.com/scl/fi/c7smvwabts8nae6yklwpl/Van-Bockstael-2025.pdf?rlkey=scyi0o3ma9ysktsjia4fvh53y&st=ge6m5wto&dl=0) provides perspectives on artisanal mining in Liberia. Global chaos is a boon for [African gold miners with shovels](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/newsletters/2025-05-09/next-africa-global-chaos-from-trump-tariffs-offers-a-boon-for-gold-miners?srnd=undefined). Could [high gold prices](https://continent.substack.com/p/mahamas-gold-rush-is-fast-and-furious?r=4ugz&triedRedirect=true) be the boon Ghana’s economy needs?

> **8\. Burkina Faso’s leader gains popularity**

Burkina Faso’s coup leader Ibrahim Traoré has [captured hearts and minds](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c1egely9v3go?at_medium=social&at_format=image&at_ptr_name=twitter&at_link_origin=BBCAfrica&at_campaign_type=owned&at_link_type=web_link&at_bbc_team=editorial&at_campaign=Social_Flow&at_link_id=1BF26C52-2F12-11F0-B167-A41FA17C1D10) across the world.
