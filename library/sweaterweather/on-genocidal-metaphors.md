I wanted to speak to a frustration I’ve had leading up to the general election in the United States—I know we’re all frustrated for various reasons, and emotions are high. Of course, it should go without saying, I’m frustrated (understatement) by conservatives. But as I limit my associations with them, I’m going to preserve my energy on that side of thinking.

However, as for the Democrats, I’ve heard all the talking points and metaphors that liberals love to throw at those of us who even lean left, at those of us who even consider a third party vote: “A vote for a third party is a vote for Trump.” (If this were actually true, why doesn’t it make you furious? What kind of democracy is that? It’s not true, though—a third party vote is a vote for who I think most aligns with my values, which is how this whole system purports to work. If a Democrat wants my vote, they could try to earn it. Instead I get shamed for voting my conscience, which only perpetuates the black/white dichotomy of Rep/Dem possibilities.)

Or the frustrating bus metaphor: “Voting is like a bus ride. No bus drops you off right at your doorstep. You ride the bus that gets you as close as possible to where you want to go.” (Sure, this metaphor seems nice, but at one of the bus stops the door opens and an entire people are being ethnically cleansed outside it. This metaphor is gross.)

It *should* be obvious that Democrats have a vested interest in shutting down even the possibility of a formidable third party, a vested interest in spreading these groupthink talking points. Be serious: would a Democrat in power ever say “Oh yes, we need another party”? No, so of course they’ll spread metaphors to shut it all down, plus [using the system to try to keep third parties at bay](https://apnews.com/article/wisconsin-jill-stein-harris-trump-lawsuit-405e8bae8ff9becfa81a1360708d59a0).

What I really want to focus on, however, is one metaphor that liberals love to shove at leftists, a metaphor that I find both wrong literally and morally wrong in its apologetics of genocide.

The metaphor boils down to this:

> **“I’m all for peace in Gaza, but we need to put on our oxygen mask before we help others with theirs.”**

The plane/oxygen mask metaphor only tells me you haven’t considered the United States’s role on the international stage. It tells me that you don’t realize or believe the ways in which the U.S. empire is the one messing with the oxygen in the first place. Why is the plane going down in this scenario? Why are you so nonchalant about that?

The colonialist United States loves to deprive humans of oxygen for its own aims, even literally. Think of its hand in [coups](https://foreignpolicy.com/2013/08/20/mapped-the-7-governments-the-u-s-has-overthrown/) around the world. Think of our history with slavery and genocide against indigenous populations. Think of its past and present [weapons and money to Israel](https://www.cfr.org/article/us-aid-israel-four-charts) (yours and my tax dollars at work, and think of how that money could be spent domestically). Think of its 750 military bases around the world, vastly discrepant from any other military in the world—[it’s not even close](https://open.substack.com/pub/theconnections/p/the-american-empire-must-die-so-the?r=sqx2a&utm_campaign=post&utm_medium=web). Think of the immensity of how the [U.S. military impacts the climate crisis](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2023/12/12/elephant-in-the-room-the-us-militarys-devastating-carbon-footprint). And you think that Harris will somehow upset this status quo, once she’s in office? The same presidential candidate who promised to have [the most lethal military in the world and who unequivocally supports Israel](https://www.nytimes.com/2024/08/23/us/politics/kamala-harris-speech-transcript.html)? History has shown that Democrats are moving right, not left—the speedy sea change on [border policy](https://kamalaharris.com/issues/) is the obvious example. And even when they claim to espouse socially left ideals, they sure love to leave things open, like Roe v. Wade.

I know why people use this oxygen mask metaphor. We’re all worried about the obvious, in-your-face fascism of the far-right. I’m very worried too. We’re all worried about what that could mean for women, trans folks, anyone queer or anyone who doesn’t fit the mold. We’re worried about ongoing stochastic terrorism. And we’re all worried about what it would mean for the already-paltry education system. But this oxygen mask story also tells me its storytellers don’t know anything about how any of the above international efforts impact us domestically. It’s easy to point fingers at the other party, using last-century German history as an apt parallel, but it’s harder to see this in the mirror.

Democrats at the head of the table claim they’ll protect us, but only for election points (there’s one at the head of the table now, also with the current presidential candidate *having power now*—have we forgotten?). They should be at their most desperate to earn our votes, but they don’t need to be, because they have voters spouting apologetics for them, ignoring even genocide. Fascism is already here:

-   Remember how so-called blue/liberal universities vilified and used violence against people protesting for Palestine, *just this year*?
    
-   Remember how the movement for Black Lives in 2020 was met with the same violence, in both blue and red states?
    
-   Remember how we had no say in Harris as the Democratic candidate?
    
-   Remember how a majority of Americans would support an arms embargo to Israel, but we still have no say?
    
-   Don’t forget [the connection between our militarized police and that of Israel](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2020/6/12/how-the-us-and-israel-exchange-tactics-in-violence-and-control).
    

So no, protestors against genocide are not single-issue voters. The connective tissue is all there. Israel is a linchpin. I hope it’s just ignorance and forgetfulness and distractedness, not malice, when Democrats talk about oxygen masks while forgetting all the tubes are in the midst of being cut already, even when the sky outside looks blue.

Even if it were true, even if the United States merely verbalized support for Israel but didn’t send money and bombs, or even if international policy had no impact domestically, I’d still be devastated by the antipathy, to paraphrase Baldwin. This oxygen mask metaphor shows complicity in genocide, and I wish Democrats would think about it for even a few minutes. I wish they’d be honest, just tell me that genocide elsewhere isn’t a red line here.

Do we mean it when we say we’re all humans with deserved rights? Aren’t Palestinian people—civilians, children—humans with deserved rights? How thick must the borders in our brains be. I’m sure I’m guilty of it too; when I think of maps, I can’t help but imagine the lines of separation on all the globes I’ve seen. Even maps in our minds can be propaganda. Clearly-marked borders were our worst invention.
