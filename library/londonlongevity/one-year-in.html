Tomorrow marks one year since we started the London Longevity Network.

What began as a small gathering has grown into a community of scientists, entrepreneurs, and innovators who believe ageing isn’t just a problem, it’s the problem.

To celebrate, we have teamed up with Venture Café London and ARIA for an event at Fora The Jellicoe, King’s Cross, 6-9 PM.

The theme: Accelerating Healthy Longevity: From Code to Cities

Here’s what we’ll cover:

  • 📈 Latest trends in longevity science and technology

  • 🤖 AI-driven discovery targeting ageing

  • 🧬 Biological testing for ageing and disease

  • 📊 Population-scale datasets for healthspan innovation

This isn’t another panel where people repeat what you already know. It’s a intersection of domains — biotech, AI, policy, startups — and the people who are building the future.

Who’s on stage:

  • Phil Newman (CEO, Longevity.Technology)

  • Raghib Ali OBE (CEO, Our Future Health)

  • Zorrie Dimitrova (COO, GlycanAge)

  • Brendan Swain (CSO, Shift Biosciences)

  • Panel with Chiara Herzog, Jake Taylor-King, Tina Woods — moderated by David Wood

What else:

  • Workshops: breathwork (Scarlett Trillia), genuine networking (Šárka Sirůčková)

  • Startup demos: Epic Life, Connectome, Cellcolabs, GlycanAge, Genie Fertility, Leap-Labs

  • And because a longer life should also be fun: DJ Tina Technoic playing a longevity-inspired techno set.

Food and drinks included.
