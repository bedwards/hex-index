**CHINA STEALS SECRETS** through a “network of defense attachés, academics, and spies operating in and out of China,” according to federal court documents, and one of them was until 2018 serving as a soldier in the U.S. Army.

Ji Chaoqun, a Chinese national, was [convicted of espionage](https://storage.courtlistener.com/recap/gov.uscourts.ilnd.356808/gov.uscourts.ilnd.356808.372.0.pdf) last month in Chicago, Illinois. A [Justice Department news release touting the verdict revealed](https://www.justice.gov/opa/pr/former-army-reservist-convicted-acting-within-united-states-unregistered-agent-people-s) that the 31-year-old had worked for years on behalf of China before joining the U.S. Army Reserve in 2016. He faces up to 15 years in prison at [sentencing in January 2023](https://storage.courtlistener.com/recap/gov.uscourts.ilnd.356808/gov.uscourts.ilnd.356808.372.0.pdf).

It certainly won’t end [Chinese spying in the United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Chinese_spy_cases_in_the_United_States_of_America). But this and a related case have revealed fascinating details on the practice—including the personal diaries, texts, and a [never-before-seen document from the Chinese spymaster](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/features/2022-09-15/china-wanted-ge-s-secrets-but-then-their-spy-got-caught) Ji dealt with. And when reading through evidence revealed in court filings, we can get a sense of Beijing’s recruiting process.
