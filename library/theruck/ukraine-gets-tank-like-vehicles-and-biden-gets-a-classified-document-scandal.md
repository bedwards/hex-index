Happy Thursday, and welcome back to **The Ruck.**

I launched [this newsletter in August 2022](https://www.theruck.news/p/welcome-to-the-ruck), and since then, I’ve settled on this format: a reported column on national security followed by a news digest called *The Rundown*. And the dispatches have gotten somewhat lengthy—last week’s [on U.S. military pilots training China’s Navy](https://www.theruck.news/p/the-us-marine-accused-of-helping) was pushing 2,200 words.

After a few months of this, I’ve realized that I’d much rather deliver either one or the other in a single dispatch—and many readers have told me they especially enjoy the news digest.

So from now on, my usual approach will be to send a weekly rundown of national security news. But when I have something interesting to say, like how I think the rise of [China’s Marine Corps](https://www.usni.org/magazines/proceedings/2020/april/chinas-marine-corps-rise) is something to watch, I will write about that instead.

What’s this mean for you? Not much, really.

I’m letting you know the format is changing—you’ll keep getting **The Ruck** in your inbox as usual. But if you’d like to make changes to your account in light of this, I totally understand. [You can click here to update your user profile.](https://www.theruck.news/account) Or, if you’re not a subscriber yet, you can sign up below:

Now, without further ado… here’s what’s happening in the national security world. Thanks for reading.

-   **[The US is sending Ukraine nearly $3 billion in additional aid, including Bradley Fighting Vehicles.](https://www.cnn.com/2023/01/05/politics/us-germany-ukraine-aid/index.html)** Despite what you may hear in some reports, they are *[not](https://www.cfr.org/in-brief/west-sending-light-tanks-ukraine-will-they-make-difference)* [tanks](https://www.cfr.org/in-brief/west-sending-light-tanks-ukraine-will-they-make-difference). Although you can technically get away with calling them “tank-like.”
    
    -   [How will Ukraine use such vehicles? Task & Purpose talked to experts.](https://taskandpurpose.com/news/military-bradley-fighting-vehicles-ukraine/)
        
        > “They provide tremendous overwatch to infantry,” said retired Army Gen. Peter Chiarelli, who led the 1st Cavalry Division and served as head of Multi-National Corps-Iraq. “You’re able to move them in a relatively secure vehicle to a position of advantage. I’m sure the Ukrainians will be able to get more \[troops\] in them because they don’t have quite as much kit as we do — possibly as many as 10 folks in the back of that thing.”

-   **[A 57-year-old Georgia man has pleaded guilty to taking $400,000 in bribes from an Afghan company he helped obtain millions of dollars in contracts,](https://www.justice.gov/opa/pr/man-pleads-guilty-bribery-and-visa-fraud-conspiracies)** the Justice Department says. He also “received bribes to sign false letters of recommendation for visas authorized for Afghan nationals who worked as translators with U.S. forces in Afghanistan.”
    
-   **[Retired Army Maj. Gen. Pat Donahoe spoke out in Army Times now that he is out of uniform.](https://www.armytimes.com/news/your-army/2023/01/05/pat-donahoe-civilian-wants-a-word-with-the-army/)** The Army inspector general concluded the two-star “had violated Army policies during three separate incidents on Twitter.” And yet [one of those instances was a military officer](https://taskandpurpose.com/news/army-general-patrick-donahoe-retires/) standing up in support of women in uniform.
    
    -   “We value the women that serve in the United States Army,” he said. “And we’ve got to say that loudly and proudly…It’s a challenge to the cohesion of the Army, when an entire population inside the Army feels that senior leaders aren’t willing to defend them publicly, and we should never step away from that duty as senior leaders.”
        
-   **[In a new op-ed, former Defense Secretary Bob Gates and Secretary of State Condoleeza Rice argue that "time is not on Ukraine's side"](https://www.washingtonpost.com/opinions/2023/01/07/condoleezza-rice-robert-gates-ukraine-repel-russia/)** and urge the West to send more to Ukraine, including, above all, "mobile armor.”
    
    -   "Both of us have dealt with Putin on a number of occasions, and we are convinced he believes time is on his side: that he can wear down the Ukrainians and that U.S. and European unity and support for Ukraine will eventually erode and fracture...For Putin, defeat is not an option.”
        
-   **[“Taiwan has asked to join discussions on China’s protest against US chip sanctions at the World Trade Organization, seeking a voice on a debate that could have ramifications for the global chip industry,” Bloomberg writes.](https://www.businesslive.co.za/bloomberg/news/2023-01-06-taiwan-asks-to-join-wto-talks-on-chinas-chip-sanctions/)**
    
-   **[President Joe Biden has his very own classified documents scandal.](https://www.washingtonpost.com/national-security/2023/01/09/biden-classified-documents-trump/?utm_campaign=wp_main&utm_medium=social&utm_source=twitter)** [](https://www.washingtonpost.com/national-security/2023/01/09/biden-classified-documents-trump/?utm_campaign=wp_main&utm_medium=social&utm_source=twitter)The Justice Department has launched a review after about 10 documents were found in Biden’s post-vice presidential office in downtown Washington, according to The Washington Post.
    
    -   **[“One additional batch” of classified docs was also found in a separate location,](https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/white-house/biden-aides-find-second-batch-classified-documents-new-location-rcna65371)** NBC reported.
        
    -   There [are major differences between the two presidential instances](https://www.nytimes.com/2023/01/10/us/politics/trump-biden-classified-documents.html), and we’re still waiting to see where the Trump case goes. But the nonpartisan view from **The Ruck** is consistent: Taking classified files home with you is wrong. So anyway…
