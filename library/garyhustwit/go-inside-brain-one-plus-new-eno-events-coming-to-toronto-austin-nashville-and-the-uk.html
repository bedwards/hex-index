Greetings friends! I’ve just returned from Australia for a week of “Eno” screenings, it was so much fun showing the film down under, and hard to believe it had been 17 years since I was last there for “Helvetica”. Qantas totally destroyed one of my bags: I mean completely shredded the bag in half and destroyed most of its contents. So that sucked. But the audiences were absolutely amazing, and a sincere thanks to the staffs of Sydney Opera House and ACMI for their hospitality and professionalism. To all the people who came out to see the film, thank you for being part of this experiment!

More exciting screening news: we’re doing a return live screening in Toronto (just announced and almost sold out already) plus new premieres in Austin, Nashville, and the legendary Glastonbury Festival in England. Speaking of the UK, we’ll be screening the film with Picturehouse at over a dozen locations in England and Scotland opening July 12! More info on that soon. And this weekend there’s a special live screening of the film at the Sheffield Doc Fest with our esteemed Director of Programming Brendan Dawes at the helm and in conversation afterwards. Hope to see you at one of these events or please tell a friend in these cities.
