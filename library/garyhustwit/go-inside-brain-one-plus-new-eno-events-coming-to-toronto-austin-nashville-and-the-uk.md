**Greetings friends!** I’ve just returned from Australia for a week of “Eno” screenings, it was so much fun showing the film down under, and hard to believe it had been 17 years since I was last there for “Helvetica”. Qantas totally destroyed one of my bags: I mean completely shredded the bag in half and destroyed most of its contents. So that sucked. But the audiences were absolutely amazing, and a sincere thanks to the staffs of Sydney Opera House and ACMI for their hospitality and professionalism. To all the people who came out to see the film, thank you for being part of this experiment!

More exciting screening news: we’re doing a return live screening in [Toronto](https://www.eventbee.com/v/eno-toronto-special-screening-6pm/event?eid=237717253) (just announced and almost sold out already) plus new premieres in [Austin](https://www.austinfilm.org/screening/eno-with-gary-hustwit-live/), [Nashville](https://www.belcourt.org/films/eno-special-live-screenings-and-qas-with-director-gary-hustwit/), and the legendary [Glastonbury Festival](https://www.glastonburyfestivals.co.uk/areas/pilton-palais-cinema/) in England. Speaking of the UK, we’ll be screening the film with Picturehouse at over a dozen locations in England and Scotland opening July 12! More info on that soon. And this weekend there’s a special live screening of the film at the [Sheffield Doc Fest](https://www.sheffdocfest.com/composition/eno-live-mix-conversation) with our esteemed Director of Programming **Brendan Dawes** at the helm and in conversation afterwards. Hope to see you at one of these events or please tell a friend in these cities.

# Go inside Brain One

[

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!BdDP!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F403dfbc7-5280-4875-a56d-902fe95f3bdf_1920x1080.jpeg)



](https://teenage.engineering/now#B-1)

Those of you who’ve been to one of our live “Eno” screenings or followed along on Instagram have caught a glimpse of **Brain One**, the generative filmmaking machine we designed with Teenage Engineering, powered by Anamorph’s custom software platform. Teenage Engineering’s in-house newsletter *Now* recently down with me [to discuss this first-of-its-kind machine](https://teenage.engineering/now#B-1), the design process behind it, and more.

Stay tuned for news about more UK and European “Eno” live events, and screenings in Vancouver, Minneapolis, Boston, Philly, Atlanta, Phoenix, Stockholm, Amsterdam, Auckland, Wellington, Barcelona, Tokyo, Italy, and dozens more cities worldwide. We’ll have news on more cinema runs and the global streaming release too.

Cheers!  
\-Gary
