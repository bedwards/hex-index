What is a **theory of everything**?

In other words, when someone hands you a theory, how do you know it’s a TOE?

I’m in the awkward position of having a channel called *[Theories of Everything](https://youtube.com/TheoriesOfEverything)* where I explicate different theories, yet I have never defined this term.

This isn’t as straightforward a question as you may think… To answer it rigorously, we’d need to explore what a “**theory**” is, what a “**[thing](https://youtu.be/SWtFU1Lit3M?t=465)**” is, and what “**every**” of that “thing” means. Indeed, it means exploring what “**unification**” even means.

Each of these bolded terms (what’s a *theory* / *thing* / *every* / *unification*) will be explored in more detail in upcoming Substack posts here, so feel free to subscribe.

Defining **unification**, for instance, isn’t as undemanding as one thinks, as although it’s commonly said that electromagnetism and the weak force have been “unified” into the electroweak interaction, you can rightfully claim this is *not* a unification because there’s mixing. [John Donoghue said this to me here](https://youtu.be/dG_uKJx6Lpg). There’s still no single interaction with a single coupling in the electroweak case.

So what? Can’t you just extend “unification of X and Y” to just mean “X and Y are brought into a coherent framework”? Sure, but then you have to define what “coherent framework” means, which is equally demanding! Indeed, EM+GR are *already* coherent with one another (you can write Maxwell coupled to dynamical spacetime), but few would say that standard EM has been “unified” standard GR. A cocktail’s not a marriage… though one may unhappily lead to the other.

There are various lists of contenders to a TOE ([I even have a cheeky Top 10 list](https://curtjaimungal.substack.com/p/here-are-10-theories-of-everything)) but today I’m writing an exposition on what I have not seen before. Namely, a *classification* of the different kinds of TOEs.

In this article, I won’t be specifying exactly what [quantum field theory](https://curtjaimungal.substack.com/p/10-more-essential-theoretical-physics) is, nor the [standard model](https://youtu.be/TTSeqsCgxj8), nor [general relativity](https://curtjaimungal.substack.com/p/general-relativity-is-not-deterministic), nor [interpretations of quantum mechanics](https://curtjaimungal.substack.com/p/the-interpretations-of-quantum-mechanics), nor [what a law is](https://curtjaimungal.substack.com/p/can-physics-explain-its-own-laws), nor what “[existence](https://curtjaimungal.substack.com/p/what-is-existence)” is, nor what “[reality](https://curtjaimungal.substack.com/p/what-is-the-nature-of-reality)” is. I’ll assume you have a rough understanding, but if you don’t you can click the hyperlinks. If you don’t understand, just nod like everyone else.

# Wikipedia Has Six Inconsistent Definitions

If you read the Wikipedia article on “[Theory of Everything](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theory_of_everything)” closely, you’ll notice it actually calls something a TOE differently almost each time; at least as of Jan 19, 2026. To the untrained eye, these look like the same thing:

1.  “Unification of the four fundamental interactions”
    
2.  “Coherent theoretical framework containing all physical principles”
    
3.  “Capable of describing all physical phenomena in the universe”
    
4.  “Harmoniously integrate QM and GR into a seamless whole”
    
5.  “Predict all the different kinds of particles possible”
    
6.  “Predicting everything from logic alone”
    

Definition 1 doesn’t imply Definition 2 because “interaction unification” doesn’t automatically yield “all physical principles.” In other words, a Lagrangian unifying the below …

… may still fail to yield statistical mechanics / thermodynamics, and potentially emergence of some other sort. For instance, there’s still controversy as to what the [second law is about](https://youtu.be/HIoviZe14pY), and if it’s been “[explained](https://youtu.be/ioXwL-c1RXQ).” But you may say this is carping. Well, carping is my middle name, but sure, let’s move on.

Definition 1 doesn’t imply Definition 3 because unifying interactions still won’t explain initial conditions / why this vacuum, cosmological constant value, why three generations of fermions, and even why consciousness (even if you’re a physicalist).

Definition 4 doesn’t imply Definition 1 because you could potentially achieve some compatibility between GR and quantum theory by keeping gravity classical (such as [Jonathan Oppenheim’s stochastic approach](https://youtu.be/NKOd8imBa2s)) or by modifying quantum mechanics while keeping GR retro ([as in Penrose’s gravitational approach](https://youtu.be/iO03t21xhdk)). You could even potentially have some version of the [AdS/CFT duality](https://youtu.be/X4PdPnQuwjY) with the standard model somewhere in the bulk. Thus, “Definition 4” isn’t even an unambiguous definition!

Definition 1 doesn’t imply Definition 5 because you can easily have a plethora of theories that claim Definition 1 but don’t uniquely predict the particles. In fact, the Wikipedia article itself says there’s a “[string theory landscape](https://youtu.be/kUHOoMX4Bqw),” but it doesn’t register the contradiction with Definition 5.

A “theory of everything” isn’t just a theory that makes consonant quantum field theory and gravity. It has to be the **standard model + gravity**. The reason is that you could potentially have a consistent theory of quantum field theory + gravity, which either doesn’t specify the matter content, specifies the *wrong* matter content, or, even worse, actively contradicts the standard model at known energies!

[Causal set theory](https://youtu.be/PgYHEPCLVas), for instance, is often said to be a candidate for a theory of everything, but there are no clean particle concepts in it. Yes, there are scalar‑field propagators, but you still have fermion doubling (a different sort than the standard lattice problem) and other issues. Also, asymptotic safety is sometimes said to be a theory of everything, yet it’s a pure quantum‑gravity theory, and matter has to be coupled separately. Furthermore, there’s no constraint that forces the standard model.

However, it’s more than just that. It’s the above, *plus* the fact that the representation content has three generations of quarks and leptons in certain representations, *plus* the 25 apologies called free parameters that characterize masses, mixing factors, CP phases, theta angle, and so forth. None of this exactly follows from “gravity is quantum.”

Thus, “quantum gravity” is a false TOE. A noble effort, and something which may be on the correct track, but it is not a TOE in and of itself. Why do I call this a false TOE? Because, yes, while Stanisław Lem introduced “General Theory of Everything” (sarcastically, as far as I can tell!) in his *Star Diaries* (1960s), the actual theoretical‑physics adoption was because of John Schwarz and John Ellis. Schwarz mentioned the moniker of “theory of everything” in a conference (1985), and because of that, Ellis published “The superstring: theory of everything, or of nothing?” in *Nature*.

Ellis said it means “My definition of a TOE — a theory to ‘unify all the fundamental interactions’ and ‘explain the number and couplings of all the elementary particles.’”

This is what I’m calling a *Type A* TOE. So let’s get to it.

**NOTE:** As you move from *Type A* downward, the trend is to get less and less rigorous. And more and more lonely.

# Type A: Hopes to

This type involves the hopes of unifying gravity and aspects of the standard model. I say “hopes” because some of them don’t yet have the standard model *uniquely* come out, but instead find that the standard model is not contradictory to its overall scheme.

These include [Woit’s approach](https://youtu.be/9z3JYb_g2Qs), [Loop Quantum Gravity](https://youtu.be/r_fUPbBNmBw), [String Theory](https://youtu.be/X4PdPnQuwjY), and even [Wolfram’s appoach](https://youtu.be/FkYer0xP37E).

# Type B: Claims to

This type claims to have a deeper theory that includes gravity *and* the standard model as direct outcomes. Plus, it even (claims to) explain more, such as what dark matter is, or dark energy.

These include theories like [Neil Turok](https://youtu.be/ZUp9x44N3uE) / [Latham Boyle’s model](https://youtu.be/nyLeeEFKk04), [Weinstein’s Geometric Unity](https://youtu.be/AThFAxF7Mgw), [Causal Fermion Systems (Felix Finster)](https://youtu.be/fXzO_KAqrh0), and [Garrett Lisi’s model](https://youtu.be/z7ulJmfFvd8).

# Type C: More than

These are generally of the metaphysical sort. They also tend to say something about consciousness. Here’s where you have not just an unification of entities with interactions (with much less specificity than any of the above Types), but also a unification of the fact of following laws and the laws themselves. That is, you explain in a single framework the “laws” the universe follows, but then simultaneously why (and how) it “follows” those laws to begin with.

This is, as you suspect, a mild bastardization of the term “theory of everything,” which was meant to refer to theoretical physics, but it’s forgivable because of its ambition and trueness to the “spirit” behind the TOE endeavour (namely, some account that explains the more ontological elements of reality).

[Tegmark’s Mathematical Universe Hypothesis](https://youtu.be/-gekVfUAS7c). Some religions count here. Also a weltanschauung like Immanuel Kant’s. Information based TOEs loosely fit here, such as [Constructor Theory](https://youtu.be/40CB12cj_aM).

# Type D: And I mean “Everything”

This *Type* is even more than a metaphysical theory that attempts to explain different facets such as biology, sociology, physics, and psychology.

I’ll often speak to professors who will say something akin to “Curt, you know, you can’t actually have a theory of everything until you incorporate economic dynamics” or “You can’t actually have a TOE if you don’t explain emotions.” etc. They are referring to some subset of *Type D*.

They are also characterized by not formalizing with LaTeX. It’s Word documents all the way down.

These are people like Ken Wilber’s weltanschauung, Tyler Goldstein’s [Theory of Every0ne](https://www.youtube.com/@theoryofeveryone), and apparently something called the Hermetic Theory of Everything. I wrote that last one down a while ago, as a note to myself to look it up, but I just did a quick search and couldn’t find more. I’ll leave it here anyhow.

I’d say *Type D* is slightly more of a bastardization of the term “theory of everything,” but it’s understandable.

EDIT: Tyler Goldstein just mentioned to me that his theory is more correctly classified as *Type C*.

# Type E: No, no… Truly… *Every*thing

The participation trophy of TOEs…

When someone criticizes a theory of everything because it’s too physics / mathy / logic‑y / formalized, what they (charitably) mean is that it has to incorporate all phenomena, including EVEN MORE questions than why isn’t there nothing, what is consciousness, psychology, sociology, and politics.

The *Type E* person thinks… Does your theory, which you claim is of “everything,” actually explain *everything*? Or does it just explain those broad topics, but not other subject matters, like:

-   Can you solve any NP‑Complete problem in polynomial time?
    
-   Why did Constantinople fall on May 29, 1453, rather than May 28 or June 1?
    
-   The absorption rate of Bounty vs. Kirkland paper towels?
    
-   Why do most people think Taco Bell is horrible, but Curt thinks it’s so gosh‑darn fantastic?
    
-   Why does your car make the noise for everyone except the mechanic?
    
-   Why does a given dog circle 3 before lying down vs. 2 or 4?
    
-   Why does IKEA furniture always leave exactly one mysterious leftover screw?
    
-   How does your ex find a way to text you despite blocking her everywhere?
    

In other words, when you claim to have a “theory of ‘everything’,” you’re actually selecting what you consider to be illustrious problems and then saying you’re solving those. You’re not solving “everything,” even though you may criticize Theory X for being less encompassing than yours under the moniker of “well, Theory X doesn’t actually solve *everything* whereas mine does (har dee har har).”

The *Type E* person is thinking that you (the *Type A*, *Type B*, *Type C*, or *Type D* person) didn’t solve *everything*. You’ve at most solved a specific set of problems delineated by some criteria, but that criterion is clearly not all‑encompassing. This means you made a specific choice about what’s worth explaining and what’s not, in the same way that the theories (e.g., Theory X) you may criticize do.

You may rightfully be thinking, “Curt… Who the heck has a TOE of *Type E*?” Actually, everyone does! As you move down the list from *Type A* to *Type E* the trend is to become more democratic. Everyone thinks they have a shot at it. Indeed, every person has so many minute models of obscure abstruse phenomena (like why I love Taco Bell) that no TOE directly explains, but that you may personally have a model of.

*Type E* is the proverbial man on the street, jaywalking into theoretical physics. Your uncle has one. Guaranteed.

# You Only Have Nine Chips

Everyone only has somewhere between six and nine chips that they can place among any of these *Types* of theories of everything. Thus, explaining one of the *Types* puts you at a deficit for explaining the others. In other words, no one has an equal right to all of these types. Some people are masters of the physics theories but poor at the metaphysical theories; some are masters of the metaphysical type but abysmal at the physics; and some can explain some of the minutiae of life without any clue as to what the other *Type*s even mean.

For the TOE developer who’s reading this, you’ve only got a finite amount of chips, and you have to decide beforehand which of these four *Types* you want to devote pretty much your entire life to.

Laypeople inadvertently place their chips on *Type E*.

The people who are within a specific *Type* either believe that you can solve the problems of any other type from **within** their *Type*, or that insights from other *Types* are not required for them to make progress within their Type. For instance, there are a variety of philosophical assumptions that you hold onto without acknowledgment as an experimental physicist or engineer (causality, uniformity of nature, some form of Occam’s Razor, independence between you and your measuring device, reductionism, determinism, empiricism). That is the realm of a *Type C* TOE, but if you’re a string theorist, you would say that none of that matters (maybe causality, if anything, but even there it’s loose). If you’re a *Type D* person, you think, “Well, don’t I already encompass the others?”

Said differently, each person within each *Type* despises those in other *Types*, and thinks their own *Type* either gives rise to the rest, or the rest is unimportant. Unity in contempt. Interdisciplinary interestingly means hating more people.

# Arguments Against TOEs

There are people like [Stuart Kauffman who argue there is no theory of everything](https://curtjaimungal.substack.com/p/stuart-kauffman-there-is-no-theory), and even more who argue that Gödel’s first incompleteness theorem says a theory of everything is impossible to construct. I think they misunderstand that physicists are going for *Type A* and *Type B* TOEs. I also think it further misses that the point isn’t to prove your TOE is correct using math itself, but rather that a “theory of everything” is a “theory” where the word *theory* here means an *empirically sufficient model*. In other words, something like “this model is commensurate with the data, and continues to make correct predictions which could in principle ([a phrase I loath](https://curtjaimungal.substack.com/p/the-unexamined-in-principle)) be falsified.” There are Barrow / Jaki-type incompleteness arguments, though, which I will write about later.

These supposed disproofs also conflate the existence of a TOE with the fact that we’ve an irrefragable proof that the said TOE indeed is the true theory of everything. They’re different matters (one of existence, confidence, and acceptance).

# So where does that leave me?

It leaves me back at the casino.

A “theory of everything” is a regulative ideal. It’s something we asymptotically approach while arguing about what “approach” means.

I said you only have max nine chips to distribute across these, but that’s if you’re *developing* a TOE. I’m of a different sort. I’m a [cataloguer / understander of TOEs](https://youtube.com/TheoriesOfEverything). This leaves me with many more chips since surveying is less arduous than creation.

My goal is to understand every theory that’s ever been theorized, and to test my understanding by explaining it back to the progenitor of the TOE, and I consider my job a failure unless I’ve elicited a genuine “yes, that’s it! You got it!”

If I had to show my bias, it would be for *Type A*, *B*, and *C* theories. I’m less interested in the *Type D* and I have almost no interest in anything practical of the *Type* *E* sort, which explains why I can sit for hours discussing the [non-renormalizability of gravity](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ve_Mpd6dGv8&list=PLZ7ikzmc6zlN6E8KrxcYCWQIHg2tfkqvR) but still cannot explain why the Wi-Fi only cuts out when I’m about to hit “Publish.”
