## Where decentralized infrastructure (DePIN) is making inroads: 6 promising use cases

*Guy Wuollet*

[DePIN protocols](https://a16zcrypto.com/posts/tags/depin) offer a new model for building and operating real-world systems. At this point the term “DePIN” might feel conflated with [decentralized energy](https://a16zcrypto.com/posts/article/decentralized-energy-reading-list/), but there are many other industry applications.

The throughline connecting them is a path to sunsetting the monopolistic, opaque, and outmoded infrastructure systems we rely on today — and reimagining them as open, permissionless networks that are owned and operated by users. DePIN can lower barriers to entry, incentivize innovation, and distribute ownership and economic rewards across almost any category that relies on massive systems of physical infrastructure.

But how is DePIN actually playing out across real-world use cases? What progress have builders made so far? And what’s left to explore? Here are six categories — **[energy](https://a16zcrypto.com/posts/article/6-use-cases-for-depin/#section--1)**, **[telecom](https://a16zcrypto.com/posts/article/6-use-cases-for-depin/#section--2)**, **[transportation](https://a16zcrypto.com/posts/article/6-use-cases-for-depin/#section--3)**, **[AI](https://a16zcrypto.com/posts/article/6-use-cases-for-depin/#section--4)**, **[robotics](https://a16zcrypto.com/posts/article/6-use-cases-for-depin/#section--5)**, and **[healthcare](https://a16zcrypto.com/posts/article/6-use-cases-for-depin/#section--6)** — where DePIN is already making inroads, and where we may be headed next.

## Answering FAQs on the end of the foundation era

*Miles Jennings*

Calling for an end to crypto’s offshoring in our recent article “[The end of the foundation era in crypto](https://a16zcrypto.com/posts/article/end-foundation-era-crypto/)” sparked some debate. In this post, a16z crypto Head of Policy Miles Jennings address five of the most commonly asked questions, including…

#### What about tax?

“Offshore foundations can offer tax benefits, but there’s more risk here than many people think (and more than advisors are willing to admit). Most founders we talk to would, in retrospect, gladly give up the tax efficiencies they gained through offshoring to eliminate the overhead those structures create.

#### Is there a role for foundations going forward?

Definitely. The main point of the piece wasn’t “no foundations ever” — it was that we should stop using them for decentralization theater. Market structure legislation forcing that change is a good thing.

Foundations (especially domestic ones) with focused mandates — like executing grant programs and coordinating ecosystem-wide efforts — will continue to be useful.

## Updates from DC: GENIUS passes the Senate

The [GENIUS Act](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/senate-bill/394/text) — the first comprehensive crypto regulatory framework in the United States — passed the U.S. Senate in a bipartisan vote on Tuesday afternoon. As a16z crypto founder and general partner Chris Dixon [wrote](https://x.com/cdixon/status/1935093472958234762): “This stablecoin bill protects consumers and gives entrepreneurs the guardrails they need to drive innovation and economic growth in crypto and beyond. Now we need comprehensive market structure legislation so the US can lead in building blockchain networks.”
