### Stablecoins are doing trillions in transaction volume

[

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2UlG!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F43c6b3d5-03c9-487a-bd0d-8d3491febec8_1536x864.png)



](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!2UlG!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F43c6b3d5-03c9-487a-bd0d-8d3491febec8_1536x864.png)

[Stablecoins](https://a16zcrypto.com/posts/tags/stablecoins/) have become a [global macroeconomic force](https://cointelegraph.com/news/stablecoins-global-macroeconomic-force-a16z).

They’ve done $46 trillion in total transaction volume in the last year, up 106% from the year before. That’s nearly 3x that of Visa’s transaction volume, and is approaching the volume of the ACH network that plumbs the entire U.S. banking system.

While these are not apples-to-apples comparisons, the sheer volume highlights just how large this trend has become. (The stablecoin figures mostly represent financial flows versus retail payments for card and other networks.)

A better measure of organic activity looks at adjusted stablecoin transaction volume, which filters out bots and other artificially inflationary activity. On this basis, stablecoins have done $9 trillion in volume in the last 12 months. This is more than 5x PayPal’s throughput.  
  
It’s worth noting that stablecoin volume is now mostly uncorrelated with broader crypto trading volume, which suggests that stablecoins have found organic use cases and product-market fit beyond crypto market activity.

### Blockchains can process more than 3,400 transactions per second

[

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sJyy!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F665e954b-cd8a-4dce-b43e-b314a1829b98_1536x864.png)



](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sJyy!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F665e954b-cd8a-4dce-b43e-b314a1829b98_1536x864.png)

In just five years, aggregate transaction throughput across major blockchain networks has increased more than 100x.

Back then, blockchains processed fewer than 25 transactions per second. Now they process 3,400 transactions per second, which is on par with Stripe’s global throughput on Black Friday — at a fraction of the historical cost.

Solana’s high-performance, low-fee blockspace now underpins everything from DePIN projects to NFT marketplaces, with its native applications generating $3 billion in revenue in the past year.

As Ethereum continues to execute on its scaling roadmap, most of its economic activity is migrating to L2s. Average transaction costs on L2s have dropped from around $24 in 2021 to less than one cent today.

-   Zcash’s shielded pool supply grew to nearly 4 million ZEC

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!ugkr!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F3f0d1ade-9637-4b7a-8471-e6326ff215e0_1536x864.png)

More than 13 million memecoins [launched](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-10-22/more-than-13-million-memecoins-show-urgent-need-for-regulation) in the last year on Solana memecoin launchpads.

A lack of regulatory clarity in the U.S. may have contributed to this surge. During the last administration, it was [arguably safer](https://a16zcrypto.com/posts/article/memecoins-tokens-regulation-policy/) to launch a memecoin than to build a crypto company or create a productive token.

This trend appears to be cooling down in recent months: There were 56% fewer launches in September than in January.

#### Stablecoins meet IRL payment rails

One of the biggest legacy payments networks is now embracing blockchain rails for real-world money movement: Western Union just [announced](https://ir.westernunion.com/news/archived-press-releases/press-release-details/2025/Western-Union-Announces-USDPT-Stablecoin-on-Solana-and-Digital-Asset-Network/default.aspx) plans to launch its own U.S. dollar-backed stablecoin (USDPT) — built on Solana and issued by Anchorage Digital Bank — alongside a new Digital Asset Network that connects crypto to cash through its global retail and banking partners. It’s designed to make cross-border transfers fast, cheap, and accessible — especially in emerging markets — while leveraging recent regulatory clarity around stablecoins.

Meanwhile, payments processor [Zelle](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/payments-processor-zelle-stablecoins-international-181854469.htm) announced that it will be using stablecoins for international transactions to “deliver faster and more reliable cross-border money movement,” the parent company said.
