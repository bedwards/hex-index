When discussing medium and long range delivery systems, one more development deserves close attention. On February 5th this year, the New Start treaty formally expired. The agreement had capped the number of deployed nuclear warheads held by the United States and Russia at 1,550 per side while also limiting the number of deployed delivery systems.

New start had been in force since 2010 and was last extended in 2021 by Joe Biden and Vladimir Putin for 5 years. Its expiration means that for the first time since 1972, the United States and Russia are operating without any formal limits on their nuclear arsenals. Broadly speaking, it is worth noting that Washington was the less enthusiastic party when it came to extending the treaty. Donald Trump had previously described the new start as a badly negotiated deal, arguing that it needed to be fundamentally revised.

The most recent talks in Abu Dhabi concluded with both sides agreeing to observe the treaty's terms for at least six additional months during which negotiations toward a new framework are expected to take place.
