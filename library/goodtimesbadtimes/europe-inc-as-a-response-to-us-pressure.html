EU Inc. is a new panuropean legal standard designed to make it easier to establish, scale, and finance startups across the European Union. Its goal is to create an optional 28th legal form operating alongside national structures such as SARL in France or GmbH in Germany, but based on a single unified rule book valid throughout the EU.

In practical terms, EU Inc. would allow a company to be registered in any member state within 48 hours, fully online for a single central registry and under identical capital rules. The initiative rests on several key pillars, a common legal statute, EUwide harmonization of ECOPS, employee stock option plans, and simplified crossber financing through standardized investment documents and stronger protection for minority investors. The objective is to eliminate the bureaucratic fragmentation that has long pushed European startups to relocate their legal structures to the United States, the so-called Delaware flip, and to make Europe a market as easy to operate in as the US or China.

As Ursula Vanderlayan emphasized, if implemented quickly and decisively, the project would not only accelerate the growth of European companies, but also attract the global capital into the EU.
