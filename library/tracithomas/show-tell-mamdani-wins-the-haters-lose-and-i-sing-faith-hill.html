Books I Read This Week

We the Animals by Justin Torres
A novel about the youngest of three brothers in upstate New York coming of age and grappling with his relationship to himself, his siblings, and his parents. I knew people liked it, and still I somehow think it is supremely underrated. It is doing so much in so few words. Torres captures the full essence of complex humans in a sentence or two. You can’t help but fall in love with this wild rag tag family even when you know the sheer intensity of it all is careening toward something combustible. I rarely cry when I read, but this one got me. It has so much heart and a driving force that is so undeniable. A wow of a book. This is our November pick for The Stacks Book Club, so please know you’ll be getting much more about this book later this month2.
