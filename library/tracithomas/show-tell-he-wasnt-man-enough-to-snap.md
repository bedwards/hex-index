# This Week in The Stacks

I did the thing I always worry about doing, I sent out this newsletter without a title. It wasn’t ideal, but I’m proud to report I survived.

October’s book club pick was *[Frankenstein](https://bookshop.org/a/3111/9780143131847)* and talking about it with was a highlight of the year. I loved the book and could’ve done about five more hours on it.

I read nine books in October and ranked them for you. I also lined up the 10 books I’m excited about in November.

Our book club pick for November is *[We the Animals](https://bookshop.org/a/3111/9780547844190)* by Justin Torres. I have never read his work, so I’m excited to read this slim novel[1](#footnote-1) about three brothers coming of age in upstate New York.

## Books I Read This Week

[

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!fcwm!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6f538ce6-9b04-4824-b999-2a1121265e02_1640x924.png)

***[Places I’ve Taken My Body](https://bookshop.org/a/3111/9780892555383)*** **by Molly McCully Brown  
**A collection of essays that “explores living within and beyond the limits of a body” shaped by cerebral palsy. Brown is a gifted writer who gives style without alienating the reader. While many of the essays began to run together for me, there were a handful that did stick out (one of forgetting and another on *[Frankenstein](https://bookshop.org/a/3111/9780143131847)*[2](#footnote-2)) and allowed me the space to meditate more on our unique relationships to our bodies.

***[Coach](https://bookshop.org/a/3111/9798347102372)*** **by Jason Reynolds  
**The final installment in the *[Track](https://bookshop.org/a/3111/9798347112821)* series, which tells Coach’s backstory as a middle school sprinter. This book does exactly what I hoped it would and what you’d expect from the series. It is cute, and silly, and has a moral that lands. This one has a slower start and doesn’t really get cooking until the final third, but then it does all come together for a strong ending.

***[Truly](https://bookshop.org/a/3111/9780063253643)*** **by Lionel Richie  
**A memoir from the music legend, my guy, Lionel Richie. Is this a great memoir, no. Is it entertaining and full of people who have impacted the last 75 years of pop culture? Yes. Yes, it is. I did actually appreciate some of the storytelling choices, but overall thought the book was way too long and wasn’t nearly as strong in the back half as it was at the start. His childhood through the early years of stardom were really compelling and engaging, but by the 90’s there wasn’t much there there. Also, the book could’ve been juicier. He plays it safe and avoids the gossip. If you love Lionel Richie this is a big yes, otherwise I’m not sure you’ll be as taken by it.  
**Fave of the week!**

***[The White Hot](https://bookshop.org/a/3111/9780593732335)*** **by Quiara Alegría Hudes  
**A mother, April, abandons her daughter and family and writes a letter ten years later explaining what happened. This book leans into the confession and shares an intimate if ultimately untidy perspective on motherhood and rage. I enjoyed the prose and Hudes is great at voice, she makes April come to life. There is an ugliness to April and how she sees the world that was fantastic, and that pulled me in at the start. By the end, it lacked the movement necessary to sustain an all vibes, no plot novel.

Imagine being Taylor Swift and writing a whole album about how your dude is this and that and you wanna go be his wife and be dicked down every day to Tuesday. And you spend all this time on your album calling his exes thinly-veiled racist shit. And then one of his exes, Kayla Nicole, dresses up as the Toni Braxton [“He Wasn’t Man Enough”](https://open.spotify.com/track/7f1Dmr246cJ9uQYdbplTbh) music video for Halloween. And she doesn’t just dress up, but [reenacts the video](https://www.instagram.com/p/DQe4klkjwwa/?hl=en), looking flawless, and executing choreo[3](#footnote-3) better than you ever could.

How badly would you want to die? I would never be able to handle the speed and accuracy of the checkmate. Like you made a whole album and she made a parody. Stunning.
