Books I Read This Week

Sad Tiger by Neige Sinno translated by Natasha Lehrer
Sinno’s memoir of her childhood with a step-father who raped her for years starting at the age of seven. The subject matter here is dark, and Sinno balances that with her use of language and exploration of purpose. She is constantly questioning her own thoughts and opinions and the power of a story and the veracity of memories. This book is honest and challenging and extremely well done.
Fave of the week!

Nature Poem by Tommy Pico
A book length poem about why the author, a queer indigenous poet, can’t write a nature poem. Fresh, funny, and full of voice, this poem is at times tender and then immediately brash and confrontational. Pico is obviously interested in stereotypes, friction with his audience, and the ways words sound and feel as they are released into the world.

Girl on Girl: How Pop Culture Turned a Generation of Women Against Themselves by Sophie GIilbert
An examination of the trends in feminism and the representation of women in popular culture from the 1990’s to today. Gilbert is an extremely talented writer. You can tell she is super smart and thoughtful, her writing feels accessible but never common. While there were times in the book that I lost her overreaching argument or that it felt redundant, on a chapter level her ability to transform her research into prose with a fierce point of view was entertaining as hell.

December Books on Deck

Very few books come out in December, mostly the readers and publishers are focused on gifts and end of year lists. Most years I don’t even bother doing a “Books on Deck” for December, because it’s such slim pickin’s. However, this year there are two titles I can’t let go unremarked upon.

  • American Canto by Olivia Nuzzi (December 2)
    Look Olivia Nuzzi is a controversial figure, and while I did not have an opinion of her like 22 days ago, the media storm around her has made it a must read book for me. If you don’t know who she is, basically she’s noteworthy for being a journalist who got fired for having had an affair with RFK Jr. Turns our she also dated Keith Olbermann (more below) and allegedly Mark “Appalachian Trail” Sanford. Many people are slut shamming her for this. Me? I do not care what she does with her body. Boring. I am always here for mess. And it sounds like if there is mess to be had Nuzzi will be the person to deliver it. So yes, I will read this (so you don’t have to).

  • 24 Hours at the Capitol: An Oral History of the January 6th Insurrection by Nora Neus (December 30)
    One of two oral histories on the January 6th riots coming out in the next little bit. This one from past guest of the podcast, Nora Neus, is the first to drop and I am excited to see what kind of crazy is captured in these pages.

Things I Love…

Books

The New York Times dropped their 100 most notable books of the year list. I always look forward to this list. It is a consistently all over the place list. Often featuring my most loved and hated reads of the year. The NYT books department is nothing is not chaotic.

This year, people online got upset with the list for a myriad of reasons (because of course they did) but the main one was that people felt like the books on the list weren’t good. And to that I would say, correct. A lot of them aren’t. But, this is where reading comprehension comes in, babes. The New York Times calls this list their most “notable” books of the year because they are including books that are “of note” even if they are mediocre (or bad in some cases) at best. They are consistent with this. Every year. We do not need to keep having this conversation. Words mean things. Notable does not mean best. It means of note.

Glad we could clear that up. Love to help.

Now. Should the New York Times have a list that features the best 100 books of the year or even 100 great books from 2025? Yeah, maybe they should.

What they do have is their 10 best books of the year1, which we should be getting this week. That is the list I am most excited about. Mostly because every year the list includes a book or two I love a book or two I actively dislike and then a book or two I have never heard of. What more are you looking for in a list?

Ok, but I do have more to say about this list that pretty much directly contradicts me in my hate section today, so stay tuned.
