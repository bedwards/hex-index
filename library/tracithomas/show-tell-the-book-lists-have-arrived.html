Books I Read This Week

Middle Spoon by Alejandro Valera
A breakup novel written in unsent emails from our narrator (a man in a polyamorous marriage) to his ex-boyfriend. This book worked for me. It is funny and tender and explores more than just heartbreak — mental health, polyamory, public health, parenting, race. The email constraint allowed the broad scope to feel grounded in a the reality of the narrator just venting. Our protagonist is a relatable obsessive who borders on annoying but, I mostly found him endearing, despite the pain, whining, and turmoil. Middle Spoon is one of those books that is easy and enjoyable but has a depth that makes it resonate on another frequency. It was a total delight.
Fave of the week!

Things I Love…

In Memoriam

I have never actually made an official “In Memoriam” section before, but it felt like I should start in honor of the passing of Alice Wong. I didn’t want to miss the opportunity to acknowledge her life. Wong was among many other things a disability rights activist, Bay Area icon, author, writer, mentor, and historian.

Her work has shaped my own thinking and understanding of disability justice and what accessibility really means. Seeing the outpouring of love and reverence for her in the last few days from some of the writers/thinkers I admire most, reminds me that her life was a powerful force for changing the world and what is possible for all of us.
