Books I Read This Week

What a Time to Be Alive by Jade Chang
Lola Treasure Gold is a 30 something whose best friend dies in a tragic accident, her viral speech at his wake launches her into online guru influencer status. I liked this book. I was expecting a satire of online culture and ended up with a much more interior meditation on grief. In some ways this book suffered from not being able to blend these two parts together, but in other ways I was pleasantly surprised with Chang’s choices and the ways she found the pleasure in grief.
Fave of the week!

The Convert: A Tale of Exile and Extremism by Deborah Baker
The story of a Jewish woman from New York, Maryam Jameelah (formerly Margaret Marcus), who converts to Islam, moves to Pakistan, and becomes one of the most celebrated and influential voices in Islam’s argument with the West. I picked this book up to satisfy a prompt for the Mega Challenge2, and knew nothing going in. The book is well crafted — relying on letters, research, and the author’s interviews — but lacked a clear point of view. I never quite knew what Baker was saying or why, she never grabbed me. The book came out ten years after 9/11 and while it might have spoken to the moment in 2011, in 2025 it felt stale and thin. I wanted more from this one across the board, especially when it came to Jameelah’s rise and impact on society.

Things I Love…

Music & Podcasts

D’Angelo? Damn.

His music reminds me so much of my high school years in Oakland. Crushes. Mix CDs. Car rides. Kick its. He defined an era for me when I was just starting to think I was grown, when I certainly wasn’t, be desperately wanted to be.

The music video for “Untitled (How Does It Feel)” is up there with the most iconic music videos of all time. And the vocals on that track are so under appreciated because the body-ody-ody is body-ody-odying. But, my man was sangin’ for real, for real.

The track that I always think of though is D’Angelo and Lauryn Hill on “Nothing Even Matters”. The song is damn near perfect. But, I will never forget riding home from dinner in SF with my brother as was blasting this song and trying to teach me how to do the snaps. Some twos, some fours. And then he also was like “do you even hear all the vocal layering…there are only two people on this track! Doesn’t it sound like it is a group?”. I love this song so much because it is so deeply tied to a time, place, and a very important person in my life. I can’t hear it and not think of my brother. Music is so fucking magical.

May D’Angelo rest in peace. The music lives.
