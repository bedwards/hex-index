Before his National Book Award–winning novel, *[Blackouts](https://bookshop.org/a/3111/9781250338068)*, Justin Torres debuted with a semi-autobiographical novel called *[We the Animals](https://bookshop.org/a/3111/9780547844190)*. That slim novel is our November Book Club pick. Set in rural upstate New York in the 1980s, this coming-of-age novel traces the experiences of three mixed-race brothers navigating the chaos of boyhood amid the intensity of their parents’ tumultuous marriage. The narrative is split into a series of vignettes, beautifully capturing the intense familial bonds that can both hold us together and tear us apart. This book was a breakout hit when it was published in 2011—it earned awards and critical acclaim, was adapted into a [feature film](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/We_the_Animals_\(film\)) in 2018, and was featured in the *New York Times’* “[100 Best Books of the 21st Century.](https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2024/books/best-books-21st-century.html)” With all the family drama and dynamics, it feels like the perfect book to read days before the Thanksgiving Holiday.
