Last Wednesday I woke up with a mission.

Rolled out of bed.

Jumped into my chair.

*And, prepared to **replace myself.***

Let me explain…

Sometimes, my workflows get interrupted when I’m deep in flow.

**It drives me nuts.**

You probably know what I’m talking about…

That one small task you do manually.

It steals a few minutes here, steals a few minutes there.

It adds up.

Whenever I’m deep in flow, and something breaks my focus, I want to rip my hair out.

So…I spent Wednesday morning automating tasks.

In other words, replacing myself.

That way I can swiftly move through my deep work. 

With my flow never being broken.

Just how I like it.

How it’s supposed to be.

It took me 1-2 hours — but it’ll save me dozens of hours in the future.

The truth is…

This is a normal Wednesday for me.

I’m always “replacing myself.”

I spend 1 day per week working to make the other 4 easier.

“Give me six hours to chop down a tree and I will spend the first four sharpening the axe.” *—Abraham Lincoln*

20% to optimize 80%

Give it a shot.

It adds up.
