The 28 Points—Is It More Interesting Who Backed Them?

When I first started preparing for this update a few days ago, my original concept was that it would be based around a detailed analysis of the text of the 28 points themselves. You can read them all here if you want. They were so outrageously pro-Russian and anti-Ukrainian in their orientation that I thought that needed to be laid out in detail.

Now that it is likely that much of the text came from Russian sources (though not all I wager) such a breakdown seems a little less urgent. However, I wanted to point out two things which reveal just how they were created to appeal to Trump and how they are set up to screw Ukraine.

The appeals to Trump are how he can immediately back out of the deal, even after getting Ukraine to pay him lots of money first. It is in point ten.

10. The US guarantee:

- The US will receive compensation for the guarantee;

- If Ukraine invades Russia, it will lose the guarantee;

Who determines this “compensation” that the US will receive for its security guarantee to Ukraine? Well it can only be the US government. What this means, is that the US government can levy a massive bill on Ukraine every year, and if Ukraine cannot pay, the security guarantee would immediately lapse. Can anything be more Trump-like? The US can screw Ukraine out of money from the start and then walk away from the deal at any time by making it unaffordable.

Note: There is also a point that the US gets half the profits from Ukrainian rebuilding, which is so Trump-like its almost funny.

The other point is the number of different points that limit Ukrainian sovereignty. Russia has no sovereignty limitations at all in this document, while Ukraine is treated as an inferior throughout. Ukraine cannot join NATO (ever!) it cannot have NATO forces stationed in its border, its military has to accept outside limitations, etc. Here are some of these points.

7. Ukraine agrees to enshrine in its constitution that it will not join NATO, and NATO agrees to include in its statutes a provision that Ukraine will not be admitted in the future.

8. NATO agrees not to station troops in Ukraine.

It is creating an enfeebled Ukraine. Though, that of course has been Trump’s wishes throughout.

Final point on this plan. You want to know another very revealing thing? It was those who had been posing as pro-Ukraine voices who came out strongly to support it (hysterically in a few cases before it was clear just how many points came straight from Russia).

One of the most revealing was the historian and commentator Niall Ferguson, who for years has passed himself off publicly as one of Ukraine’s great supporters. Well, as soon as the 28 points came out, Ferguson, amazingly, decided that they were jolly good and solid basis for negotiations.

This public embrace of the Trump-Putin plan did not go well for Ferguson, as those who believed that he had honestly backed Ukraine (when he really always prioritized Trump) were aghast.

However, who do you think rode to his rescue—none other than JD Vance! The greatest enemy of Ukraine in the US administration, said Ferguson was on to something.

Who also loves the 28 points—well supposedly that great supporter of Ukraine, General Kellogg. He even came out and said how they needed to be forced on Ukraine after it was reported that they were Russian influenced. I remember when people said he would do the right thing—-ha.
