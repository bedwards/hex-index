Welcome to the 2028 election.

#### **The US Becomes A Mouthpiece To Pass On Putin’s Demands**

I’m starting this update later in the week, yesterday, after the 28 points had been released, and while the Trump administration was in the process of threatening Ukraine if the Ukrainians did not agree to them. People started looking at the English-language text of the 28 points and noticed weird linguistic ticks. In a nutshell, many of the phrases were commonly used Russian phrases that seem to have been translated directly into English. Here [is one story](https://www.dailykos.com/stories/2025/11/22/2355147/-The-peace-plan-for-Ukraine-was-written-in-Russian-first-The-linguistic-evidence-is-damning), and [another story](https://www.theguardian.com/world/live/2025/nov/21/europe-latest-news-russia-ukraine-war-updates-zelenskyy-putin-g20?filterKeyEvents=false&page=with%3Ablock-69204d2e8f08a77eddc21bbe#block-69204d2e8f08a77eddc21bbe) about the linguistic analysis. It certainly seems a strongly made case (though I am no expert on the passive voice of the Russian language).

First, we need to acknowledge what this means. Not only has the Trump administration ended its “Long Con” as I wrote yesterday—the con being that Trump and his acolytes were actually honest brokers and wanted to work with Putin and might hammer Russia. It has even gone further. The Trump administration is now a mouthpiece for Russian demands and an agent to have Russian wishes met.

As I wrote in that piece, the idea that Trump and his administration could be honest brokers or even supporters of Ukraine was a con. They changed tack when there first strategy of bullying Ukraine blew up in their faces. Since then they have bided their time, done a great deal of playacting, but were always going to return to what they were—Putin supporters because of Trump’s basic wishes to work with the Russian dictator.

The reality that the administration seems happy to take text directly from Russian documents and pass that on as official US positions, I hope, ends any debate over this. The con was a con, the US government and Russian governments are in lockstep. Indeed, [the Trump Administration is even willing to threaten Ukrainians if they do not agree to the Russian plan by the end of the week](https://www.reuters.com/world/europe/us-threatens-cut-intel-weapons-press-ukraine-into-peace-deal-sources-2025-11-21/).

And btw, where are those secondary sanctions on Chinese, Indian and Turkish oil companies we were promised on November 21? Any sign of them?

The US is no honest broker, it is not Ukraine or Europe’s friend. It is a supporter, indeed a mouthpiece of brutal dictatorship that is trying to conquer the land and subjugate the people of a democracy.

Face facts and move on.

#### **The 28 Points—Is It More Interesting Who Backed Them?**

When I first started preparing for this update a few days ago, my original concept was that it would be based around a detailed analysis of [the text of the 28 points themselves. You can read them all here if you want.](https://news.sky.com/story/trumps-28-point-ukraine-peace-plan-in-full-including-land-kyiv-must-hand-to-russia-and-when-elections-must-be-held-13473491) They were so outrageously pro-Russian and anti-Ukrainian in their orientation that I thought that needed to be laid out in detail.

Now that it is likely that much of the text came from Russian sources (though not all I wager) such a breakdown seems a little less urgent. However, I wanted to point out two things which reveal just how they were created to appeal to Trump and how they are set up to screw Ukraine.

The appeals to Trump are how he can immediately back out of the deal, even after getting Ukraine to pay him lots of money first. It is in point ten.

> 10\. The US guarantee:
> 
> \- The US will receive compensation for the guarantee;
> 
> \- If Ukraine invades Russia, it will lose the guarantee;

Who determines this “compensation” that the US will receive for its security guarantee to Ukraine? Well it can only be the US government. What this means, is that the US government can levy a massive bill on Ukraine every year, and if Ukraine cannot pay, the security guarantee would immediately lapse. Can anything be more Trump-like? The US can screw Ukraine out of money from the start and then walk away from the deal at any time by making it unaffordable.

*Note: There is also a point that the US gets half the profits from Ukrainian rebuilding, which is so Trump-like its almost funny.*

The other point is the number of different points that limit Ukrainian sovereignty. Russia has no sovereignty limitations at all in this document, while Ukraine is treated as an inferior throughout. Ukraine cannot join NATO (ever!) it cannot have NATO forces stationed in its border, its military has to accept outside limitations, etc. Here are some of these points.

> 7\. Ukraine agrees to enshrine in its constitution that it will not join NATO, and NATO agrees to include in its statutes a provision that Ukraine will not be admitted in the future.
> 
> 8\. NATO agrees not to station troops in Ukraine.

It is creating an enfeebled Ukraine. Though, that of course has been Trump’s wishes throughout.

Final point on this plan. You want to know another very revealing thing? It was those who had been posing as pro-Ukraine voices who came out strongly to support it (hysterically in a few cases before it was clear just how many points came straight from Russia).

One of the most revealing was the historian and commentator Niall Ferguson, who for years has passed himself off publicly as one of Ukraine’s great supporters. Well, as soon as the 28 points came out, Ferguson, amazingly, decided that they were jolly good and solid basis for negotiations.
