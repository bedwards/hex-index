Hi All,

I was writing a piece comparing the Trump and Biden Administration’s positions on Ukraine’s future when [the Bloomberg story broke yesterday with transcripts of a conversation between Steve Witkoff and a Yuri Ushakov, a very senior Putin adviser](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-11-25/witkoff-discusses-ukraine-plans-with-key-putin-aide-transcript). As such, I have made a quick pivot and will talk about that story, and release the Biden/Trump piece tomorrow (or the day after if my US readers want a break for Thanksgiving).

The Bloomberg piece was almost ideally and tragically timed to go with the piece I had released earlier in the day yesterday. This argued that people are so desperate to make excuses for Trump that are willing to court disaster.

Here is what that piece said about this phenomena when it comes specifcally to Trump, Russia and Ukraine.

*And yet, what we hear is not outright condemnation of Trump and his frankly malevolent plans but, disconcertingly, a chorus of excuses on his behalf; attempts to say that he did not know and, ultimately was not responsible for the actions of his administration. The attempts to separate Trump from the catastrophes of his own administration’s actions is unprecedented. First it was said that this was some rogue operation by stupid and naive Steve Witkoff, who was freelancing and hoodwinked by the clever Russians.*

Almost immediately after the Bloomberg story was written, the chorus of Trump excuses started again saying such things as: “Trump did not know about the 28-point plan” (laughable) or “Trump was fooled by Witkoff” (risible). This need to try and say that Trump was not and therefore ultimately is not responsible for the policies of his administration is deplorable and shows why Trump has been able to do so much damage as people sit by and make excuses for him. Just a few points to show why.

-   The Bloomberg story actually proves Trump was being kept in the loop. Witkoff would only be advising the Russians in this matter if Trump was going to be updated on the situation. Why would Witkoff be talking about how Trump should be approached, unless he knew Trump would be approached—on exactly these issues. So the whole context of the Witkoff-Ushakov discussion was predicated on Trump being consulted.
    
-   The reaction to the Bloomberg story shows Trump was more than happy with Witkoff’s behavior and indeed confirmed Witkoff’s role by saying he will soon be off to Moscow to represent the USA.
    

The second point is the most extraordinary. The Bloomberg story can easily be read as strongly supporting the idea that Witkoff is acting as a Russian agent of influence. One would think that if this was either a surprise to Trump or something that made Trump upset, the White House would go into overdrive to get rid of or at least demote Witkoff.

The opposite happened, the White House and Trump personally have gone to great lengths to reconfirm Witkoff’s central role and defend his actions. It started almost immediately after the Bloomberg story broke when a White House spokesperson said the story was a big nothingburger.

That was just the start. Soon Trump himself was clearly confirming that Witkoff remained his point man for talks with Putin, [and would soon be off to Moscow (possibly with Jared Kushner](https://x.com/kaitlancollins/status/1993470104932433988?s=20)—they seem to be a team on this) to meet with Putin. Trump said:

*[“Now, Steve Witkoff is going over maybe with Jared. I’m not sure about Jared going, but he’s involved in the process, smart guy, and they’re going to be meeting with President Putin, I believe, next week in Moscow,”](https://abcnews.go.com/International/kremlin-aide-preliminary-agreement-place-witkoff-moscow-visit/story?id=127887384)*

And the [Russians have just confirmed, btw, that they expect Witkoff in Moscow](https://www.rferl.org/a/russia-ukraine-peace-talks-witkoff-moscow-trip-ushakov/33605936.html) in the coming days.

Then, Trump went further and [specifically endorsed and defended how Witkoff had negotiated with the Russians.](https://www.politico.eu/article/donald-trump-backs-steve-witkoff-after-leak-reveals-advised-vladimir-putin-aide-ukraine-deal/) Trump said what Witkoff was doing is typical negotiating behavior.

*“No, but that’s a standard thing, you know, because he’s got to sell this to Ukraine. He’s got to sell Ukraine to Russia. That’s what he’s, that’s what a deal maker does.”*

*“You got to say, look, they want this. You’ve got to convince him with this. You know, that’s a very standard form of negotiation. I haven’t heard it, but I heard it was standard negotiation, and I would imagine he’s saying the same thing to Ukraine, because each party has to give and take,”*

So there we have it. Trump is more than happy with Witkoff’s behavior and has confirmed that Witkoff is his point-man for the Moscow talks. The shocking thing is that some people thought otherwise.

Actually, this is typical Trump behavior. Witkoff is only the latest of his close associates who have acted like a Russian agent of influence. Trump has regularly employed and promotes people who act like Russian agents of influence, such as Paul Manafort, Michael Flynn and Steve Bannon. In fact, Trump himself regularly acts like a Russian agent of influence, as I outlined in this piece published in May.

It would be nice if someone around Trump acted like an agent of influence for freedom and democracy. But that seems too much to ask.

So there we have it. We now have proof, from the horse’s mouth so to speak, that Witkoff is doing exactly what Trump wants and Trump knows it. Can we stop, once and for all, making excuses on Trump’s behalf? The USA under his leadership is a danger to freedom—admit that and act on it.
