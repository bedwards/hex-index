Although Dalkey Archive did publish two works by Desmond Hogan (*[A Farewell to Prague](https://bookshop.org/a/164/9781564788542)*, *[The House of Mourning and Other Stories](https://bookshop.org/a/164/9781564788559)*), this episode covers *[The Ikon Maker](https://bookshop.org/a/164/9781843513872)*, Hogan’s first novel from 1976, reissued in 2013 by Lilliput Press. [Vince](https://www.vincentfrancone.com/) and Chad talk about this short, moving book about a woman whose adult son has moved to England and fallen out of touch, about the forthcoming (in 2039?) opera of *Finnegans Wake*, about Hogan’s disturbing conviction and ensuing disappearance, about the way sexuality is portrayed in *The Ikon Maker*, and much more.

The next book to be featured in this series is *[Threshold](https://www.amazon.com/Threshold-Doyle-Rob/dp/1526607085/ref=tmm_pap_swatch_0)* by Rob Doyle. (Who also [wrote about the reissues](https://stingingfly.org/review/farewell-prague-ikon-maker/) of both *The Ikon Maker* and *A Farewell to Prague.*)

This episode’s music is “McLeyvier” by Rich Aucoin.
