## The 28-Point Ukraine Peace Plan

Ukrainian political scientist Ivan Katchanovski [published](https://x.com/I_Katchanovski/status/1991604970467000457) the details of the 28 point plan earlier today, with a full English translation from documents leaked to the Ukrainian media. The New York Post later confirmed all these details in American reporting of the document.

The document is likely a developed draft by US officials of the “no paper” circulated by Russian officials following the Anchorage summit.

The Russian President and Foreign Minister have referred to this document and there was speculation that it contained 31 points. John Helmer has reported that the 28 point plan is a US adaptation.

There is no reason to suppose that Russian officials, or event the US Cabinet has agreed to these documents. It may still be an advanced draft of the “no paper.” A “no paper” is a diplomatic document drafted without prejudice or commitment to enable open discussion of possible terms of negotiation. It provides a framework for a process, but does not signal agreement

There are reports that US President Special Envoy to Ukraine may have leaked the document to sabotage negotiations and in dissatisfaction with the directions of negotiations. Reports of his January departure may support this. However, early reports from Ukraine are that Ukrainian officials may well defy the USA on both this plan and the corruption issue. Speculation about the source of leaks is generally unhelpful.

As you will see in reading the 28 points, the document is heavily skewed to US interests. There are likely to be significant departures from any initial Russian official draft document. There are points proposing action by the EU, Britain and Ukraine that the USA cannot deliver. During negotiations since early 2025, the USA has shown no capability of securing the agreement of its nominal allies.

## Analysis of the 28-Point Plan

But what is my analysis?

First, I would break down the 28 point plan into seven broad topic areas.

1.  Political Measures (Clauses 1, 20 Denazification/Civil Right, 24-26)
    
2.  European /Ukraine Security (Clauses 2-5, 10)
    
3.  Demilitarisation/NATO (Clauses 6-9)
    
4.  Reconstruction (Clauses 11-14, *19*)
    
5.  Global Security (Clauses 15-18, *19*)
    
6.  Territory (Clauses 21-23)
    
7.  Implementation (Clauses 27-28)
    

These topics deal to some degree with the explicit objectives of the Russian military operation and elements of the causes of the conflict. However, as the Transnational Foundation and Jan Oberg speculated - on the basis of rumoured press reports - the 28-point peace plan is another American hustle. It is not based on any form basis for conflict settlement, such as TFF will soon propose and as I have outlined in my Ukraine Endgame videos (see below).

In particular, the plan fails to involve Europe, Britain or Ukraine in the conflict settlement. This is a plan for the USA to leave the war and to get the loot, as I have been arguing has been the USA strategy since February.

## Clause-by-Clause Analysis

**The full text of the plan for your own assessment follows. I will provide a brief analysis and comment. I will provide more interpretation in my upcoming video next week on Europe and Ukraine’s perspective on the Ukraine Endgame.**

*1\. Ukraine’s sovereignty will be confirmed.*

**Comment:** This initial clause on political measures and sovereignty suggests there is no plan to extinguish the Ukraine state. Its vague wording (“confirmed”) suggests an American redraft.

*2\. A full and comprehensive non-aggression agreement will be concluded between Russia, Ukraine, and Europe. All ambiguities of the past 30 years will be considered resolved.*

**Comment:** The first of five clauses on European/Ukraine security begins with a dishonest denial of USA involvement in the failure to negotiate collective security since 1991. The exclusion of the USA from the agreement is again a pose of mediation.

*3\. It is expected that Russia will not invade neighbouring countries and that NATO will not expand further.*

**Comment:** A vague political statement to deal with European constituencies. Likely to have come from USA.

*4\. A dialogue will be conducted between Russia and NATO, mediated by the United States, to resolve all security issues and create conditions for de-escalation, thereby ensuring global security and increasing opportunities for cooperation and future economic development.*

**Comment:** Astonishing nonsense - a dialogue with NATO mediated by USA, the Commander of NATO!! It may be the start of a process perhaps; but is again oriented to USA media needs not peace process.

*5\. Ukraine will receive reliable security guarantees.*

**Comment:** This is a vague starting point aimed to bring Ukraine in but offers no clarity on a central issue. Again given the specificity of proper Russian statements on potential security guarantee partners at Istanbul negotiations this clause is a regression.

*6\. The size of the Armed Forces of Ukraine will be limited to (6)00,000 personnel.*

**Comment:** This would represent a higher figure than pre-2022 army from memory and so limited progress on demilitarisation. It is likely a highly negotiable number.

*7\. Ukraine agrees to enshrine in its constitution that it will not join NATO, and NATO agrees to include in its statutes a provision that it will not accept Ukraine in the future.*

**Comment:** This is a dual commitment with clear action on a central issue. Given precision of language it likely represents absolute bottom line of Russia.

*8\. NATO agrees not to deploy troops in Ukraine.*

**Comment:** This is a vague commitment which again is in part beyond the USA’s scope to agree to.

*9\. European fighter aircraft will be stationed in Poland.*

**Comment:** This is an American commitment of European resources and represents a no stand down approach.

*10\. U.S. Guarantees: The United States will receive compensation for the guarantee. If Ukraine invades Russia, it will lose the guarantee. If Russia invades Ukraine, in addition to a decisive coordinated military response, all global sanctions will be reinstated, recognition of new territories and all other benefits of this deal will be revoked. If Ukraine without cause launches a missile at Moscow or Saint Petersburg, the security guarantee will be considered invalid.*

**Comment:** This clause represents the United States accident insurance policy - ‘compensation for the guarantee’.. We break the peace and you pay for it. Again it seems drafted for media purposes not implementation

*11\. Ukraine retains the right to EU membership and will receive short-term preferential access to the European market while the issue is under consideration*.

**Comment:** This first clause on reconstruction exposes the flawed foundation of this peace plan. How can the USA negotiate access to EU or assert as a non-EU state the right of a non-EU state to join the EU? It reflects central issue on which EU must be engaged.

*12\. A powerful global package of measures for the reconstruction of Ukraine, including but not limited to:*

*a. Creation of a Ukraine Development Fund to invest in high-growth sectors, including technology, data-processing centres, and artificial intelligence.*

*b. The United States will cooperate with Ukraine on the joint reconstruction, development, modernisation, and operation of Ukraine’s gas infrastructure, including pipelines and storage facilities.*

*c. Joint efforts to restore war-affected territories, including the reconstruction and modernisation of cities and residential areas.*

*d. Infrastructure development.*

*e. Extraction of minerals and natural resources.*

*f. The World Bank will develop a special financing package to accelerate these efforts.*

**Comment:** I will not assess the detail, but this clause represents a bid by the USA to control and to profit from reconstruction. The squabble over loot and war debt continues.

*13\. Russia will be reintegrated into the global economy:*

*a. The lifting of sanctions will be discussed and agreed upon gradually and on an individual basis.*

*b. The United States will conclude a long-term economic cooperation agreement aimed at mutual development in the fields of energy, natural resources, infrastructure, artificial intelligence, data-processing centres, rare-earth mining projects in the Arctic, and other mutually beneficial corporate opportunities.*

*c. Russia will be invited to return to the G8.*

**Comment:** These vague promises reflect prior rhetoric of the oligarchical faction within Russia and the desire to appeal to Trump’s ‘deals’ mindset. It is an advance on economic cooperation but with a highly graduated approach and assumption of US control. It fails to recognise the USA does not control Russia’s relations with BRICS.

*14\. Frozen assets will be used in the following way: US$100 billion of frozen Russian assets will be invested in U.S.-led reconstruction and investment efforts in Ukraine. The United States will receive 50% of the profits from this undertaking. Europe will add another US$100 billion to increase the total investment available for Ukraine’s reconstruction. Frozen European assets will be unfrozen. The remaining frozen Russian assets will be invested in a separate American-Russian investment vehicle that will implement joint American-Russian projects in areas to be determined. This fund will be aimed at strengthening bilateral relations and increasing shared interests in order to create strong motivation not to return to conflict.*

**Comment:** Remarkably, the USA is proposing to redeploy Russian frozen assets to US control and to take half the profits. Again the conflict between EU and USA is evident.

*15\. A joint American-Russian working group on security issues will be established to facilitate and ensure the fulfilment of all provisions of this agreement.*

**Comment:** A USA-Russia working group only will not work.

*16\. Russia will legislatively enshrine a policy of non-aggression toward Europe and Ukraine.*

**Comment:** This is best interpreted as a confidence building measure and likely reflects Russian proposals. Perhaps the USA should do the same - for Russia, Europe, Ukraine, the world!

*17\. The United States and Russia will agree to extend the validity of treaties on the non-proliferation of nuclear weapons and arms control, including START-1.*

**Comment:** This clause represents a small concession to recover some framework for nuclear arms control with the START treaties.

*18\. Ukraine agrees to remain a non-nuclear state in accordance with the Treaty on the Non-Proliferation of Nuclear Weapons.*

**Comment:** A fundamental issue and I would defer to nuclear arms control experts on the wording. However, again it is a commitment by a party external to the agreement.

*19\. The Zaporizhzhia Nuclear Power Plant will be restarted under IAEA supervision, and the generated electricity will be split equally between Russia and Ukraine (50:50).*

**Comment:** The Nuclear power plant is a key issue, and there have been arguments over rival national companies who may control its operation. This reflects development from an earlier American proposal to take full control of the power plant. This clause may depend on longer term jurisdictional and territory outcomes - who controls full Zaporizhzhia oblast?

*20\. Both countries undertake to introduce educational programmes in schools and society that promote understanding and tolerance of different cultures and the elimination of racism and prejudice:*

*a. Ukraine will adopt EU rules on religious tolerance and protection of linguistic minorities.*

*b. Both countries agree to lift all discriminatory measures and to guarantee the rights of Ukrainian and Russian media and education.*

*c. All Nazi ideology and activity must be rejected and prohibited.*

**Comment:** This clause on the surface deals with Russian objectives of Denazification and concerns with the repression of Civil Rights in Ukraine over the last tend years. There is an explicit reference to Nazi ideology. However, reliance on educational programs is a weak response to this issue given the extent of embedding of discrimination and toxic nationalist ideologies in the Ukraine state. Notably there are no fundamental issues concerning the internal political constitution of Ukraine in this plan

*21\. Territories:*

*a. Crimea, Luhansk, and Donetsk will be recognised de facto as Russian, including by the United States.*

*b. Kherson and Zaporizhzhia will be frozen along the line of contact, which will mean de facto recognition along the line of contact.*

*c. Russia renounces other annexed territories (probably referring to Russian-occupied parts of Kharkiv, Sumy, and Dnipropetrovsk oblasts – Ed.) that it controls outside the five regions.*

*d. Ukrainian forces will withdraw from the part of Donetsk oblast they currently control; this withdrawal zone will be regarded as a neutral demilitarised buffer zone, internationally recognised as territory belonging to the Russian Federation. Russian forces will not enter this demilitarised zone.*

**Comment:** This proposal leads to a de facto agreement confined to four territories with negotiation over scope of withdrawal zone. A broader peace process may need to consider self-determination processes for non-Russian de facto controlled parts of Ukraine, which may be implied in next clause.

*22\. After future territorial arrangements are agreed, both the Russian Federation and Ukraine undertake not to change these arrangements by force. Any security guarantees will not apply in the event of violation of this commitment.*

**Comment:** The reference to future territorial arrangements suggests a negotiated outcome beyond the de fact recognition of the four oblasts already incorporated into the Russian Federation under its constitutional processes.

*23\. Russia will not obstruct Ukraine’s commercial use of the Dnipro River, and agreements will be reached on the free transportation of grain across the Black Sea.*

**Comment:** This clause reflects the centrality of the Dnipro river and Black Sea coast to the economic viability of the remnant Ukraine state. Access to commercial use of the river and grain transportation is feasible under multiple future territorial arrangements, in which Russia controls parts of Ukraine west of the Dnipro and Odessa.

*24\. A humanitarian committee will be created to resolve outstanding issues:*

*a. All remaining prisoners and bodies will be exchanged on the “all-for-all” principle.*

*b. All civilian detainees and hostages will be returned, including children.*

*c. A family reunification programme will be implemented.*

*d. Measures will be taken to alleviate the suffering of conflict victims.*

**Comment:** These reflect commitments for political settlement of humanitarian issues, as have been worked on in Istanbul negotiations.

*25\. Ukraine will hold elections 100 days after the agreement is signed.*

**Comment:** In the absence of any agreements with Ukraine on changes to its political constitution (banning of political parties etc) this 100 day timeline for an election seems unlikely to deliver a free and fair outcome. It may secure an election before next year’s mid-term elections. It may be contingent on calls within Ukraine for a Government of National Unity.

*26\. All parties involved in the conflict will receive full amnesty for actions committed during the war and will undertake not to file claims or pursue complaints in the future.*

**Comment:** This may shelve the ICC charges against Russian officials and would put an end to talk on both sides of war crimes tribunals. It does little to promote social peace in the post-conflict environment, a key issue raised by Jan Oberg and TFF.

*27\. This agreement will be legally binding. Its implementation will be monitored and guaranteed by a Peace Council headed by President Trump. Predetermined sanctions will apply in the event of violations.*

**Comment:** This implementation clause is in reality an American communications clause aimed at promoting Trump for solving another war. The Peace Council chaired by Trump is modelled on the bogus Gaza plan rather than a proper UNSC process. John Helmer has suggested that Russian proposals for proper UN oversight of the process were removed by the USA.

*28\. Once all parties have agreed to and signed this memorandum, the ceasefire will enter into force immediately after both sides withdraw to the agreed positions so that implementation of the agreement can begin.*

**Comment:** Ceasefire and withdrawal is proposed to occur after the agreement.
