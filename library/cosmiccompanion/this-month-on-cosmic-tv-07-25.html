July will see yet another outer planet changing signs, introducing us to the vibes of the “fire and air era” of the coming years, in which “spirit” (fire) and “mind” (air) are going to be the central evolutionary drivers. With Uranus’s entry into the air sign of Gemini on July 7, we will not only have all of the slow-moving planets in air or fire, but we’ve also got Saturn, Uranus, Neptune and Pluto all placed at the very first degrees of their respective zodiac signs, forming a triangle (blue lines below) pointing to the Aries Point, the very beginning of the zodiac – a very rare constellation indeed:

Uranus entering Gemini on July 7, 2025, forming a talent triangle (blue lines) with Pluto in Aquarius and the Saturn-Neptune conjunction on the Aries Point

The symbolic meaning of this pointer-shaped constellation involving so many ‘beginnings’ isn’t hard to interpret. It's like the cosmos saying, “hey, guys, this is not a new chapter. This is the very first page of a whole new book!”

Another thing that is really peculiar about this triangle formation (and which only becomes clear at a second glance) is that it affects the first degrees of all zodiac signs in some way or another. So if you have planetary placements anywhere at the beginning of any sign, the larger collective developments tend to play a major role in your personal life at the moment. Those with planets at the beginning of the fire and air signs will be especially tuned in to the current zeitgeist and will probably be more open to whatever the future holds in store for them and willing to change and experiment. Those with placements at the beginning of Aries, Cancer, Libra and Capricorn might feel more resistance as they are called to take a major step in a maturation process. Those with placements at the beginning of Gemini, Virgo, Sagittarius and Pisces could be in for a surprise or an unexpected turn of events, whereas those with placements at the beginning of Taurus, Leo, Scorpio or Aquarius are invited to a longer process of weeding out what’s outlived its purpose.

This constellation will also have an interesting effect on the astrological monthly weather forecast because it creates a repetitive ‘weather pattern’: Whenever the Sun, Moon or a personal planet enters a new sign they will ‘trigger’ the constellation and bring the themes to life. So, in the next few months, we will see peaks of planetary action for a few days and then longer periods of relative planetary quiet.

Mercury retrograde in Leo: Who’s running the show?

(July 19 – August 11, 2025)

Too many new things for you? Well, then let’s talk about something you can rely on: Mercury retrograde. Every 3 months there is another Mercury retrograde phase, and this month it’s due again. The planet of ideas, communication and logistics will go backwards from July 18 until August 11, thus extending his stay in the sign of Leo until the beginning of September. This makes about eight weeks, in which we will have plenty of time to ponder about everything we associate with the sign of Leo: Hot summer sun and fun, our “selves”, our creative output, children and education, performance, management and leadership. This retrograde phase is a great time to slow down, look inward and reflect on what you enjoy doing or what you consider your ‘great work’. Maybe there’s a piece of information missing or you need to revise a part of your product. Leadership issues might arise as well as complications and delays in finding a successor for a vacant executive position. If you are working on a project, the situation might force you to rethink how to best organize it. Motivation could drop temporarily so take time to reconnect to your heart and to who or what is really dear to you.

Fortunately, this Mercury retrograde phase doesn’t seem to be as bumpy as others, although holiday logistics could prove a bit challenging here and there. By scheduling plenty of time to get to your holiday destination anyway and to making sure that everything is correct with the documents of your accommodation you should be fine, though. It is not until the middle of August that we will see a hard aspect and a potential for a confrontation and clash between group interests (Pluto in Aquarius) and an individual’s claim for leadership (Mercury in Leo). A popular repeat of ‘ordinary people’ versus ‘king’ in politics will probably remind us of this constellation once we get there …

Let’s get back to July and the individual weeks:

July 1-6:

The first seven days of July are dominated by one major theme: change of values and relationships. As Venus, the planet of love and appreciation, approaches and crosses the threshold to the sign of Gemini, she first embraces the planet of liberty and independence (Uranus), then pays respect to the planet of responsibility (Saturn), finally gets some inspiration by the planet of spirituality (Neptune) and commits to a process of group transformation (Pluto). If you have planetary placements or sensitive points at the end of Taurus and the beginning of Gemini, this succession of triggers is likely to first infuse your relationships with distance and detachment, then make you voluntarily commit to your duties while not forgetting to dream a little and seriously realizing the need to change the way you relate. Venus will be in Gemini the whole month, providing those of you with planets in Gemini with love and support. The other air signs Libra and Aquarius will also benefit from Venus’ presence and the fire signs get their fair share as well.

However, eros is likely to be found in the mind rather than in the emotions. This month both Venus and Mars will be in the ‘brainy’ signs of Gemini and Virgo respectively. Both signs are ruled by Mercury. If she enjoys talking to you, take this as her way of showing her affection. If he spends hours to fix your PC, consider this as his way of wooing. For her it’ll be important to be open and connect, and for him it’ll be important to be useful, a dynamic which will become more obvious towards the end of the month.

July 7-13:

Monday will see this week’s only astrological big event: Uranus will finally dip his toes into the sign of the Twins – at least mathematically, by a whopping 2 arc seconds. On a personal level, this shift will probably be not very noticeable unless you are very sensitive. Uranus won’t move much more than a degree this month anyway, so Uranus will basically spend the entire month on Gemini’s doorstep. However, history has seen some of the larger natural disasters upon the entry of an outer planet into a new sign, the most memorable being the Japanese earthquake and devastating tsunami on March 11, 2011, which also triggered a nuclear disaster at the Fukushima Daiichi Nuclear Power Plant and which coincided with Uranus entering Aries on that very day and Neptune being on the last degree of Aquarius about to move into Pisces.

Apart from a regular Full Moon, there is no other major planetary action in the second week of July.

July 14-20:

Keep your schedule light this week as our mental processes will start to slow down. Mercury will stop in his tracks on Friday the 18th and prepare to go backwards. The necessity to reschedule might become apparent towards the end of the week, so it’s good to be on your toes. However, potential changes of plan will probably be met with ease. The inspiring Mercury-Venus contact might make us a little flirtatious and color our communication with grace.

July 21-27:

The last two weeks of July will be characterized by the tension between Venus in Gemini and Mars in Virgo. Our need for fun and ease might be in conflict with our work-ethic that demands a detail-oriented and conscientious way of solving some current practical problems at hand. Those of you with planetary placements around the middle degrees of the mutable signs of Gemini, Virgo, Sagittarius and Pisces might be especially torn between the need to be useful and the need to socialize and have fun. To avoid sour feelings, make sure to schedule some time for work and as much quality time to chat with your friends.

The New Moon at the beginning of Leo on the 24th will be interesting as it symbolizes a seeding moment of creativity and centralized leadership. It will lie exactly opposite Pluto in Aquarius, standing for the democratic power of the people, but also digital technology. Together with the other outer planets of the triangle constellation mentioned above it will form another configuration, namely that of a cradle. To me it looks as if a way could be found to harness the tension between centralized and decentralized powers to initiate far-reaching changes. This cradle configuration is said to have protective qualities in difficult times, but can also indicate an accumulation of power on one side to the exclusion of the rest.

July 28-Aug 3:

On July 31 Venus will enter Cancer. With this shift, relationships will now be centered more around emotional needs. Venus will immediately receive a hostile eye from the Saturn-Neptune conjunction in Aries. Values like sensitivity, nurturing and the protection of home and hearth will grow in importance in the coming weeks, but will clash with the narrative of the holy duty fight in the first days of August.

August promises to become an interesting month as assertive Mars moves through America’s house of foreign affairs and towards the highest point in its natal chart on August 8.
