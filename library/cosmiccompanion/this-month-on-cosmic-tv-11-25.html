The concert of complex planetary patterns continues

November continues with last month’s planetary configurations in slightly different variations, which means that this month we will be given some time to manage and process the major themes that featured prominently in October. Again, we will see attempts at hammering out a compromise to soothe old wounds of anger and aggression in order to create a protective and nurturing environment (T-square between Chiron in Aries, Venus in Libra and Jupiter in Cancer). If you have planets somewhere around 24° Aries, Cancer, Libra or Capricorn, you are likely to be torn between ‘war and peace’ or ‘me and the other(s)’ in some way. Your urge to do something about this ongoing situation might be strong especially at the beginning of the month as it affects your sense of home and belonging.

We will also see the Cradle configuration again, this month even twice, one at the beginning of the month and the other at around the 20th. This indicates a situtation in which the volatility and the potentially disruptive energy that is symbolized by the planetary oppositions within the Cradle (between Mars and Uranus and Sun/Moon and Uranus respectively) could be absorbed and stabilized by a complex and highly developed support system. Those of you who have planetary placements at the beginning of the air sings of Gemini, Libra or Aquarius or at the end of the water signs of Cancer, Scorpio and Pisces could be ‘recruited’ to become part of such a support system - if you are not already engaged in such a venture.

A time for reflection and soul-searching

The New Moon together with Mercury and Venus in the last degrees of Scorpio will activate the Grand Water Trine again on the 20th, coloring the entire last week in November. With so much emphasis on water and especially on Scorpio, there seems to be a great need, but also a great opportunity for emotional cleansing. It might be good to remember what each of the water signs stand for in the therapeutic process: Jupiter in Cancer reminds us to create or look for a safe and empathetic space in which you can open up emotionally. The New Moon together with Mercury and Venus in Scorpio remind us to dig deep and be brutally honest with our feelings. Saturn and Neptune in Pisces remind us to be non-judgmental in the process and let go and release whatever we have found. If you have significant planetary placements in the last degrees of Cancer, Scorpio or Pisces, you can especially benefit from this therapeutic three-step indicated by the Grand Trine. (For a more detailed description of these configurations see last month’s “This month on Cosmic TV (10-25)”).

Mercury retrograde (Nov. 10-29)

And this month’s Mercury retrograde might just come in handy to support this process. As most of you know, the retrograde phase of Mercury is not the greatest time to start or push projects forward. It is a good time to revise documents and plans and rework things, but also to reconsider and take time to think things over. Keep your schedule light during this phase as you will probably have to respond to cancellations as well as transportation and communication mishaps more frequently than usual.

Whether you are in the teaching professions or still a student yourself, avoid covering too much new ground and instead plan to do a revision of the material that you have so far dealt with. This is especially true in November because Mercury is in the sign of knowledge-hungry Sagittarius in the first two weeks. We might be enthused about our intellectual capacity and rush forward into the academic battle, just to notice soon that we have been too fast and need to backtrace some of our steps. As soon as Mercury goes back into Scorpio on the 19th , you might find that the issue that is slowing down your studies might be of an emotional rather than an intellectual origin. Mercury will station direct on the 30th and pick up speed in the first two weeks of December. By the time Mercury has reached the sign of Sagittarius again in the middle of December, our mental engines should be back on track and happily ploughing ahead.

Another indicator that November is a month of reconsideration rather than of action is the fact that by the middle of the month most of the slower-moving planets, including Jupiter, Saturn, Neptune and Uranus, will also be in retrograde motion. This means that, on the sociopolitical and collective level, things are ‘under revision’ as well, so that taking major decisions especially regarding business matters does not seem very wise this winter as the underlying circumstances could keep shifting.

Let’s take a look at the individual weeks:

November 1-9:

The month starts off with a week full of planetary action. Venus is still in Libra, providing grace and diplomacy to the way we approach our relationships. However, getting our need for relational harmony met in the face of the antagonism of wounded souls might prove challenging on the first weekend of the month while Venus opposes Chiron in Aries. With the Mars-Neptune trine, it’s possible to wiggle your way through a difficult situation by relying on your gut-feelings and acting on your instincts.

However, as soon as Mars enters Sagittarius and opposes Uranus in the middle of the week, things could get explosive as nerves are on the edge because a situation might take a sudden and unexpected turn. Those who resonate strongly with the first degrees of Sagittarius, Gemini, Pisces or Virgo have to be especially careful as the slightest provocation can ignite an issue and cause an overreaction. It might be helpful to recognize the difference between changes that are necessary for progress and those that arise from self-serving desires so that the high-voltage energy can be funneled into more constructive channels.

On the 7th Venus leaves her home sign of Libra and enters the passionate sign of Scorpio, demanding emotional depth and honesty from the way we relate. Issues of loyalty, trust and attachment can arise and clash with the ideals of freedom and detachment as Venus squares Pluto in Aquarius on the weekend. Those of you with significant planetary placements at the beginning of Taurus, Scorpio, Leo or Aquarius might feel particularly affected by a situation that could require a decision.

November 10-16:

On Monday future-oriented Mercury in Sagittarius will shift gears and start its retrograde phase. We might have already noticed a slowing-down of our mental processes in the week before, heralding a more inward-oriented phase. However, in my experience the first days of the retrograde phase will keep us especially busy because unforeseen changes are likely to emerge suddenly and ask us to adapt. With Mercury in the mutable sign of Sagittarius, travel plans could be particularly affected as well as matters relating to education and foreign affairs. On Wednesday, mind and personal willpower align as Mercury and Mars come together to make a conjunction. This could help us voice our opinion strongly and make our point and add gumption to the way we respond to the volatility of circumstances.

The rest of the week is relatively quiet in terms of planetary aspects, which could provide us with some room to breathe and time to digest the recent developments.

Nov. 17-23:

In the third week of November the Grand Water Trine already starts forming with the Sun traveling across the last degrees of the sign of Scorpio. It’s a good time to let yourself be inspired by the Lotus flower, a plant that is often associated with the sign of Scorpio as the impeccable beauty of its flower emerges from the murky depths in the mud, and start engaging in a cleansing process, whether this is physical or emotional. This is also a great time to work with your imagination. So, if you enjoy being creative, expressing your inner healing process can result in beautiful works of art. Those of you who are sensitive to the Moon’s cycles might pick the 20th for a creative Moon meditation as the New Moon aligns with the Grand Trine as well as the Kite configuration.

The middle of the week could remind us of the upheavals and sudden changes at the beginning of the month. Mercury as well as the Sun will be in opposition to Uranus then. Those of you resonating to the last degrees of Taurus, Leo, Scorpio or Aquarius or the beginning of Gemini, Virgo, Sagittarius or Pisces could feel a nervous inner tension peaking. An intolerance of restrictions can cause us to break away from the rut or other people may show up and be a catalyst for change.

November 24-30:

I always like to think of Mercury in retrograde motion as a researcher who is on the hunt for a piece of information that he or she has overlooked or someone looking for the right combination of things to unlock a secret. With Mercury meeting Venus on the Scorpio tip of the Grand Water Trine this week we could see a culmination of this process when love embraces mind and mind embraces love. But it is more than the sweet talk of a regular Mercury-Venus conjunction. This combo connects with Jupiter, the planet of growth, and Saturn and Neptune, which are both revisiting the spiritual sign of Pisces one last time before their final entry into Aries next year to usher in the new era om 2026. So, if you take time to go inward and listen, you might be drawn back to your heart’s unfinished stories and spiritual lessons that have so far remained incomplete. Take time to write down your hunches and insights this week and let them steep in the cauldron of your soul. Once Mercury has shifted into forward gear and goes over the last degrees of Scorpio in December again, a deeper understanding of the issues involved might have emerged from the depths so that a closure seems possible.

As if to remind us not to get too passionate in our search for potential issues and get stuck in the muck, a Venus-Uranus opposition on the 30th jolts us out of our attachments and teaches us to expect the unexpected in our relationships.
