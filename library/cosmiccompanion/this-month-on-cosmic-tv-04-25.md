If you have found the last four months to be confusing and dragging along, I’ve got good news for you: All this back and forth will finally be over in the middle of April and we might be able to finally get this year off the ground! Since the beginning of December all the fast-moving planets Mars, Mercury and Venus have been passing on the retrograde baton to each other, indicating an exceptionally long period of constant reconsideration and reevaluation in different areas. In the course of this four-month period, quite a few of us will have changed their attitudes, alliances and their minds – and likely more than once.

Mercury will already turn direct on April 8 and Venus will follow on April 14. Planning will become more reliable in the course of this month as Mercury will slowly pick up speed, and matters that we have been forced to revise or which we have been mentally chewing on in the last four weeks can finally see a closure toward the end of April. The adjustment of relationship matters could take a little longer, but by the middle of May we should be forging ahead.

As much as we are tempted to gladly look ahead, in the first two weeks of April careful planning and systematic execution is high on the agenda as the first half of the months seems important for our progress in the near future. It’s interesting that both Mercury and Venus first shake hands with Saturn before changing gear and turning direct. It’s as if task-master Saturn has to provide some ‘disciplinary’ advice or wants give out reminders regarding communication and relationships. Situations might arise that require a clearer definition of tasks, rules, roles and goals. Especially if you have planetary placements around 25° of Pisces, Cancer of Scorpio, a prudent approach in the first two weeks could pay off in the long-run.

Another planetary peculiarity in April is that Venus will be hanging around the degrees of Saturn in Pisces for the entire month, addressing matters of appropriateness in relationships. Practical considerations as well as paying or not paying respect to each other’s boundaries could play a large role in the way we relate to each other. Some of us might see themselves forced to administer ‘tough love’, whereas the ones on the receiving end could temporarily suffer from feelings of insecurity or rejection, particularly if you have planetary placements around 25° of Gemini, Virgo, Sagittarius or Pisces. If we are skilled enough to blend compassion with responsibility, we might be able to develop the proverbial iron first in a velvet glove to steer us well through sensitive circumstances.

As slow as the month might start off, in the middle of April there will be a noticeable shift from the predominant element of water to fire. If the first two weeks of April have been too much about managing each other’s needs and vulnerabilities for you, you will get your share of blazing action in the second half, when we will also see some tension building.

Let’s take a look at the individual weeks:

**April 1-6:**

The month starts off with Neptune’s second day in Aries. Although we are already seeing massive resistance in the streets against autocratic rule in many countries reflecting the building Neptune-Saturn conjunction (see my last post “Fighting for a collective renewal”), the effects of Neptune’s entry into a new sign is often experienced on the collective level rather than individually - unless you have significant planetary placements at the very beginning of Aries, Cancer, Libra or Capricorn - like French far-right politician Marine Le Pen. On March 31, a French court found the three-time presidential hopeful guilty of embezzlement, sentencing her to four years' imprisonment and a five-year ban from seeking elected office. In her birth chart, Neptune in Aries is about to form a conjunction with her “Achilles’ heel” Chiron in Aries and make tense aspect to her Cancer Moon, the ruler of her 10th house of career goals, social standing and reputation, indicating a time in which she might subjectively feel like a decision is imposed upon her by forces that are seemingly ‘out of her control’, forcing her to sacrifice her career ambitions. Since she is very popular in France and the Aries Point is involved here astrologically, we can expect a huge fallout following this sentence, especially when the Saturn-Neptune conjunction will have become exact in August and form an exact T-square together with her Moon and transiting Mars. This triangle harmoniously blends a creative drive, a love for imagery, a structured and consistent approach and a dash of innovation into a productive team of talents. Whether you are an artist in the widest sense or work in a people’s business, the next two weeks are a good time to manifest your creative ideas.

**April 7-13:**

This feeling of being able to draw on a well-balanced cooperation with the people you feel close to and being able to rely on a well-rounded skills-set will continue in the second week of April. Mars has already been in supportive aspect to Saturn all March, encouraging consistent effort; and it looks as if we could now reap what we sowed in March as things might easily fall into place. In addition, Mercury and Venus will shift into forward gear this week (on April 8 and April 14 respectively), promising an increasing ease in outwardly expressing and promoting what we’ve been working on.

On the weekend, however, some discord could enter the picture. The Aries Full-Moon conjunct Chiron and square Mars heralds a more challenging and conflict-oriented time in the second half of April. The topic of leadership or who is number one could be a touchy issue; and if you have planetary placements around the last degrees of Aries, Cancer, Libra or Capricorn, you might be directly involved in a competitive triangle.

**April 14-20:**

Mercury will enter fiery Aries on the 17th , turning our attention to matters of immediate concern. We might start the week off by daydreaming, though, imagining to be active and dreaming of great deeds. The Mercury-Neptune conjunction could temporarily blur our perception and our sense of reality. But as soon as Mars moves into Leo on Friday and connects with Mercury in Aries, the archetype of the hero fully emerges and wants to be expressed. This is when the fiery and action-oriented temperament will take over and passion is king. If you have planetary placements at the beginning of the fire and air signs you want and can get a lot of things done now. But there is also the danger of easily shifting into dynamic overdrive and taking on too much. With both Mars and Mercury in leadership-oriented fire signs, a sense of entitlement and an attitude of ‘might is right’’ could inform our actions, which will not go unchallenged.

**April 21-30:**

And we won’t have to wait long for the individual will-(to)-power, Mars, to clash with the power of the collective survival instinct, Pluto. The last week of April is characterized by the building opposition and tension between Mars and Pluto, which will be exact on the last weekend of this month. We will also see a lot of feistiness and strong and triumphant language again in the course of this week, reminding us of the first weeks in November 2024, the time of the aftermath of the US presidential election. Since this is the third and last time that Mars opposes Pluto, we could see the final act of what was initiated under tension in November and December 2024.

The principle of love, fun and relating seems to be temporarily fenced in by necessities while Venus is in a very tight embrace with Saturn all week. Although tempered and curbed, there is an inspiring sextile to Uranus on the 29th that promises a spark of buzzing electricity in our relationships.

The month ends with Venus being about to cross the Aries Point. Will we have a heroine enter the picture in May? If you want to know, tune into the next episode of This month on Cosmic TV!
