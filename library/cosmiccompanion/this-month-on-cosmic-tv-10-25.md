**Four unusual patterns shape the month ahead**

I remember that I used to be happy to find one or two major aspects between outer planets that I could write about in this monthly forecast section. That was already considered big astrological news! Now it seems like the skies are as addicted to superlatives as Donald Trump: Each month seems ‘historical’ in that it has even more rare configurations on offer than the last one. October has got four of them in this order: another Kite spanning the Aries Point, a T-square, a Grand Water Trine and a Cradle.

Astrology students will be excited to see that these rare patterns actually do exist, but the sheer number of them in such a short time is a mind-boggler even for experienced astrologers. What makes them interesting in terms of their political significance is that they all clearly interact with the national charts of the US and Israel and the birth charts of their political leaders Trump and Netanyahu, amongst others. Although these configurations blend into one another, let’s take a look at each one separately to get a feel for how they could color the month of October before we take a brief look at the individual weeks.

**Venus in Libra: The Cosmos plays its ace**

We have already seen three Kites forming in the last two months, each with a planet entering the sign of Libra: first Mars at the beginning of August, then Mercury and the Sun at the end of September. Each time, steps were taken by the US government to get closer to peace talks, whether this was regarding the war against the Ukraine or Gaza. The first attempt at peacemaking, Trump’s meet-up with Putin, didn’t gain much traction. Traditional astrologers would argue that this was due to Mars being on “foreign territory” in Libra, which prevented Mars from acting as designed. The recent two steps towards a ceasefire in Gaza seem to be more promising, although things are far from being cut and dry while I’m writing this. Fortunately, the cosmos has another ace up its sleeve: Venus will be the last planet to enter Libra, forming the last kite of this sequence on October 14th.

Even though the ‘love planet’ Venus is not considered a heavy-weight among the planets, it could be the one to turn the scales this month as Venus will be strong in its home sign, Libra, the very sign of the scales and of contracts and cooperation. Venus might be able to soothe the opposite side of the kite formation, in this case Neptune in Aries, which stands for the ideal of the war hero. She could make cooperation more palatable to the warring factions.

This recent back and forth between ‘going it alone’ or ‘cooperating with the other’ is not only a current dynamic in international politics. If you have planetary placements or sensitive points around 0° Aries or 0° Libra, you might have been flip-flopping between these two extremes while trying to steer your ship in a storm of fast and far-reaching changes. Wherever you or someone you know have not been able to make up their mind to jump on board recently, the middle of October could seal the deal. This time around, peace and cooperation actually does have a chance.

As if to say “It’s not that easy, guys!”, the more challenging T-square will form just three days after the harmonious kite configuration. On October 17th, the Sun in Libra opposes Chiron in Aries and both are square Jupiter in Cancer. So again, we have a war and peace theme and cardinal energy involved, so we might feel the urge to set impulses to get things going. Attempts to bridge the divide might trigger old feelings of rejection, though, and ignite anger issues and discord caused by a feeling of having been denied one’s existential rights. Chiron in Aries can express itself as a raw and wild energy of acute pain and frustration. Since it’s square Jupiter in Cancer, there is the danger that aggressive reactions can get in the way and frustrate the hope of creating a secure domestic sphere.

However, if handled wisely (and a lot of conscious effort and energy will need to be put into this to work), the energies involved can be used to find a fair deal to redress the harm caused while trying to get the warring parties under one “big roof”. If this somehow reminds you of the Israel-Gaza conflict, you will not be surprised to hear that this configuration exactly aligns with the axes in the natal chart of Israel (see below) and also with Netanyahu’s birth chart. (Unfortunately, as with Russia, there isn’t one definite chart for Palestine due to its complex history). If you have planetary placements around 24° of the cardinal signs of Aries, Cancer, Libra and Capricorn, you will probably be able to relate to these themes.

The tensions of the T-square will subside quickly, but the challenge to manage the pain and anger of rejection and protect what we consider our home and family will stay with us until the beginning of next year, when the square between Chiron and Jupiter will move out of exactitude. It will see its final resolution next summer.

**Heal the World: The Grand Water Trine**

The last week of October sees half of the planets forming a harmonious Grand Trine in the element of water, namely Jupiter in Cancer, Mercury and Mars in Scorpio and Saturn and Neptune in Pisces. In the chart below it looks like a big blue triangle. Each of the water signs symbolizes a specific step and set of skills needed in the process of emotional healing. And if we connect all the water signs as in this formation, they all come together to create a big potential for healing and creativity. If you have planetary placements especially in the later degrees of the water signs, you will probably automatically tune into these empathetic vibes. As beautiful and strong as this Grand Trine may look, don’t expect miracles to happen during those days since supportive aspects can also easily be overslept and pass almost unnoticed. Nevertheless, there will be an ease of flow that will facilitate looking into the soul’s depth and accept whatever is there and which can be used for insights and creative purposes as well. If you work in the social sector or as a counsellor or therapist, you might be able to use this energy for the wellbeing of your clients. Writing and story-telling might be the tool of choice to unearth and process memories and emotions.

**The Cradle: A cushion amid complexity**

The month ends with a trapezoid-shaped configuration which is called a cradle, which, in this case, almost looks like a pram or a ship (see below). The cradle is said to indicate circumstances that emerge to stabilize, protect and remedy a situation. The sense of protection and stability in a cradle formation is created by shutting out a larger part of the world or anything that lies beyond the protected zone, suggesting the creation of a strong bias. A certain one-sidedness might lie in the air in the last days of October. The Mercury-Uranus opposition that forms on Oct. 29th across the first degrees of Gemini and Sagittarius as the essential part of this configuration speaks of surprising news and sudden changes of mind as well as a potential clash between reason and faith and between science and religion. The elements involved are again air and fire, the energy of which can exhaust and fizzle out very quickly. If you have planetary placements in the first degrees of air and fire, enjoy being on a roll, but don’t rely too on feeling of protection to be enduring.

Let’s take a look at the individual weeks:

**October 1-12:**

The month starts off with attempts to verbally appease and soothe wounded souls or act engaging toward people who seem like loners. Sweet talk will not get us very far, though, and we should be guarded against getting heat from them despite best intentions if we cannot offer a fair deal or understand their position.

Mercury enters Scorpio on the 6th. Our minds are now wired to probe, dig deep and do investigative and difficult mental work for the rest of the month. Communication will be focused, controlled and secretive, but gain in penetration power and as Mercury is slowly approaching Mars in Scorpio. If you have planetary placements around the first degree of Taurus, Leo, Scorpio or Aquarius, be careful not to leave a lasting negative impression with your black belt in verbal karate. Words are not taken lightly at the moment and your words might be more caustic than you think around the 7th when Mercury squares Pluto. Friday will see a Venus-Saturn opposition, which can best be used by doing a reality check of your relationships.

**October 13-19:**

It looks like this is going to be the most intense week in the month. On Monday we will wake up to Venus changing signs into Libra and forming the kite configuration described above. Our hearts might open wide and be willing to convince the world of the value of equality, compromise and fairness. However, love has a rather transpersonal flair now as Venus teams up with all the outer planets for a couple of days.

On Thursday and Friday vulnerabilities around leadership qualities and being the number one could surface again and test our diplomatic skills. If you have planetary placements around 24° of Aries, Cancer, Libra or Capricorn, these two days could highlight what needs to be done to develop an integrative and nourishing vision to protect the ones that we care for. With the Mercury-Mars conjunction on Sunday, a feeling of urgency could prompt us to speak up and act with courage. Our minds are as sharp and cutting as a scalpel, promising success to the planning and execution of complex and secret operations.

**October 20 -26:**

In the run-up to the Grand Trine on the weekend, we will have to deal with a New Moon and a Sun square Pluto, which, on the one hand, can provide us with great determination to push through obstacles in order to manifest our purpose. But it also symbolizes the possibility for power games, which can be played with great obstinacy and dark satisfaction. If you have planetary placements around the first degrees of Taurus, Leo, Scorpio and Aquarius, you might be drawn into an all-or-nothing-situation that can potentially shift power structures around you.

Fortunately, there is something to look forward to: the weekend with its healing Grand Trine. A weekend at the Spa would be a perfect expression for this energy if you opt for the relaxing side of this configuration. If you decide to dive deep into your soul, be prepared to have a big box of Kleenex next to you as emotions will well up asking to be released.

**October 27 - Nov 2:**

In the last week of October Neptune will return to its own sign of Pisces, adding to the creative and empathetic qualities of the Grand Water Trine and leaving Chiron as the the only major celestial body left in the sign of Aries for the next three months. I hope this means that times will be little bit more peaceful and less aggressive and militant than during Neptune’s and Saturn’s first visit to Aries this summer, and that people will mull over the ease with which authorities have deployed troops and weapons to get their will. We will see if we will be able to manifest more of Aries’ constructive sides when Saturn and Neptune enter Aries again at the beginning of next year.

October could end with surprising news and potential turning points as Mercury forms an opposition to Uranus on the 29th . Nevertheless, we might feel on supportive ground for the time being while the configuration of the cradle lasts.

Will we see another series of awesome configurations in November? If you want to know, tune into the next episode of This month on Cosmic TV!
