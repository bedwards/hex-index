**Reconciling war and peace**

It’s an image that conveys stability and harmony despite the tensions indicated by the oppositions. And that’s exactly the promise of this month’s signature constellation: With Mars moving into diplomatic Libra on August 7, there is a potential for using the urge to cooperate and make peace (Mars in Libra) to confront the authoritative drive to forge forward and conquer(Saturn/Neptune in Aries), while also taking the idea of an individual’s greatness and superiority (Mercury in Leo) and the power of the people or groups interests (Pluto in Aquarius) into account.

Since this kite-configuration is exclusively formed by planets in the elements of fire and air and involves fickle Uranus in Gemini, the seeming stability of this constellation might dissolve quickly. In simple words: it looks like fast and large-scale efforts to impose peace or create a balance of some kind. As ingenious as certain moves might be, there is the danger that current tensions and issues might find a temporary containment but no long-lasting solution. This does not only apply to the political arena (and to the US in particular as the chart and the government of this country is immediately affected by this constellation), but you might find similar situations in your own life, especially if you have significant planetary placements or sensitive points at the beginning of Aries, Leo, Libra or Aquarius.

Speaking of the US: There has been some speculation in the astrological community about the possibility that America could get involved in a war this year since Uranus has just entered Gemini. The War of Independence, the Civil War and America’s involvement in World War II all coincided with Uranus in Gemini, leading some astrologers to associate this placement with periods suggesting a larger scale military conflict. Although the period of Uranus in Gemini (2025-2033) can signify a potential split of the world in two larger factions, and both Mars (indicator of aggression) and Uranus (indicator of rebellious reaction) are indeed in Gemini in the US natal chart, I would be cautious to assume that the mere entry of transiting Uranus into Gemini means immediate war for the US. I think we could get close to a significant moment in US history while Mars in Libra will be on the US Midheaven around August 8, affecting the US government and triggering the configuration described above. But to me it seems like tensions can find an outlet of an economic sort or can be redirected so that, hopefully, a larger military conflict can be avoided – at least for the time being.

**Rendez-vous of the benefics**

The other promising constellation is the meet-up of lovely Venus and generous Jupiter in Cancer on August 11-12 (on the US-Sun , by the way). Although these two benefics always meet once a year and indicate an auspicious time, their tête-à-tête this month is particularly meaningful as it happens in Jupiter’s sign of exaltation. If you have sensitive points or planets in the middle of Cancer, Scorpio or Pisces and start out with a new project shortly before those days, things could run with an exceptional ease while resources might be more available to you than at other times. Others might be lucky in finding their dream home or are met with open arms and experience a satisfying sense of belonging. Although this conjunction is also fortunate for those of you with planetary placements in the middle degrees of Aries, Libra and Capricorn, too much of a good thing could spoil a couple of otherwise pleasant and joyful days if you are not careful.

**Mercury turning direct**

Did you have to deal with too many communication difficulties or delays in the last two weeks of July? Then you will be looking forward to Mercury turning direct again on August 12. Until then the best way of using this energy is to revise and re-think creative projects or matters of management, leadership and organization. Even if the weather does not play along where you live, leisure activities, entertainment and fun are on our minds as long as Mercury will stay in Leo, which will be until the beginning of September.

**Let’s take a look at the individual weeks:**

**Aug. 1-10:**

This month starts off with a feeling of insecurity and rejection in relationships. With Venus in Cancer squaring Saturn-Neptune in Aries, tender hearts could get easily hurt by the demands of a situation that calls for action rather than emotions. Those of you with planetary placements at the beginning of Aries, Cancer, Libra or Capricorn might be torn between the necessity to be assertive and clear and the need for closeness and affection.

If you have felt easily attacked for not living up to a perfect ideal in the recent weeks or have been occupied with too many unnecessary details, there’s good news: Mars will leave the sign of critical Virgo and enter Libra on the 7th, shifting our motivation to act from fixing things that don’t work to actively building bridges between people. Until the end of September, work needs to be done to address issues of justice and injustice and create fairness in relationships.

However, toward the weekend, while this month’s signature constellation is slowly forming, tensions are going to build. Moves to initiate a rapport with antagonistic or competitive parties can meet quite some resistance. Taking communication routes around the actual issue and involving third parties to reach an agreement might prove more successful than dealing with the tension head-on.

**Aug. 11-17:**

The issues of the weekend will keep us busy during the second week of August. Luckily, the week starts off with two auspicious celestial events, which can also be used to find a solution to the issue at hand: Love planet Venus will form a generous “feel-good” conjunction with benefactor Jupiter at 13°-14° Cancer, giving us the opportunity to tap into a pool of empathy and forgiveness and create a space of protection and care, especially if your chart resonates with the middle degrees of the water signs. Cosmic messenger Mercury will turn direct on Monday, shifting the focus from thinking about past achievements or wounds of injured pride to creative ideas about how to turn recent disappointments and confrontations into opportunities.

As if to throw in another cosmic helping hand, on August 16 Mercury hooks up with action planet Mars in Libra. They will accompany each other in an inspiring sextile for the rest of the month. This indicates two weeks of well-aligned thoughts and actions and encouraging words if we choose to put in some effort.

**August 18-24:**

After such a dynamic and dramatic overture to the month, the third week of August seems relatively quiet in terms of general planetary action. This might be a good time to start looking at the path ahead while digesting the incidents of the last two weeks. The New Moon in Virgo on the 23rd in a hard aspect to Uranus might remind us of the disruptive side of new communication technologies. If your birthday happens to be on the weekend, the year ahead might challenge your need for managing things with exactitude and diligence and ask you to find new processes and solutions to keep up with the speed of innovations.

**August 25-31:**

If you haven’t been on summer vacation yet, with Venus entering the sign of Leo on Monday, the next four weeks are a good time to go on a trip as the sunny and playful holiday mood will get an extra extension until the third week of September. On Tuesday and Wednesday, though, we might have to overcome a pump in the road first, as lavish spending habits and the need for special attention could take center stage and might not find everybody’s approval. Royal tastes could become a bone of contention in relationships as well as politics when Venus opposes Pluto in Aquarius on the 26th and 27th. However, Venus will form a protective cradle configuration with Uranus and Saturn-Neptune at the same time, so that a solution should present itself relatively easily this week.

Retrograde Saturn is hovering on 0° Aries this week and will leave belligerent Aries and return to Pisces on the 2nd of September. Will the intensity of international conflicts subside in fall? If you want to know, tune in to the next episode of This month on Cosmic TV!
