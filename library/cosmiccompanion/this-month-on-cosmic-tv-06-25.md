*(Seen in Cologne in May 2025)*

Alrighty, we have finally arrived in June, one of the most significant months in 2025! No more bending ourselves into pretzels in an attempt to carry on with a situation that is no longer working. It’s time to fish or cut bait! And it’s time to get things moving! When Jupiter enters the sign of Cancer on June 10, three of the five slow-moving planets will be in signs of cardinal mode for the next three months, signaling to us as a collective to finally take heart, take the initiative and move on into new territory this summer!

**Should I seize the opportunity or not?**

This shift into leadership-mode will not go unchallenged, though, as what is already established might clash with newly emerging directions and perspectives. Before Jupiter, the planet of optimism and opportunities, can get too comfortable in the sign of Cancer, he will immediately get a scrutinizing side-eye from Saturn, who will probably have a few sobering facts and tests up his sleeve to keep the planet of expansion within bounds. Of course, I’m talking about this month’s signature configuration, the Jupiter-Saturn square, which will become exact on June 15th. In the run-up to its exactitude some of you might feel an increasing tension not unlike two tectonic plates rubbing against each other.

If you have significant planetary placements at the very beginning of the cardinal signs of Aries, Cancer, Libra or Capricorn, you might be especially sensitive to this increasing pressure. You could find yourself in a situation in which major shifts need to be initiated or you might be about to take a significant step yourself – whether internally or externally. You might be conflicted now, but if you know what matters most to you, in a few months from now you could look back and realize that June was the crucial month when things were set in motion or you made up your mind. So, in the first two weeks in June life could force us to (re-)examine whether we are ready to take the next step - and consider what is needed to take the next step.

Jupiter and Saturn are the so-called social planets, meaning that the dynamics they are involved in often manifests visibly in our society and manifest in the area of legislation. If you don’t have planetary placements in your birth chart that are sensitive to this constellation, your daily news will keep you informed about how these these conflicting forces of restriction and growth, of defeating and defending are playing out in the socio-political arena.

**Spiritual growing pains?**

If you feel reminded of the processes and growing pains we went through in the last summer and winter months, you are right: This is the last of the three Jupiter-Saturn squares since the first one in August 2024. But the situation this June is a little bit more complex because another player has entered this scene in the cosmic drama: Neptune, one of the outer planets, pointing beyond the personal and socio-political sphere. Neptune has joined forces with Saturn to form the most unlikely couple in the heavens: the planet of the sublime, the subtle realms and the ideal goes hand in hand with Saturn, the planet of the manifest, the tangible and the real. It’s hard to tell how this liaison will play out. It probably depends on whether and how we can reconcile our spirituality with the demands of the real world and see our delusions for what they are. During Saturn’s and Neptune’s liaison in the summer months, we will probably see a wide spectrum ranging from a glorification of aggressive behavior triggered by disappointment and frustration, feelings of standstill and dissolution to the emergence of our inner, spiritual authority.

In many ways, conflicting and opposing principles come together this June and the outcome is uncertain. Whatever path of growth might open up for us now, it will also be tested against our highest ideals and spiritual vision (since Jupiter will also square Neptune while entering Cancer).

**A surprising spin of events**

The days around the 15th are further heated up by another planetary “call for action”: Mars in Leo will square Uranus in Taurus, indicating unexpected twists and turns and sudden changes. As with fireworks and electricity, the high-voltage will-power of this Mars-Uranus combo needs to be applied and handled with care for the results to be constructive. However, knee-jerk actions and reactions are also likely in the middle of June, probably setting the scene for the rest of the month. If you have important planetary placements in the last degrees of the fixed signs of Taurus, Leo, Scorpio or Aquarius, you might be able to channel this energy into moving mountains or changing direction in your life. But beware of ill-considered action due to excessive pride or unyielding stubbornness.

Is this going to be a dramatic month? Well, it sounds like it. But whatever will happen under these constellations, it is important to keep in mind an old astrological saying about any transit:

“When our life is on track and we are open for it, we receive assistance from the planetary cycles; When our life is off track, we receive a course correction.”

Let’s take a quick look at the individual weeks:

**June 1-8:**

Apart from these larger developments, the first week of June can be very stimulating as we are invited to enjoy the mental flexibility and sociability of the Gemini season. Venus in Aries adds some love, passion and enthusiasm to the mix while it forms a lovely and stimulating sextile to Jupiter on Tuesday. Mercury is still in its own sign of the Twins and strong now, so if you have to complete a writing assignment or do academic work or research, this week is very promising as it will be “crowned” by a feeling of successful completion with Mercury conjoining Jupiter on Sunday.

**June 9-15:**

The second week in June looks decidedly more mellow with Venus having just entered her home sign of Taurus on Friday and Mercury going into Cancer. We might turn away from social gatherings and focus on the simple and the sensual things like enjoying a good cup of coffee in our armchair or the company of our family and our pets. Home might also be the place where we can find meaning and a sense of fulfillment as Jupiter slips into the nurturing sign of Cancer on Tuesday. Nevertheless, we will feel a growing tension building toward the weekend, when powerful and attention-loving Mars in Leo will make its square to erratic Uranus. The activities that we might have planned for the weekend could have surprising turns. Those of us with planetary placements at the end of the signs of Taurus, Leo, Scorpio or Aquarius, could feel pushed to the edge and free themselves of limiting circumstances in a rash move. In any case, having no expectations will help you adjust to the volatility of these days.

**June 16- 22:**

This seems to be the week that we have been internally or externally been preparing (and maybe bracing) for all month as it brings together all of the themes and issues mentioned in the introduction. From June 15-18 Jupiter’s square to Saturn will be exact, indicating the peak of a possible litmus test challenging our trust, what we have believed in so far and what we have envisioned for our future. Jupiter will slide into an exact square to Neptune the very next day and linger there until the 23rd , shifting our attention ever so slightly to a subtle feeling of bewilderment as if a solid frame of reference was slowly dissolving.

And as if to strike a balance to a feeling of having less to anchor us, Mars enters the earth sign of Virgo on June 18. This is significant in so far as it is the first time since September 2024 that the planet of action does not derive its motivation from rather self-centered interests like fighting for the top position or getting our needs met. Instead, being useful and finding a practical solution to help and support people will generally guide our actions until the beginning of August.

**June 23-30:**

The last week in June features a generous New Moon in Cancer on Wednesday the 25th , highlighting our urge to nurture, heal and protect. Although Jupiter, the jester, is conjunct the New Moon, the mood is rather serious, as the general situation might require us to focus on substantive issues. We could be torn between wanting to withdraw into the security of home or to harness the raw, passionate energy of Aries to forge ahead in a disciplined way.

Communication master Mercury will enter Leo on the 27th and oppose Pluto on the 29th . Will we back-paddle into a period of power play and “me, me, me” thinking in July? If you want to know, tune in for the next episode of “This month on Cosmic TV”!
