May and June are going to be two important months of transition, in which we will collectively move from a confusing period of readjusting to emerging new circumstances to a period of accelerated change in summer. At the beginning of May we are still dealing with the aftermath of the powerplay between Mars in Leo and Pluto in Aquarius, the confrontation between single-handed decisions and individual willpower and the imperative and demands of the collective and its evolution.

*Planetary placements on May 1, 2025*

The opposition on April 26 was the third and last one of these two power planets since the beginning of November. Those of you who have planetary placements or sensitive points at the beginning of Leo and Aquarius (and also those with placements at the beginning of Taurus and Scorpio) might have experienced alternating phases of power and powerlessness and attempts of domination since then. If we have taken the low road of these forceful energies, this could have manifested itself in an intense desire for revenge when we have suffered rejection. If we have taken the high road, we might have learned to develop a regal staying power that is in line with evolutionary change. If you feel like you have missed the opportunity to develop some strength along the way, do it like Spain: investigate the reasons for your recent ‘power outage’ during the last Mars-Pluto opposition (which fell exactly on Spain’s AC-DC axis, see below). And then fix the bug to ‘empower’ yourself again. It’s never too late to learn.

Before we move into new territory this summer, there is one more ‘astrological plot line’ that is awaiting its ‘grand finale’ in the coming two months and which will most probably play out in the socio-political sphere for us to witness. This plot line has its roots in the summer months of 2024, when the so-called ‘social planets’ Jupiter and Saturn formed the first of three squares and when the wish and need to expand and invest collided with conservative forces that demanded to stick to old standards. Those of you living in Germany will remember the irreconcilable tensions within the governing ‘traffic light coalition’, which was already obvious to the participants involved as early as August ’24 (the charts of the most prominent figureheads involved were all sensitive to the middle degrees of the mutable signs that this constellation touched). But the tensions didn’t have consequences until the second Jupiter-Saturn square in the winter months of 2024, when the coalition fell apart and new general elections were scheduled for February 2025.

Now that a new German parliament has formed and Friedrich Merz from the conservative party CDU is expected to be elected Chancellor on May 6, the third and last square between Jupiter and Saturn is slowly forming again to become exact in the middle of June. Jupiter will still be in Gemini and Saturn in the very last degrees of Pisces this month, therefore we could see a replay of familiar debates about restricting the immigration law and tightening the belt for fringe groups and the less well-off (Saturn in Pisces) and investments into infrastructure (Jupiter in Gemini). However, as Saturn enters the sign of Aries on May 26 with Jupiter to follow and enter Cancer on June 10, the scenery might shift dramatically and the struggle to create stable and sustained growth could suddenly depend on totally different factors than before and demand decisive action regarding the regulation of aggression and power and the protection of domestic interests.

We cannot know for sure, but the American Federal Reserve could be pushed into the spotlight starting at the end of May and send ripples through the socio-economic world as its chart is highly sensitive to the Aries Point and 0° of the cardinal axis:

On a more personal level, those of you who have planetary placements or sensitive points in the last degrees of the mutable signs of Gemini, Virgo, Sagittarius or Pisces will need to find a way to reconcile the wish for expansive communication and involvement with the necessity to keep chaos at bay by limiting yourself to what the Higher Self demands. In other words: Don’t spread yourself too thin. Stay centered, trust the process and the door for appropriate connections will open at the right time. If you have planetary placements at the beginning of the cardinal signs of Aries, Cancer, Libra or Capricorn, necessities might impose a strict timeline on you toward the end of May so prepare yourself to put in some extra hours and discipline to reach your goal.

Besides these overarching themes we also have some good personal opportunities in May, so let’s take a look at the individual weeks:

**May 1-11:**

This year’s month of May, which we usually think of as a pleasant and peaceful time, has to offer more drive, passion and surprising twists than usual. Venus and Mars will each be in fire signs the whole month and Venus in Aries will be center-stage right from the start. While the tremors of the recent Mars-Pluto opposition are still reverberating, the first days of May can offer a welcome upbeat constellation: Venus meets Neptune at 1° Aries and provides the opportunity for very romantic and passionate moments. After April’s overdose of harsh relationship-realism, we might long for the knight in shining armor or the inspiring muse to take us into a dream world of heroes and heroines – or simply meet the world with an open heart. As long as you don’t buy into your own fantasies, a short trip of our hearts into the ideal world of Neptune might protect our relationships from corroding cynicism. If you have planetary placements around the first degree of the fire and air signs, you might benefit from this passionate and creative impulse.

With the Mercury-Jupiter sextile at the beginning of the week, our thoughts tend to revolve around the potentials of a new venture. These are good days to go wild and brainstorm all of the project’s possibilities. Be prepared to bring along enough flipchart paper. It might be good idea, however, to wait for the practical attitude of Mercury in Taurus (May 11-May 26) before you put anything into practice because ideas tend to be a tad too grandiose this week. The period of Mercury in Taurus can give you the needed grounding to separate the feasible ideas from the impractical ones and motivate you to get down to work.

**May 12- 18:**

Mercury’s entry into the sign of the Bull on Sunday has a catch, though: it’ll square off Pluto in the first days of the second week. Communication might not be as direct and impulsive as in the weeks before, but an inability to let go of certain opinions can lead to avoiding discussions altogether and trying to solve an issue by riding it out. If you have planetary placements at the beginning of the fixed signs of Taurus, Leo, Scorpio or Aquarius, chances are that you might find yourself in a conflict in which none of the involved parties wants to budge.

This tendency to dogmatism will likely increase towards the weekend when Mercury in the middle degrees of Taurus squares Mars in Leo. Doubts about who’s the leader could provoke verbal confrontations. On Sunday, the Sun conjunct Uranus (and the fixed star Algol) in the last degrees of Taurus could highlight this rigidity of principles and set the scene for an aggressive reaction in the first half of June, when Mars will challenge these degrees (where Trump’s MC happens to be). On a less dramatic note, especially if you resonate with the middle degrees of the fixed signs, you could be caught between proving your excellence or opting for a simple, practical solution this week.

**May 19-25:**

The tensions might spill over into the third week of May, but this week we are in for a cosmic treat: Venus in Aries will harmoniously align with Mars in Leo on Tuesday, and they will magnetize the whole week. This is a wonderful and very idealistic meet-up of our two cosmic lovers - and it’s a passionate one. Don’t expect to get some respite now because fire wants to blaze and be active. But you can rest assured that the mood is up and the male and female principles are mutually supportive of each other. If you have planetary placements or sensitive points in the middle degrees of the fire or air signs, let yourself be ‘passionized’ and follow your enthusiasm.

The week ends with a Mercury-Uranus conjunction electrifying and stimulating our brain cells. This is a good time for the conception of practical innovations.

**May 26-31:**

The last week of May feels different and very light as all the planets are already in or will enter a fire or an air sign - with one exception, namely Uranus in Taurus. Saturn will have already shifted from Pisces into Aries on Monday and Mercury will move into his own sign of Gemini the day after, fanning the blaze of our fiery drive with mental activity. I can’t help but getting the image and the sound of a very active newsroom with its peculiar humming and buzzing. There is definitely the danger of nervous over-stimulation now, not unlike having drunk too much caffeine. As exciting as this week may be, with many a thought spent on new beginnings, it might be a good idea to once in while remember that we have a body to take care of as well and sit down for a breathing exercise.

Will things slow down a tad in June and give us some respite? If you want to know, tune in for the next episode on “This month on Cosmic TV”!
