**The general spirit of 2026**

As cool as it might be right now at the turn of the year, 2026 promises to get hot and stormy. The final conjunction of Saturn and Neptune on the Aries Point in February will give us the cosmic go-ahead to venture out into the new era. The time of problem avoidance and burying our heads in the sand is over. Taking action is what is needed. As with every beginning, there will probably be a lot of trial and error since familiar general assumptions will be called into question and dislodged. As soon as Jupiter enters Leo in July, half of the impersonal planets will be in fire and the other half in the element of air. We are collectively tasked to put a lion in our hearts and muster the courage to act on our ideas. A potential danger of so much fire and air is a lack of grounding and realism, which could make quite a few of us scatter-brained and running around in hyperactive mode. Overstating one’s case and overshooting the mark is another downside of too much fire and air. Fortunately, Saturn in Aries can provide the necessary structure and discipline to manifest our ambitions. An unconventional and intuitive approach might serve us best when trying to find a way out of difficult situations. If you resonate strongly with the elements of fire and air, 2026 could get quite an adventuresome year.

**Major themes and constellations in 2026**

**The talent triangle of the outer planets: Big Tech, AI and the new space race**

The speed of the recent technological and digital developments and revolutions will continue as symbolized by the talent triangle formed by Pluto, Uranus and Neptune, and which will be in place until the end of this decade. Neptune in Aries at the center of this constellation with fan the spirit of competition while Pluto in Aquarius, indicating the increasing power of future-oriented and digital corporations (‘Big Tech’), aligns harmoniously with Uranus in Gemini, symbolizing a rapid ‘collective learning process’ or the mining, processing and use of collective digital data – in short: the further development of artificial intelligence. Apart from AI, the technology sector might be especially inspired by warfare and military applications, but scientific breakthroughs in other areas are not excluded, of course. In line with the symbolism of Pluto in the air sign of Aquarius, those who rule the collective knowledge, aka the internet, as well as the skies and the space will wield the power over the masses in the years to come. The new space race that began when Pluto entered Aquarius in 2024 is likely to take off with this triangle-constellation between Pluto, Uranus and Neptune.

Those of you who have significant planetary placements in the first four degrees of Aries, Gemini, Libra or Aquarius might find themselves involved in or even at the forefront of these new technological developments.

Despite the fascinating range of possibilities and potential synergies that these new technologies can offer, we are already seeing the shadow expressions of Pluto in Aquarius socially, which is widespread alienation and a feeling of individual powerlessness and insignificance in the face of the artificial ‘brain power’ and the speed in which changes are taking place. The collective life has shifted from tangible concerns and personal interaction to abstract digital connectedness. The emphasis on the element of fire in 2026 and 2027 might be a welcome and needed vitality injection to counterbalance the increasing abstraction in our daily lives.

**The Saturn-Neptune conjunction on the Aries Point: crossing the final threshold into a new era**

The big astrological event of the Saturn-Neptune conjunction on the Aries Point (0° Aries) in the middle of February, which symbolically marks the beginning of the ‘new era’, might almost go unnoticed. The reason for this lies in the contradictory energies of Neptune and Saturn. Neptune in Aries wants to take the brakes off and idealize the raw vitality and self-assertive qualities of this first fire sign of the zodiac whereas Saturn wants to put a lid on it or get it under control. However, the heat will increase as soon as Saturn separates from this conjunction. An urge to forge ahead with a pioneering spirit and a strong thirst for conquest will erupt as soon as all the personal planets (including Mars) will have crossed that point and will have entered Aries by the middle of April. The time of March/April might be the most belligerent but also encouraging and action-packed period in 2026.

**Neptune in Aries: The myth of the warrior**

The sign which Neptune is in colors the collective imagination and shapes the dream and the mythological narrative that characterizes that whole period. With Neptune being in the sign of Aries from 2026-2037, it’s the archetype of the warrior and the story of survival, self-assertion and victory which will be “in fashion” and tend to get glorified. This subconscious longing for a powerful and potent self-made man can play into the hands of many a power-hungry political leader and justify their aggression as being a form of ‘God’s wrath’ . But one should not forget that this very first sign of the zodiac can also display quite some innocence and idealism, wanting to fight for a ‘good cause’. In Aries life is met with the courage of inexperience and ignorance and the will to explore. Aries symbolizes new life and the striving for light like the first shoots in spring that have managed to pierce through the earth to meet the first rays of sunlight. Therefore, while Neptune is in Aries, we could see a kind of spiritual pioneering spirit emerging.

**Saturn in Aries: “Tame the flame”**

Saturn will make its final entry into Aries on 14 February 2026 and stay there until April 2028. As a so-called ‘social planet’, it tells us something about the sociopolitical circumstances and the reasons that necessitate the conception and passing of new laws and regulations. As we have already seen since last summer, when Saturn dipped into Aries for the first time, the dominant political concern has shifted from regulating the state support of the disadvantaged and needy and curbing the incoming stream of refugees (Saturn in Pisces) to military preparedness (Saturn in Aries). Aries is about mobilizing forces and overcoming adversaries. So, it comes as no surprise that in many European countries like Germany the reintroduction of a compulsory military service is being discussed as the potential threat through external hostile powers like Russia is increasing.

But the enemy does not necessarily have to be an external power. Political leaders might see themselves forced to also take action against hostile forces within their own country, whether the threat is real, fake or imagined (which will probably be difficult to tell apart during the Saturn-Neptune conjunction at the beginning of the year). This year’s accumulation of planets in Aries in April/May will happen in the 4th house of the US chart, which signifies the country’s domestic ground. It will be interesting to see how the US government will respond to these internal challenges.

On a more individual level, the restrictive quality of Saturn can be initially felt as serious and heavy and curbing the natural enthusiasm when contacting planets or sensitive points in the fire signs of Aries and / or in Leo and Sagittarius in your chart. However, with time and patience you might find that you can learn how to channel your energies more efficiently and thus become more effective. If you have planetary placements in the first half of Cancer, you probably find the current zeitgeist very raw and crude and might tend to withdraw. Setting up structures within which you can move securely and plan your progress might help to deal with the situation productively. If you resonate strongly with the first half of Libra, you might be confronted with a demanding authority that tests your diplomatic skills. Setting reasonable limits and risking to make yourself unpopular might create the right balance and create respect. If you have planetary placements in the first half of Capricorn, the hot-headedness and selfishness of the current spirit of the times will probably rub against your sense of continuity and moderation. Demanding circumstances or authorities could test your resolve - but as someone with planets in Capricorn, patience and time is on your side.

**Jupiter and the Leo-Aquarius polarity: Autocracy versus democracy**

Jupiter, the other one of the so-called social planets, has so far been in Cancer since last summer. In popular astrology, Jupiter is called the ‘lucky planet’ because we often feel a certain buoyancy and ease in the area that is affected in our chart. This does not always manifest as a grand opportunity and can be rather subtle, especially when Jupiter is in a water sign. Those of you with planetary placements in Cancer, Scorpio and Pisces, for example, might have felt as if a kind of protective hand has guided them through difficult situations in the recent six months. The water signs and, to a lesser degree, the earth signs will continue to benefit from Jupiter’s stay in Cancer. When Jupiter enters Leo at the beginning of July, those of you with planetary placements in the fire signs (and, to a lesser degree, the air signs) will get their share of a boost for the following 12 months.

However, Jupiter is much more than just about ‘feeling good and being lucky’. Jupiter is the other so-called ‘social planet’ and its sign placement informs us about the general vision, world view or generally accepted morale behind political decisions. It can indicate the current guiding ideal or the ‘ends that justify the means’. With Jupiter in Cancer, protecting the security of family, home and country has been the general rationale. With Jupiter in Leo, we could see a shift to a generosity of spirit or a sense of ‘noblesse oblige’ of the socially and economically better-off. On the other hand, there is the danger that the desire for greatness and supremacy can blow some egos out of proportion, resulting in an excessive self-confidence of those in charge which could make them blind to potential dangers. I am reminded of President Lyndon B. Johnson, who had Jupiter in Leo in his natal chart, and his vision of a “Great Society” and his pledge to redouble the war on poverty, but also his personal pride that prevented him from withdrawing from Vietnam, which resulted in a disaster (quote: “I will not be the first American President to lose a war”). What’s more, Jupiter will be in opposition to Pluto in Aquarius in July, which re-establishes the polarity and tension between autocracy versus democracy. I hope we have learned the lessons of the past to manifest the life-affirming potentials of Jupiter in Leo.

**The year 2026 as it unfolds – the first six months**

2026 is divided into two parts. The six-month period until the beginning of July will start rather cool and collected. The first quarter could feel like taking one big step after another, month after month, thoroughly working through the themes of material security and responsibility, the desire for equality and independence, spiritual longing and dissolution as all the personal planets march through Capricorn, Aquarius and Pisces like a planetary troop. Likewise, those of you with planetary placements in the cardinal sings of Aries, Cancer, Libra and Capricorn could be the first ones to feel pressurized to take action from early to late January, then those with planets in Taurus, Leo, Scorpio and Aquarius from end of January until about mid-February. March through April could be a time when those of you with planets in the mutable signs of Gemini, Virgo, Sagittarius and Pisces might have to face changing circumstances that force you to jump through many a hoop in order to adjust.

As already mentioned above, the months of April and May stick out as the planetary bunch travels through Aries and calls us to action. This can be a rather tumultuous period, but also the time that finally gets us going. If you plan to get a project off the ground, the first week of June looks promising for a kick-off with Venus applying to make a conjunction with Jupiter in Cancer.

**The second half of 2026**

In a way, the second half of 2026 might resemble and remind us of the second half of 2025, but with the added fire, passion and grandiosity of Jupiter in Leo. From July on, the planets start fanning out across the zodiac and continue to form complex constellations until the end of the year. What might have seemed simple at the beginning of the year might turn out to involve more factors and parties than expected. The Jupiter-Pluto opposition at the beginning of July kicks off a series of Kites and Cradles that speak of cooperation efforts across several disciplines and sectors to contain tensions. We will also see the theme of negotiating war and peace again.

What’s different from 2025 is a Grand Cross at the end of September / beginning of October, signifying a potential crossroads in collective developments (those of you with planets around the first degrees of Taurus, Leo, Scorpio or Aquarius might also be personally affected). Negotiation endeavors to could get stuck because of parties refusing to make any concessions. At the same time (on 3 October) Venus will start her retrograde period in Scorpio and then move back into Libra, indicating that relations and alliances will need to be reconsidered in fall, a process that will take until the middle of November to be completed (Venus will go direct again on 15 November). The year will end with a Grand Fire Trine in December, hopefully enhancing the enthusiasm of the holiday season.

As you can see, the Mercury retrograde phases in 2026 will mainly involve the water signs of Pisces, Cancer, and Scorpio. So in 2026, above-mentioned retrograde periods of Mercury might offer insights into our habitual emotional reactions and help us to uncover buried feelings that, when revealed, can reconnect us with life and enhance our intimate relationships.

All in all, 2026 promises to become a very interesting and dynamic year. In this spirit, I wish you all a Happy New Year!
