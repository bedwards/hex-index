The fireworks of awe-inspiring configurations of the last months are over since the first weekend of December when the last of the grand water trines became exact. The subtle current that carried us through October and November with a certain effortless grace is slowly subsiding, giving way to a more focused energy. The remaining weeks before Saturn and Neptune meet precisely on the Aries point (to finally ring in the new era) will be peculiar in their own way. It looks as if all the fast-moving planets have decided to concentrate forces in Sagittarius this month to then jointly march on into the signs of Capricorn and Aquarius:

However, what sounds like an easy journey for our planetary bunch might initially get off to a rather unwieldy start: Whether it’s Mars, the Sun, Venus or Mercury, they will all have to pass Saturn’s reality checkpoint this month and deal with the uncertainty and vagueness of Neptune. For us, this can feel like a lot of energy damming up from the middle of December on until Christmas, asking us to question, reevaluate and let go of old expectations and assumptions. With all these planets in Sagittarius squaring Saturn and Neptune, we could get a course correction wherever we have been too optimistic, naïve or over-confident particularly concerning our vision of a free world and some of our cherished ideals. Those of you who have planetary placements in the last degrees of Gemini, Virgo, Sagittarius or Pisces will be especially sensitive to this potential challenge to whatever you know and have so far believed in. With an attitude of sincere humbleness, though, we might be able to keep the faith while successfully adapting to the changing circumstances of these times of transition.

The 19th and 20th of December

The 19th and 20th of December will fall right into the peak time of this planetary ‘congestion’ and challenge to our beliefs, the very days when all eyes will probably be on the interstellar comet 3i/Atlas because it will be closest to Earth then. The internet has been abuzz with speculations about whether this is an alien artefact or just an unusual comet as claimed by NASA. Astrology cannot answer this question, of course, but it is interesting that this astronomical peculiarity has sparked a serious debate about our understanding of human civilization within the context of the universe, a discussion which will find a focal point just as the tension of the constellation described above is peaking with a New Moon occurring right on the ‘hot degree’ of 28° Sagittarius.

More data about this comet’s activity is expected from Hubble, Webb and ground telescopes as it gets closer in the week before the holiday season, hoping to give answers to questions like: Is there evidence of life in other solar systems? And: Are we the only sentient beings in the universe? Are there extraterrestrials that are far more developed? People with an emphasis in the last two signs of the Zodiac are often especially attuned to and interested in general question like this. Therefore, it is not surprising that Avi Loeb, astrophysicist, cosmologist and professor at Harvard University, who has challenged mainstream science by proposing that the interstellar object 3I/ATLAS could be an artificial probe or a “mothership” from an alien civilization, has a lot of planets in Aquarius and Pisces in his birth chart:

Unfortunately, we don’t have a birth time, but with Pluto crossing all his Aquarius planets in the next 10-15 years (and already starting in 2026), we can make an educated guess that Avi Loeb’s life will undergo drastic changes – whether 3i/ Atlas turns out to be an alien artefact or not.

But back to this month’s planetary bunch: The accumulation of planets in Sagittarius is not all bad, though. The desire to follow our ideals and our hope for a better future is great this month, not only because X-mas time is around the corner. When Mercury joins the jolly bunch in the optimistic sign of Sagittarius on the 12th, it might seem as if someone has set all the jingle bells in motion and fairy lights illuminate our personal path for a few days. Those of you with planetary placements in the fire signs of Aries, Leo and Sagittarius will be especially open to these positive and uplifting vibes. But the exuberance of this Sagittarius stellium can also spill over into the air signs of Gemini, Libra and Aquarius.

Let’s take a look at the individual weeks:

December 1-7:

Since Mercury shifted into forward gear on 30 November our mental attention has turned away from introspection back to our external environment. Catching up with a few things that have been left untouched in the last four weeks come into view again. Those of you who have planetary placements or sensitive points in the last degrees of Cancer, Scorpio or Pisces will pick up on the ease and flow of the last of the Grand Water Trine series which forms on the first weekend of December. Enjoy the deep connection that is now possible with the people that you feel close to.

December 8-12:

The second week starts with the first bump in December’s rocky road. Mars in Sagittarius runs into a square to Saturn, putting a brake on actions that have so far been based on idealism in the recent weeks. Serious concerns make it necessary to take decisions that honor the reality of the situation. Issues with those in authority might surface and demand that you to take on more responsibility. If you feel frustrated or slowed down (especially if you have sensitive points or planetary placements in the last degrees of Gemini, Virgo, Sagittarius or Pisces), focus on immediate concerns and take one step at a time.

Towards the end of the week, our mental focus will shift from the deep waters of our soul to what the future holds in store for us as Mercury leaves Scorpio and enters Sagittarius on Friday. The generous mood of the festive season will take hold of our minds and color our outlook on life until the end of the month. Nevertheless, you might want to postpone big shopping plans until next week because we could feel a bit drained and confused on the weekend when Mars squares Neptune. The more we want something the more elusive it could be. Be prepared that hidden agendas could come to the fore now.

December 15-21:

Our plans and actions will gain more traction this week as soon as Mars enters Capricorn on Tuesday. With a consistent and patient approach you can achieve a lot within the next six weeks, but don’t take minor issues too seriously toward the middle of this week when the Sun squares Saturn. Should you feel challenged in your self-confidence, try not to take difficulties too personally and get a larger view of the situation and adjust the way you manage your life to the necessities of the moment.

On the weekend you may want to leave room for a little bit of escape and create some time to relax. The Sun will be square Neptune, so our creative imagination could be very active but the overall vitality could be somewhat subdued. However, choosing between duty and pleasure might not be that easy now as Venus will square Saturn. It might be a good idea to concentrate on one thing that you love doing or creating and make it happen on the weekend to counteract any potential feelings of loneliness that could crop up especially if you have planetary placements or sensitive points in the last degrees of Gemini, Virgo, Sag or Pisces.

December 22 – 28:

As if ordered for this special occasion, Venus will connect with Neptune on the days before the Christmas holidays to create a magic and romantic mood. There is the tendency to avoid anything harsh, and we might be especially susceptible to flattery and pleasing circumstances. Although we could be tempted to overlook some discrepancies in our relationships, this could be just the right thing to do to be able to enjoy a few holiday moments.

Reality will kick in soon enough when Venus enters Capricorn on the 25th. With the Sun, Venus and Mars in this earthy winter sign, practical dependability and sincerity will color the end of the month. The Mercury-Chiron trine on the weekend can provide the opportunity to address anger issues and wounds of rejection and put them into perspective.

December 29, 2025 – January 7, 2026:

On the last days of this year, it seems as if we are given the opportunity to mentally go over what has happened in December. Despite Mercury still being in the jovial sign of Sagittarius, we might be more reflective and serious at the turn of the year when Mercury will meet Saturn’s reality checkpoint on the 30th. This will be followed by a Mercury-Neptune square on New Year’s Day, which could simply indicate a bad hangover or a day that is perfect for daydreaming or distracting yourself. If you let your mind wander, your intuition might give you a few clues as to what to put into practice in January.

Especially if you have planetary placements or sensitive points in the middle degrees of Taurus, Virgo or Capricorn, you can look forward to a powerful meet-up of the Sun, Venus and Mars in Capricorn on the 7th , which could provide the energy you need to get the year off to a practical start.
