**How many planets can occupy one zodiac degree?**

When looking at the planetary placements in the next four weeks, one cannot help but get the impression that in March the cosmos is trying to figure out how many planets can be crammed into one zodiac degree. And the degree I’m talking about is a special one: it’s the Aries Point, the very first degree of Aries and the very first degree of the zodiac. It has a special significance because it’s the point of the vernal equinox, symbolizing the beginning of a new development cycle. Every year when the Sun reaches this point on April 21st , the day is as long as the night, marking the beginning of spring, the reawakening of nature and our vital forces. So far, nothing special. But this March half of the planets will be either approaching, meeting on or retrograding back to this point. And the ‘planetary squeeze’ will tighten the further we get to the end of March.

*Planetary placements on March 29, 2025 (day of Aries New Moon)*

Pretty intense, isn’t it? Half of the planetary bunch plus the lunar North Node are huddling together around the Aries Point, more or less for the whole month. It looks like a big cosmic convention that is assembling to discuss how to kick-start a new cycle. The symbol of Mercury is not even visible anymore because it’s so crowded at the Aries Point. And it’s not only the fast-moving planets that will cross this point. Neptune will move into Aries on March 31 for the first time since 1861, the beginning of the American Civil War. Saturn will follow two months later on May 26, 2025. So, this is a time of significant collective endings and beginnings and geopolitical shifts (More on this in my next article about the historic Saturn-Neptune conjunction in Aries).

If you happen to have planetary configurations or sensitive points around the Aries Point itself, the next two months will be intense. I could imagine that you might feel quite a bit squeezed yourself or get the feeling that it’s all a bit “too much”, not knowing where to get started first. Similarly, those of you who have significant placements around the first degree of the zodiac signs of Cancer, Libra or Capricorn (and the last degrees of Gemini, Virgo, Sagittarius or Pisces), will feel the shift into collective unknown territory and hear the Aries call to “fight for yourself” very clearly as well. The good news: most of the planets will spread out in the coming months, forming flowing and harmonious aspects to Saturn and Neptune at the Aries Point. So, what might look a little uncoordinated, impulsive and chaotic in March and April could take on a more distinctive shape in summer.

Fortunately, Mars has been in forward gear since February 25, which is good in times that demand action. Some of you might have already noticed that it has become a little easier to initiate things. Fast action is not indicated though, as Mars is still very slow. Its applying trine to Saturn will be in place during the entire month, which can add determination and discipline to our efforts to manifest our plans and projects.

**Values and relations revisited: Venus retrograde (3 March – 14 April)**

However, it seems as if, in some respects, we have been too hasty in our recent attempt to forge ahead. Venus and Mercury, already in Aries, will go retrograde this month. It looks as if both are called back to the beginning, aka the Aries Point. Before we solidly set our feet onto new ground, we will need to revisit the values that we have subscribed to and take a second look at our relationships and alliances. A possible disagreement on values might not be dramatic but enough to shift or question our past perception of a loved one, friend or ally for some time. As with any other Venus retrograde phase, there is a probability that important women or significant others from the past can resurface either literally or in our memory, which can prompt us to review this past relationship and its influence on our self-esteem and the way we relate.

**Reconsidering recent beginnings: Mercury retrograde (16 March – 8 April)**

To add to the complexity, in the second half of the month we are also invited to revise and reconsider the ideals and the motivation with which we thought we could tackle certain issues in March. With Mercury retrograde in Aries and Pisces, our personal will-power and desires are highlighted as well as our dreams and illusions, which all might need some rethinking. As usual in times of Mercury retrograde, it might be a good idea to secure your data before the middle of the month and equip yourself with patience in matters regarding communication, organization and logistics. March might be good time for sounding out the situation and some negotiations, but not for closing deals and signing agreements as too many factors are still pending.

Let’s take a quick look at the individual weeks:

**March 1-9**:

The dream team Mercury and Neptune is active on the first three days of the month. For those of you who celebrate carnival this weekend, this is a perfect constellation to put your heightened imagination to work and create a great costume. The marine theme might be especially popular this carnival. But there is more to this constellation than promising a joyful three-day escape from reality. Mercury and Neptune come together exactly on the lunar North Node in Pisces, suggesting a time for deep inspiration and insights into matters of the soul. The current nodal axis with the North Node in Pisces and the South Node in Virgo is a collective invitation to overcome any kind of inappropriate subservient mindset (shadow side of Virgo) by remembering your spiritual origin (constructive side of Pisces). The North Node has been in Pisces since January and will stay there for the next 18 months. So this is a theme that will stay with us for a little while, but this March is especially highlighted because of the total lunar eclipse on March 14, see below.

On Monday, Venus in Aries already stops in her tracks and will be going backwards until April 14. It might be worth going over recent relationship issues that have surfaced and where you might have prematurely signaled a willingness to cooperate – whether on the personal or the political level. You will have ample time in the coming weeks to readjust your list of values that you would like to stick to in the future. We might not yet be quite ready for this reconsideration work, though. Mercury enters Aries the next day, indicating that our minds are focused on pushing forward and getting ahead. On Sunday, a strong trine between Sun/Saturn and Mars/Moon could indeed support us in our efforts to get things done.

**March 10-16:**

The astrological highlight of this week is the total lunar eclipse on the 14th. If you happen to live in North America and have clear skies, you will be able to see the Full Moon entering the earth shadow and becoming reddish in color, which is why this eclipse is also called a ‘Blood Moon’.

*‘Blood Moon’ and the visibility of the Lunar Eclipse on March 14, 2025*

Whether visible or not where you live, astrologically this Full Moon eclipse does not only happen to be conjunct Saturn, the planet of karma, which is currently in Pisces. It also falls across the opposition between Saturn in Pisces and the Pluto-Uranus conjunction in Virgo in the charts of those born in the middle of the sixties, who are currently experiencing their second Saturn return and their initiation into elderhood. Therefore, it could be especially significant for a whole generation whose healing journey includes liberating their souls from patterns of self-harming obedience and the effects of past-life master-servant relationships. Don’t worry if you miss the day of the eclipse and need more time for this process of emancipation and for coming up with your own life-affirming definition of the term “service to mankind”. Astrologically, eclipses are said to be effective for about six months.

**March 17-23:**

Last week’s themes seem to spill over into the third week of March with the Sun exactly conjunct the North Node and then Neptune on the last degrees of Pisces. What’s more, Mercury has just turned retrograde on the weekend, turning our mental attention inward to reconsider and revise some of the plans to tackle current pressing issues for the next three-and-a-half weeks. A passionate adventure with a dash of depth and intimacy might just be right on the weekend as the Sun conjoins the planet of Love in Aries.

**March 24-31:**

Just as the planets are closing in on the Aries Point toward the end of the month (and possibly creating quite a bit of pressure), Venus meets up with Neptune, providing us with the potential of love and compassion at the same time. Relationships might tend to get idealized this week, but an open heart and an open ear can certainly help to alleviate our pains. Just don’t forget to put off your rose-colored glasses once we approach April.

The month ends with slow-moving Neptune making its important first step into Aries on the 31st. Will we already be able to see the narrative of the lone warrior as the hero of the new era? Or does this mean that impulsiveness will abound in April? If you want to know, tune into the next episode of This month on Cosmic TV!
