The month of August gave us an illustration of what experienced astrologers would call an astrological truth: So-called ‘harmonious’ constellations often promise more than they can deliver. The spectacular harmony of August’s symmetric ‘house configuration’, in which all of the outer planets took part, did match with the great show that Trump put on in Alaska to welcome Putin to establish himself as the peace maker number one on this planet. Since the Saturn-Neptune conjunction on the Aries Point was part of the picture, one could expect a highly visible event with a potential impact on world affairs. America’s natal chart and its Midheaven was directly affected by this constellation, suggesting that everybody’s eyes would turn to the US and its current president. And the world did.

But the hopes that were attached to this meeting of Trump and Putin soon collapsed like a house of cards, mirroring the fleeting character of the elements of fire and air that were exclusively involved in this planetary configuration. Has the peace-making process stopped altogether now? The planetary movements suggest that we will see echoes of August’s events in late September when Mercury and the Sun will enter Libra, triggering the Aries Point again. And, again, we will see an impressive configuration. This time it’s a beautiful ‘kite’:

Will we see another display of smoke and mirrors? Peace-talks that are just ‘hot air’ with no consequences? The situation in September is a little bit different. The kite configuration is flanked by two so-called ‘hard aspects’, namely an intense Mars-Pluto square and a relationship challenging Venus-Uranus square. Both put quite some pressure on the situation. With squares, something’s got to give and a decision has to be made. Since the Mars-Pluto square triggers Putin’s Scorpio Ascendant (if his official birth data can be trusted), he could be the one dominating the headlines this time around, feeling pushed to take forceful action.

What does this mean for the rest of us? Above configuration is quite exceptional in that it affects all of us who have planetary placements at the very beginning or the very end of *any* of the zodiac signs. And I think it’s safe to say that this means most of us. I wish I could break it down for every zodiac sign, but I’m afraid this would go beyond the scope of this article. Suffice it to say that those of you whose natal planets occupy the first degrees of Aries, Cancer, Libra or Capricorn (or the very last degrees of Gemini, Virgo, Sagittarius or Pisces) will be confronted with the same themes that surfaced at the beginning of August, especially regarding issues around exaggerated or selfish demands imposed by people in authority that stifle any spontaneous self-expression or sense of agency. Around September 20, communicative Mercury and the illuminating Sun will go over August’s trigger-point in Libra again so that these matters could end up on the negotiation table then. The Swiss received quite a blow at the beginning of August when Trump suddenly shocked them by imposing hefty tariffs on their exports despite the fact that Switzerland had negotiated an agreement with the US regarding the US tariffs. Not only the Swiss were surprised about Trump’s move, but people all over Europe were wondering why Donald Trump was picking on Switzerland. Maybe astrology can give an answer? Let’s take a quick look at a specific feature in Switzerland’s natal chart: Its natal Mars is in Libra, indicating a preference to solve conflicts in a diplomatic way. What’s more, Mars is at 0° Libra, exactly opposite the Aries Point and therefore highly sensitive to the current Saturn-Neptune conjunction in Aries.

Switzerland’s Mars had already been under pressure since June when the Saturn-Neptune conjunction was starting to form the opposition. What does this mean? When transiting Saturn is making an opposition to our natal Mars we often find ourselves in situations that hardly leave us any wiggle-room – like Switzerland in the early summer when they were confronted with Trump’s high tariffs. And with a Libra-Mars under pressure the first reaction is to negotiate and schmooze our way out of a difficult situation. That’s exactly what the Swiss government tried to do. But when transiting Mars finally hit Switzerland’s natal Mars on August 7 (see chart on the left), Trump played his bully-card and imposed a whopping 39 % tariff, which made the Swiss furious about the received injustice.

Some communication channels could open in this matter and make negotiations possible again around the 20th when transiting Mercury and the Sun will trigger the same degrees (see chart on he right). However, Mars in Libra is only willing to play nice as long as you respect its sense of fairness. If you don’t, it can either turn ice-cold or into a surprisingly fierce and cruel enemy. With transiting Mars in hard aspect to Switzerland’s natal Pluto in Aries at the same time, I have the feeling that this lovely Alpine country will probably show its teeth – or completely rethink its foreign relationships.

**Back to familiar terrain**

Enough about Mars in Libra, let’s turn to an important collective undercurrent. Those of you who think that the changes in summer came way too fast and way too soon will probably be happy to hear that, starting on September 5th, all of the outer planets will be in backward motion, indicating that we’re headed back to familiar territory. Saturn will also turn retrograde on Sept. 2nd and will cross the Aries Point in backward motion. It will dip back into Pisces again and Neptune will do the same in October. Since both of the planetary heralds of the new era will be withdrawing from the public scene, it seems as if the cosmos wants to give us some time in the fall to integrate some of the lessons we’ve learned during the summer and to collectively catch up with unfinished business. In spiritual terms, we might have to deal with the “left-overs of the Kali Yuga” until Saturn and Neptune will meet again in February next year at 0° Aries.

Let’s turn to the individual weeks in September:

**September 1-7:**

After a couple of potentially disruptive days that can put our minds into restless mode, September starts off with a week of high energy and enthusiasm, which can best be channeled into being of service to others. With Mars in Libra, cooperation is key and we might find ourselves working hard to create a friendly and harmonious atmosphere in order for others feel at home or at ease with a certain situation. In general, saying ‘no’ to other people’s requests and needs could be hard this month, but especially so this and next week when Mars will be forming a tight square to “Ye, we can”-Jupiter. Those of you with important planetary placements around 20° of Aries, Cancer, Libra or Capricorn will be the ones affected the most. The weekend could be particularly demanding in this respect, making it clear that we might have bitten off more than we can chew.

Which makes it all the more important to listen to the voice of reason and temperance, aka Mercury in Virgo. The planetary messenger will be in this solution-oriented sign from the 2nd until the 18th, providing a reasonable framework and an analytical mindset with which we can implement our ideas and ground our idealism. However, the lunar eclipse on Sunday will remind us that the best solutions are found when we are connected to our intuition.

**September 8-14**:

We might still feel a bit on overdrive on Monday and Tuesday, but as the week progresses, the tension will die down. The rest of the week is relatively uneventful in terms of general planetary interaction. This does not mean that nothing will be going on in the world (and your birth chart might be individually triggered by any of the moving planets), but there is a good chance that we are granted to take a deep breath and relax after the restlessness of the first week – and prepare for the next peak and week of planetary action. Sensitive souls could already feel the growing attraction between the cosmic lovers Venus and Mars as they are as they are getting closer to forming a sextile, flowing aspect.

**September 15-21:**

And this aspect will become exact on Tuesday. This is a fun combination as Venus in Leo loves to entertain and be entertained - and Mars in Libra is willing to do everything to please Venus. This sounds like the perfect recipe for having a good time with each other. It’s a sextile, though, so you will need to put in a little bit of effort to get the sparks flying. If you have planetary placements around 25° of the fire or air signs, you are likely to pick up on this positive vibe.

The rest of the week could leave us puzzled since there is so much going on and so many different influences are at play that it is hard to find a common denominator. Wednesday and Thursday might confront us with “either-or” situations and a curious mixture of critical thinking, irritability and the desire to build bridges. People could be more touchy than usual, so beware the well-known mines in your relationships. This is all the more important since Mercury enters Libra on the 18th and draws our attention to how we relate. Issues that were significant at the beginning of August could reemerge and call for a solution or a new beginning as above-mentioned kite formation will be building toward the weekend and be topped off by a New Moon in Virgo joining the configuration on Sunday.

The playfulness in relationships will give way to a sobriety and practical approach as Venus leaves Leo and enters Virgo on the 20th. If we do not heed the need for renewal in our relationships, the weekend could surprise us with unexpected turns in matters of love.

**September 22-28**:

The last week of September will see a sudden shift in mood. All of fast-moving celestial bodies will have changed into or enter a different sign. While fair play and equality is high up on the agenda with three planets in civilized Libra, Mars enters Scorpio and forms an intense Mars-Pluto square on the 24th . Primal drives and the survival instinct suddenly loom large and could thwart or spoil the ease and beauty of the moment indicated by the grand air trine formed by the Sun, Uranus and Pluto. Those of you with planetary placements at the beginning of Taurus, Leo, Scorpio or Aquarius will be challenged to direct this high-voltage energy into constructive channels while you might find yourself involved in power struggles in which neither side wants to give in. While this Mars-Pluto combo can indicate an impulse to assert oneself at all costs, this energy in the fixed signs of Scorpio and Aquarius can also provide us with the tenacity and determination for completing an arduous task and achieving a long-term goal.

**September 29 – Oct. 5:**

Our thoughts and ideas revolve around helping and caring for other people this week. Partners as well as the welfare of the family are at the center of our attention. However, with the Mercury -Jupiter square, we need to be careful not to promise more than we can deliver. As the week progresses, it becomes increasingly important to consider vulnerabilities regarding our primal right to exist.

Venus is due to cross 0° Libra in the middle of October. Will we see yet another attempt of peace-making next month? If you want to know, tune in to the next episode of “This month on Cosmic TV”!
