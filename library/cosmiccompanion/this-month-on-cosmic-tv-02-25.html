Have you been feeling like reacting to circumstance rather than taking deliberate steps forward in the recent weeks? If so, then you might be sensitive to Mars’s retrograde phase in Cancer, this month’s most prominent astrological feature. The good news: Mars, the planet of action, is going to finally turn direct this month with the promise of some relief and the feeling of slowly being able to move on with our affairs. The bad news is that this will not happen until the end of the month (on February 25, to be exact), and the planetary go-getter won’t be on new territory until the beginning of May. So, from February through April, we might still be busy coping with the fallout of some of the events that rocked our lives in October, early November and January, when Mars was traveling the same degrees in Cancer.

With Mars in the sign of the protective and motherly sign of the Crab, it’s domestic concerns and home-grown issues that that are likely to demand our attention and energy. There is the desire to protect our own home and local turf, whether on the individual or the political level. Approaching things sideways or in a roundabout fashion might be more effective these days than tackling issues head-on. While things seem to progress very slowly, an intense search into the moral and ethical motives underlying our actions as well as an inquiry into our ambitions and goals could help putting us onto a readjusted path once the motor starts running again.

As we are prone to act on our moods and gut feeling rather than on our brains, we tend to be more inconsistent in our actions. However, we’ve got a supportive Mars-Saturn trine throughout the whole month, which provides the necessary discipline and clarity, so that some things actually can get done despite initial resistance. The water signs of Cancer, Scorpio and Pisces will especially benefit from this constellation, and if you have significant planetary placements around 17° in these signs, a grand trine in water can form, which promises a harmonious blend of stability and drive. As in the case of Scorpio Friedrich Merz, CDU’s designated candidate for Chancellor of Germany, for whom this constellation might make the difference on February 23, 2025, the day of the German parliamentary elections:

The second part of February could get quite intense, though, particularly for those of you who have planetary placements or sensitive points around 17° of the cardinal signs of Aries, Cancer, Libra and Capricorn. Mars will be hovering on that very degree for almost four weeks, in which the cosmic warrior is also squaring Chiron in Aries. While you might feel pressurized to make an important decision, frustration, anger and pain could (re-)surface with those who feel marginalized and bereft of their independence, their right to exist or to occupy space. This is also the time when parliamentary elections will be held in Germany and I’m sure the political campaign as well as the end-result will reflect some of these issues (More on this in my next article). If you can, I would recommend postponing your important decision until the last week of the month when Mercury joins stabilizing and practical Saturn and trines Mars.

Let’s now turn to the individual weeks:

February 1-9:

The first week in February starts off with a burst of planetary activity. It seems as if we’ve got very different radio channels running on high volume at the same time. The most exceptional constellation on the weekend is the rather passive but imaginative and exceptional get-together of Venus and Neptune in Pisces, which happens to tie in the Moon on the 2nd, all happening exactly on the Lunar North Node. The first days of February are great for charity purposes and meditation, but we could also decide to lose ourselves in romantic adventures to reach exceptional highs or to escape the present reality. If you resonate strongly with the last degrees of Pisces, you are given a rare opportunity to tap into a well of empathy and acceptance. Seen from a more collective perspective, this combination seems like a compassionate good-bye and non-judgmental letting-go of an era that is coming to an end.

As soon as Venus enters the sign of Aries on Tuesday, dream time is over and the battle cry of the warrior maid will be heard. It seems as if the female principle is the first to rush into the pioneering and individualistic sign of Aries as she dares to step onto new ground. The Venus-Pluto sextile on Friday can provide the scene for a powerful expression of female emancipation.

“Think big!” might be the motto for a few days, as Mercury in Aquarius trines Jupiter in Gemini on the 4th. If you resonate to the middle degrees of the air signs of Gemini, Libra and Aquarius, there is the opportunity to turn a wealth of information into a vision for the future. However, over-confidence and the tendency to make sweeping statements is a potential danger this week – as always when Jupiter is involved.

The third constellation that becomes mathematically exact this week is the Mars-Chiron square, which will accompany us all through February and March and into early April. During this time, the fear of not being able to assert ourselves and get what we want can make us overly defensive and feel easily slighted, prompting inconsiderate reactions or covert maneuvers, especially if you resonate to the late middle degrees of Aries, Cancer, Libra or Capricorn. Give yourself time to figure out how domestic and family interests can be aligned with your desire to follow your own interests.

February 10-16:

On Monday we could wake up to a sudden urge to break free, leave the well-known tracks of thought behind and shake up the people around us with unexpected statements or decisions. The rebel could get stirred in you especially if you have planetary positions around 23° of the fixed signs of Taurus, Leo, Scorpio or Aquarius. This urge or tension will probably peak on Wednesday when the Full Moon in Leo will shine a clear light on what might need to be changed or renewed.

The general clarity and objectivity of mind could wane on Friday as Mercury will enter the sign of Pisces. For the next two weeks a kind of meandering mental attention could lead us wherever our attention is needed most. On a political and collective level, our minds could be preoccupied with the disenfranchised of the world.

February 17-23:

Looking at the second half of the month, there doesn’t seem to be much going on planetary-wise until Mars turns into forward gear on the 25th . It seems a little as if something is being played over and over again, as if the needle of a record player had got stuck. The only planetary action worth noting this week is the Mercury-Jupiter square on Thursday, which can be a potentially confusing day as a wealth of data could be dumped on those of you who resonate to the middle degrees of Gemini, Virgo, Sag or Pisces. Those of you working in IT, brace and prepare for cases of information overload.

February 24-28:

As the month closes, we’ve got a whopping 4 planets and a New Moon in Pisces, the last sign of the zodiac, emphasizing the need for closure, letting go and preparing for a new beginning. Although such an emphasis in Pisces can give you a general a feeling of being suspended in limbo (or water), we are fortunate enough of having Mars turning direct on the 25th and Mercury conjoining Saturn in Pisces, indicating that some of the seemingly jammed situations of the recent weeks could start to resolve themselves as serious commitments are made and practical solutions are found and agreed on.

Can we finally rush forward with unrestrained energy in March? If you want to know, tune in to the next episode of ‘ This month on Cosmic TV’!
