Every time I return to this newsletter, I remember why I do it: to write honestly about the food system, share bean recipes, and connect with people who care about the food they eat and where it comes from.

But over the past year, something else has become clear: Many of you are also trying to reach people with your work, and you’re trying to figure out how to do it well.

Some of you are long-form writers trying to translate your voice onto social media.
Some of you run small businesses and want more visibility.
Some of you have a mission, message, or story you want to share, but aren’t sure where to start.
And some of you are just here to learn about the food system + recipes (which, honestly, is also perfect).

While I’ve been growing my own presence on Instagram to spread the message of eating more sustainably, I’ve also been working behind the scenes as a freelance content creator, including creating content for Burlap & Barrel. All of that learning has made me want to make this newsletter more useful for you — whether that means more stories about the food system, more bean recipes, or guidance for anyone who wants to show up online with clarity and purpose.

Iluminate’s Gift Guide (not all beans) 🎁

  1. Burlap & Barrel spices. Yes, I work for them, but they also have incredible spices, and the variety is unmatched. Playing with flavor has become my new favorite thing. No piece of toast is without Black Urfa Chili or Silk Chili now, and every pot of beans gets Shallot Powder, Purple Striped Garlic, and Bay Leaf Powder as a base. My family was fighting over who got to take home the Pepita Pumpkin Spice over Thanksgiving! Their bundles are a great place to start for yourself, or as a gift, like the Fundamentals Collection, the Chef’s Collection, or you can build your own. They also have a Black Friday sale happening right now, so it’s a good time to buy!

  2. If you are interested in cooking more beans from scratch in 2026, aside from good beans, you also need a good Dutch Oven. It has to be big enough to cook 1lb of beans, and it’s nice to be able to stick it in the oven if you ever want to try the oven method. I bought this one from Lodge last year to save money (boo Amazon) instead of buying a Staub or a Le Creuset. It’s holding up OK with some chips on the handle, but I’ll likely invest in this Oval one when the time is right.

  3. Now let’s talk beans. If you want to eat more beans, you need to buy more beans! The first step is to stop thinking that a $9 bag of beans is expensive. How much do you spend on animal protein?! You can easily get five meals from a bag of beans, and these are the fancy heirloom varieties; they are worth it! You can absolutely buy the dry beans from the grocery store, but they won’t have the varieties that make cooking beans from scratch worth your time. My top recommendations are Mayacoba, Ayocote Pinto Beans, Gigante Beans, and Tiger Eyes for starters. Of course, Rancho Gordo is who most people think of when buying heirloom beans, but there are many others on the scene! I signed up for Buttermilk Bean’s Winter Bean Share to support local, and if you are feeling extra fun, you can order directly from Iacopi farms, which seems to be the secret beans that chefs are serving at their restaurants. My friends at Beanstory are also running a sale on their delicious Black Tepary beans, where you can buy 1 and get a second bag for free! This deal applies to people living in New York only and you can get in touch with them through Instagram or hello@beanstory.co - let’s support small bean businesses!

  4. Now what should we cook? If you want to eat more beans, I highly recommend this cookbook, Bold Beans Full of Beans: Delicious Beany Recipes to Obsess Over

  5. And finally, if you have space in your kitchen, love the idea of cooking more beans from scratch, but don’t want it to take hours, it’s time to get an Instant Pot. It’s not cute, but boy does it get the job done. If you are looking for something similar, my friend, , highly recommends VitaClay, which she says works like an Instant Pot without the intensity of the pressure valve release.

Gems of the Week ✨

It’s been a while since I’ve shared some of my favorite things, so here are a few!

  • Elizabeth Gilbert’s book - The Signature of All Things. If you like plants, history, and storytelling, you will like this book.

  • Stissing House - I’d been hearing about the restaurant upstate for years, and we finally made a day trip out of it. You likely need a reservation - although we showed up right when it opened and we were able to snag a table - but this is worth it for a special occasion. The ambiance transported us, the fireplaces, the seamless service, and of course, the food. It’s a very animal protein-heavy menu, but we were blown away by many of the dishes, including the artichoke, radicchio Caesar, the chicken, and the very tall coconut cake.
