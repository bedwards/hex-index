*Democrats hold a news conference following today’s votes on health-care premiums. (Photo by [Heather Diehl](https://www.gettyimages.com/search/2/image?artistexact=Heather%20Diehl))*

Today the Senate [voted down both competing health-care packages](https://www.politico.com/live-updates/2025/12/11/congress/senate-rejects-health-care-bills-00686855?nname=playbook-pm&nid=0000015a-dd3e-d536-a37b-dd7fd8af0000&nrid=04af5284-bd73-4a8d-9533-aea39970cbac) tied to the Affordable Care Act’s expiring subsidies, the [stakes of which I wrote about last week](https://substack.com/home/post/p-180812131).

In back‑to‑back votes, Democrats’ three‑year extension of enhanced A.C.A. tax credits failed to clear the 60‑vote threshold, despite [support from four Republicans](https://thehill.com/homenews/senate/5644661-republican-senators-democratic-obamacare-bill/). A G.O.P. plan to shift funds into health savings accounts also fell short.

With neither proposal advancing, federal premium tax credits now look set to expire at the end of this month.
