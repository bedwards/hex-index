Good Tuesday night --

As the polls close, we wanted to share with you a memo we developed for our supporters today.

***MEMO***

To: Save Austin Now supporters

From: Save Austin Now co-chairs **Matt Mackowiak** & **Steven Brown**

Date: 11/4/2025

Re: Our Work Together to Defeat Prop Q

\---

Dear generous Save Austin Now supporters –

In 58 days, the largest grassroots army in Austin history rallied to execute a nearly flawless campaign to defeat the largest property tax increase in city history.

-   Total raised: **$306,695**
    
-   Individual donors in this campaign: **900+**
    

With that budget, here is what our campaign produced:

-   WEBSITE: A new SaveAustinNow.com website was created with specific content and tools to educate, persuade, motivate and turn out voters
    
-   MAIL: We sent 84,000 pieces of mail across Austin
    
-   DIGITAL: We dominated digital in this campaign, with tens of thousands of people visiting our Prop Q calculator, and absolutely massive reach across X, Facebook, Instagram, YouTube and Google.
    
    -   Ad impressions: 1.31 million
        
    -   People Reached: 352,210
        
    -   Impressions per Person: ~4
        
    -   Video Ad Views: 196,718 (three unique ads produced and distributed)
        
-   RADIO ADS: Five weeks of radio ads on 590am, 1370am, 1300am, 98.1 FM and 98.9 FM
    
-   HIGHWAY SIGNS: 60 large 4x8 highway signs installed across Austin in all parts of the city
    
-   YARD SIGNS: We purchased and deployed nearly 1,500 yard signs to individuals across Austin
    
-   BILLBOARDS: Two massive billboards with a print ad for 3 weeks in North Austin and Central Austin
    
-   TEXT MESSAGES: Nearly 200,000 turnout text messages were sent out
    
-   EMAILS: We emailed a 70k email list 30 times over nearly 60 days, with an average open rate of 35%
    
-   ROBO CALLS: Nearly 64,000 robocalls to turn out our supporters
    
-   DOOR HANGERS: 12,000 door hangers were placed on renter doors in downtown, West Campus and Mueller
    
-   POLLING: We polled likely voters in mid-October to see where the race stood and review cross tabs for better targeting and modeling.
    
-   ID WORK: One vendor texted 188,000 Austinites to identify their position on Prop Q. Another vendor sent 100,000 text messages to simulate daily tracking, identify voter trends, execute a voter ID program, and drive turnout.
    

Thank you for your support!
