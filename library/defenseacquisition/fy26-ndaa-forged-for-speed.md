The FY26 NDAA started a year ago with the introduction of the **[FORGED Act](https://defenseacquisition.substack.com/p/rebuilding-the-dib-with-the-forged)** by SASC Chairman Sen Wicker followed by the HASC’s **[SPEED Act](https://armedservices.house.gov/legislation/the-speed-act.htm)** by Reps Rogers and Smith. Both bills provided approaches to streamline acquisition, inject private capital into the procurement process, scale new entrants, bolster supply chains and more rapidly field hedge capabilities.

This legislation has been paired with a half a dozen executive orders focused on acquisition reforms, an [Acquisition Transformation Strategy and additional SECWAR direction](https://media.defense.gov/2025/Nov/10/2003819439/-1/-1/1/TRANSFORMING-THE-DEFENSE-ACQUISITION-SYSTEM-INTO-THE-WARFIGHTING-ACQUISITION-SYSTEM-TO-ACCELERATE-FIELDING-OF-URGENTLY-NEEDED-CAPABILITIES-TO-OUR-WARRIORS.PDF). To have the Senate, House, White House, and the Pentagon aligned on bold reforms to drive speed, agility, and a revitalized acquisition system and industrial base is a once-in-a-generation event. Now is the time to EXECUTE!

The NDAA passed the House by [312-112](https://clerk.house.gov/Votes/2025320) on Dec 10 and is expected to pass the Senate with bipartisan support shortly.

The NDAA covered a wide-range of topics across the 3,096 pages of [legislative text](https://armedservices.house.gov/uploadedfiles/rcp_text_of_house_amendment_to_s._1071.pdf) and 636-page [Joint Explanatory Statement](https://armedservices.house.gov/uploadedfiles/fy26_ndaa_joint_explanatory_statement.pdf). We try to get to the heart of the changes here and pithily identify what changes acquisition professionals and industry partners should care the most about.

### **Quick Assessment**

\---

-   While the **PAEs are now established and get new authoritie**s, this should be viewed as the first step in what will hopefully be a continued transfer of power to accountable and knowledgeable professionals who can work collaboratively with operational users and deliver capability fast. Without improved budget authority and congressional support for killing underperforming programs we worry that PAEs will not be as effective as they could be - and critics will use that to diminish the role. Need appropriator support for PAEs to deliver on the promise. We were disappointed to see the Major Capability Activity Area provision not make the final cut as that was a promising pathway to portfolio-based budgeting.
    
-   Along with PAEs, **Combatant Commanders get new authorities** and set the path for them to become quasi-acquisition shops. While some may cringe at that (and we have been skeptics in the past) the time is right to provide them with greater control of their destinies in the near-term. This is a huge lesson from Ukraine.
    
-   The NDAA overall is strongly **Commercial First**. There are many provisions that will be incredibly impactful to moving in the direction of fielding available, highly capable technology at scale and speed. We were very disappointed that some provisions to support new entrants (non-traditional contractors who are investing billions of their own money) did not make it, but we hope that contracting officers will recognize the potential here and honor privately developed military solutions as commercial products more regularly.
    
-   The era of **novel business models** that have greater appreciation for private capital investments is about to begin. The inclusion of consumption-based models is a great first step, but DoW needs to 10X its pursuits here and we hope the Hill will continue to support greater authorities here. While DoW has some smart people working this at higher levels, we need greater training at the lower levels to exercise its full potential.
    
-   We were disappointed to see Capability Based Pricing not make the cut. We saw the antibodies against that come out but think the critics were incredibly short sighted as the pricing process is broke today and this would have provided a path to a new paradigm. We’d still like to see COs start employing the intent here even without the explicit direction from Congress.
    
-   The **multi-year procurement authority** is a powerful step forward in generating new capacity and capability with a set of lower cost, more highly scalable capabilities - and bringing more balance to the low-high mix of capabilities. We need this same approach across many other munition lines and hope the Hill will build on this approach as new lower cost weapons come online.
    
-   This NDAA brings fresh focus to **contested logistics** which despite whatever capabilities we field, could be the challenge that makes us pull back as the adversary makes sustaining forces in theater harder and harder.

### **Our Top 10 Provisions in the FY26 NDAA**

\---

#### Sec. 1802. Establishment of the Role of Portfolio Acquisition Executive.

Adds new 10 USC 1732 to establish Portfolio Acquisition Executives.

PAEs will be the senior acquisition official to lead a portfolio of capabilities, with authority for plans, budgets, and execution of programs and projects in the portfolio.

Key duties will include:

-   Coordinating with the relevant service chief when evaluating, modifying, or implementing requirements determinations, performance objectives, procurement quantity objectives, and materiel readiness objectives.
    
-   Liaising and **collaborating directly with operational users** of programs to receive regular feedback to ensure the effectiveness and suitability of capabilities.
    
-   Employing **iterative development cycles** with the authority to modify, discontinue, or **terminate** the development of capabilities—
    
    -   That no longer align with a joint capability requirement or other capability requirement established by the Secretary or relevant service chief; or
        
    -   That are experiencing significant cost growth, technical or performance deficiencies, or delays in schedule
        
-   Collaborating with **mission engineering** functions of the DoD to conduct cross-service technical and operational activities to coordinate integration of emerging technologies, prototypes, and operational concepts, as appropriate
    
-   Ensuring effective market research, prioritized use of OTs for prototypes and the procurement of **commercial products and commercial services**.
    

PAEs are expected to **be assigned dedicated personnel** and not **through matrixed**, collateral duty, or dual-reporting arrangements.

**Our Take:** The establishment of the PAEs, by both the Department in the [Acquisition Transformation Strategy / memo](https://media.defense.gov/2025/Nov/10/2003819439/-1/-1/1/TRANSFORMING-THE-DEFENSE-ACQUISITION-SYSTEM-INTO-THE-WARFIGHTING-ACQUISITION-SYSTEM-TO-ACCELERATE-FIELDING-OF-URGENTLY-NEEDED-CAPABILITIES-TO-OUR-WARRIORS.PDF), and codified in Title X per this NDAA could be one of the biggest transformations in defense acquisition history. [Pivoting to portfolio management](https://defenseacquisition.substack.com/p/think-portfolios-not-programs) practices will enable greater speed, interoperability, mission impact, efficiencies, and regrowth in the industrial base — if done properly. PAEs will need to shape a [core set of portfolio metrics](https://defenseacquisition.substack.com/p/peo-portfolio-metrics). It will be interesting to see how PAEs terminate programs as one would suspect they would coordinate with Service leadership before terminating a major program.

The PM - PAE - SAE structure is great, yet at least one Service will need an SAE exemption to implement their complex approach. Assigning dedicated personnel (not matrixed) to the PAEs will cause them heartburn. The collaboration with users, rapid iteration, mission engineering, use of CSOs and OTs elements are all gold.

#### Sec. 1826. Exemptions for Nontraditional Defense Contractors

For contracts, subcontracts, and agreements provided by nontraditional defense contractors (per [10 USC 3014](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title10-section3014&num=0&edition=prelim)), **shall be** exempt from the following:

-   [DFARS 252.242-7006](https://www.acquisition.gov/dfars/252.242-7006-accounting-system-administration.). Cost Accounting System
    
-   [DFARS 252.234-7002](https://www.acquisition.gov/dfars/252.234-7002-earned-value-management-system.). EVMS
    
-   [DFARS 252.215–7002](https://www.acquisition.gov/dfars/252.215-7002-cost-estimating-system-requirements.). Cost Estimating System
    
-   [DFARS 252.242–7004](https://www.acquisition.gov/dfars/252.242-7004-material-management-and-accounting-system.) Material Management and Accounting System
    
-   [DFARS 252.245–7003](https://www.acquisition.gov/dfars/252.245-7003-contractor-property-management-system-administration.) Contractor Property Management System Administration
    
-   [DFARS 252.244–7001](https://www.acquisition.gov/dfars/252.244-7001-contractor-purchasing-system-administration.) Contractor Purchasing System Administration
    
-   [DFARS 252.242–7005](https://www.acquisition.gov/dfars/252.242-7005-contractor-business-systems.) Contractor Business Systems
    
-   [DFARS 215.407](https://www.acquisition.gov/dfars/215.407-special-cost-or-pricing-areas.) Special Cost or Pricing Areas
    
-   [10 USC 3702](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid:USC-prelim-title10-section3702&num=0&edition=prelim) Required cost or pricing data and certification
    
-   [FAR Part 31](https://www.acquisition.gov/far/part-31) Contract Cost Principles and Procedures
    

The requirements **may be** **waived** or modified with a written determination approved by the head of the relevant contracting activity. May only be delegated to a senior contracting official or a more senior official. The justification must explain why application of such requirements is in the best interest of the federal government.

**Our Take:** This is a major win to reduce the barriers of entry for new defense tech companies and the burdens to do business with the DoW. Exempting these requirements enables the [NDCs](https://aida.mitre.org/demystifying-dod/traditional-vs-non-traditional/) to continue to operate with novel business models and speed. It will be interesting to see which HCAs impose some of these requirements on NDCs for what they see as justifiable cases.

#### Sec 873. Combatant Command Experimentation Authority

Provides combatant commanders the authority to conduct experimentation, prototyping, and technology demonstrations to support the development and testing of innovative technologies and capability solutions to address operational needs.

Upon completion of an experiment, prototype, or technology demonstration, if a combatant commander submits a written determination to the senior contracting official that the demonstrated technology or capability meets the operational need of the combatant command, that determination may be used to fulfill the following requirements: sole source justification, validated capability needs statement.
