Almost nothing ever goes exactly according to plan.

You can't plan for absolutely everything. They're going to go wrong. Someone isn't going to arrive on time despite their attempt to. Either due to traffic along the way or construction they hadn't planned on avoiding. Maybe they got a call from a family member or friend which made them late because they had to deal with that first. People get sick, they trip and fall, they don't get the job that they want or find out things they don't want to know. It's an unfortunate reality that reality doesn't conform to what we believe is best for us.

It takes a lot of effort to even begin to make sure that things don't go horribly wrong simply because of random chance or human decision making. Very few people have the kind of knowledge and forward planning necessary to eliminate all the possible things that can go wrong. Largely because they don't have the ability to see the future. However, if you study enough about human behaviour and the potential for reality to cause problems, you can find a way around them. Plan for them far enough ahead to ensure that even if they do go wrong. They can leave earlier than they would need to make sure that despite the possibility of traffic and calls from friends and family, you can still make it there at the expected time. You can still hold the event you want to have.

Previously in this space, we've looked at the idea of starting out on a journey of self improvement, then we looked at how to work harder when you feel like there are people who are better than you. More recently we've looked at the idea of having goals and how this impacts your ability to improve. That's not the end of the story though. Just because you have set yourself a goal to worker harder and push yourself further in your journey towards what you want doesn't mean you will have no problems along the way. Someone will get in your way or even actively work against you. There is always this potential, but when it comes right down to it, the smarter that a person is, the more likely they are able to create problems for you.

“If you've miscalculated by even a decimal...”

“There is not a decimal, a fraction, an infinitesimal variable beyond my thought, beyond my mind. Even now the seven thousand seven hundred and ninety eight variations of this argument I have anticipated and know how it ends.”

“Then how does it end?”

“The only way it can end, with my victory.”

Barry has had his fair share of difficulty thrown his way over the past several seasons. Not all of them were bad, even if they were sometimes going wrong. Thawne actually wanted Barry to improve. Enough that he actually worked to take away obstacles from Barry's path who might stop him from pushing himself harder and faster.

“Savitar, Zoom, Thawne, their powers are nothing compared to the power of limitless thought.”

Thawne only wants Barry to deal with the types of problems which he can either protect Barry from or ones that help him get towards what he wants, which is to go home. Zolomon wanted to keep Barry's greatest obstacles from him until it was worthwhile for Barry to know. One that allows him to help Barry push himself further. Savitar had the benefit of knowing what was going to happen because he was from the future and knew about what happened before.

“You may be the fastest man alive, Allen. But I'm the fastest mind.”

DeVoe is much different than any of the others. He knows more than all of them combined and can out calculate the possibilities. He can see so much of what could happen and plan to push things in a particular direction. One of the biggest problems with DeVoe is why he wants to do what he's doing. While Thawne, Zolomon and Savitar all wanted terrible things, mainly destruction of something or someone. What DeVoe wants is to help people. To make people better than they currently are. Or at least what he believes to be better than they have been so far. In some ways DeVoe is the mirror image of Barry.

“Good people don't destroy lives and call it noble.”

Thankfully for most of us in the real world, such people don't actually exist. Although there are people who believe they have that power or should have it. Those who want to give themselves power like that. Find a way to control people's lives and make them the kind of people they think they should be. So much so that they're willing to hurt others in the attempt. Someone with that kind of thinking can do a lot of damage to people. Make their lives worse. Doing it all in the attempt to change the way people live for the better.

“No one is fearless, but real heroes they find a way to rise above that fear and make a difference anyway. Even if it kills them in the process. So this is me, rising up... hopefully without the dying part.”

Barry and DeVoe have that in common on many levels. They both want to make things better and to help people push themselves to better places. Where the differences between Barry and DeVoe come into play is with the fact that Barry doesn't want to force them to do what he wants. He will encourage and push where possible to choose the better option. One that will help people rather than hurt them. DeVoe on the other hand doesn't have the same faith in humanity and willingness to believe that human beings will eventually get to a better place.

“DeVoe is ruthless. He's not gonna stop until he's taken her powers and her life. He's not gonna go easy on her so neither can I.”

“I am so sorry that you had to experience that. But Barry we can't be ruthless like DeVoe. He's willing to sacrifice anyone and anything. Just remember that the next time you're pushing too hard okay? You of all people know that heroes aren't made over night.”

As a result, he's willing to do terrible things to people. Manipulate people's lives in all kinds of disturbing ways. Give them abilities which don't just make their lives better but also can cause them to suffer. He does this because they are the types of powers he wants and can use to his advantage in his almost single minded attempt to fix humanity. Somewhat ironically, in order to save humanity he has to give up his own and move beyond such ideas. Emotions like love and compassion and mercy are obstacles in his way towards the saving of humanity. While at the same time DeVoe uses these feelings which Barry clings to as obstacles in Barry's path.

“Pessimism isn't smarter than optimism. In this life the bravest thing you can be is optimistic.”

People who want to make the world better can often use the compassion and love of other people against them. Most people have things like compassion and love and mercy. It makes it hard for them to imagine the idea of people who don't. Or people who are willing to do terrible things either because of or despite their compassion towards others. When they find someone who might be doing this, they are more likely to believe that they have positive motives. This allows them to be taken advantage of. Some of the worst things people have done are from a place of compassion and mercy.

“I knew that the same thing in you that I fell in love with had the potential to destroy us... your humanity. I couldn't let that get in the way of us achieving our goal... the enlightenment.”

Finding a way to overcome this obstacle isn't very easy. Largely because it pushes them into a place where they might eventually give up their own compassion and mercy in the name of love. After all, if someone who is so intelligent believes that their compassion for others is in the way, they must have a point. Even if they don't let go of their mercy towards people, that doesn't mean it won't end horribly for people.

“Emotion is the father of all error. Chemicals in our brains that distract us, impede progress. The most destructive and ridiculous imbalance of all is that thing you feebles call love.”

We see this in many ways through DeVoe's wife Marlize and how she reacts to the changes her husband goes through. She has incredible compassion for other people. It's part of why she was willing to go along with DeVoe's plan in the first place. She cares about humanity and its future. Yet the terrible things which have happened to her and other people don't allow her to see the goodness in them. She instead focuses on who they will end up becoming thanks to her husband's plans to remake people into much better versions of themselves. As things progress though she can see the way in which her husband changes and loses many of the things which lead her to fall in love with him in the first place.

“Perhaps there were variables that we did not consider.”

“As I already explained, there is no variable beyond my examination.”

“Even less apparent, the last time Barry Allen saw you, you took the life of his close friend and now he sees you and he pauses. You really don't see it? You can calculate near infinite outcomes across multiple earths but you cannot account for how someone is feeling.”

Naturally, friends and family are the biggest obstacles to overcome. They are after all the people who you would give up everything for. You want the world to be better for them. If they aren't on board, you end up destroying everything. So you would go along with them and whatever they wanted no matter how bad the thing they want you to do might end up being.

“Having the ability to help someone but do nothing, that is a far worse death than anything the Trickster has for you. But rising above that fear and saving those lives? That's a greater life. So rise up, I don't think it's a stretch for you.”

“You really believe I can do that?”

“Yes, you just need to believe it yourself.”

This is part of what makes Barry different. He refuses to let go of his compassion and mercy despite the obstacles in his way. Regardless of whether his feelings lead him down a bad path which hurts him and even the people around him. He's a better person and thanks to him, his friends and family want to be the kind of person he is. Not by being forced to do it, but by making the choice through his example.

“You know what makes a great speedster? It isn't their speed. It's being the light that everyone needs when the world grows dark. It's the kinda person they are. The kinda person that always wants to help. That's why being The Flash is your destiny. It's your way of helping others, but it isn't mine.”

When confronted with this reality, there's a temptation to try and find ways around it. To think that because you're so smart and you can think things through on a higher level, you just don't have to do the hard work other people have. It's tempting to believe that you can skip ahead in the process and get to the end of the journey before having to put in the hard work.

“We all have to decide who we are. This is who I am.”

But we'll get into that when we explore season 5.
