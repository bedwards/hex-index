We all have some kind of goal.

When we're a kid, these goals are chosen for us. Things like learning to talk and walk. Your parents have these goals for you and you're expected to achieve them. Once you have a handle on these things, you end up with other goals pretty quickly. You're expected to learn things like what to do and what not to do, such as learn to dress yourself without help and getting up on time. Although people can help you with these things, they are fundamentally your goals to achieve and other people expect you to achieve them. Eventually, you're brought other goals to achieve like going to school and learning about the world. Goals like this are harder to achieve but hopefully if you've learned to achieve the other ones you have enough knowledge to actually go through and do these in a way that helps you grow.

In doing this though, you learn that you're supposed to start achieving your own goals and having your own ideas about where you want to go in the world. You have to make decisions about much longer term, difficult goals you can have and being able to achieve them is not as for certain as some of the others. These earlier goals actually seem really simple by comparison. What you also learn is that other people have other goals, some of which might cause problems for you in getting what you want for yourself. This conflict can cause a lot of problems and make people do some crazy things in order to find a way to get there.

Previously in this space we looked at the [beginning of a journey](https://tvphilosophy.substack.com/p/the-flash-season-1-the-beginning) and all the important parts of the starting. Then we looked at how it can be important to [push yourself harder](https://tvphilosophy.substack.com/p/the-flash-season-2-working-harder). To not get lazy about your knowledge of things so that you can do more than what other people have figured out before. Push yourself beyond your limits and how that can give you all new insights into the world and the potential you have. This isn't the end of the story though. Because of course one of the best ways to come to push yourself and not get lazy, is to have goals. Knowing where you're going is an important part of the journey. Making sure that you don't just aimlessly wander off into strange directions or hurt people in the process.

How do you actually do that though?

> “There's only one thing I know about life. I know some things happen by chance, and some things happen because we make them happen.”

This is very much where we find Barry at the beginning of season 3. He's put so much effort into becoming someone who has established himself as someone with knowledge and abilities beyond ordinary people. Not only as someone who reacts in the moment but who pushes himself to do more when the moment requires it. However, he hasn't really had a goal to achieve in any serious way. He's been mostly aimless and uncertain about himself. Now with so much having happened to him because of Reverse Flash and Zoom, he wants to actually achieve something. Mainly to avoid some of the horrible things that have happened to him because of what his enemies have done.

> “How do we ever know if our actions now impact the future?”

Of course, the problem with having goals is that just because you have them, doesn't mean that they're always good ones. You can have bad goals. Even if they aren't intended to be bad, they have can consequences you don't intend and cause problems for yourself and the people around you. So much so that you want to fix them in some way. Doing that can have its own issues as well. You can end up creating more problems rather than fixing the original problems you were trying to solve. Which means that success and failure are not always clear cut. Something you have to figure out if you want to actually have goals. Making sure you don't mess things up or at least minimize the amount of damage you end up doing.

> “We're not gods, we're men, who for whatever reason have been given extraordinary abilities. The question you need to ask yourself is 'What kinda hero are you gonna be?' Are you just going to take a do-over every time you make a mistake? Or will you live with them and move forward?”

Barry needs to figure this out very early on. He has a goal about wanting to fix the fact that his mom died and all the consequences that happened as a result. How it destroyed his life in so many ways and set him on a path. Having this goal for Barry means that he has to interrupt and ultimately stop Reverse Flash's goals of hurting his mother and father. This might seem like a good idea because obviously what Eobard has intentionally bad goals. Hurting people is generally not a good goal to have. But stopping people can also have terrible results. Especially when you're talking about time travel. As a result, Barry's positive goals have negative consequences. Something he has to learn not to do.

> “You have to trust that in the long run the decisions you make are the right ones. I was always too good at forgiving myself Allen, and you were never good enough.”

When you try to do this, you're going to make mistakes. A mistake isn't the same as failure. They can have similar parts to them but in the end they're very different things. If you make a mistake, it's possible to correct it. To make up for an error so long as you don't do it with bad intentions. Such as drawing and accidentally smudging the paper in some way. Maybe turn that smudge into a shadow or a blood stain or a beam of light depending on the colour you were using at the time. You can integrate a mistake into your goals and the future ideas you might have. Hopefully in doing so the people around you can understand why you did it and that your intentions were good. They won't always and in that case you need to put in extra effort to deal with the fallout from the mistake.

> “The more you deny someone their potential, the more they're going to look for it elsewhere.”

Barry knows a thing or two about making mistakes. He has made so many of them. In the beginning it was all about mistakes he didn't intend to make. He didn't really know what he was doing and that meant things wouldn't always work out the way he wanted them to. Nowhere is this more clear than in how he handled Thawne. Then he made mistakes because he was trying new things to push himself further and do more than he ever thought possible. Now he knows that mistakes will probably happen and yet he chooses to make them anyway. Mainly because he has a goal in mind and these mistakes are okay so long as they get him closer to his goal.

> “You made a mistake, so what? I'm sure Barry made a lot of mistakes at first too. Don't let this destroy your confidence you're meant to do this Jesse, you're meant to be a speedster.”

Failure on the other hand is pretty catastrophic. It can make things completely destroyed and unable to be fixed. No matter how hard you try, this is who you are and what you have to live with. Even if you know that the bad things are coming, you can't do anything about it. These are the moments we try our best to avoid because they tend to pile on each other. Once a seriously bad thing happens, more bad things are likely to happen, leading down a terrible path. Yet these places can be something other people have goals towards.

> “There may be a darkness inside of you but if you face it, it will make it easier for the good side of you to win.”

Barry has had his fair share of failures at this point in his journey. He's failed against many different people with bad intentions. The two biggest of these being his inability to stop Thawne and Zolomon. So much of what Barry wants has been taken from him as a result of Reverse Flash and Zoom. His mother died because of Reverse Flash's hatred of him and desire to ruin Barry's life if he can. While Barry's father died thanks to Zoom's own failure to get Barry to do what he wants. Although in the end him killing Henry Allen gets Zolomon what he wants. It turns his failure into a success and Barry's success into a failure.

> “I didn't steal anything from you Clariss. I was just trying to put things back to the way they were supposed to be.”
> 
> “This is how my life was supposed to be. For years I felt like something was missing, and now I know why.”
> 
> “I'm sorry, I didn't mean to do this to you.”
> 
> “Yeah well you did and here I am. Your rival, back again.”

Because of course having goals fundamentally is about having a future. Everyone has a future, even the bad people with terrible intentions. So all goals are focused on the future. Most people don't know for sure what the future will be. No matter how much planning or what ideas they might have, it's always unknown. They have to guess and be uncertain about whether or not they will achieve their ultimate goals. It's a gamble with your own life and the potential for you to have what you want. Although if you actually know the future, that's less of a problem.

> “This isn't you talking it's the powers, they're messing with your mind. You're sick.”
> 
> “I'm broken Barry. But what do you care? Cause you got Iris, you got your happy ending, everyone else be damned. So, I'll tell you what you guys let me go and I will leave you to your sad and miserable lives.”
> 
> “No, we're not abandoning you.”
> 
> “You did this to me.”

Which is the main problem that Savitar represents. Being for all intents and purposes a god. Someone who knows exactly what he needs to do. The kind of power almost no one could ever possibly hope to achieve. Some have tried but even they weren't able to do things. He knows exactly what's going to happen and how they might change in the future. Which Savitar would prefer you to believe because it serves his purposes and creates the conditions he needs to actually achieve the power of a god.

> “To be a god you just have to make people believe you are.”

As a result, you can't really beat him in any serious way. His goals are already figured out and in some sense he knows whether his goals will work out. He can plan ahead for any eventuality and put up obstacles in the way of a good guy plan like Barry's to figure out how to stop him.

> “The thing they never tell you is that nothing ever stays the same. So the harder you work to take down the bad guys, the more they up their game.”

A good villain more than anything knows how not only to set goals and consider the possibilities, but also how to get in the way of the good guy. To give himself the best chance of actually stopping someone from getting the way of their evil plans. For something like that, you need awareness of the tools you have available to you and the knowledge of how to use them to your advantage. You have to know things other people don't.

> “You wanna be the villain, this is what they do. They kill their friends because nothing matters to them anymore, right? Right? Come on, kill me Caitlyn.”
