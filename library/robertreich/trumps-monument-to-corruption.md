Friends,

Trump isn’t just destroying the White House to make room for a vanity ballroom — he’s selling it off to the highest bidders, who conveniently need favors from his regime.

The giant ballroom is Trump’s monument to corruption.

Google and Amazon are [both chipping in](https://www.factcheck.org/2025/10/trumps-white-house-ballroom-sparks-questions-about-funding-and-ethics/) with massive donations. They both just so happen to have [massive antitrust lawsuits](https://prospect.org/2025/10/28/heres-what-trumps-ballroom-donors-want/) working their way through the courts. Amazon [is also suing](https://prospect.org/2025/08/25/2025-08-25-federal-appellate-court-finds-nlrb-unconstitutional/) to get the National Labor Relations Board declared unconstitutional. But I’m sure their ballroom donations have nothing to do with that, right?

I suppose that Apple’s support for the ballroom isn’t related to its [own legal problems](https://prospect.org/2024/03/27/2024-03-27-bump-in-the-road-apple-doj-lawsuit-auto/) — or its desire to [remain exempt](https://appleinsider.com/articles/25/08/06/apple-exempt-from-100-semiconductor-tariffs-thanks-to-its-100b-us-investment) from Trump’s tariffs.

Oh, and Meta. It’s also involved in a [major antitrust lawsuit](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/05/27/technology/meta-antitrust-trial-concludes.html) with the Federal Trade Commission right now. But I’m sure the company is just donating because Mark Zuckerberg is a patriot.

Surely it’s not because Meta and other Big Tech companies stand to gain handsomely if Trump maintains his [corporate-friendly AI policy](https://www.whitehouse.gov/articles/2025/09/president-trump-tech-leaders-unite-american-ai-dominance/).

Other generous donors to the ballroom project include cryptocurrency players like [Coinbase, Ripple, and even the Winklevoss twins](https://prospect.org/2025/10/28/heres-what-trumps-ballroom-donors-want/).

I’m sure the Winklevoss twins would be thrilled if Trump kept up his crypto-friendly policies, which *he’s* also cashing in on.

And Coinbase’s donation probably has nothing to do with the company’s being under an active regulatory [investigation](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/05/15/technology/coinbase-sec-investigation.html), right?

Another donor: The railroad giant [Union Pacific](https://prospect.org/2025/10/28/heres-what-trumps-ballroom-donors-want/), which is eyeing a [$72 billion megamerger](https://www.commondreams.org/news/union-pacific-norfolk-southern-merger-report) that needs approval from federal regulators.

Another: [Comcast](https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/white-house/list-donors-trump-new-white-house-ballroom-east-wing-rcna239481), which needs government approval for the [mergers and acquisitions it pursues](https://tracxn.com/d/acquisitions/acquisitions-by-comcast/__7JxypYsnb1VsWCiQ_lrCGvHzsxtHKJjq_WPi5an152I#list-of-acquisitions).

As [does](https://prospect.org/2025/10/28/heres-what-trumps-ballroom-donors-want/#:~:text=Separately%2C%20Blackstone%2C%20the%20private%20equity%20firm%20run%20by%20ballroom%20donor%20Stephen%20Schwarzman%2C%20just%20announced%20an%20%2418.3%20billion%20deal%20alongside%20TPG%20to%20take%20mammogram%20rollup%20Hologic%20private%2C%20which%20presumably%20Schwarzman%20wants%20the%20government%20to%20approve.) billionaire private equity executive Stephen Schwarzman, the CEO of Blackstone.

Companies that [survive](https://lockheedmartin.com/content/dam/lockheed-martin/rms/documents/training/IITSEC-Fact-Sheet-Final.pdf) on [government contacts](https://www.wired.com/story/palantir-government-contracting-push/) are also chipping in, including [Palantir](https://prospect.org/2025/10/28/heres-what-trumps-ballroom-donors-want/) and [Lockheed Martin](https://prospect.org/2025/10/28/heres-what-trumps-ballroom-donors-want/). How kind of them.

The Supreme Court has narrowed the [definition](https://www.brennancenter.org/our-work/analysis-opinion/supreme-court-weakens-safeguards-against-state-public-corruption) of “[bribery](https://www.skadden.com/insights/publications/2024/07/us-supreme-court-holds-that-federal-bribery-law-does-not-criminalize-gratuities)” to the point where a specific favor has to be demanded in advance of payment. So we can’t say this is bribery … exactly.

But the writing’s on the wall — perhaps literally. These donors [are likely to get their names](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/trump-white-house-ballroom-donor-names/#:~:text=Also%20under%20consideration%3A%20listing%20donor%20names%20on%20a%20website%2C%20though%20no%20final%20decision%20has%20been%20made.%C2%A0) etched into the new White House building itself.

Could there be a more fitting monument to the Trump presidency?

That’s because in Trump’s White House, *everything* is for sale — even the building itself.

Memo to all of us: One of the first things to be done when sanity and legality are restored to Washington — demolition of Trump’s ballroom memorial to corruption.
