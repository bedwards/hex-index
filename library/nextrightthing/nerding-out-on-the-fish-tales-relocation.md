We have been regular customers of [Fish Tales](https://fishtalesonline.com/) in Cobble Hill since we first moved to the neighborhood in 2023. We are so grateful it exists because it makes it possible to get fresh, high quality fish year round, which is the only reason we can stick to the heart healthy Mediterranean diet we aim for most days (I have really high genetically determined cholesterol). Their wild king salmon is our favorite but we’ve never gotten anything bad from there. As an owner-operator myself I am fascinated by the tiny decisions that make a business run well and Fish Tales’ recent relocation is a shining example of present and thoughtful business ownership.

Earlier this year Fish Tales relocated to a bigger spot just a few storefronts away from the original location. What I saw was a small business owner taking over the reins from his father and modernizing the business as they expand and optimize. I am writing from my own perspective as a fellow business owner with a shop a few blocks down and also as a long time customer. I did not interview John or Chris for this piece, I’m just so happy to see a family business evolve like this. In my last post I wrote about how important it is to be present in your restaurant so you know what the challenges and bottlenecks are in order to improve them. This relocation and expansion makes it clear to me that both Chris and John are like-minded business owners in the sense that they are actually at the store working the same job as their staff. I know for a fact that Chris is often in the shop because I buy fish from him several times a week.

### The Old Fish Tales

It was a small shop and sometimes it would get very crowded and there could be some confusion about the line. For example, someone could be waiting to have their fresh shrimp cleaned and peeled and the person behind them might not know it’s their turn to order. Bottleneck. This is a small thing but over time it becomes an annoyance for the staff, having to manage bodies in a space. Especially around the holidays it would get very crowded. In the new store there is more space and there are stanchions that clearly communicate where you are supposed to order and form a line. These tiny operational issues accumulate and the new layout directly solves them.

In the old space they had a limited prepared foods menu. My husband sometimes got fish tacos or a lobster roll if he was already going there to pick something up for dinner later on. It wasn’t necessarily a lunch destination but a nice bonus that you could get something to eat for lunch. I’ve noticed that they’ve expanded their lunch menu and it’s more obvious to the consumer that it is an offering because of the screens showing the menu behind the fish display. Before it sort of felt like IYKYK. There are also prepared dinners that you can pop into the oven and have dinner on the table with little to no effort or clean up. So smart! They know their customer is short on time and will pay a premium for convenience.

### The New Fish Tales

I also noticed they changed their POS system to Toast. I don’t know if they were on Clover or something similar before but Toast makes sense for them as they have a lot of inventory to keep track of (lots of SKUs, perishables, items sold by weight). Toast has a lot of functionality and allows you to keep track of minutiae. Personally, I prefer Square for our Breakfast restaurants because we have a very simple menu and not a lot of inventory. We use Toast for our West Coast operations. I find it unnecessarily difficult to use and too robust for our needs. Simplicity is at the core of our business as well as being a foundational pillar for me personally.

I also noticed upgrades to the security system, the sound system, the payment system. Chris clearly thought about what he needed to modernize the business to make it both easier to operate and a more efficient experience for the customer. I can confirm that it is faster now to buy my dinner!

Why am I writing about one hyper specific shop in Brooklyn? Because it’s a beautiful example of an owner at work, responding to real challenges and making them better. This wasn’t someone who just hired an architect and said make it bigger, make it better! These upgrades were very thoughtful and very smart for the business. I find it interesting to see what was a priority for them besides having slightly more space. It may be confusing to some as to why a shop would relocate just a few doors down– it is expensive and it is a lot of work but it is clear that this was an investment in the longevity of the business. I commend Fish Tales on a meaningful relocation/expansion.

And now for a very easy dinner I make at least once a week featuring wild king salmon!

*\*Important note on any recipes I share: I am not a cook and I don’t really like to cook. I cook out of utility and necessity with a focus on nutrition and ease. I like a one pot situation or a simple protein, veg and carb. I’m very approximate with my measurements.*

This is my go to salmon recipe:

1.  Put rice in rice cooker (you must get one if you don’t have one, you will never have mealy or undercooked rice again!).
    
2.  Marinate salmon in 1 tsp miso, a couple splashes of mirin, couple splashes of soy, glug of sesame oil.
    
3.  Set broiler to high, roast for about 7-10min.
    
4.  While you’re doing that sauté some greens (I prefer broccoli or bok choy from the farmers market if possible) in garlic, salt and EVOO.
    

It’s SO GOOD and easy and healthy! Tell me if you try it.
