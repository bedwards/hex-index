### **SpaceX updates**

Given Boeing’s Starliner doesn’t seem to be ready anytime soon to take a crew to the ISS, SpaceX has **[been awarded three more commercial crew mission contracts.](https://spacenews.com/nasa-awards-spacex-three-additional-commercial-crew-missions)**[](https://spacenews.com/nasa-awards-spacex-three-additional-commercial-crew-missions)

Both BocaChica and Florida Starbases have been making a lot of progress, including multiple cryotests to the Starship (see the video below)

And also **[new infrastructure upgrades on both bases](https://www.teslarati.com/spacex-starbase-starship-factory-tent-era-over/)**.

With last week’s launch, they have already launched **[nine Falcon 9 rockets in nine weeks](https://www.teslarati.com/spacex-nine-launches-nine-weeks-2022/)**: nice figure! 🙂

### **Rocket Lab & tons of sats!**

SpaceX is not the only company speeding up its launch cadence. Rocket Lab successfully launched its mission **["](https://www.nasaspaceflight.com/2022/02/the-owls-night-continues/)*****[The Owl’s Night Continues](https://www.nasaspaceflight.com/2022/02/the-owls-night-continues/)**”* from their new launchpad. This second launchpad allows them to **launch a rocket every three days**. Yeah, you heard it right: they can now launch 10 rockets a month. Wow! Let’s see if there’s enough demand for them to max out this investment.  

These weren’t the only successes of the week. **[ULA’s Atlas 5](https://spacenews.com/atlas-5-launches-goes-t-weather-satellite/)** successfully launched GOES-18, a satellite that will be used to monitor the weather, and China has also launched **[seven new commercial satellites](https://english.news.cn/20220305/423a07a728384c4d92b6110088567506/c.html)** last week. 

And this all makes us think: how are we going to make this sustainable in space? Well, hopefully, there are many companies exploring in-orbit satellite servicing. Northrop Grumman announced they are going to launch a **[new satellite-servicing mission in 2024](https://spacenews.com/northrop-grumman-to-launch-new-satellite-servicing-mission-in-2024/).** I don’t know if you’ve seen this video explaining their mission, but it is certainly impressive. I recommend watching it! 

### **More progress and observations!**

NASA has begun the assembly of **[Europa Clipper](https://www.jpl.nasa.gov/news/nasa-begins-assembly-of-europa-clipper-spacecraft)**, which is scheduled to start its journey to visit Jupiter’s icy moon Europa in 2024.

A black hole eating another black hole? Yes! Look at this illustration of an event a group of astronomers recently identified. The small one seems to be approaching the big one in **[a course to collide with it.](https://www.jpl.nasa.gov/news/astronomers-find-two-giant-black-holes-spiraling-toward-a-collision)**[](https://www.jpl.nasa.gov/news/astronomers-find-two-giant-black-holes-spiraling-toward-a-collision)
