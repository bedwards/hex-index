### **NASA’s getting nervous…**

Now that NASA is getting nervous about the end of the ISS, they have asked the commercial companies working on commercial stations if they could go faster with additional funding… but there are things money can’t buy, and [](https://spacenews.com/additional-funding-unlikely-to-accelerate-commercial-space-station-projects/)**[it seems most of those companies agree these things take time](https://spacenews.com/additional-funding-unlikely-to-accelerate-commercial-space-station-projects/)**. 9 Women Can't Make a Baby in a Month :)

Something that they want to foster is healthy competition amongst the commercial players, so they have decided is that they **[will fund a second company to work on a Lunar Lander](https://spacenews.com/nasa-to-support-development-of-second-artemis-lunar-lander/)** [](https://spacenews.com/nasa-to-support-development-of-second-artemis-lunar-lander/)so that they can compete against SpaceX as part of the Artemis mission. Blue Origin? Dynetics? We’ll see who this money is for

### **SpaceX’s innovation**

SpaceX did something very cool this week. Althought this has yet to be confirmed, it seems it added a **[Starlink satellites dispenser to the Starship prototype](https://www.teslarati.com/spacex-starship-s24-starlink-dispenser/)**. Interesting! This means they are already testing ways for the Starship to start delivering satellites on their trips to orbit. We don’t know if that prototype will even fly yet, but this is the kind of innovation and quick iteration that is blowing the industry’s minds. Go, SpaceX!

### **A new cosmic map maker**

Progress on the plans to launch the next cosmic map maker! **[NASA’s SPHEREx](https://www.jpl.nasa.gov/news/nasa-finalizes-plans-for-its-next-cosmic-mapmaker)** will be able to scan the entire sky every six months and create a map of the cosmos. Launch date, April 2025. Nice!

### **And a new martian drone!**

Sener Aerospacial is going to build a martian drone for the ESA! Following the steps of NASA’s Ingenuity and everything we’ve learned from this experience, this company **[has been awarded a contract to develop](https://www.aerospacemanufacturinganddesign.com/article/sener-aerosacial-aerdron-develop-flying-mars-rover/)** the next flying machine for the red planet.

### **Successful spacewalk**

ISS astronauts Raja Chari and Matthias Maurer successfully performed the final spacewalk of **[Expedition 66.](https://www.nasaspaceflight.com/2022/03/us-eva-80/)**

### **JWST, one step closer**

All 18 mirrors of the James Webb Space Telescope **[are now correctly aligned!](https://www.nasaspaceflight.com/2022/03/james-webb-align-mirror/)** This is a very important milestone for the telescope.
