### **Martian updates** 

This week, a lot of activity on Mars! NASA's **Curiosity** Rover **[measures intriguing carbon signature on Mars](https://mars.nasa.gov/news/9113/nasas-curiosity-rover-measures-intriguing-carbon-signature-on-mars/)**. Scientists are trying to look for hypotheses that rule out the biological option (ie: how can this be produced without stating there’s life on Mars?), but they need more data, experiments and time to find the right explanation. Interesting! 

NASA’s **Perseverance** Rove**[r solved the problem it had last week with some rocks that were obstructing](https://mars.nasa.gov/mars2020/mission/status/359/ejecting-mars-pebbles/)** where it needed to put the samples of rocks it was collecting. Well done! 

Also on Mars, NASA's **InSight** **[enters safe mode during regional Mars dust storm](https://mars.nasa.gov/news/9110/nasas-insight-enters-safe-mode-during-regional-mars-dust-storm/)**. Nothing to worry about, though. This is normal, as a way to save energy, and when the storm clears out, the team checks how it has impacted the solar arrays. 

In the meantime, the European martian robot is [](https://spacenews.com/exomars-on-schedule-for-september-launch/)**[on schedule for a launch in September](https://spacenews.com/exomars-on-schedule-for-september-launch/)**. **ExoMars** is on track to be launched on a Proton rocket on September 20th, according to the European Space Agency. 

### **Mission updates** 

This week **Webb** space [](https://earthsky.org/space/james-webb-space-telescope-30-days-of-terror/)**[telescope will arrive at L2, its final destination](https://earthsky.org/space/james-webb-space-telescope-30-days-of-terror/)**. From there it’ll get prepared to start its scientific mission. Also this week, the closest asteroid to Earth **[in two centuries](https://www.clickorlando.com/news/local/2022/01/18/closest-asteroid-to-earth-in-next-2-centuries-to-fly-by-tuesday-nasa-says/)** [](https://www.clickorlando.com/news/local/2022/01/18/closest-asteroid-to-earth-in-next-2-centuries-to-fly-by-tuesday-nasa-says/)will fly by next Tuesday. What a week! 🙂 

Talking about L2, that’s als**[o the destination of ESA’s Plato mission](https://www.esa.int/Science_Exploration/Space_Science/Plato_exoplanet_mission_gets_green_light_for_next_phase)**, which this same week got the green light to continue after a critical review. This European mission will launch in 2026 and the spacecraft will use its 26 cameras to look for exoplanets. So exciting!

### **Commercial space** 

Can you imagine using a Starship to take cargo and troops anywhere on Earth in a matter of minutes? This week **SpaceX**, which passed the **[2000 Starlink satellites launched mark](https://spacenews.com/spacex-passes-2000-starlink-satellites-launched/)**, won a +$100 million Air Force contract [to demonstrate technologies for point-to-point space transportation](https://spacenews.com/spacex-wins-102-million-air-force-contract-to-demonstrate-technologies-for-point-to-point-space-transportation/). How amazing would that be? 

**OneWeb**, the owner of another satellite mega-constellation, has signed a s**[ix-year contract to bring internet to India](https://spacenews.com/oneweb-forges-indian-distribution-partnership/)**. 

**Sony** is launching this year a CubeSat with [](https://spacenews.com/sony-putting-camera-in-orbit-for-shutterbugs-not-scientists/)**[a camera for artists and educators](https://spacenews.com/sony-putting-camera-in-orbit-for-shutterbugs-not-scientists/)** (not for scientists or the army this time!). There’s space for all!
