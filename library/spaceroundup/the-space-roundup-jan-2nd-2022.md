### Moon updates

**[Passive thermal control for the moon!](https://spacenews.com/nasa-funds-thermal-control-solutions-for-harsh-lunar-environments/)** [](https://spacenews.com/nasa-funds-thermal-control-solutions-for-harsh-lunar-environments/)NASA has funded a company called Advanced Cooling Technologies with $5M to research ways to protect landers, rovers, and habitats from the extreme cold and heat of the moon without energy consumption. Currently, we need nuclear-based batteries to sustain thermal protection systems, but we’d need more efficient technologies if we want them to be sustainable. 

China and Russia are planning to sign a legally binding agreement to build an international **[scientific lunar outpost by 2035](https://www.globaltimes.cn/page/202112/1243731.shtml)**. Very very interesting. While the US focuses on the lunar orbit for a permanent base (the Gateway), they want to go directly to the surface of the moon. Will they be able to achieve it? We’ll see!

### SpaceX

On the last day of the year, [](https://www.teslarati.com/spacex-2021-almost-2-billion-new-funding/)**[SpaceX raised $330 million](https://www.teslarati.com/spacex-2021-almost-2-billion-new-funding/)**, which takes the total funding of the year to approximately $1.85 billion. Starship development is expensive! 

Apart from being expensive to develop, it seems bureaucracy is also NOT helping a lot. **[The Federal Aviation Administration is postponing the permissions](https://spacenews.com/faa-delays-completion-of-starship-environmental-review/)** because it needs at least two more months to complete an environmental review of Starship orbital launches from Boca Chica. That means more delays. Sad 

### ISS, good news!

The good news is that after all discussions and public speculation, the Biden administration has extended operations on the **[International Space Station through 2030](https://www.upi.com/Top_News/US/2021/12/31/biden-administration-extends-iss-operations-2030/2361640984601/)**. That gives the US eight additional years while they consider transitioning to other models. Nice. Remember they have recently started funding companies to explore the development of commercial stations in space.

### **The year ahead**

Watch out for 2022! This year is going to bring us amazing missions.

Some of the missions that are the most exciting to me include the first **SLS** and **Starship** flights, **JWST** operations, China and India's **solar** observatories, and the first moon landings of the **CLIPS** (Commercial Lunar Payload Services Program) program.

But not only NASA is going to the moon: it seems everyone wants to go to the moon this year! The list of countries sending probes, rovers, robots, landers is amazing: India, Russia, UAE, Japan, Korea, Ukrania… the year of the moon?

There are also several amazing deep space missions, such as **Psyche**, **Juice**, **ExoMars**, and **DART’s** arrival and collision. What’s your favorite mission?

I think all these missions (and more I didn’t mention) are going to have us super excited about our progress as a species and will keep amazing us with new discoveries. I hope I’ll be there to share all these updates with you week after week 🙂
