> I thought it might be cool for your readers to know that Johnny Marks — writer of some of America’s most prolific holiday songs — was [Jewish](https://18doors.org/shining-a-light-on-the-largely-untold-story-of-the-origins-of-rudolph-the-red-nosed-reindeer/). > I first came upon the name “Annapolis” in Tom Clancy’s book *The Hunt for Red October*. Later, after finishing all the Clancy novels (including those that were made into movies: *Patriot Games*, *Clear and Present Danger*, and *The Sum of All Fears*, and some that were not, like *Cardinal of the Kremlin*), I learned so much about the city and the US Naval Academy. For Clancy readers, “he was from Annapolis” carries clear meanings: someone thoroughly trained, drenched in naval pride and lore, career-oriented, and lineage-tagged just like West Point and Quantico serving as codewords for the character and tradition of other professional networks.
