American record producer

Andy Wallace (born 1947) is an American record producer and audio and mixing engineer.[1][2] Over the years, he focused exclusively on mixing.[3] Wallace is described as "legendary"[4] in the recording industry and has worked on many best-selling and influential records, mainly in heavy metal, alternative rock, grunge and other styles of rock and roll, including with Nirvana, Linkin Park, System of a Down, Slayer, Avenged Sevenfold, Sepultura, and Jeff Buckley. Wallace won a Grammy for his work on The Globe Sessions (1999) by Sheryl Crow.

Career

[edit]

Wallace was born in New Jersey.[5] From the 1970s he worked as a musician, playing bass guitar and singing backup vocals; his favorite music as a performer was "old R&B" inspired by Stax Records and Little Richard.[4] He began his recording career as a chief engineer in his own studio in Los Angeles.[6] After a few years of moderate success, he returned to New York City during the emergence of hip-hop in the early 1980s.[6]

Following the breakout success of the 1986 production of the Run-DMC–Aerosmith collaboration on "Walk This Way" with Rick Rubin,[6] Wallace went on to work with The Cult, Slayer, Prince, Bruce Springsteen, Sepultura, Nirvana, Jeff Buckley, Sonic Youth, Rage Against the Machine, Guns N' Roses, Linkin Park, Paul McCartney, Avenged Sevenfold, and many others.[2]

In February 1999, Wallace shared a Grammy Award for Best Engineered Album, Non-Classical with Tchad Blake and Trina Shoemaker, for their work on Sheryl Crow's album The Globe Sessions.[7]

As of May 2021, over 120 million album units had been sold worldwide that contained a credit to Wallace.[1][8]
