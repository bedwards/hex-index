The Janitor's Secret Universe

When Henry Darger died in 1973, one day after his eighty-first birthday, his landlords went to clean out his small Chicago apartment. What they found was staggering: a handwritten novel spanning over fifteen thousand pages, hundreds of paintings some stretching more than ten feet wide, and an autobiography that somehow veered into thousands of pages about a fictional tornado. This reclusive hospital janitor, who had lived in near-total poverty and spoken to almost no one for decades, had secretly created one of the most extensive artistic works of the twentieth century.

Nobody knew.
