HOOT → Deadline: Nov 27 | Fee: Yes | Pay: No | Open for Poetry | ☑︎ Sim Subs | ☑︎ Some Reprints | R: 180 days | A: 16.33% | 3K+ followers | United States | 2011 — “HOOT is a postcard. A very nice-looking one. With writing on it! It is also a little more complicated than that. It is a brief, displayable, shareable literary magazine.”

Our one cent: I’m a sucker for good print lit mags and while HOOT also has a stellar digital presence, their print idea is so cool! They send out monthly print issues on a literal postcard. How freakin’ awesome is that? Honestly, this is the kind of sweet and out of the box innovations I’d love to see more of in lit mag land.

Folklore Review | Theme: Winter Solstice → Deadline: Nov 28 | Fee: No | Pay: No | Open for Poetry, Fiction | ☑︎ Sim Subs | ✖️ Reprints | R: 42 days | A: 9% | 2K+ followers | United States | 2023 — “Speculative-ish literary magazine that specializes in pieces based on folklore.”

Our one cent: Now, I do love all things folklore but that’s not the only reason I’ve handpicked this one. I’m absolutely in love with their design, art choices, and the beautiful work they publish. One thing they do very well is make the work easy to read, something that, unfortunately, isn’t as common even in well-funded bigwig mags.

Susurrus → Deadline: Nov 30 | Fee: No | Pay: No | Open for Nonfiction, Poetry, Fiction, Multimedia | ☑︎ Sim Subs | ☑︎ Some Reprints | R: 30 days | A: 19.34% | 3K+ followers | United States | 2021 — “An online literary arts magazine for artists from the American South”

Our one cent: A pandemic-era lit mag that’s lasted and grown! They’ve won awards already and you can easily read the anthologies they’re contributors have been a part of. On top of that, they’re quite accessible when it comes to acceptance rate, so if you have work ready to go and need a bit of a validation boost, consider subbing here!

Rattle | Theme: Ekphrastic Challenge → Deadline: Nov 30 | Fee: No | Pay: $50-$200 flat | Open for Poetry, Multimedia | ☑︎ Sim Subs | ✖️ Reprints | R: 180 days | A: 1% | 60K+ followers | United States | 1995 — “Our mission is to promote the practice of poetry,”

Our one cent: An ancient (well, in lit mag time anyway) mag that just gets more relevant and exciting by the year. I can always count on Rattle to always have a relatable and readable poem and now on their newly redesigned stunning website! They’re currently open for the monthly ekphrastic challenge so in case you need some inspiration, check out their featured art and write a poem responding to it in some way!

New Orleans Review → Deadline: Nov 30 | Fee: Some | Pay: $50-$500 flat | Open for Nonfiction, Fiction, Poetry, Multimedia | ☑︎ Sim Subs | ✖️ Reprints | R: 180 days | A: <1% | 8K+ followers | United States | 1968 — “A journal of contemporary literature and culture, New Orleans Review is a publication of the Department of English at Loyola University New Orleans, and is partially funded by the Kendall Michelle Daigle Memorial Endowment for English.”

Our one cent: A consistently top-notch uni-backed mag that’s published everyone from Pablo Neruda to Joyce Carol Oates to Anne Dillard and more! But don’t let that discourage you, they still love work from emerging writers and are, in fact, of the best career-building mags for young writers!

Contemporary Verse 2 → Deadline: Nov 30 | Fee: CA$3 | Pay: Yes | Open for Poetry | ☑︎ Sim Subs | ✖️ Reprints | R: 180 days | A: 21.33% | 8K+ followers | Canada | 1975 — “The mandate and primary aim of Contemporary Verse 2 is to educate, engage, and expand public appreciation of the poetic art form by sharing and promoting high-calibre, original verse and critical writing by local, national, and international poets.”

Our one cent: A Canadian mag that loves work from folks all over the world. And though they’re focused strictly on poetry, they love hybrid and experimental stuff. CV2 is one of the leading lit mags in Canada so if you want to get some acclaim from the land of maple syrup and moose and beavers and free healthcare, submit!

Poet Lore → Deadline: Nov 30 | Fee: No | Pay: $50/piece or $50 flat | Open for Poetry | ☑︎ Sim Subs | ✖️ Reprints | R: 180 days | A: 3.65% | 7K+ followers | United States | 1889 — “America’s oldest poetry magazine, publishing in print since 1889. Poet Lore is a biannual print journal of poetry, featuring the finest in contemporary writing. We love featuring poems that broaden the spectrum of what poetry is – and can be – on the page.”

Our one cent: I’ve had a great experience publishing with them personally and I also got the chance to meet one of the editors in person at AWP (she was lovely!!). Their print issues are utterly stunning and I can tell you they have an excellent readership from personal experience! If you want you work read, nominated for prizes, and just beautifully printed and shared online, then send in your poems now!
