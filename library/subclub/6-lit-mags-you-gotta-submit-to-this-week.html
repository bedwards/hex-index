The Bowery Review: NY’s Best and Only Humor Magazine | Theme: Funny Short Stories → Deadline: Nov 28 | Fee: No | Pay: No | Open for Fiction | ☑︎ Sim Subs | ✖️ Reprints | R: 2 days | 300+ followers — “The Bowery Review is looking for funny short stories, poems, cartoons, gags, and jokes! Please send us what you’ve got to theboweryreview@gmail.com.”

Our one cent: This is a brand new lit mag focused on funny stuff—what’s not to love? Started by Tim Latterner who was a former writer at MAD, National Lampoon, CollegeHumor and Playboy, I feel like we can certainly expect cool things from this mag. Get in first before they blow up!

Prosetrics The Literary Magazine | Theme: Aurora → Deadline: Dec 31 | Fee: No | Pay: No | Open for Fiction, Multimedia, Poetry | ☑︎ Sim Subs | ✖️ Reprints | R: 14 days | A: 6% | 1.5K+ followers | Netherlands | 2022 — “An independent literary magazine that publishes poets, writers, artists, both traditional and digital, and photographers.”

Our one cent: Another beautiful new-ish lit mag publishing across genres, they are fully self-funded and do not do any ads or sponsorships. I must say I have a soft spot for mags that have these kind of ethos and I’m always rooting with extra gusto for their success! Prosetrics is both print and digital and they’re currently open for a themed call coming out in the New Year. Send you word evoking “the quiet architecture of winter” with “moments of inwardness”.

Neon and Smoke → Deadline: Jan 19, 2026 | Fee: No | Pay: $20 flat | Open for Fiction, Multimedia | ☑︎ Sim Subs | ✖️ Reprints | R: 7 days | A: 10% | Under 100 followers | United States | 2025 — “We publish bold, boundary-blurring fiction, poetry, and art—beautifully strange, darkly funny, or achingly human. Genre with a literary edge: speculative, horror, suspense, surreal, or hybrids welcome. $20 payment for fiction. Sim subs okay.”

Our one cent: I’ve only just noticed I’ve got 3 new mags in a row, and y’know what, it’s not my fault!! There are just so many cool new lit mags landing on the same every day, it feels like, which is a wonderful thing that ought to be celebrated so there. Neon and Smoke in particular stands out because they want to “blur the line between literary and genre, between what’s real and what almost is.” Send them you weird little mystical pieces, folks!

The Maine Review → Deadline: Nov 20 | Fee: $3 | Pay: $25-$50 per piece | Open for Multimedia, Poetry, Fiction, Nonfiction | ☑︎ Sim Subs | ✖️ Reprints | R: 186 days | A: 3.52% | 2K+ followers | United States | 2014 — “A biannual online literary journal that publishes culturally significant and innovative writing by writers living in Maine, across the country, and around the world.”

Our one cent: Alright, time for some of the more established baddies. The Maine Review is an award-winning lit mag publishing acclaimed writers alongside new ones. Though they do love writing around “Maine heritage” they want work from all over the world!

Horizon Review → Deadline: Nov 24 | Fee: No | Pay: $25/piece | Open for Fiction, Poetry, Nonfiction, Multimedia | ☑︎ Sim Subs | ✖️ Reprints | R: 60 days | 100+ followers | United States | 2025 — “Introducing Horizon Review, a new literature and fine arts magazine dedicated to serving the Southwest. Started under our parent company, FUSION, Horizon Review publishes literature, arts, and open media from artists in the Southwest.”

Our one cent: Back to my brand new lit mag fetish, sorry not sorry. Horizon Review is currently open for their inaugural issue. They’re connected to FUSION, a theatre and arts company and a a center for arts and culture in Downtown Albuquerque.

frankie magazine | Fee: No | Pay: No | Open for Nonfiction, Multimedia | ✖️ Sim Subs | ✖️ Reprints | 30K+ followers | Australia — “The frankie ‘personality’ is funny, friendly, smart, cute, rude, naughty, interested, serious and caring. frankie readers expect the unexpected – if you can imagine your idea running in another Australian publication, then you’ll have to re-think your pitch.”

Our one cent: Bit of a curve ball of sorts for y’all, but I couldn’t resist—just go and see how gorgeous their issues look and how aesthetic their website is, oof wow! They’re open to pitches from writers “who can provide sharp-eyed perspectives and narratives, bursting with personal insight and wit.”
