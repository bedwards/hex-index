Hey y’all,

Last month I was reminiscing about shopping at [Borders](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Borders_\(retailer\)) around the turn of the century. In particular, I was thinking about their sign:

[

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tYZ_!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1cf2238c-f8ea-4e25-a9e5-a0e59fe450b3_453x368.heic)



](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!tYZ_!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1cf2238c-f8ea-4e25-a9e5-a0e59fe450b3_453x368.heic)

*Books, music, movies.* (I didn’t remember the “Cafe” part. Probably because I didn’t drink coffee back then!) Those are still the art forms I care the most about — the ones you used to be able to go out and hold in your hands for around $20.[1](#footnote-1)

I thought it’d be fun to start a new series where we take an imaginary visit to Borders and I select one favorite book, album, and movie based on a theme.

Today’s theme: **Radioactivity.**
