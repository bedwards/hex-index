3.  “In a world that’s on fire, what do we do with art? Like, what can music actually do?” I am a gigantic fan of [Fela Kuti](https://felakuti.bandcamp.com), so I’ve been looking forward to Jad Abumrad’s podcast series, *[Fela Kuti: Fear No Man](https://www.npr.org/2025/10/15/nx-s1-5539126/fela-kuti-podcast-fear-no-man)*. I finally listened to the first two episodes — on headphones! the mix is great! — and I’m hooked. (Favorite thing I’ve learned so far: allegedly, Fela’s band auditioned to be the house band on Disneyland’s Jungle Cruise ride… but they were deemed not African enough!)

6.  “I consider that a man’s brain originally is like [a little empty attic](https://www.goodreads.com/quotes/227359-i-consider-that-a-man-s-brain-originally-is-like-a), and you have to stock it with such furniture as you choose.” To give my brain-attic a rest, I’ve been reading a bunch of Arthur Conan Doyle’s [Sherlock Holmes](https://standardebooks.org/collections/sherlock-holmes) stories. I read *[A Study in Scarlet](https://standardebooks.org/ebooks/arthur-conan-doyle/a-study-in-scarlet)*, I’m now reading *[The Sign of the Four](https://standardebooks.org/ebooks/arthur-conan-doyle/the-sign-of-the-four)*, and I’m hoping to move on to *[The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes](https://standardebooks.org/ebooks/arthur-conan-doyle/the-adventures-of-sherlock-holmes)*, which is made up of the short stories that ran in *[The Strand Magazine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Strand_Magazine)* and made the character famous.  
      
    
7.  Japanese Chill: I [mentioned](https://substack.com/@austinkleon/note/c-178594124?) I wanted to read a book that had the same vibe as [Hiroshi Yoshimura’s music](https://hiroshiyoshimura.bandcamp.com/music) or the movie *[Perfect Days](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt27503384/)*. More than one person recommended Nantsume Sōseki’s 1906 novel, *[Kusamakura](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kusamakura_\(novel\))*. The story is about an artist who retreats to a hot springs hotel in the mountains and meets a mysterious woman. (A fun complement to Sherlock, as the narrator spends much of his monologue discussing art-making and seems to have it out for detectives! “Once you start asking why, it all turns into detective work,” he says. “The usual novels are all invented by detectives… they’re utterly boring.”)
