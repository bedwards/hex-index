1.  “I am so *bored* by A.I. One of the things I love about the theater is: A.I. can’t do it. I couldn’t be less interested in computers and fake things. I like people. 2.  As much as I am personally offended on multiple levels — creative, spiritual, political, ethical, moral… you name it! — by A.I. and the tech oligarchs who push it on us, I am also with my friend [Alan Jacobs](https://blog.ayjay.org/the-last-one-of-these/): “[Everyone knows](https://blog.ayjay.org/everyone-knows/). [Everyone](https://blog.ayjay.org/thats-still-how-it-goes-everybody-still-knows/). We really don’t need any more stories explaining what people are doing to themselves.”   
      
    What I *am* really interested in is how A.I. is pushing people to articulate what they love about art, like Ethan Hein did in his recent post, “[AI slop predates AI](https://ethanhein.substack.com/p/ai-slop-predates-ai)”:  
      
    “I love 1980s hip-hop because there’s no way to predict it from projecting trends in 1970s pop. It questions basic assumptions! It’s missing elements that you might have thought were necessary (like melodies) and it brings in elements you didn’t know you wanted until you heard them (like samples and turntable scratching.) Now the 1980s rap exists, it’s easy to feed it into an AI as training data and get more 1980s rap, but there’s no way that AI could have produced 1980s rap if it was only trained on 1970s pop.”  
      
    This is a long-standing obsession of mine: how often it’s what humans *leave out* — by design or by necessity — that makes their art *not* average. ([Because I’m out here looking for what](https://austinkleon.substack.com/p/10-scraps-from-my-notebook) *[isn’t](https://austinkleon.substack.com/p/10-scraps-from-my-notebook)* [average](https://austinkleon.substack.com/p/10-scraps-from-my-notebook).)

5.  Music documentary: The great [Penny Lane](https://lennypane.substack.com/about) sent me a sneak peek of her new documentary about kids’ music, *[Happy and You Know It](https://people.com/new-documentary-explores-how-terminally-uncool-childrens-music-became-sleeper-hits-11864940)*, which premieres on HBO on Christmas Day. (I watched it with my 13-year-old, who was a big fan of Penny’s other music doc, *[Listening to Kenny G](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mmgg5XObNl8)*.) I loved seeing [Chris Ballew](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chris_Ballew) (aka [Caspar Babypants](https://babypantsmusic.com/home)) in his PNW element, strumming a 3-string uke in front of a fire and hanging out with his wife, the artist [Kate Endle](https://www.kateendle.com). (I was reminded how much [I loved his interview with Chase Jarvis](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=w6XHp5BE8dE).) I also enjoyed the thoughtful commentary of critics [Rob Harvilla](https://robharvilla.com/the-podcast/) and [Willa Paskin](https://slate.com/podcasts/decoder-ring) — I want to listen to their podcasts! (Do check out [Penny’s newsletter](https://lennypane.substack.com) — she writes honestly about what it’s like to make art and documentaries right now. Maybe start with her recent posts about [marketing and making trailers](https://lennypane.substack.com/p/trailer-drop) and [not getting into Sundance](https://lennypane.substack.com/p/i-didnt-get-into-sundance-again).)  
      
    
6.  Music book: I knew from his newsletter *[Another Thought](https://anotherthoughtanotherthought.substack.com)* that I was teed up to like Dusty Henry’s [33 1/3](https://www.bloomsbury.com/us/series/33-13/) entry, *[20th Century Ambient](https://geni.us/8qQAsiA)*, but I was delighted by how expansive he was in telling the story of ambient music, bringing in some of my favorites like [Satie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erik_Satie), [King Tubby](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/King_Tubby), and [Yellow Magic Orchestra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yellow_Magic_Orchestra). (If you’re into this kind of thing, see also: David Toop’s *[Ocean of Sound](https://geni.us/7zfa)*, Geeta Dayal’s *[Another Green World](https://tumblr.austinkleon.com/post/132034324916)*, and Michael Veal’s *[Dub](https://geni.us/NRtRtAL)*.)
