[Hey y’all](https://www.instagram.com/reel/CJUISYon2xa/),

Here are 10 things I thought were worth sharing this week:

1.  My 13-year-old coder spotted a copy of Cory Doctorow’s *[Enshittification](https://geni.us/Yp66y)* on our coffee table and got a big grin on his face. I said, “You can read it if you want!” and he proceeded to do so. I asked him if he’d like to write a review for the newsletter. Like his old man, he likes to keep it brief: “A humorous, engaging, and political guide to the mishaps of the Internet. Frequent Internet users should definitely read this book.” (Here’s [a recent interview with Doctorow](https://www.honest-broker.com/p/the-honest-broker-launches-an-interview).)  
      
    
2.  I started reading [Julian Gough](https://www.juliangough.com) almost 20 years ago when I came across his wonderful [essay on comedy vs. tragedy](https://www.prospectmagazine.co.uk/essays/57826/divine-comedy). For the past three years, Julian has been writing his next book in public — *[show your work!](https://austinkleon.com/show-your-work)* — using his newsletter, *[The Egg and The Rock](https://theeggandtherock.com)*. “A non-fiction exploration of the strange fact that our universe, in its development, behaves far more like an egg than a rock.” Like most of [the physics books](https://geni.us/FQ498) I like to read, I barely understand his “[Blowtorch Theory](https://theeggandtherock.com/p/the-blowtorch-theory-a-new-model)” model of structural formation in the universe, but I find his ideas and his enthusiasm for them quite thrilling. If you’d like to dip in, a great start is [this podcast](https://theeggandtherock.com/p/a-splendid-overview-of-the-current). Here are a few of my notes doodled while listening:

3.  “I look at my life with a sense of humility because I cannot honestly take credit for any of it. You know, it just happened.” I saw a [short clip](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o1MVAOIKzI0) of actor Anthony Hopkins promoting [his new memoir](https://geni.us/xvJNAly) on *[The Late Show with Stephen Colbert](https://www.cbs.com/shows/video/MGsAL2ExnHMpLCKhL90n_wObOIdJ5Mfv/)* and had to watch the whole thing. (Don’t miss him [reciting](https://www.instagram.com/reel/DQdJw79Effv/) T.S. Eliot’s “[The Love Song of J. Alfred Prufrock](https://www.poetryfoundation.org/poetrymagazine/poems/44212/the-love-song-of-j-alfred-prufrock).”) Makes me want to memorize my [favorite poems](https://austinkleon.substack.com/p/fridge-poems), practice [the piano](https://www.instagram.com/reel/CmCa8vrLUz9/), and maybe even grow old!  
      
    
4.  The next day I thought, “Oh, maybe I’ll re-watch *[The Silence of the Lambs](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0102926/)* and answer some emails.” I got sucked in again by [the opening titles](https://www.artofthetitle.com/title/the-silence-of-the-lambs/), and two hours later, I had answered zero emails. That night I started (and spent the weekend finishing) [the Thomas Harris novel](https://geni.us/DKC3) for the 3rd time. (If I ever get invited to [The Criterion Closet](https://www.criterion.com/closet-picks?srsltid=AfmBOoqOIVYLPIXK-ZzxxxDbfKOUe2LJHMRLuVlfTX5vuuWc1BsHo7FC), the [Blu-Ray](https://www.criterion.com/films/528-the-silence-of-the-lambs) is the first thing I’ll choose — the [behind-the-scenes featurette](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XvnRrWUpvMc) is quite good!)  
      
    
5.  I stole a line from [Bob Dylan’s “Mississippi”](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GZFB6ZhtGOg) for my November mixtape:
    
    There are two [Geese](https://geeseband.com) songs on there because I love those kids. I won’t gush again like I did [last week](https://austinkleon.substack.com/p/even-the-moons-frightened), but Meg and I saw them live a few nights ago and they were great. A band that can pull off covering [The Stooges](https://www.stereogum.com/2328489/geese-cover-the-velvet-undergound-and-the-stooges-in-san-diego/news/) *and* [New Radicals](https://pitchfork.com/news/geese-cover-new-radicals-you-get-what-you-give-watch/)? Hell yeah.

6.  Animated [pizza night](https://austinkleon.substack.com/t/pizza-night): We all liked the stop-motion of *[ParaNorman](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1623288/)* (2012) and the kids like *[The](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0101178/)* [](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0101178/)*[Ren & Stimpy Show](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0101178/)* so much I went ahead and got the complete collection for [cheap on DVD](https://geni.us/DFavNO). (While watching, we got to revisit the concept of the “[Gross Up](https://austinkleon.com/2020/12/29/the-art-of-the-gross-up/).”)  
      
    
7.  Austin, TX: I plan on spending a lot of time on my bike this weekend checking out [Austin Studio Tour](https://www.atxstudiotour.com) and [Texas Book Festival](https://texasbookfestival.org). Friday night, [Valerie Fowler’s](https://www.instagram.com/p/DQrtuYzAGFa/?hl=en) having an open studio. Saturday, some great YA authors are coming: [R.L. Stine](https://texasbookfestival.org/directory/author/r-l-stine/) (“Who‘d want to write for adults? [Horrible audience](https://www.instagram.com/p/DQhr7YDkaSJ).”), [Jason Reynolds](https://texasbookfestival.org/directory/author/jason-reynolds/), [Peter Brown](https://texasbookfestival.org/directory/author/peter-brown/), [Nathan Hale](https://texasbookfestival.org/directory/author/nathan-hale/) (my kids LOVE his stuff), and more. Sunday, I’d like to see [Dan Chaon](https://texasbookfestival.org/directory/author/dan-chaon/), [Patricia Lockwood](https://texasbookfestival.org/directory/author/patricia-lockwood/), and [Peter Ames Carlin](https://texasbookfestival.org/directory/author/peter-ames-carlin/). (I’m sure I’ve missed a ton of folks — tell me who you plan to see!)  
      
    
      
    
8.  I like this idea: “[Creativity is just deviance put to good use](https://www.experimental-history.com/p/the-decline-of-deviance).” (As I’ve said before, “[Be the weird you wish to see](https://austinkleon.substack.com/p/be-the-weird-you-wish-to-see).)  
      
    
9.  I also like a tuna fish sandwich, so I enjoyed this obituary of artist [Alison Knowles](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/10/31/arts/alison-knowles-dead.html?unlocked_article_code=1.zE8.b0ub.2XbMh6qArmZV&smid=url-share). (A good reminder that I still need to read Allan Kaprow’s *[Essays on the Blurring of Art and Life](https://geni.us/cr0t)* — he taught one of my favorite artists, [Nina Katchadourian](http://www.ninakatchadourian.com).)
