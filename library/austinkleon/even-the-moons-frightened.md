[Hey y’all](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D6wWPwz4BAM),

Happy Halloween. Here are 10 things I thought were worth sharing this week:

1.  “Gatherings are the creation of a temporary alternative world.” Priya Parker and I had a nice chat about [the creative power of Halloween, gathering, jokes, pranks, and other creative mischief](https://austinkleon.substack.com/p/the-art-of-messing-around). (Don’t miss my notes underneath the video — they’re more intelligent, probably, than anything I said.)  
      
    
2.  “[Cheever’s is an autumnal world](https://www.theparisreview.org/blog/2018/08/23/vodka-for-breakfast-on-the-melancholy-of-cheevers-journals/), all burning leaves and dying roses, rain-wet stones, house lights, ‘beards of gold-green weed.’” I’ve been switching back and forth between reading *[The Journals of John Cheever](https://geni.us/mhbBmlN)* and *[The Stories of John Cheever](https://geni.us/1wWManA)*, which contains [one banger after another](https://www.theguardian.com/books/2012/nov/16/short-story-john-cheever): “Goodbye, My Brother,” “The Enormous Radio,” “The Country Husband,” and especially “The Swimmer,” which I read around this time every year. (I love to listen to [Anne Enright read it in her Irish accent](https://www.newyorker.com/podcast/fiction/anne-enright-reads-john-cheever).)  
      
    
3. 4.  Two video games with horror elements my kids and I like to play: *[Limbo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Limbo_\(video_game\))* and *[Inside](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inside_\(video_game\))*, both developed by the indie studio [Playdead](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Playdead).  
      
    
5.  In my ongoing quest to be a [Curious Elder™️](https://austinkleon.com/2019/04/24/youth-spies-and-curious-elders/) I dove deep into one of my 10-year-old’s current passions: [analog horror](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Analog_horror). The glitchy VHS vibes reminded me of one of [my favorite quotes](https://tumblr.austinkleon.com/post/42199119914) from Brian Eno’s diary, *[A Year with Swollen Appendices](https://geni.us/hApyCdd)*:
    
    > Whatever you now find weird, ugly, uncomfortable and nasty about a new medium will surely become its signature. CD distortion, the jitteriness of digital video, the crap sound of 8-bit — all these will be cherished and emulated as soon as they can be avoided.
    
    Speaking of cherished old mediums, here is some dad lecturing I will tolerate: [Henry Rollins teaching you how to handle your vinyl records](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ZLtfo9p-Ydo).
    
      
    
6.  Geese’s *[3D Country](https://geesebandnyc.bandcamp.com/album/3d-country)* was one of [my favorite things last year](https://austinkleon.substack.com/p/another-year-on-the-notebooks), but it took me a minute to warm up to their latest album, *[Getting Killed](https://geesebandnyc.bandcamp.com/album/getting-killed).* I’m all in now: I even snagged a secondhand ticket to their [sold-out show](https://uproxx.com/indie/geese-concert-review-getting-killed/) in Austin next month. Something I hear in this band that I detect in my own Gen Z kid: they are unabashed about [their influences](https://open.spotify.com/playlist/0aGZKcLxsXabBHSMAECMj9?si=90a26cf43beb4753). They don’t seem to have the same musical hangups that I do and they put things together that I wouldn’t put together. [It’s thrilling to watch](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tw91e6Nurfc). They’re also [a real](https://stevenhyden.substack.com/p/geese-is-a-great-american-band) *[band](https://stevenhyden.substack.com/p/geese-is-a-great-american-band)*, pushing and pulling [against the grid](https://fluxblog.substack.com/p/fluxblog-567-geese-vs-the-grid). Maybe the punkiest thing about them is that they seem to choose their most abrasive songs for the lead-off tracks on their albums, effectively thinning the herd of who’s willing to keep listening. Their track twos, on the other hand, are [downright](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0m1abZODAJ0) [groovy](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=y3udPM2ehAo). (A more welcoming entry point for all might be lead singer [Cameron Winter](https://waltermartin.substack.com/p/ok-lets-talk-about-cameron-winter)’s solo album, *[Heavy Metal](https://cameronwinter.bandcamp.com/album/heavy-metal)*.)  
      
    
7.  If you’re a [Kraftwerk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kraftwerk) fan like I am, your jaw will drop while browsing [The Florian Schneider Collection](https://www.juliensauctions.com/en/auctions/the-florian-schneider-collection). (I named *[Computer World](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_World)* as one of my favorite records in [this fun interview with Jacqui Devaney’s newsletter](https://dinnermusic.substack.com/p/dinner-music-157-the-weekly-lineup) *[Dinner Music](https://dinnermusic.substack.com/p/dinner-music-157-the-weekly-lineup)*.)  
      
    
8.  “Everyone in Leeds was into doom-laden stuff, but we wanted to do something more uplifting.” RIP [Dave Ball](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/10/23/arts/music/soft-cell-david-ball-dead.html?smid=nytcore-ios-share&referringSource=articleShare) of Soft Cell. (Might throw on *[Non-Stop Erotic Cabaret](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-Stop_Erotic_Cabaret)* next week — [good](https://austinkleon.com/2024/11/17/dance-in-the-ruins-a-november-mixtape/) *[Dance in the Ruins](https://austinkleon.com/2024/11/17/dance-in-the-ruins-a-november-mixtape/)* [music](https://austinkleon.com/2024/11/17/dance-in-the-ruins-a-november-mixtape/).)  
      
    
9.  October always goes by too fast. 10.  Your assignment this week comes from [Gary Shteyngart](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/10/20/opinion/no-kings-protest-chicago-tactical-frivolity.html?smid=nytcore-ios-share&referringSource=articleShare): “Frivolity and absurdity are kryptonite to authoritarians who project the stern-father archetype to their followers. Once the pants are lowered and the undies of the despot are glimpsed, there is no point of return…. What is happening to us is as serious as a guillotine. We must harness our best creative, humorous and frivolous selves in order to keep it from falling.”
