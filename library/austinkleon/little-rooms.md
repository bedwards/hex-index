[Hey y’all](https://tumblr.austinkleon.com/post/800128652358680576),

Here are 10 things I thought were worth sharing this week:

1.  “Great things start in little rooms.” That’s [André 3000](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bYL760JQjPk) of [Outkast](https://rockhall.com/inductees/outkast/) in his Rock and Roll Hall of Fame acceptance speech, referencing [Jack White](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_AAKmn18LvQ) of [The White Stripes](https://rockhall.com/inductees/white-stripes/), who told young artists in his acceptance speech to “get your hands dirty and drop the screens and get out in your garage or your little room and get obsessed.” In my opinion, White’s “[Little Room](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t6RRsU-P_kk)” is the greatest song ever written about success. Here it is in its entirety:  
      
    “*Well, you’re in your little room  
    and you’re working on something good  
    but if it’s really good  
    you’re gonna need a bigger room  
    and when you’re in the bigger room  
    you might not know what to do  
    you might have to think of  
    how you got started  
    sitting in your little room!”  
      
    *
    
2.  “I wonder if a single thought that has helped forward the human spirit has ever been conceived or written down in an enormous room.”  
    —Kenneth Clark, *[Civilisation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Civilisation_\(TV_series\))*  
      
    
3.  We saw [Jeff Tweedy](https://jefftweedy.com) at the Paramount here in Austin, TX playing songs off his new triple record *[Twilight Override](https://jefftweedy.bandcamp.com/album/twilight-override)* — aka “[Sad-inista](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/09/25/arts/music/jeff-tweedy-twilight-override.html?unlocked_article_code=1.008.5QRx.2u-iXbfDbIrk&smid=url-share)” — with a band of musicians he’s known since they were little kids: [Macie Stewart](https://maciestewart.com) on violin, siblings [Liam Kazar](https://liamkazar.com) and [Sima Cunningham](https://www.simacunningham.com) on guitar and bass, and Tweedy’s own sons, [Sammy](https://www.instagram.com/sammytweedy) and [Spencer](https://spencertweedy.substack.com), on keyboard and drums. This family-based lineup was particularly special to me because Jeff has the last quote in *[Don’t Call It Art](https://austinkleon.com/dont-call-it-art/),* my forthcoming book about what I learned from playing with *my* two sons:

4.  Congratulations to [Hrishikesh Hirway](https://hrishikesh.substack.com) on the 300th episode of *[Song Exploder](https://songexploder.net)!* It’s been a favorite podcast of mine for over a decade. Don’t miss the [recent episode](https://songexploder.net/a-ha) about the evolution of [A-Ha’s “Take on Me.”](https://hrishikesh.substack.com/p/take-on-me) One of the things that makes the show great is that Hrisihi is a musician himself and a real student of the craft of songwriting. (Bonus link: Last year he [interviewed Jeff Tweedy](https://songexploder.net/jeff-tweedy) about his book *[How To Write One Song](https://geni.us/OpfF2F0)*.)  
      
    
5.  Matt Berninger’s cure for writer’s block: [Writing on baseballs](https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/6755600/2025/10/29/the-national-matt-berninger-baseball-and-writing/?source=athletic_user_shared_article_copylink&smid=url-share-ta).  
      
    
6.  With the kids: We saw cartoonist [Nathan Hale’s](https://www.nathanhaleauthor.com) well-honed draw-and-talk about [Lewis and Clark](http://www.chadwbeckerman.com/chadwbeckermanblog/2016/8/29/history-of-hale) at the Texas Book Festival last weekend and it was hilarious and great. My boys love his *[Hazardous Tales](https://geni.us/RF5SRR7)* books. We’ve had a couple of really good [pizza nights](https://austinkleon.substack.com/t/pizza-night) together, too: We liked the horror-movie-for-the-whole-family *[Sketch](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt26238710/)* (2024) and the better-than-it-needed-to-be *[Chip ‘n Dale: Rescue Rangers](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt3513500/?ref_=fn_t_2)* (2022), both of which mixed animation with live action to really good effect. (Earlier this year we loved Robert Zemeckis’s 1988 classic *[Who Framed Roger Rabbit](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0096438/)*.)  
      
    
7.  Austin, TX: If you’re doing the [Austin Studio Tour](https://www.atxstudiotour.com) this weekend, I loved the show of [turn of the 20th century photographs of Austin](https://www.instagram.com/p/DQmvJMBk_Hq) in the restored carriage house and peeking inside the most-definitely-haunted house at the [Flower Hill Center](https://www.atxstudiotour.com/host/flower-hill-center/).  
      
    
8.  “Hans Andersen slept in this room for five weeks — which seemed to the family AGES!” [How guest Hans Christian Andersen destroyed his friendship with Charles Dickens](https://www.theguardian.com/books/2017/sep/10/charles-dickens-hans-christian-andersen-letters-correspondence-auction).  
      
    
9.  “First he became aware how much he needed the winter to rest and mend; then he suspected the winter wasn’t long enough to mend him.” I re-read Denis Johnson’s novella *[Train Dreams](https://geni.us/sBzJ)* in anticipation of the [film adaptation](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt29768334/) coming to [Netflix](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_Nk8TrBHOrA) later this month. It’s as sad and beautiful as I remembered, but also funnier. I recommend listening to [this 2003 event](https://tinhouse.com/podcast/tin-house-live-denis-johnson-2003/) when Johnson read from the story and discussed his writing with writer [Chris Offutt](https://tumblr.austinkleon.com/post/139565620461). Johnson’s [writing advice](https://www.instagram.com/p/DQB8u-_DqAt/?__d=1%3F%2F): “Write in exile, as if you are never going to get home again, and you have to call back every detail.”  
      
    
10.  “To me poems / feel like rooms / you enter / again and again.” My latest typewriter interview is with poet [Matthew Zapruder](https://austinkleon.substack.com/p/typewriter-interview-with-matthew).
