## 1\. YouGov finds Trump at record low with independents

David Montgomery over at YouGov [published](https://today.yougov.com/politics/articles/53377-donald-trump-slump-job-approval-among-independents-economist-yougov-poll) the following chart of Trump’s approval rating among independents this week. The data is from the weekly polls YouGov conducts on behalf of *The Economist* (data which is becoming key to comparing Trump’s position with voters in his second term to similar data from his first term):

[

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!24Vi!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdd78985a-4e49-465a-8e77-551d96ac6814_729x530.png)



](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!24Vi!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdd78985a-4e49-465a-8e77-551d96ac6814_729x530.png)

This chart is this week’s lead story because it shows how unprecedented a position Trump is in with the voters who are most likely to determine the outcome of close elections. While Trump is roughly as unpopular overall as he was at this point in his first term (see below) the difference in his support with marginal voters is potentially consequential for next year’s midterms — and 2028 after that.

[

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FJO7!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdc06567d-791d-4a5d-9ae4-ae380e9d1abe_864x541.png)



](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FJO7!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdc06567d-791d-4a5d-9ae4-ae380e9d1abe_864x541.png)

The new data comes as Democrats are hitting new highs in the race for Congress next year. In YouGov’s new poll, the generic ballot is D+7 — close to the 8-point margin the party had at this point in YouGov/*The Economist*’s polling [for the 2018 midterms](https://d3nkl3psvxxpe9.cloudfront.net/documents/econToplines_dA7lkUw.pdf). The average generic ballot poll is still not quite as rosy for Democrats, but [the trend is currently their friend.](https://fiftyplusone.news/polls/generic-ballot/generic-ballot)

As the result of the 2025 elections suggest, Republican Senators will want to make the 2026 midterms about anything other than Donald Trump and his [negative impacts on prices and affordability](https://www.gelliottmorris.com/p/seven-data-driven-takeaways-from). Polls confirm this strategy. In the chart below, I show estimates from a [MRP](https://sites.stat.columbia.edu/gelman/research/unpublished/MRT\(1\).pdf) model of my *Strength In Numbers*/Verasight polling data that takes a national poll and transforms it into estimates of state-level opinion. The chart shows Trump’s net job approval at the state level, broken down by political party (ignore the outliers, which are likely due to sampling error):

[

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SG_a!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9679e9a7-30f3-454e-8c02-6fe1136ffb08_775x947.png)



](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!SG_a!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F9679e9a7-30f3-454e-8c02-6fe1136ffb08_775x947.png)

Take note of two things: First, more independents in every single state say they disapprove than approve of how Trump is handling his job as president. And the margin is not even close, at roughly -35 in the average state.

Second, note that my estimated net approval among independents in the average state is close to YouGov’s national estimate of net approval among independents (-35). This provides some external validation of my estimates.

Based on this data, if I’m a Republican Senator up for re-election in 2026 (looking at you, Susan Collins), I’m looking for every opportunity available to distance myself from Trump. Maybe, for example, that means [voting against him](https://goodpoliticsbadpolitics.substack.com/p/trumpepstein) and in favor of the release of the government’s “Epstein files.” Politico [reports](https://www.politico.com/live-updates/2025/11/12/congress/house-epstein-vote-gop-defections-00649624) that GOP leaders expect mass defections from the party line (up to 100 members or more) on the vote next week.

More to come on both of these subjects — independents and state-level opinion — with the release of our latest *Strength In Numbers*/Verasight poll this week.
