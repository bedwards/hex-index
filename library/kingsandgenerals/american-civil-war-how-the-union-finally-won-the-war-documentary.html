As the only European power with extensive holdings in North America, Great Britain had a more urgent interest in the American Civil War. However, if it had to fight a total war like the Americans did, this army would be rapidly depleted.

Britain would have to form a new army from new volunteers and conscripts just like the Americans were. When the Total War finally arrived, Britain would need a much larger pool of trained and capable recruits.
