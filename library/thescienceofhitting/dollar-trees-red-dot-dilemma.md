From “**[Dollar Tree:](https://thescienceofhitting.com/p/dltr-raising-the-bar)** [](https://thescienceofhitting.com/p/dltr-raising-the-bar)**[Raising The Bar](https://thescienceofhitting.com/p/dltr-raising-the-bar)**” (October 2025):

“Management must remain relentlessly focused on serving the low opening price point, a key point of differentiation relative to many retail competitors. With ~85% of SKU’s still at or below $2, I’m confident this distinction remains intact; it’s critical, in my view, to hold this line in the years ahead, keeping a large majority of the SKU’s at or below $2. Multi-price is a lever to be pulled rarely, and when there is a compelling incremental reason to do so.”

Using same store sales (SSS) as a proxy for customer adoption, we can see that Dollar Tree’s strategic evolution remains on track: FY25e comps / SSS growth is now expected to be [north of 5.0%](https://corporate.dollartree.com/_assets/_bf20c2975a4ab518732ccadb5a3bf4f7/dollartreeinfo/news/2025-12-03_Dollar_Tree_Inc_Reports_Results_for_the_Third_300.pdf), compared to the initial 3.0% - 5.0% guidance provided [in March](https://corporate.dollartree.com/_assets/_bf20c2975a4ab518732ccadb5a3bf4f7/dollartreeinfo/news/2025-03-26_Dollar_Tree_Inc_Reports_Results_for_the_Fourth_281.pdf). As a result, average unit volumes (AUVs) across the banner rose by >20% over the past five years to ~$2.1 million.
