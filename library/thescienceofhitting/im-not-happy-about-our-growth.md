From “**[More Than A Place To Stay](https://thescienceofhitting.com/p/more-than-a-place-to-stay)**” (May 2025): *“The net result is low-single digit FY25e operating income (EBIT) growth. At ~$138 per share, or an enterprise value of ~$81 billion, ABNB trades at ~31x FY25e EV/EBIT. It seems Mr. Market remains somewhat willing to look past these near term questions. It’s a conclusion that I can appreciate, but one I also suspect could be subject to change under the right scenario - specifically, if volumes and rates in the core U.S. business come under pressure… I continue to watch this situation with much interest – but I’ll remain on the sidelines for now.”*

Despite meaningful progress on its global expansion efforts, as evidenced by [the >2x higher growth rate](https://s26.q4cdn.com/656283129/files/doc_financials/2025/q3/v2/Airbnb_Q3-2025-Shareholder-Letter.pdf) for nights booked in expansion markets versus their [five primary markets](https://thescienceofhitting.com/p/more-than-a-place-to-stay), North America and Europe still account for ~43% and ~38% of TTM revenues, respectively. The pressure on North America [has continued](https://thescienceofhitting.com/p/more-than-a-place-to-stay), with revenue growth decelerating to just ~3% YoY in [Q3 FY25](https://www.sec.gov/ix?doc=/Archives/edgar/data/0001559720/000155972025000030/abnb-20250930.htm).

With ~40% of Airbnb’s business now growing low-single to mid-single digits, mid-teens growth for the other ~60% of the pie results in consolidated YoY revenue growth that’s fighting to stay in the double-digits: as shown below, [management’s guidance](https://s26.q4cdn.com/656283129/files/doc_financials/2025/q3/v2/Airbnb_Q3-2025-Shareholder-Letter.pdf) implies TTM revenues will be +9% YoY in Q4 FY25.

The other challenge is near term pressure on profit margins, which has arisen as a result of [expansion market mix shift](https://research.alpha-sense.com?docid=ET-115705393-1901714662&utm_source=alphasense%20platform&utm_medium=document%20share&utm_content=ET-115705393-1901714662&utm_campaign=1764520389725) and the impact of investments to support the company’s [strategic growth initiatives](https://thescienceofhitting.com/p/more-than-a-place-to-stay), which management has [previously](https://research.alpha-sense.com?docid=ET-115705393-1954355507&utm_source=alphasense%20platform&utm_medium=document%20share&utm_content=ET-115705393-1954355507&utm_campaign=1763920148039) pegged at ~$200 million in FY25e, or nearly 2% of revenues. As you can see below, FY25e guidance implies EBIT margins of ~21% - down roughly 400 basis points from the ~25% reported in FY23. Unlike the EBITDA metric that management provides, this accounts for the impact of stock-based compensation - an expense line that is currently at **~12% of TTM revenues**.

The net result of the above – slowing revenue growth rates and EBIT margin compression – is **~1%** FY25e operating income growth, to ~$2.6 billion. As the multiple has come in, to ~25x FY25e, I think Mr. Market is expressing some concern about what that equation will look like in the year(s) ahead. (Many websites understate Airbnb’s fully diluted share count - [see page 15](https://s26.q4cdn.com/656283129/files/doc_financials/2025/q3/v2/Airbnb_Q3-2025-Shareholder-Letter.pdf); as a result, they then present a EV/EBIT multiple that’s 2-3 turns too low.)
