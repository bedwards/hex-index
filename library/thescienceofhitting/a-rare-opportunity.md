On December 5th, Netflix announced a deal to acquire Warner Bros (the Studios & Streaming / S&S business of WBD) [for ~$82.7 billion](https://ir.netflix.net/investor-news-and-events/financial-releases/press-release-details/2025/NETFLIX-TO-ACQUIRE-WARNER-BROS--FOLLOWING-THE-SEPARATION-OF-DISCOVERY-GLOBAL-FOR-A-TOTAL-ENTERPRISE-VALUE-OF-82-7-BILLION-Equity-Value-of-72-0-Billion/default.aspx). The logic of the deal, in terms of the acquired content / IP, is self-evident. Given Netflix’s unrivaled global scale in DTC video streaming, a position that supports a business with run rate revenues of ~$48 billion, I agree with co-CEO Ted Sarandos – “[These assets are more valuable in our business model, and our business model is more valuable with these assets](https://s22.q4cdn.com/959853165/files/doc_events/2025/Dec/05/Netflix-to-Acquire-Warner-Bros-Investor-Call-Transcript-docx.pdf)”. By virtue of Netflix’s scale, they can maximize content value to a degree others cannot replicate.
