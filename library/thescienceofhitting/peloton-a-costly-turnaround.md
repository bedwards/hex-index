From “**[Peloton: A Steep Hill](https://thescienceofhitting.com/p/peloton-a-steep-hill)**” (February 2025):

“I’m focused on three key factors: (1) their ability to stabilize, and then grow, CF Product and subscription revenues; (2) their ability to improve CF Product gross margins, such that this business contributes >$100 million of gross profits on current volumes; and (3) their ability to drive continued OpEx improvements, with historic results suggesting it isn’t overly demanding to expect run rate OpEx on current revenues to decline by $200 - $400 million.”

[On October 1st](https://www.wsj.com/tech/peloton-raises-membership-prices-rolls-out-new-equipment-0cccae4a?msockid=1c302391d69563690da33083d70562fb), Peloton announced a series of equipment and software updates across its range of offerings, along with a price increase on its All-Access (CF) membership to [$49.99 per month](https://support.onepeloton.com/s/article/Peloton-Membership-Membership-Fee-Changes?language=en_US); inclusive of the [June 2022 price increase](https://www.onepeloton.com/press/articles/update-on-pricing?msockid=1c302391d69563690da33083d70562fb), from $39 per month to $44 per month, the monthly price for All-Access has risen by nearly 30% over the past three and a half years.
