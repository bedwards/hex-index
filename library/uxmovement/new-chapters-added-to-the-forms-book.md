When I wrote the book, “*[Dos and Don’ts for Form Input and Selection](https://uxmovement.gumroad.com/l/dodontform),*” I was imagining a bible of best practices for designing forms. It would simply tell designers what to do, what not to do, and why. It would be dominated by visual examples, not so much text. I can proudly say that this book has become what I have imagined.

I have added 3 new chapters to the book with a total of 9 chapters now. The new chapters cover best practices for designing steppers, pickers, and submit buttons.

If you’re a designer or developer who wants to learn how to design forms with optimal UX, this book is for you. It’s an easy read and can serve as a checklist to share with your team. Now, whenever you’re designing a form, you’ll know exactly what you need to do no matter what the component.
