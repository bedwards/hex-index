So insurance is effectively mandatory. Almost any large festival will hold a massive policy covering both general liability and more unique risks like event cancellation and artist non-appearance. Very few insurers, reportedly about 10, will actually underwrite these policies given their massive scale, giving them quite a bit of leverage in setting terms. Every policy is unique, but much of the design and operations of festivals is therefore dictated by the insurer.

For liquor liability coverage, they might require that sales are limited to two at a time. For general liability coverage, they might set security staffing minimums. For artists non-appearance coverage, they might prohibit the festival from booking particularly troublesome artists. For event cancellation coverage, they might require an on-site meteorologist.

But while these insurance policies make event management a sustainable business, they are also now holding the industry back. With three cancellations in 6 years, it's no wonder insurance rates are going up.
