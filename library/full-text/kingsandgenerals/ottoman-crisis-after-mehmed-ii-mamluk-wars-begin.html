The goal of the campaign was to secure Mulavia's coastal lands and assert Ottoman dominance over a region that had long resisted imperial control. At the center of this campaign stood the fortresses of Akaman and Kilia, strategic strongholds whose capture would reshape the balance of power in the region as they sat at the confluence of the Danube Delta and the Black Sea. For the first time in almost 8 years, an Ottoman army was led by its sultan as the sounds of war drums once again echoed through the Balkans. But as the Turkish armies were once again victorious in Europe, news from Asia began flooding the Sultan's tent. With the annexation of the Caramanid Baik back in 1468, the Ottoman Empire now bordered the Mammluk vassal states of the Dulkaderids and Ramisanids. For many years, the royal courts of Cairo and Constantinople had regarded each other as rivals, both claiming ownership of Caraman and the buffer states situated between their realms.

During this period, Mamlikch customs officials often harassed Ottoman pilgrims visiting the holy sites of Jerusalem, Mecca, and Medina as they were seen as potential spies. The two states almost came to blows during the height of Jem Sultan's rebellion when Cairo harbored the Ottoman prince and even assisted him militarily in seizing the throne against Beazid. Due to all of these developments, diplomatic relations between the two states began to deteriorate rapidly by 1482. Further hostilities occurred the following year when Bosk Bay of the Dulkaderids declared his allegiance to Beaazid and began besieging the Mammlheld town of Malletia.

Although defeated and forced to switch sides in 1485, the Dulkader ruler had sparked a limited border war between the local governors of Ottoman Anatolia and Mamluk Syria. In 1485, Caragos Memed Pasher of Caraman invaded the Ramisanids and occupied Adana and Tacas while Yakup Bay of Caiseri invaded the Dulkaderids and defeated a Mamlac army near Malatia. On the sea, Herikata Ammed Pashia commanded the Ottoman navy and moved toward Alexandria to cut off the Mamluks from the region of Salicia. The Ottoman army was estimated at around 60,000 men. After the great battle, Adana and Tarsus fell to the victorious Mammluks soon afterward who strengthened their regional fortresses as the status quo was reestablished.
