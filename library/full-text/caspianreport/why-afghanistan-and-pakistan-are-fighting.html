The Durand line split ethnic Pashtune communities between Afghanistan and what later became Pakistan.

The line itself was based on the range of British rifles, not on where people actually lived. Little to no consideration was given to geography, history, or the realities on the ground. For the locals, the agreement was a disaster. It cut through communities that had coexisted for centuries.
