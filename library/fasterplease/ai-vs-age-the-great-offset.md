**My fellow pro-growth/progress/abundance Up Wingers in America and around the world:**

Lots of folks have talked themselves into something approximating a techno-rapture. And not just on [r/singularity](https://www.reddit.com/r/singularity/) over at Reddit.

Silicon Valley spins scenarios of superintelligence. Washington whispers a growth surge that might fund guns, butter, and a senior safety net without trade-offs. Investors imagine a market boom like a never-ending 1990s.

All in all, American civilization on the brink of reinvention — or so the pitch goes.

But there’s another side to that tempting trade, one worth considering.

A new analysis from JPMorgan’s long-term strategy team, “The race between AI and demographics,” dumps a half bucket of room-temp water on possibly overheated speculation. I think even the most AI-optimistic — including me, I guess — should give it a fair hearing.

The gist of the bank’s harsh math: Whatever artificial intelligence economically bestows, demographics are primed to reclaim. What’s more (depressingly for ultimate Up Wingers), the two forces look roughly equal and opposite. The rich world’s AI awakening may simply hold economies in place, more or less.

Ugh, what fun is that?
