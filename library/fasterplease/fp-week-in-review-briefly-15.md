## **Essays and Q&As**

**[⚔️☠️ The Billionaire War Against Death](https://fasterplease.substack.com/p/the-billionaire-war-against-death)**

[

![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!eoml!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4470e642-e476-4d04-95b3-736647824e27_682x381.png)



](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!eoml!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4470e642-e476-4d04-95b3-736647824e27_682x381.png)

**Why It Matters.** For the superrich, radical longevity has shifted from symbolism to strategy. A desire that once manifested in pyramids, alchemy, and charitable foundations is now a serious investment thesis. Billionaires increasingly treat aging not as fate, but as a technical problem whose solution needs merely to be financed.

**What’s New.** Silicon Valley and finance elites are pouring billions into cellular reprogramming, age-reversal drugs, and longevity platforms. UBS reports roughly four in five billionaires now expect to live much longer than previous generations — and believe science will deliver.

**The Trend Line.** This bet builds on steady progress. Goldman Sachs shows the longevity frontier has risen by about 0.25 years per year for 150 years. A seventy-year-old today resembles someone in their mid-fifties in 2000, and working lives are already lengthening.

**Up Wing Up Shot.** If biology becomes upgradeable, consequences won’t stay elite. Longer lives could reshape work, politics, and inequality. History suggests diffusion for the rest of us, not permanent exclusion from the fruits of science.
