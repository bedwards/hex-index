**[Perhaps yodeling could become a practice during Advent and Christmas.](https://apnews.com/article/unesco-heritage-yodeling-switzerland-bb78db76b3b5225c7daf2c8b3c18dd2a)** Which Christmas songs could be yodeled?

> GENEVA (AP) — Switzerland’s [long-celebrated yodeling](https://apnews.com/article/switzerland-yodeling-unesco-9db642e20b4cc36c94639edc8ec9d314) has received a response from [the U.N. cultural agency](https://apnews.com/article/what-is-unesco-explainer-us-funding-6797042db1016bacf0d522366dbe809a): The Alpine tradition of chant and song is indeed worthy of classification in a list of the world’s cultural heritage.
> 
> A committee of Paris-based UNESCO, meeting in New Delhi, on Thursday listed yodeling in its list of intangible cultural heritage.
> 
> Yodeling was selected among [67 traditions](https://www.unesco.org/en/intangible-cultural-heritage/committee-2025) honored by UNESCO in the Indian capital, including [Italian cooking](https://the%20u.n.xn--'s%20cultural%20agency%20gave%20foodies%20on%20wednesday%20another%20reason%20to%20celebrate%20their%20pizza,%20pasta%20and%20tiramisu%20by%20listing%20italian%20cooking%20as%20part%20of%20the%20worlds%20intangible%20cultural%20heritage-2e97msuazh./), Ghanaian highlife music, the fermented Kyrgyz beverage Maksym and the El Joropo music and dance tradition in Venezuela.
> 
> The list is different from the UNESCO World Heritage List, which enshrines protections for physical sites that are considered important to humanity, like the [Pyramids of Giza in Egypt](https://apnews.com/article/pyramid-egypt-cairo-chamber-antiquities-c104c9479b110b85567e61b9ee4bcc0e).
> 
> “Yodeling can be performed by individuals, small groups or choirs, and sometimes with instruments like the accordion,” said UNESCO.
> 
> “It features rich harmonics and is often performed at concerts, parties and competitions, with participants typically wearing traditional regional costumes,” the assessment said.
> 
> Switzerland’s government pushed the candidacy with UNESCO, and promoters insist it’s far more than the mountain cries of yesteryear by falsetto-bellowing male herders in suspenders on green mountainsides: It’s also a popular form of singing.
> 
> The government says at least 12,000 yodelers take part through about 780 groups of the Swiss Yodeling Association.

Michael Bird, Origen, gospel discrepancies:

> Origen’s view of Gospel harmonization is pivotal for understanding his doctrine of Scripture, one that is both critical and theological.
> 
> In his *Commentary on John*, Origen acknowledges that there are narrative discrepancies between the Gospels. He suggests that if such discrepancies cannot be resolved, one faces hard choices: either giving up trust in the Gospels as divinely inspired and credible records, or seeking their truth in a deeper, spiritual sense rather than in strict historical or material details.
> 
> Accordingly, Origen proposes that true harmony and true meaning in the Gospel narratives should be sought “anagogically,” that is, through mystical or spiritual interpretation rather than insisting on literal or historical precision.
> 
> He argues that careful students should not abandon the Gospels if historical harmonization proves too difficult; instead, they should accept the four Gospels as inspired texts whose truths are not found in their outward, material letter but in their spiritual significance. Thus, Origen sees apparent discrepancies not as cause for doubt, but as invitations to deeper interpretive inquiry. …
> 
> This is a good example of Origen’s approach to Scripture. For him, something like “inerrancy” pertains not to the literal and historical level, but to the spiritual and theological level of meaning. This is very different to Augustine’s view of Gospel harmonization, spiritual meaning, truth, hermeneutics, and authority which is moral literal (and slightly ironic given that Augustine is often thought to prefer allegorical readings!).
> 
> In sum, Origen’s approach challenges the demand for absolute historical harmony in the Gospels and models a doctrine of Scripture that is both reverent and yet finds its center of gravity in spiritual interpretation rather than in literal meaning.
>

**[AI sources and your local librarian](https://gizmodo.com/librarians-arent-hiding-secret-books-from-you-that-only-ai-knows-about-2000698176)**:

> Everyone knows that AI chatbots like ChatGPT, Grok, and Gemini can often hallucinate sources. But for the folks tasked with helping the public find books and journal articles, the fake AI bullshit is really taking its toll. Librarians sound absolutely exhausted by the requests for titles that don’t exist, according to a new post from [Scientific American](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/ai-slop-is-spurring-record-requests-for-imaginary-journals/).
> 
> The magazine spoke with Sarah Falls, the chief of researcher engagement at the Library of Virginia, who estimates that about 15% of all emailed reference questions that they receive are generated by AI chatbots like ChatGPT. And the requests often include questions about fake citations.
> 
> What’s more, Falls suggests that people don’t seem to believe librarians when they explain that a given record doesn’t exist, a trend that’s been reported elsewhere like [404 Media](https://www.404media.co/librarians-are-being-asked-to-find-ai-hallucinated-books/). Many people really believe their stupid chatbot over a human who specializes in finding reliable information day in and day out.
> 
> A recent post from the International Committee of the Red Cross (ICRC) titled, “Important notice: AI generated archival reference,” provides more evidence that librarians are just exhausted [with it all](https://www.icrc.org/en/article/important-notice-ai-generated-archival-references).
> 
> “If a reference cannot be found, this does not mean that the ICRC is withholding information. Various situations may explain this, including incomplete citations, documents preserved in other institutions, or— increasingly—AI-generated hallucinations,” the organization said. “In such cases, you may need to look into the administrative history of the reference to determine whether it corresponds to a genuine archival source.”

Her library estimates that 15 percent of emailed reference questions it receives are now ChatGPT-generated, and some include hallucinated citations for both published works and unique primary source documents. “For our staff, it is much harder to prove that a unique record doesn’t exist,” she says.
