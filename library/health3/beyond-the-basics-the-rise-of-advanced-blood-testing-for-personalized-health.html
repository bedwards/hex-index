You’ve likely already heard of Inside Tracker, the “advanced” blood testing company that’s regularly advertised on biohacking, longevity, or of Function Health, co-founded by Dr Mark Hyman: a new category of D2C “science-backed” blood testing companies are popping up across the US and Europe, claiming they can help you “understand and optimize your health”. What defines these tests? Who are the key players? What’s the science underscoring their claims, and what tangible benefits do they bring? Let’s dive in.

What Are Online Preventative Health Tests?

The idea of getting comprehensive blood work for preventative health isn't novel: many family GPs recommend it during annual checkups.

Inside Tracker, launched in 2010 and advised by longevity researcher David Sinclair, pioneered a new category of "advanced" preventative blood tests that go much further: tapping into the latest clinical research, they analyze a broader spectrum of biomarkers - many of which are not typically assessed in mainstream medicine unless you’re already sick. By diving deeper into our biology, these next-gen blood testing companies deliver more personalized insights across lifestyle, nutrition and sometimes exercise, to optimize your health. Conveniently, they can be ordered online (companies either send a phlebotomist to your home or coordinate a visit to a local Quest Diagnostics). With pre-arranged pricing, they're also more cost-effective than going directly to the lab.

What’s truly innovative about these tests?

Firstly, they employ advanced biomarkers grounded in cutting-edge science - between 10 to 100. For instance, they’d measure ApoB and Lp(a) - factors that are increasingly recognized as superior predictors of cardiovascular disease over traditional lipid panels.

Second, is the way these companies run the analysis of these biomarkers:

  • They compare your biomarker’s data against pre-defined categories (such as “needs work”, “good” or “great”) which sometimes depend on your gender and age-group (did you know? reference ranges from your lab tests are often derived from average values from broad populations and do not account for individual’s background such as gender, or age), which allows for a more accurate and personalized assessment of health and risk. Some companies like Inside Tracker also group biomarkers into “subgroups” (like Hormones, Nutrients, etc) and give you an overall score to avoid screening through 100+ biomarkers individually.

  • Some of them may cross reference your biomarker’s data against your medical history, your family’s and your lifestyle (although not all do this).

  • Then, they use machine learning to compute these datasets, look for patterns and run them against the latest scientific literature to deliver personalized insights and recommendations for your health.

In essence, these tests leverage data and technology to deliver a “supercharged” health analysis. It’s exactly what one would hope for in a lab test or preventive check-up: clear, research-backed results with actionable, individualized insights.

What Are The Key Benefits?

So, what are the benefits of getting these “supercharged” health analysis and personalized reports?

  • What these companies claim to be the main benefit of their product is to know your “baseline”. This means knowing where each biomarkers stands, getting a personalized plan to optimize each of them, then being able to track the impact of these optimizations over time - which is why many of these companies advocate for routine testing, at least biannually (Function Health’s membership includes 2 tests/year so you can retest easily for example).

  • Some companies take a more medical approach and actually help detect and predict risk for major diseases early. Meaning that rather than looking at your biomarkers individually, they build your risk profile for major diseases, which may help put your health data and actions into perspective.

  • However, it's important to underscore that these online testing services don't assert to be full-fledged disease screening tools. Their primary objective is the improvement of biomarkers: for accurate disease detection, it's advisable to consult your GP or doctor.

Who Are the Key Players & Their Offering?

We’ve identified more 12 companies offering these next-gen blood analysis across the US and Europe. Not all have the same approach, and as a result the depth and usefulness of the insights they provide can vary significantly. What are the key differences?

1. Groups and total number of biomarkers

Top companies in the space offer look at anywhere between 10 (Levels) to 100+ (Function Health) biomarkers of your health.

Most tests focus on two crucial health “buckets”: metabolic health and cardiovascular health, because of the widespread prevalence and impact of these health conditions on mortality and overall lifespan. For context: 40% of Americans suffer from metabolic syndromes, 1 in 3 adult have pre-diabetes, 80% of which don’t know they have it. Same goes with cardiovascular disease: 48% of U.S adults have cardiovascular disease, with 3 in 5 undiagnosed. These tests of the latest biomarkers that allow us to better assess our risks and detect diseases (far more advanced than those currently included in routine lab tests).

Beyond the primary biomarkers for metabolic and cardiovascular health, some companies expand their offerings to include other buckets of biomarkers recommended for healthspan and general health:

  • Hormonal Health: Evaluating sex hormones like oestradiol, testosterone, and progesterone is essential since imbalances can lead to significant health complications.

  • Organ Health: Monitoring the well-being of vital organs, especially the liver and kidneys, is imperative for overall health.

  • Nutritional Biomarkers: Certain nutrients play a defining role in longevity, with Vitamin D being a standout.

  • Biometrics for Longevity: Some companies measure biometrics that correlate with longevity, such as grip strength or waist circumference, which are strong indicators of overall health.

  • Biological Age Assessment: While more controversial, some companies offer tests to determine one's biological age.

  • Additional Biomarkers: Other valuable tests look into gut health, essential nutrients, adrenal function (especially cortisol levels), and more.

2. Medical vs. Wellness


This is the most critical distinction: some companies prioritize a medical perspective, while others lean more towards wellness. We’ve bucketed them in 3 categories:

  • Digital-Only Approach: These companies will upload your results into a digital “dashboard” that will show you where your biomarkers are out of range and provide lifestyle, nutrition and supplementation advice. While this puts data interpretation and subsequent actions in your hands – making it quick and autonomous – it can be challenging. Parsing through multiple biomarkers without a medical context can be overwhelming, and users might struggle to pinpoint whats’ most important & how to integrate all advice at once in their routine. Companies that do this: Inside Tracker, Function Health, Base, Viome, Aware.

  • Consult Approach: These companies not only provide a digital dashboard but also facilitate an online consultation with a longevity specialist. The added layer of a professional consultation can't be understated. If an issue is detected, an MD can guide you to the appropriate care pathway. More importantly, they help contextualize your results and better understand your health risks. Getting context will help understand why certain changes in lifestyle are important since that’s a hard thing to do. Companies that do this: Instalab, Kor, Mito Health, ModernAge.

  • Consult & Coaching Approach: The most holistic of the three, this approach combines analysis, consultation and personalized coaching: you're not only informed about your health but are also guided on actionable steps to enhance it by a team of experts - health coaches, nutritionists and sometimes physicians. It makes it easier to integrate personalized health plans into your routines, and more likely get motivated to achieve real results. Companies that do this: ModernAge, Turi Health, Aura, Wild Health, Lifeforce, and to some extent, Forward Health.

So, are they worth it?

The value you derive from these tests will differ based on the scope of biomarkers you choose (focusing on longevity versus general health) how these insights are delivered to you. Most will equip you with the right lifestyle, dietary, and supplement recommendations. But, will a digital summary persuade you to make changes to your lifestyle to move your biomarkers the right direction, or do you require a doctor's insight to truly grasp their significance? You might also need help and coaching embedding these recommendations into your daily life.

Regardless, the trajectory is clear: advanced blood tests are becoming the cornerstone of preventative health. Envision a future where these are standard, more affordable tests that, aided by AI, provide precise, tailored advice. Envision a future where this blood-based data is coupled with wearable or continuous monitor data and updates your metrics real time. Coupled with declining lab expenses and availability of wearables, we're looking at the next frontier in personalized health.
