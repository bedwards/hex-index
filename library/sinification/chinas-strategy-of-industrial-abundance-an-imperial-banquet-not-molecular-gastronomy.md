Forming part of what has been described by Dan Wang in his recent book as an “[engineering state](https://www.ft.com/content/261a0eaa-7fb9-4052-ac78-f4d8d9969e72)”, the Chinese policy establishment often lets the term “system” do the conceptual heavy lifting: building a complete “industrial system”, developing a “national innovation system” and strengthening “systemic capacity”. In an increasingly orthodox narrative that is perhaps best exemplified by the economist Lu Feng’s (路风) [concept](https://www.sinification.com/p/industrial-maximalism-lu-feng-on) of the “complete industrial system”, technological innovation is understood not primarily as the product of nimble minds or freewheeling vision, but as the natural outgrowth of a holistic system in which supply- and demand-side inputs are calibrated for its emergence.
