Sinification's March 2026 digest gathers Chinese strategic commentary on the US-Israeli military strike on Iran and its cascading implications for the global balance of power. Chinese analysts frame the war as evidence of American strategic exhaustion — what they call "riding a tiger and unable to get off" (骑虎难下).

Wang Jiangyu of City University of Hong Kong treats the refusal of traditional US allies to participate as a threshold event, arguing it "sets a precedent that will make it much harder for America to rally allies in future wars — this may be the last war Washington can launch with any semblance of dignity." Di Dongsheng of Renmin University draws a pointed historical analogy: "China is following the script the United States played in the early stages of World War One to profit from the war," expecting accelerated exports of Chinese new-energy vehicles, storage, solar, and wind equipment.

Morgan Stanley's China chief economist Xing Ziqiang offers the sharpest rebuttal, warning that "$130 oil for a quarter could drag China's GDP growth to around 3% or lower. Markets are severely underpricing this risk." The digest assembles voices from Shi Zhan, Li Cheng, Wu Xinbo, Zheng Yongnian, Wei Leijie, Zhou Tianyong, Yao Yang, and others — offering a spectrum of views from strategic confidence to economic anxiety about China's domestic reform ceiling and the narrowing window on Taiwan.
