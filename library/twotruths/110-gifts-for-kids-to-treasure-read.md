## **Something to read**

[This](https://go.shopmy.us/p-31418072) adorable book series (with a subscription option so that one arrives each month) comes with “moon-themed” stories plus a trinket or lovie. The company also sells [fairy house-making kits](https://go.shopmy.us/p-32942596), “lunar calendars,” and [other keepsakes](https://www.fullmoonfairy.com/collections/trinkets).

For budding readers, [this](https://go.shopmy.us/p-31078485) dragon-themed series and [this](https://go.shopmy.us/p-31078697) one about unicorns in a lovable make-believe world are popular in our house. *—C*

My 6-year-old can’t get enough of [this](https://go.shopmy.us/p-32942798) 29-book series that follows a young girl—who can secretly talk to animals—as she helps at her uncle’s rescue zoo. It’s wholesome, exciting, and educational. *—K*

My 3-year-old recently received [this](https://go.shopmy.us/p-32944318) illustrated collection of verses as a gift, and now he runs to his room requesting poetry for bedtime every night. *—K*

Speaking of poetry: [This](https://www.amazon.com/Stopping-Woods-Snowy-Evening-Robert/dp/0525467343) beautiful book turns one of Robert Frost’s most famous works into a wintry wonderland of a bedtime story. *—K*

The gift that keeps on giving and helps keep print media alive: magazine subscriptions! [This one](https://www.underthemoonlightmagazine.com/) is a favorite in my house—it brings the coziest, most magical vibes each new season. We’re also loving [this one](https://honesthistory.co/), which delivers well-researched history lessons alongside beautiful illustrations. *—K*

Adding [this](https://spark.kids/) one! I subscribed my kids to this monthly activity magazine about a year ago and will be re-upping soon, if only to continue to see the delight they feel when it arrives in the mail. *—C*

### Resources and Support For Maternal Mental Health

-   [Emergency assistance](https://www.911.gov/calling-911/) is available 24/7 at 911
    
-   [The National Suicide Prevention Lifeline](https://988lifeline.org/?utm_source=google&utm_medium=web&utm_campaign=onebox) is available 24/7 at 988
    
-   The [National Maternal Mental Health Hotline](https://mchb.hrsa.gov/national-maternal-mental-health-hotline) (1-833-TLC-MAMA or 1-833-852-6262) provides access to a trained counselor 24/7 and is available in Spanish and English
    
-   [Postpartum Support International](https://www.postpartum.net/) provides educational resources on PMADs, free support groups, webinars, advanced trainings for providers, and more
    
-   Postpartum Support International’s [provider directory](https://psidirectory.com/) includes a list of thousands of trained professionals organized by state
    
-   [The Motherhood Center](https://www.themotherhoodcenter.com/) offers counseling, support groups, and webinars
    
-   [The Postpartum Stress Center](https://www.postpartumstress.com/referral-list) offers educational resources, counseling, a referral list of trained providers, and advanced training for providers
