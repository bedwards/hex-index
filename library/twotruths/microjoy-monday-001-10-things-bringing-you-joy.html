In a world of heavy headlines—many of which, yes, we deliver in this newsletter—we’re excited to share something else: a list of the little things bringing joy to the mothers in our community.

These powerful yet fleeting moments are what Kelsey calls “motherhood microjoys”—brief flashes of awe and delight that occur throughout your day so quickly that they’re easy to miss. But pay attention to them—and, even better, start keeping an inventory of them—and they could make you happier overall. (The more you train your brain to look for good things, the more you’ll find, and the more joy you’ll inevitably experience.) Scroll down toward the bottom of this post for a quick primer on how to start your journal.

We hope you enjoy these 10 special moments shared with us by our community. If you want more tips and reminders to strengthen your own micro-joy journaling practice, join Kelsey’s Instagram community, ‘Motherhood Microjoys.’ Plus, stay tuned for more from our ‘Microjoy Monday’ series—because we seriously believe we all need a lot more happiness in our lives.

Motherhood microjoys: 10 things bringing you happiness this week

A reframe on ‘1 more minute’
“When my daughter asks for 1 more minute, and we touch our pointer fingers together.”

A way to root in the present moment
“Pointing out all the construction vehicles and hearing the joyful squeals from the back seat.”

A sensory experience
“Oh! The smell of my baby’s head! For some reason it is even more addictive at 7 months.”

A reminder as the kids grow up
“My Velcro baby came home from school and said — ‘I always like to be with you, I love you.’”

And another
“My 12yo son is much taller than me and hugs me so long every time I see him.”

On the awe of simply observing
“When 6yo & 2yo have conversations, just them. Existing.”

Watching them grow into their own
“My 3yo said a prayer before dinner, totally unprompted.”

When their questions surprise you
“My first grader asked this a.m., ‘What’s on your agenda today, Mom?’”

On moments of nostalgia
“Watching my 16yo put on mascara. She was just a baby!!!”

Watching empathy develop before your very eyes
“My toddler asked to kiss my baby’s owie all better.”

Resources and Support For Maternal Mental Health

  • Emergency assistance is available 24/7 at 911

  • The National Suicide Prevention Lifeline is available 24/7 at 988

  • The National Maternal Mental Health Hotline (1-833-TLC-MAMA or 1-833-852-6262) provides access to a trained counselor 24/7 and is available in Spanish and English

  • Postpartum Support International provides educational resources on PMADs, free support groups, webinars, advanced trainings for providers, and more

  • Postpartum Support International’s provider directory includes a list of thousands of trained professionals organized by state

  • The Motherhood Center offers counseling, support groups, and webinars

  • The Postpartum Stress Center offers educational resources, counseling, a referral list of trained providers, and advanced training for providers

SUPPORT YOUR MENTAL HEALTH WITH POSTPARTUM SUPPORT INTERNATIONAL (PSI). PSI is a global champion for perinatal mental health that connects individuals and families to the resources and support needed to give them the strongest and healthiest start possible. Visit postpartum.net for information on perinatal mental health disorders, access to 30+ free, online support groups, an online provider directory, the PSI HelpLine, local support coordinators, a perinatal mental health discussion tool, specialized support resources, and more. Call the PSI HelpLine toll-free at 1-800-944-4773 for basic information, support, and resources. Support via text message is also available at 800-944-4773 (English) and 971-203-2773 (Español). Remember: You are not alone. You are not to blame. With help, you will be well.
