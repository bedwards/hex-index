*Why don’t Amtrak trains have changing tables on them?* That was the question we found ourselves asking as we put together this issue and came across the [Baby Changing On Board Act](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-bill/248). The bill, sponsored by Congresswoman Lauren Underwood, just passed through the House and would make travel more family-friendly. It points to just one of the countless barriers families face when traveling.

Travel is not easy, especially during a season when schedules notoriously don’t line up and paychecks may more readily go to camp or childcare.

*And* travel is important. It’s good for your health. It’s one of the main things we remember from childhood. (Fun read: Cassie’s [‘The Power of Traveling with an Infant—Even if They’ll Never Remember It.’](https://www.cntraveler.com/story/the-power-of-traveling-with-an-infant))

So, this week we’re bringing you:

-   Some news that will hopefully streamline some aspects of travel
    
-   Logistical reminders you might not know (about traveling with formula, pumping, or packing)
    
-   What’s worked for us over the years
    
-   A case for boredom
    
-   A critical tidbit about keeping little ears safe
    
-   Sheets that make your bed at home feel like a five-star hotel (the ultimate luxury)
    
-   And just for fun, some hotels we’ve visited and loved around the world
    

For us, travel reminds us that change is constant: Even when we return to the places we love over and over again, they’re different. *We’re* different. We go new places and we discover new things—about ourselves, our kids, and the world around us.

At its core, travel is about spending time together, venturing out, and the joy of experiencing a little bit of newness or nostalgia (or both), and there are *so* many ways to do that.

*—Cassie & Kelsey*

**Two things can be true:  
***I accidentally fell into the habit of traveling with almost nothing to occupy my kids. It wasn’t an intentional decision—and sometimes they’ll pack coloring supplies or something of the sort on their own—but often, they lose interest quickly. If we’re flying, sure, they’ll watch the TV in front of them on a plane, or they’ll come up with something else to do. I’m certainly not against packing toys for kids…and sometimes, I do! But I’m also a big believer in letting kids be bored; it’s something we’ve lost track of as adults. We can pretty much always be entertained. I write a lot about mental health and know the benefits of daydreaming and of simply…being. Travel is a wonderful time for kids to embrace boredom and develop their sense of curiosity about the world around them. —C*

# **» PS: A quick note about kids’ headphones**

Something I’ve learned through my health reporting: Volume-limiting features are a must for small ears; kids are more susceptible to noise-induced hearing loss. Good headphones made for kids limit the volume to 85 decibels. Continued exposure to noise above this level can be damaging. (Normal conversation rings in at about 60 dB; an average airplane cabin hits about [80 to 85 dB](https://www.cdc.gov/niosh/topics/aircrew/noise.html#:~:text=Hearing%20protection%2C%20especially%20during%20takeoff,75%2D80%20dBA%20during%20landing.).) Plenty of products today sell headphones that limit the volume to those levels. I’ve used [these](https://purosound.com/collections/headphones) from Puro and [these](https://happyplugs.com/en-us/collections/model-kids) from Happy Plugs. *—C*

# **The more you know**

-   **About flying while pregnant…** When I was about 30 weeks pregnant with my first daughter, my best friend and I took a trip to Sedona. We hiked the red rocks just before sunset, explored the Grand Canyon, drank tequila (her) and mocktails (me), and saw psychics. It’s a trip I’ll always remember fondly; my last before I became a mother. I [wrote about it—as well as what to know about traveling while expecting](https://www.cntraveler.com/story/what-to-know-about-flying-while-pregnant)—for *Condé Nast Traveler*. FWIW, The American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists (ACOG) [states](https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/committee-opinion/articles/2018/08/air-travel-during-pregnancy) that “in the absence of complications, occasional air travel is safe for pregnant women.” But the piece includes some other basics, like when you might need a doctor’s note to fly (it depends on the airline), potential risks (be sure to stay hydrated and move around), and tips for a comfier trip. *—C*
    
-   **About bringing formula and breast milk through airport security...** Although not every TSA agent is \*[quite up to speed](https://www.instagram.com/p/CdWne-wLvqL/?hl=en)\*, you can do it—and, yes, in quantities greater than 3.4 ounces. Formula, breast milk, and puree pouches are considered “[medically necessary liquids](https://www.tsa.gov/travel/security-screening/whatcanibring/items/baby-formula#:~:text=Checked%20Bags%3A%20Yes&text=quart%2Dsized%20bag.-,Formula%2C%20breast%20milk%2C%20toddler%20drinks%2C%20and%20baby%2Ftoddler,of%20presence%20of%20breast%20milk\).),” per TSA regulations. This policy holds for ice packs and other accessories required to keep things cool, too.
    
-   **About breastfeeding in public and private spaces…** It’s [legal](https://www.ncsl.org/health/breastfeeding-state-laws) in all 50 states, D.C., Puerto Rico, and the Virgin Islands. Period.
    
-   **About lactation rooms and pods…** The [Friendly Airports for Mothers Act](https://www.congress.gov/bill/115th-congress/house-bill/302/text?q=%7B%22search%22%3A%22Public+Law+115-254%22%7D&r=1#toc-HFDD0C59F6F4D416EAFC34303214EEFFF) required that all medium and large airports have a dedicated lactation area in every terminal by the end of 2021; the [Friendly Airports for Mothers Improvement Act](https://www.congress.gov/bill/116th-congress/senate-bill/2638?q=%7B%22search%22%3A%5B%22Friendly%20Airports%20for%20Mothers%20Improvement%20Act%22%5D%7D&r=1&s=2) stated small airports had to do the same by the end of 2023. Look for [Mamava pods](https://www.mamava.com/)—freestanding lactation stations in airports (and major transportation hubs like bus and train stations, and even Walmarts); you can find all of their spots [here](https://www.mamava.com/find-mamava). Or, if you want portable privacy, pack a comfy cover. We like these by [Bodily](https://shrsl.com/3yogb) and [Amma](https://weareamma.com/products/bone-cocoon?variant=15629613334630).

# **Booking it: Hotels we’ve visited & loved for family vacations, girls’ trips & adults-only weekends**

We visited [this](https://www.seaportboston.com/) Boston hotel for a “staycation” for my middle daughter’s birthday. It has an indoor pool, sweeping water views, and a surprisingly kid-friendly restaurant that serves bento box-style dinners. *—Cassie*

[This](https://ash.world/hotels/the-dean) posh 52-room hotel in downtown Providence is the perfect home base for a night out with friends, a concert, or an adults-only weekend.

You’ll wake up to mountain peaks out your window at [this](https://theglenhouse.com/) off-the-main-drag yet deep-in-the-mountains boutique hotel in New Hampshire. A [full-service outdoor activity hub](https://greatglentrails.com/) across the street and a sweet indoor pool make it perfect for even the littlest kids.

The views at [this](https://www.cliffhousemaine.com/) oceanfront resort overlooking an epic craggy coastline and the Atlantic will have you thinking you’re on the Amalfi Coast, not southern Maine. Bring the kids or go adults-only: There’s a beautiful lobby restaurant with walls of windows, a kid-friendly eatery, pools, and more.

Every time we visit family in Palm Springs, we book a “lazy river day” at [this](https://www.omnihotels.com/hotels/palm-springs-rancho-las-palmas/wellness/pool/splashtopia) gorgeous resort. A cabana reservation provides access to the property’s wide array of pool options, including ‘Splashtopia’—which boasts a beach, jacuzzi, waterslides, and splash pad all wrapped in a lazy river that we spend hours floating around. *—Kelsey*

My brother and sister-in-law live in Hawaii; we visited them this past Christmas, and [this](https://www.turtlebayresort.com/) is one of the more stunning resorts I’ve been to in a while—the only one on Oahu’s famed North Shore. Pretty much everything here works for kids, including a shallow pool with built-in chaise lounges, a beach for snorkeling, on-site stables with ponies, and two water slides. *—C*

The beauty of Hawaii + the magic of Disney! [This](https://www.disneyaulani.com/) resort and spa caters to families with a supervised kids’ club for ages 4 to 8, endless pool options and beach activities, shows and storytelling, character experiences (Moana fans, assemble!), and more. My sunrise yoga sessions here are a core memory! *—K*

[This](https://www.fairmont.com/en/hotels/boston/fairmont-copley-plaza.html) is my favorite hotel in Boston. We go every year around the holidays for dinner in its historic lobby restaurant, Oak Long Bar + Kitchen and (usually) an overnight. The kids love the resident black lab that snoozes in the lobby; we love the historic vibes and central location. *—C*

My college roommate is from Miami, so I try to make a point of going down there every few years; this year, I went twice. [This](https://www.carillonhotel.com/en/) oceanfront spot has the biggest spa and wellness center on the Eastern Seaboard. *—C*

My husband and I have stayed in [these](https://tulumhotelmiamor.com/) waterfront casitas on the rocky coast of Tulum twice now, and I have to physically restrain myself from going back again and again. It’s a bohemian dream within walking/biking distance of ancient ruins, beach access, and culinary gems. *—K*

# **Baby on board: A new bill that benefits families on the go**

The House just passed the Baby Changing on Board Act. This bill would require new Amtrak trains to be equipped with baby changing stations in all restrooms.

“Parents and caregivers shouldn’t have to worry about whether there’s a safe, clean place to care for their kids while traveling,” [said](https://underwood.house.gov/media/press-releases/underwoods-bipartisan-baby-changing-board-act-passes-house) Rep. Lauren Underwood. “I am so pleased that this legislation has passed the House and is now one step closer to becoming law. This common-sense, bipartisan legislation is a practical and necessary step to make sure families have access to the amenities they need on Amtrak.”
