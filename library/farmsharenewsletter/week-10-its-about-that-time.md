## Roasted Ratatouille Review

This is one of my all-time favorite dishes to make for its no-measure approach. It’s very forgiving, so don’t worry if you have more or less of one vegetable or another: it always works out.

***Let’s review:*** Gather all of your ratatouille vegetables: tomatoes, eggplant, peppers, onions, summer squash, and garlic.

[

![Summer vegetables. ](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!JfAS!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F02c5f447-6d1b-456a-a376-99f65e6cd996_680x453.jpeg "Summer vegetables. ")



](https://alexandracooks.com/2017/09/01/roasted-ratatouille-pasta/)

Roughly chop everything. As you chop, pile the vegetables into your largest roasting pan, seasoning each layer of vegetables with salt. Pour olive oil and vinegar over the top. Crack pepper over the top.

[

![A pan of vegetables on a kitchen counter. ](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!bQVX!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F2224711d-f55b-4425-b194-0047d1522f1d_680x453.jpeg "A pan of vegetables on a kitchen counter. ")



](https://alexandracooks.com/2017/09/01/roasted-ratatouille-pasta/)

If your pan looks like this, don't worry! (And don't stir either.)

[

![A pan of vegetables in the oven. ](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!Ob4X!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F31bc668d-4488-4d19-8d6b-54f11ee37141_680x453.jpeg "A pan of vegetables in the oven. ")



](https://alexandracooks.com/2017/09/01/roasted-ratatouille-pasta/)

Transfer the pan to the oven and roast at 400ºF for 45 minutes. At this point, when the vegetables have released some of their juices and have shrunk down considerably, you can remove the pan, and carefully give it all a stir.

Return the pan to the oven and continue to cook for 2 hours (or more or less), stirring halfway, until the vegetables become completely stewy, almost jammy in texture.

[

![A pan of roasted ratatouille. ](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!JOx7!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F507b051b-3293-4cec-9568-0beb901f2058_700x1050.jpeg "A pan of roasted ratatouille. ")



](https://alexandracooks.com/2017/09/01/roasted-ratatouille-pasta/)

Spread your ratatouille over toast, toss with pasta, use as a layer in a **[summer lasagna](https://alexandracooks.com/2017/09/21/roasted-eggplant-sauteed-swiss-chard-lasagna/)**, or simply eat with a spoon. Ratatouille freezes beautifully, too, so don't be afraid to pack it into quart containers, and stash it away for future use.

[

![Quart containers filled with roasted ratatouille. ](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!r3ha!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fbucketeer-e05bbc84-baa3-437e-9518-adb32be77984.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fc8ae823e-a9c8-476d-9e90-cbd02145f83b_680x453.jpeg "Quart containers filled with roasted ratatouille. ")



](https://alexandracooks.com/2017/09/01/roasted-ratatouille-pasta/)
