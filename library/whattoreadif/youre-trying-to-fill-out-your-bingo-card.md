Hi friends,

Welcome to August. Summer reading [Bingo cards](https://whattoreadif.substack.com/p/youre-ready-to-win-summer-reading-014) are due Friday, September 9 — a little over a month from now — so I thought it was a good time to spotlight a few favorite books that will help you get five-in-a-row.

And, now, what to read if …

# **You’re Looking for a “Beach Read” (however you define it)**

### [Lies and Weddings by Kevin Kwan](https://bookshop.org/a/22722/9780385546294)

I did not know *Lies and Weddings,* the latest from the author of the *Crazy Rich Asians* trilogy, was a retelling of Anthony Trollope’s *Dr. Thorne* until after I started. I also haven’t read *Dr. Thorne,* but am fortunate enough to have a friend, Aya Martin Seaver, who writes a [Trollope newsletter](https://ayamartinseaver.substack.com/).

She explains, “There’s a reason [Julian Fellowes adapted this novel](https://www.nytimes.com/2016/05/20/arts/television/review-julian-fellowes-hosts-doctor-thorne-a-british-period-drama-on-amazon.html) in 2016; it’s a classic Victorian story of marriage, romance and complicated property inheritance. Trollope begins with the mystery of Mary Thorne, a ward of Dr. Thorne’s: is she a lady or an illegitimate daughter of a country doctor? The stakes escalate when her childhood best friend Frank falls in love with her. Can they marry for love or must Frank restore his family’s wealth by marrying the face-cream heiress, Martha Dunstable? There’s a rich load of side characters, a confusing will and a secret baby.”

Kwan’s retelling, set in modern day, features Rufus Leung Gresham, the future Earl of Greshambury, whose mother, a former Hong Kong supermodel, is scheming to find him a rich wife to solve the family’s money problems. She figures her daughter’s multi-million-dollar wedding is the perfect place to find her son a bride. The only problem is that Rufus has no interest in going forward with the plan because he’s in love with Eden Tong, the literal girl-next-door who lives on the Gresham estate with her middle-class father.

While I’m not sure I loved *Lies and Weddings* quite as much as the *Crazy Rich Asians* books, it’s still frothy, fun and the perfect summer read. Set in luxe cities and hotels from Marrakesh to Hawaii, and featuring a hot mic scandal, a contested will and multiple Lamborghinis, I devoured this novel in a single weekend.

**Additional options include:** Adrienne Brodeur’s *[Wild Game](https://whattoreadif.substack.com/p/winter-counts-counterfeit-wild-game)* or *[It Happened One Wedding](https://whattoreadif.substack.com/p/you-consume-a-lot-of-pop-culture)* by Julie James

# **You Need to Check the “Book that Takes Place over the Summer” Box**

### [Now is Not the Time to Panic by Kevin Wilson](https://bookshop.org/a/22722/9780062913500)

Few books have ever transported me to a specific time in my life like *Now is Not the Time to Panic*. Listening to it, I felt like I was a teenager enjoying long summer days again.

[

![Now is Not the Time to Panic by Kevin Wilson*](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FKjy!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F458bc748-49d1-4b01-b977-1e31b61e5ef8_1592x2409.jpeg "Now is Not the Time to Panic by Kevin Wilson*")



](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!FKjy!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F458bc748-49d1-4b01-b977-1e31b61e5ef8_1592x2409.jpeg)
*Love this cut paper look.*

That’s where the similarities between me and the main characters — Frankie and Zeke — end. While I was a lifeguard, the two of them sparked a panic in their small town of Coalfield, Missouri, when they hung hundreds of posters with the phrase “The edge is a shantytown filled with gold seekers. We are fugitives, and the law is skinny with hunger for us.”

To the two teens, it means everything and nothing. As more and more posters appear, Coalfield residents become convinced it’s part of a sinister plot, and the constant fears have dangerous consequences. The friends never admitted their role in the “Coalfield Panic,” but 20 years later, when a reporter calls Frankie, now a successful author, and asks about it, it forces her to return to that summer.

There’s a reason *Now is Not the Time to Panic* hit [multiple best](https://www.usatoday.com/picture-gallery/entertainment/books/2022/02/22/usa-today-critics-favorite-books-of-2022/6643263001/) of [2022 lists](https://www.nytimes.com/2022/12/29/books/review/9-new-books-we-recommend-this-week.html). It’s a joy to read (the audiobook narrated by actress [Ginnifer Goodwin](https://libro.fm/audiobooks/9780063272651) is a particular treat) and a celebration of art, friendship and summer. Frankie and Zeke are the type of characters who worm their way into my heart and stay there. (Also: Kevin Wilson, on the off-chance you are reading this, I would love Shantytown merch – a print, a t-shirt, a mug?).

**Additional options include:** *[Out East](https://whattoreadif.substack.com/p/vacation-books-out-east-meant-well-quiet-zone)* by John Glynn or *[I Killed Zoe Spanos](https://whattoreadif.substack.com/p/dutch-house-killed-zoe-what-happened-lacey)* by Kit Frick

# **You’re Looking to Cross of the “Book with an Animal” Box**

### [Iona Iverson’s Rules for Commuting by Clare Pooley](https://bookshop.org/a/22722/9781984878649)

I was talking with a friend last week about how we both (perhaps weirdly?) enjoy our commutes. I use my time on the metro to read (no surprises there), catch up on my correspondence (text messages and emails) and, occasionally, just to think. For a book that celebrates the ride to work, check out Clare Pooley’s *Iona Iverson’s Rules for Commuting.*

[

![Iona Iverson’s Rules for Commuting by Clare Pooley](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sgnx!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd9b92a9c-ed88-49e9-a60d-fa7eb650a98c_1696x2560.jpeg "Iona Iverson’s Rules for Commuting by Clare Pooley")



](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sgnx!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fd9b92a9c-ed88-49e9-a60d-fa7eb650a98c_1696x2560.jpeg)

Every day, 57-year-old advice columnist and former It Girl Iona Iverson rides the train ten stops from her home to her office with her dog Lulu. Iona enjoys watching the other passengers and bestowing secret nicknames on them, but she never engages. That is until another commuter chokes while on the train one day. Sanjay, a nurse, performs the Heimlich maneuver on Piers, who Iona mentally refers to as “Smart-But-Sexist-Manspreader.” Sanjay’s actions save Piers’s life and spark a surprising friendship among the train riders.

I am a sucker for [any book](https://whattoreadif.substack.com/p/youre-spending-labor-day-on-a-hiking) about a [quirky group](https://whattoreadif.substack.com/p/the-girls-who-went-away-act-like-it) of people who come together to build an unlikely community and *Iona Iverson* fits that bill. Besides Iona, Sanjay and Piers, there’s Martha, a bullied teenager, Emmie, a twenty-something working at a digital ad agency and Jake, a personal trainer. All of them are a little lost — but I loved spending time with this bunch. It’s a timeless tale of overcoming life’s tough moments with the help of friends, without ever feeling trite or saccharine.

**Additional options include:** Mike Chen’s *[Vampire Weekend](https://whattoreadif.substack.com/p/vampire-weekend-why-we-read-never-blow-a-kiss)* or *[The Headmaster’s List](https://whattoreadif.substack.com/p/headmasters-list-everything-i-need-fishermen)* by Melissa de la Cruz
