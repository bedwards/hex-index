## Stories to Watch

### U.S. Government Shutdown & Mass Layoff Threats

Last week marked the beginning of a new federal [shutdown](https://www.usatoday.com/story/news/politics/2025/10/05/government-shutdown-weekend-trump-democrats/86506071007/) as Congress failed to pass budget legislation before the deadline (which, to editorialize for a moment: good. the Democratic party needs to do everything it can to obstruct the Trump administration’s unlawful, authoritarian squeeze—at minimum forcing them to compromise, and ideally halting their actions altogether).

The administration weaponized the standoff by threatening “irreversible” layoffs and altering federal employees’ out-of-office messages to blame Democrats (despite Republicans controlling the executive, judicial, and legislative branches), sending legal and ethical alarms [ablare](https://www.politico.com/news/2025/10/01/shutdown-agencies-hatch-act-00590757?fbclid=IwZXh0bgNhZW0CMTEAAR5IVAVBNvQul3laO-vYPPbnfVTtQxDguZoIX3xubAB4Tai1Hd8gPEbqROTARw_aem_uDiK-5PyORreNBDJV9mhpw). Essential services continued, but hundreds of thousands of federal workers faced furloughs and uncertainty, deepening public frustration and political brinksmanship.

**Flash Take:** This shutdown isn’t *just* a fiscal crisis; it’s a stress test for the administrative state under conditions of executive brinkmanship. By weaponizing the shutdown as a tool for structural downsizing and narrative control, the Trump administration is signaling a shift from temporary budget impasses to governance through disruption. Polycritically, this event shows us how fiscal tools can be deployed as instruments of political consolidation, weakening institutional trust and amplifying downstream shocks across governance, markets, and public services.
