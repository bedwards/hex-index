everywhere you go and whatever you might be doing as long as you have an electronic device with a microphone speakers or gyroscope that device might be listening not just your phone but also your laptop tablet smartwatch tv or your iot enabled home appliances are all participating in the most clandestine and unavoidable method of tracking node location and behavior ultrasonic cross-device tracking so what exactly is it and why is it the most egregious invasion of our privacy let's say you're watching your favorite tv program and as a regular viewer you have your phone with you or somewhere close in that room in the old days that will be the end of the story but in this day and age your tv is an audio beacon and your phone is a receiver beacons are emitting high frequency sounds and receivers are listening for those signals during commercial breaks your tv will emit ultrasounds that you won't be able to hear but your phone and your dog will you may notice your dog acting strange but it will notice your phone receiving those signals and listening for instructions in this case it will generate an identifier with information about you watching a particular tv program at a given time your phone will save this information and make it available for apps on your phone to read and transfer to advertisers what just happened there is called ultrasonic cross-device tracking it's a method of linking multiple devices of a user to track their behavior and location it works with all devices equipped with regular microphones speakers or even through gyroscope sensors ultrasonic audio beacons can be embedded in any form of medium tv programs websites online videos apps on your phone or even billboards and banners and it works both ways using ultrasounds audio beacons can always detect when your phone is nearby and likewise apps on your phone can listen for approximate audio beacons to track what you do and see this method of tracking is usually completely permissionless and secret you won't receive any notifications and oftentimes you'll never know what apps on your phone or websites you visit have ultrasonic beacons or receivers in
