Why and How Italy’s Invasion of Egypt Failed

by Kings and Generals · Kings and Generals · Read full article

In the late 1920s, fascist dictator Benito Mussolini rose to power in the only recently unified Kingdom of Italy, seeking to earn a place among the greatest European colonizers of the age like France and Britain. Mussolini increasingly urged imperial expansion, arguing Italy needed an outlet for its booming population. He envisioned a hedgemony over the Mediterranean Denubian Balkan region with further conquests stretching from the strait of Gibralta to the straight of Hormuz. When Hitler invaded Poland on September 1st, 1939, Mussolini hesitated to enter the war.

It was only on June 10th of 1940 at the very cusp of France's fall when he felt confident enough to declare war on the Allies. While the war in Europe raged, Mussolini's focus shifted to North Africa, where he dreamed of building a paniterranean empire that could revive the glory of ancient Rome.
