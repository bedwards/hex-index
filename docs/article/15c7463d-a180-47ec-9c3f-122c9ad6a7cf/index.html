Let’s get started:

  • Social media is fueling the decline of local trick-or-treating by promoting “destination neighborhoods,” which consolidates the activity into a few, often affluent, areas and turns a walkable community tradition into a car-dependent event.

  • Bangkok’s formalized moto-taxi system uses expensive, tradable jackets to limit driver supply, creating high barriers to entry and a stark divide with the massive, informal app-based gig economy. This regulated system, once a political win, has struggled to adapt and compete with the onslaught of ride-hailing apps.

  • Helvetas is building more resilient cities in Bolivia by strengthening urban planning capacity, promoting citizen participation through initiatives like innovation labs, and implementing digital tools. The model, now applied in 13 cities, uses nature-based solutions and circular economy principles to address climate change and social inequality.

  • A new academic article explores why 259 U.S. transit agencies adopted fare-free public transport during the COVID-19 pandemic. The study found that knowledge-sharing networks, the need for physical distancing, and providing financial relief to essential workers were key drivers.
