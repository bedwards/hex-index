Here it is, folks. The golden button. So, 2025 was a huge milestone for the channel. We passed 1 million subscribers, which is kind of a surreal thing to say out loud because that is just so many people.

I really can't wrap my head around that. I've been making YouTube videos since 2014. That's over a decade now. I have a lot more gray hair now than that floppy-haired grad student talking about the Dead Sea Scrolls.

But the number itself is just symbolic. What I'm more grateful for is who makes up that 1 million person audience. You, the Religion for Breakfast audience, are amazing. If you've ever watched one of these videos carefully, if you've ever shared it with a student or a friend or just helped make the conversation better in the comments, thank you so much.

This channel exists because of you. From the very beginning, I started this channel with a very straightforward goal. to improve your religious literacy. Not to defend religion, not to attack it, but to help people better understand what religion is, how it works, and why it matters in human history, human culture, and everyday life.

Sometimes that means publishing breadandbut religious literacy videos. Just looking back over 2025, I published what is holy, what is Coptic Christianity, what is alism, basic questions that you could see popping up in a classroom and deserve careful, academically grounded answers. And sometimes it means publishing videos that are a bit more outside the box that force us into religious studies, theory, and method. Are fandoms religious?

Are animals religious? Oh man, this poor hamster is just crying out to God. But the goal has always been to take religion seriously as a human phenomenon and to give people better tools for thinking about it without sensationalism and without oversimplification. And as we head into 2026, that mission feels more crucial than ever.

We've built real momentum, but the work of understanding religion is still unfinished. Spoiler alert, we didn't solve religious sectarianism. We didn't solve hatred or prejudice against particular religions, and we didn't solve the habit of talking about religion without actually understanding it. In fact, in some ways, the stakes around religious literacy feel higher now than when I started the channel over a decade ago.
