Our members and patrons have early access to the next six episodes. Become one by pressing the join button under the video or via the links in the description and pinned comment. The year is 1917.

World War I has been raging since the summer of 1914. In the West, Germany has been stalemated in the mud since October of the first year. In the east, it is a different story. Under Thesar, Russia struggles to hold the central powers back.

In late 1915, the Zar took personal command of the army against the recommendation of his closest advisers. His tenure as commanderin-chief of the army has seen devastating defeats at the hands of the central powers. From the lakes of East Prussia to the foothills of the Carpathians, the Russian army has failed. Nothing close to a successful offensive has been achieved, much less a decisive victory.

Indeed, the central powers have squeezed the Russian army to the brink of disaster. The front line had been pushed deep into imperial territory. German soldiers were at the Gulf of Ria in the north. In the center, Pinsk had been occupied.

In the south, the front followed the line of the Carpathians to the Black Sea. The war caused living standards to drop precipitously, and the unrest has been troubling the Zar's government for months. In March of 1917, protests across the Russian Empire broke out. When combined with the brutal repression of the Zsar government, war fatigue, and inattention to basic governance, these protests had all the ingredients required for an escalation.
