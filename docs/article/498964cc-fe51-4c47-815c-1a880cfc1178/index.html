The soil sample was collected in a remote Indonesian jungle by a Christian missionary. A few grams of dirt scooped into a sample bag and shipped to the United States. Little did anyone know what those few grams would contain. A drug so special that it Chinese characters literally mean the antibiotic of the ages.

It has been nearly 70 years since its market entry. In today's video, I want to pay tribute to an iconic compound, the unique, mysterious, infuriating, but life-saving antibiotic vancomy. For over three decades, our drug of last resort. Stafloccus Aurorius or staff as I shall call it is a spherical bacteria that appears in bunches.

They remind me of grapes, but like murder grapes. Staff has long been a special thorn in the medical world. For the purposes of our story, there are two things we need to know about staff. First, it is a grandpositive bacteria.

The term has a technical definition, but it means staff has a thick almost meshlike cell wall made from, among other things, pepidoglycin, a big molecule. The second thing to know about staff is that it sucks. When people are down on their luck, it is among the first to come and kick them, causing opportunistic infections that can get very serious, particularly in hospitals. Today, it is one of the leading causes of death by infection.

In 2017, the CDC estimated staff to be responsible for 119,247 hospitalizations and 19,832 deaths in the United States. A staff infection is no joke. What makes this joke even less funny is that staff grows and changes fast. It picks up some antibiotic resistant gene somewhere and then before it spreads like wildfire and now all the staff bugs are resistant.

Penicellin first entered the hospitals in 1941 immediately changing the prognosis of such infections. But almost as soon as physicians started using penicellin, staff started becoming resistant to it. I hate it when they do that. The first paper detailing this phenomenon was published in 1942, literally a year into the so-called penicellin revolution.

Penicellin belongs to a class of drugs called betalactums. And these work by inhibiting an enzyme that staff uses to produce his pepidoglycin cell walls.
