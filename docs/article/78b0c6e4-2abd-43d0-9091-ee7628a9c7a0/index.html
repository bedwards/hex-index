Wars are never simple. Despite the efforts of some governments, war resists the clarity, certainty or the clever narratives supported by AI slop videos we wish to impose on them.

The past week was yet another illustration of this phenomenon. Ukrainian forces achieved some of the most operationally significant gains of the past year, reclaiming a swathe of territory in the south while striking deep into Russia’s military-industrial complex. Diplomacy continued its chaotic and erratic journey.

The Iran War continues under the euphemism of “excursion” used by Trump this week. This reminds us of the “special military operation” description given to the Ukraine full-scale attack by Putin in February 2022, which was a way of camouflaging the reality of the war from the Russian people.

With casualties mounting, a new Iranian blockade in the Strait of Hormuz, and Trump pleading for European and Asian assistance in his latest social media post, hopes of a short war in Iran have ended. The Iranians now control the clock, and they are happy to keep this conflict rolling along until Trump grows weary of it. They can read the polling on Americans’ views about the war just like anyone else. Welcome to the longer, non-excursion war with Iran.

In the Pacific, Taiwan's Legislative Yuan paralysis on defence spending continues, and the U.S. has deployed U.S. Marines from Japan, and U.S. THAAD missile defence systems from Korea, to the Middle East. There has been a curious lack of air incursions around Taiwan by the PLA, and more pledges from the Japanese PM to increase defence capabilities.

Ukraine

The Ground War: Ukraine Seizes the Initiative in the South. The most important battlefield development of the past week, and perhaps for the past year, was Ukraine’s near-complete liberation of occupied territory in Dnipropetrovsk Oblast. On 10 March, Major General Oleksandr Komarenko, head of the Main Operational Directorate of Ukraine’s General Staff, confirmed to RBC-Ukraine that more than 400 square kilometres of territory had been recaptured since the start of the Oleksandrivka axis operation in late January 2026. Three small settlements remain to be cleared.
