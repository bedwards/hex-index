This is the Vancouver Seaw Wall, a 28 km bikeway that's so long it's actually considered the world's longest continuous waterfront bike path. It's a bit of a niche title, but 28 straight kilm of bikeway is still really impressive and even more impressive when you consider that lots of cities have parts of their waterfronts that look like this or or even this. I happened to be in Vancouver this past June and when I heard about the Seaw wall, I knew that I wanted to check it out. Actually, I wanted to do more than that.

I wanted to ride all 28 km from the Vancouver Convention Center downtown all the way out to Spanish Banks Park on the doorstep of the UBC campus. Would I make it all in one go or my feeble legs give out? And will I reach an epiphany about the merits of long-distance bicycle paths? Well, there's only one way to find out.

Let's ride the Vancouver Seaw Wall after the bike bell. First things first, I need a bike. I rented one from Spokes Bicycle Rental. I picked it up because it was close to the start of the seaw wall, but I just want to give them a quick review.

Professional staff, quality bikes, fast service, easiest rental I've ever done. Here's my trusty bike. I got an ebike, but I could only put it on the absolute lowest setting because otherwise I wouldn't have the range to get it back. That's a little foreshadowing for you.

Now that I have a bike, I need a map. Here's where I'm going. As far as I know, there's no official map of the Seaw Wall bike path. I've heard it starts at Crab Park, but I've also heard it starts at the convention center.

After some on-site reconnaissance, I think the convention center is the right starting point. Anyway, the path moves from the convention center through Cole Harbor, then enters iconic Stanley Park. It's larger than New York City Central Park and feels wilder. It's like a little slice of West Coast nature in the city.

After the seaw wall wraps around Stanley Park, it continues through the West End, then into the Falls Creek area. After pulling that loop, it heads through the Kitalano neighborhood before getting into Spanish Banks Park way over near the University of British Columbia. ...
