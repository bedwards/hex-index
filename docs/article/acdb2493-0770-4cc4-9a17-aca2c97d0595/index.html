Russia Fires Drones Into (NATO) Poland

On the night of the 9th to 10th of September, Russia launched another massive air strike against Ukraine. What made this attack unprecedented, however, was that Moscow directed more than 20 drones into NATO airspace, Poland. For the first time in history, Polish citizens received text message notifications warning of quote denutralization of objects that violated the territory of the Republic of Poland. This is an act of aggression that created a real threat to the safety of our citizens.

Read a statement from Poland's operational command of the armed forces. Polish and allied forces were placed on full alert. Four major airports in eastern and central Poland. Jesuona, Lubin, and Warso, Shopen, and Modin airports were shut down to give fighter jets free reign to track and engage incoming targets.

According to NATO's later statement, the response included Polish F-16s and Awax surveillance aircraft, Dutch F-35s, responsible for most of the shootowns, German Patriot batteries, and Italian refueling tankers. The last drone was downed at 6:45 a.m. local time. The drones identified so far were largely Gerbera type decoys, most of which fell after running out of fuel.

One reached as far as the locality of Hunishkov in central Poland and another drifted toward the Baltic coast. It cannot be ruled out, however, that some of the drones carried explosive payloads. In the town of Viri, one drone struck a residential building, causing significant damage. Footage shows a NATO fighter destroying a Russian drone like with an A9 or A 120 missile.

This marked the first time in history that NATO combat aircraft shot down Russian offensive weapons over alliance territory. Ukraine reported that Russia launched 458 aerial object that night, including 250 Ganas, the Russian Shaheds. Air defenses downed 413 with the reminder either slipping through or crashing over Poland. Polish Prime Minister Donald Tusk urged calm but confirmed that at least 19 drones had crossed into Polish airspace coming not only from Ukraine but also from Bellarus.

He announced that Poland had invoked article 4 of the NATO treaty. A mechanism for urgent consultations when an ally feels threatened. Meanwhile, President Carl Navroski declared that a meeting of the National Security Council would be convened within 48 hours. Moscow denied involvement, claiming there was no proof that drones were Russian.
