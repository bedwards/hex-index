Excerpt from the original article on Matt Bell

I returned home yesterday evening from a week spent in Baltimore at the AWP Conference, where (if you haven’t been) 10,000 or so writers and writing teachers gather annually to talk writing and reading, eat and drink literally everything in the city, shout ourselves hoarse, and go to innumerable late night off-site events in cafes and bars, each of which features thirty geniuses reading for seven minutes apiece, plus one bad actor who reads for fifteen. It’s one of my favorite events, in large part because so many of the people I love and admire will be there at the same time.

At this year’s conference, I gave one reading at an event generously organized by Ben Niespodziany (where I shared a little bit of the novel I just finished writing—thanks to everyone who said kind things afterward!) and two panels, one on “The Art and Craft of the Craft Book,” where I spoke alongside Heidi Czerwiec, Jehanne Dubrow, Jason Schneiderman, and Nicole Walker, and one on “Rewriting Stories That History Forgot,” with Sandra Simonds, Maurice Carlos Ruffin, and Joseph Scapellato. I also spoke at a memorial gathering for Dan Wickett, an old friend and mentor who was also my boss when I worked as an editor at Dzanc Books, as well as the founder of the Emerging Writers Network, an important early literary blog that championed so many writers of my generation.

Several people who attended my panels asked if I’d be publishing my comments somewhere, and this seems to be the appropriate spot! Thanks to everyone who came and wanted to see these notes again—including the lady in the audience who yelled at me to slow down my very fast speaking voice, haha. These are my notes for speaking, so they’re rougher than my usual fare here, but hopefully they’re still useful. After that, there’s a brief note about Dan’s celebration of life, plus an old story of mine and the Baltimore art that inspired it almost 20 years ago (and that I visited again this past week). It’s kind of a grab bag newsletter! Thanks for reading, as always, and thanks to everyone who said hi in Baltimore this past week. I came home feeling renewed and enthused to get back to writing, and I hope you all did too.
