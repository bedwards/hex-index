By Dan Silvestre · One Productivity ·Oct 20, 2024 · 3 min read

I started running in early 2021.

The first time I went for a run I lasted 8 minutes (where all start somewhere).

The whole time I was thinking:

This is stupid. And really, who likes to run anyway? I can’t do this. I need to stop.

…but I kept running.

Not long after, I got a watch… some running shoes… and started logging my runs.

Then, I completed a 5K…

Soon after, I completed a 10K…

Eventually, 15K…

And on September 26 2021, I completed a half-marathon in 2 hours:

Then December rolls around, and I completed my first marathon in Belize at 4 hours 12 minutes:

The journey of going from my 1st run to completing a marathon in less than a year was no walk in the park (no pun intended)…

…but frankly, there was no other option.

I had to invest hours of brutal training.

I had to run hundreds of sweaty miles.

I had to hire a coach with hard programs.

Why did I have to do all of that?

My body needed to be conditioned for what I was about to put it through.

My entire cardiovascular system needed an upgrade.

Nobody could do that for me except for me.

Sure, I had guidance…but when it was all said and done? It was up to me and me alone.
