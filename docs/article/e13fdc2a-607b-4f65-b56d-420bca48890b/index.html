Baseball IQ Weekly Recap (9/26 - 10/03)

By James LeDoux · Baseball IQ ·Oct 4, 2020 · 3 min read

Say what you will about whether the expanded playoffs are good for the league in the long run*, but the sheer quantity of playoff baseball this past week has been a shock to my system in a very good way. It feels like a reward for the late start and all those months where sports should have been on TV, but weren’t.

*My personal take is that it’s too friendly toward below-average teams. There’s no reason a team like this year’s Brewers should be rewarded for a season where they never once played above .500.

News from Around The League

  • The Unbridled Joy of Being—and Watching—Fernando Tatis Jr. (Sports Illustrated)

  • Bob Gibson has died at 84. Read this profile for an explainer on just how good he was (spoiler alert: he was very good) (STL Today)

  • Interviewing mic’d up outfielders mid-game is one of the most underrated and fun things in sports broadcasting today. Ramon Laureano had some colorful language to share when the ball went his way mid-interview (SB Nation)

  • This isn’t exactly news, but the playoffs are happening. There are several places to keep up with the games you can’t catch live. (mlb.com/postseason, ESPN’s playoffs page, etc.)
