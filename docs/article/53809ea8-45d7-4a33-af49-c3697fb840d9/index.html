We’ll put conference tournament probabilities here. There won’t be commentary for all of them, and what commentary there is will be poorly-researched with a few typos.

31. Ivy League

Come on, Ivies, get yourselves a real tournament. Yale won the regular season title and gets the gift of opening with a road game.

30. MEAC

Not much to say here. Howard is having the best season in program history, so there’s that.

29. Big West

The Big West ranks as the 11th-best league, breaking the high for the league set last season. They’ll undergo some membership changes next season. Among them is the departure of Hawaii, who’s trying to make their first NCAA tourney since 2016.

28. American

The AAC is relegated to one-bid status yet again.

27. Atlantic-10

Saint Louis is given a better shot at a bid than VCU by every single bracketologist despite the two being in adjacent spots in WAB (41st/42nd). And both are very near the at-large cut line.

26. WAC

The WAC closes up shop after this event. It was a remarkably resilient league that could have imploded after 8 teams fled the bloated 16-team league after the 1999 season. But it still sent multiple teams to the NCAA tournament as late as 2010. However, continued attrition took its toll on the league and reduced to 7 teams this season, it was forced to play a triple round-robin to fill out a conference schedule.

25. SEC

The SEC produced the highest-scoring games in league play, finishing first in average offensive rating and fourth in tempo. Five of the top eight seeds are in the top 30 in adjusted tempo and the league placed 8 of the top 21 teams in the country in offensive rating. So you should see a lot of points in these games.

24. Big East

There’s bound to be drama at MSG. For example, Xavier is one of nine teams to never finish outside the top 100 in my ratings. They’ve been around the cut line all season, enter the Big East tourney at #97. and may need to beat Marquette in the first round to keep the streak alive.
