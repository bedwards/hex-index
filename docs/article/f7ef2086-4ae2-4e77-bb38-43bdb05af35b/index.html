So, let's imagine you're a government minister of an imaginary country and your brief is to ensure energy security for every one of your citizens while also reducing greenhouse gas emissions as quickly and effectively as possible. It's a tricky one because you know you get lots of your energy by burning carbon-based fuels that release massive quantities of those gases into the atmosphere. You get some good news when you meet with a group of delegates from a completely new energy generation industry who explain that their technologies don't emit any greenhouse gases at all during their operational lifetime. You think that's an absolutely splendid solution and send them off to get cracking.

But then you meet with another group of delegates from the existing carbon-based energy generation industry and they tell you that those new fangled technologies are intermittent and you can't rely on them all the time. So, where's your energy security now? Hey, and anyway, they say you'd have to build out massive amounts of expensive infrastructure to accommodate the new stuff. Plus, you'd have to add lots of other complicated new technology onto your electricity grid to coordinate it all.

Luckily for you, though, they explain, they got a much better solution. They will simply bolt giant vacuum cleaners onto the smoke stacks of all their power stations and factories to suck up all their greenhouse gases and store them all neatly away somewhere. So they never get into the atmosphere in the first place. You think this is also an absolutely splendid solution until you start reading through the small print.
