Almost every country in the world today has a central bank. In fact, there are only two countries in the world that have their own currency and do not have a central bank. And those are Touvalu with a population of just 9 half,000 people and Kiraabati with 132,000 people. Now, despite being such tiny counter examples, they realistically also both generally default to using the Australian dollar for most serious transactions anyway.

The reason that central banks have become a nearly universal feature of every economy on Earth is because they work. Especially in our highly integrated global economy with dozens of floating fiat currencies, they have become a check and balance on economic recklessness. They are an emergency backs stop in times of crisis, a partially independent regulator on inflation and employment. And beyond that, there are the slightly more boring aspects of central banks as institutions that handle a lot of the administration that goes into keeping a currency functional.

These are all jobs that have become significantly more difficult in recent years as the world becomes more integrated, debts continue to balloon, and inequality undermines the tools that these banks have to work with. And of course, to make matters worse, all of these responsibilities have been called into question as the US government has tossed up the idea of scrapping the Fed altogether. Something that's caught the attention of everybody from global bank executives to conspiracy theorists that think the whole show is run by lizard people. Now, from the get- go, realistically, this is very unlikely to actually happen.

The Fed is the most powerful financial institution on the planet, and unwinding its place in the global economy, would be borderline impossible. But it's also not without precedent. Central banks have been abolished before, including in the USA. And what's more is that the actual effectiveness of these institutions has been called into question more and more, as they not only have to tame their economies, but they are increasingly often at odds with their own governments to do so.

With such powerful institutions acting in such unintuitive ways with so much of a consequence on our everyday lives, it's almost understandable that people just assume that something sinister is going on in these places.
