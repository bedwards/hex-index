One of the largest airports in the world is being built in the Philippines. Twice as big as JFK International and nearly three times bigger than Sydney Airport, New Manila International will engulf a staggering 2,500 hectares of land. And once complete, it'll be able to welcome 100 million passengers every year. Oh, and within 30 years, sinking land and floods could render this $15 billion project completely defunct.

In its bid to lead the way for Southeast Asia, the Philippines is taking an enormous gamble on Namia or New Manila International Airport. You see, the nation's biggest and busiest airports, Nino International, is completely oversubscribed. Last year, it welcomed 50 million people through its terminals. That's even higher than prepandemic numbers.

But Nino Ayino wasn't designed for this level of traffic. In fact, it was only meant to handle between 35 and 40 million passengers every year. And that's led to this place getting a reputation for major crowding and delays. When airports run into this issue, they often go for expansion to solve their problems.

It's generally a lot simpler to keep the main logistics hub in one place, not to mention a heck of a lot cheaper than building a whole new airport. But there's a problem. Nino Ayino is completely locked in. It's surrounded by thousands of homes split into baron gays.
