  • Ben Franklin effect

    The article uses this psychological phenomenon to explain why asking a favor from someone can paradoxically increase their affection for you, serving as a tactical tool for initiating adult friendships.

  • Propinquity

    While the author laments the difficulty of making friends without the forced proximity of school or office, this concept explains the scientific mechanism behind why shared physical spaces and repeated unplanned encounters are the primary drivers of new social bonds.

  • Interpersonal ties

    The article's focus on expanding a social circle beyond close family and cousins highlights the sociological importance of 'weak ties'—casual acquaintances who often provide the bridge to new communities and opportunities that tight-knit groups cannot.
