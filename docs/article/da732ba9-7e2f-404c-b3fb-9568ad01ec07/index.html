France has not been having a great time lately. It's been almost 2 years to the day since we last looked at its economy. And within that time, what was already a country going through some dramatic changes has clearly become even more turbulent. Within just that time since our last update, they have been through five different prime ministers, a clear sign of deep political uncertainty.

Now, there are a variety of factors contributing to the minister's musical chairs and some fringe issues to go beyond purely economics. But at the heart of this all is the simple fact that the French economy is stuck between several rocks and several hard places. The economy has not seen sustained growth for almost two decades now, which by itself is not ideal, but it's also not inherently devastating. France has historically prided itself on its egalitarian culture, strong social safety net, robust workers rights, and overall very high standard of living.

Most countries in the world, by comparison, would be blessed to freeze at this level of prosperity. The problem really is that this isn't sustainable stagnation. Every year, the country grows older, putting a larger nominal burden on those famously generous retirement programs. Debt has been creeping up pretty consistently to fill in the gap, but that can't go on forever.

The country could tax young workers more to fund these problems, but they are already struggling with consistent brain drain. Immigration could fill in those skills gaps, but that's become increasingly unpopular, not only as a social flash point, but also as another economic trend that is arguably just kicking the can down the road. If the country can't tax more, can't responsibly take on more debt, can't supplement its labor force, and can't cut benefits, then it really is just destined to flip-flop between politicians who promise they have the magical cure to an impossible situation. Now, all of this is clearly true for France.

But the thing is, it's also not unique to France. Almost every major developed economy in the world right now is dealing with basically the same set of unforgiving variables, but France seems to be taking it much harder than most.
