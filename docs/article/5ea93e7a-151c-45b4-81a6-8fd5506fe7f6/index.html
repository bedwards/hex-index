Hi, I'm Kushian Avdar and this is Crash Course, Futures of AI. To help us understand exactly how it could transform our world, some researchers like to break down AI into levels. The first level is narrowly transformative AI. This is the kind of technology that could lead to practically irreversible change, but only in a specific area like the spinning jenny did for textiles.

But some AI models have the potential to change all the most important aspects of our society. We call that second level transformative AI and it's on par with inventions like electricity and the internal combustion engine which transformed our entire world. And then there's another third level, a theoretical level that we're building up to that could make even bigger changes, ones we can't exactly imagine yet. radically transformative AI could impact not just our day-to-day lives, but how we measure progress, find worth, and think about our place in the world.

Most people think AI with human level intelligence, known as artificial general intelligence, or AGI, would be radically transformative. It's hard to predict when AGI will hit the streets or the cloud. Some people think we're really close, like a year or two away, but some others put that number closer to the year 2030 or even 2050. A small subset of people think we're centuries away from AGI, if we achieve it ever.

But even without AGI, we're already in for some pretty big world restructuring. One really clear way we're seeing AI restructure our world already is in the economy. And to help us understand exactly how, the industrial revolution is probably the most helpful example.
