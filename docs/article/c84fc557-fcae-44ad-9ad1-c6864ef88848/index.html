The worst productivity system ever created

By Dan Silvestre · One Productivity ·Oct 24, 2024 · 3 min read

Albert Einstein famously said:

“If you can’t explain it simply, you don’t understand it well enough.”

I’d add to that and say:

“If you can’t execute it simply, you don’t understand it well enough.”

What do I mean?

Well, lots of business leaders and entrepreneurs like to build big, complex teams and systems.

They live in a self-created storm of complexity, and everyone suffers because of it. 

Believe it or not, I used to be one of those guys. 

A few years ago, I felt like I had the secret to productivity.

I spent countless hours researching and building what I thought was the best productivity system in the world.

The only problem?

I spent more time working on my system…than, you know, actually…working.

In my pursuit of building the best productivity system, I found myself doing the exact opposite of what a world-class productivity system does.

So, I took some time to reflect, and I realized something…

A great productivity system helps you:

  • Prioritizes what’s important.

  • Finds time for deep work.

  • Stays on top of things so nothing falls through the cracks.

And a great system runs like this:

  • Quick setup time — up and running in a few minutes.

  • Highly flexible — that’s the nature of your work.

  • Easy to maintain — it’s the support system, not the main act.

I got back to work.

When it was all said and done, the Minimalist Productivity System resulted from hundreds of iterations…

… Boiled down to the absolute minimum number of things required. 

It runs on 3 apps only.

Listen, the fact that you’re reading my emails says one thing: 

You’re ahead of the curve when it comes to productivity and performance.

That’s your strength, however, at a moment’s notice it will turn into your weakness…

… That is, if you fall down the “complexity rabbit hole.”

Take it from someone who’s been there, done that…

Minimalism > Complexity

This is the way.

Simple is hard, but simple is better.

Simple takes years and years of boiling down complexity to its bare bones.

Not everyone makes it to the end of the road like I have.

Albert Einstein probably knew that better than anyone.

And that’s one reason why I’m so proud of my Minimalist Productivity System.

Normally…this system is locked up inside the Elite Performer Vault.
