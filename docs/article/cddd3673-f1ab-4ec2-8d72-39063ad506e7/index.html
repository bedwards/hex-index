Are we good to go? Whereas the petal known as the pollinator was first conceived and brought into existence by one Joshua Heath Scott in the state of Mississippi on the second day of May in the year of our Lord 2009. Born not of some grand creative vision, but of suffering, specifically the ungodly near biblical amount of pollen that descends upon the American South each spring. A substance so relentless and abundant that it rendered the creator functionally blind, swollen of face, and dependent upon rotating pharmaceuticals of antihistamine just to exist as a person.

And whereas the afor pollinator was so named because of its fuzz circuit produced a tone not unlike the way its creator felt during pollen season. Thick, aggressive, slightly unhinged, and impossible to ignore. And whereas the Pollinator did serve faithfully across two versions, the version one and the version two, providing Hendrick's adjacent tones to the masses, cleaning up beautifully with the guitar's volume knob and generally conducting itself with honor upon pedal boards both great and small. And whereas the aforementioned Pollinator was in due course retired from active production in the year of our Lord 2017, not in disgrace, but in dignity.

its circuits folded, its bias knobs stilled, its germanmanium transistors returned to a state of peaceful rest. And whereas there exists in the city of Los Angeles, in the great state of California, a company known as Betronics, a family operated guild of sonic artisans who by choice or by fate have devoted their entire professional lives to the aesthetic, spiritual, and tonal legacy of the bee. And where and whereas it has come to the attention of the grantor that no name in the history of effects pedals has ever been more naturally more cosmically more obviously belonged to a bee themed pedal company than the name Pollinator. A fact so self-evident that the grantor is frankly embarrassed he did not think of this sooner.
