Transcript excerpt on Kings and Generals

The 1500s to the 20th century were an a of colonial expansion when the nations of Europe dominated much of the world. This domination was achieved primarily through their naval technology which saw great powers like Spain, France, and especially Britain outpace the rest of the world with ships of wood and sail and later powered by steel and steam. The British Royal Navy being the world's supreme power was confirmed after the legendary battle of Trfalga in 1805. That was until the rise of the German Empire some 70 years later.

Kaiser Wilhelm II sought to rebalance Europe, giving a speech in Hamburg on June 18th of 1897 in which he remarked that the British Navy is strong enough to defy any hostile combination. Germany has practically speaking no navy. Before everything else, I must provide myself with a navy. In 20 years time, when the navy is ready, I shall speak a very different language.

Welcome to our video on the naval arms race between Germany and Britain in the leadup to the World Wars in which the rising star of Central Europe challenged the traditional juggernaut of the seas while the Royal Navy fought to retain its supremacy.
