#29: NYC's Urban Textscape, Helsinki Eliminates Traffic Deaths, and Vacation Homes

By Raymond Berger · Urbanism Now ·Aug 5, 2025 · 3 min read

Let's get started:

  • Helsinki has gone a full year without a single traffic-related fatality, a remarkable feat achieved via new speed limits, speed cameras, and many infrastructure changes. (Submitted by Oriane)

  • A new visual essay from The Pudding explores NYC’s “urban textscape” by recording text from signs, graffiti, and advertisements found in public spaces using Street View. A pretty amazing technical feat along with a great analysis.

  • An analysis from Construction Physics shows that over half of America's 4.8 million vacation homes are packed into just 4% of its census tracts. These homes are limited by the same low construction productivity and restrictive zoning that impacts housing in cities.

  • Chronotrains, lets you see how far you can travel by train from any European station in up to five hours, using clever isochrone mapping and real travel data. It’s a fun, practical tool for trip planning or exploring Europe’s best, and worst, connected cities.
