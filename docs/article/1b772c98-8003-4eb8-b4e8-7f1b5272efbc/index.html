Longevity Resources [updated Aug 13, 2021]

By Nathan Cheng · Longevity Marketcap ·Aug 13, 2021 · 4 min read

Here’s a list of longevity science and industry resources to get you started. If you have any suggestions please leave them in the comments below.

Blogs / Posts

  • Laura Deming’s Short Longevity FAQ - A > 10 min read on the basics of longevity science.

  • Jose Luis Ricon’s Longer Longevity FAQ - A more in-depth read on the biology of aging.

  • Hallmarks of Aging - A review (2013) of nine different proposed mechanisms of aging. Cited by everyone but getting a little old. There’s still a lot of debate over what causes aging, but these hallmark targets are a good start and some are well-validated in mice (and being tested in humans today).

  • Fight Aging! Blog and Newsletter - by Reason. The newsletter is a weekly summary of the newest anti-aging science papers and news.

  • How to build a biotech - Celine Halioua’s writings. (Founder @ Loyal)

  • How to start a biotech company in the longevity industry - a guide written by Reason (Fight Aging! / Repair Biotechnologies)

Websites

  • Longevity.technology - Industry and science news website. Daily newsletter.

  • Lifespan.io - Non-profit organization, news, longevity research crowdfunding platform, resources

  • Agingbiotech.info - database of longevity companies and resources

  • Longevity List - longevity industry jobs board, company database, investor database

  • Longevity Marketcap - list of publicly traded longevity biotechs

  • Aging Analytics - databases on companies, trials, organizations, people in longevity. From DKV.

Podcasts

  • Translating Aging Podcast - Sponsored by BioAge Labs, features interviews with the leading founders, scientists, and investors in longevity.

  • The Longevity Biotech Podcast - Interviews with founders and investors in longevity biotech. Hosted by Nathan Cheng and Robert Ziman.

  • The Simple Biotech Podcast - James Ruhle interviews founders and CEOs of (mostly) longevity biotech companies.

Books

  • Juvenescence: Investing in the Age of Longevity - written by Jim Mellon, patron saint of longevity investing / British billionaire. Covers both science and industry.

  • Lifespan: Why we age — and why we don’t have to - written by David Sinclair, professor of Genetics at Harvard. A good book for general audiences.

  • Ending Aging - written by Aubrey de Grey, director of SENS Research Foundation. The book that started the revolution for many people (including myself)

  • Replacing Aging - written by Jean Hebert, professor of neuroscience and genetics at Albert Einstein College of Medicine.
