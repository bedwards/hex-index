Iran is simultaneously one of the most powerful and vulnerable economies in the world. It has immense and often unwelcome influence over its region, the largest energy reserves of any country on the planet, and a massive population that is remarkably well educated. On top of that, its position in the world could arguably not be more ideal. It's strategically placed with a large coastline along some of the busiest trade routes on the planet, and it's almost exactly at the center point of the global population, making potential business with both established centers in Europe and rapidly developing markets in Asia a golden opportunity.

This isn't a theoretical possibility either. Directly across the Persian Gulf, its neighbors Qatar, Bahrain, the UAE, Aman, and even Saudi Arabia are all enjoying the spoils of their fabulous oil wealth. And yes, they clearly have problems of their own. But with an average GDP per capita 10 times higher than Iran's, they at least have the wealth to pretend those problems don't exist.

So yeah, the problem is that despite all of these advantages, Iran is also one of the most vulnerable countries in the world. Its economy is based heavily on oil and gas exports, which is bad for any country at the best of times because it creates dependency on a single volatile industry that is extremely easy to exploit. But of course, for Iran, it's even worse because it's hard for them to even sell the oil that they do have. Iran's economy has almost become defined by the sanctions that it's lived under for the past 45 years.

Trading directly with a country outside of very narrow exceptions is enough to get individuals, companies, and even entire national economies blacklisted from global trade and finance. This endless tension, of course, came to a head a few weeks ago when the US conducted strikes on the nation's believed nuclear facilities. Something that was a not so subtle escalation from its conflict with Israel, the de facto rival regional power. Now, this video is not about those strikes or speculation on what they may mean for the future.
