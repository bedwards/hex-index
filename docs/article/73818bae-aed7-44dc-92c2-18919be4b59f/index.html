Explore these related deep dives:

  • Antipodes

    The narrator's claim that the 'old geography' recognizes the Antipodes provides the crucial historical context for the story's central conflict: the Glaziers' doctrine that the dome is a fixed sphere versus the narrator's knowledge that the world extends beyond it.

  • Cocalus

    Identifying Cocalus as the legendary King of Sicily who sheltered Daedalus explains the story's specific allusion to a ruler who 'needed us for our expertise' while simultaneously plotting to eliminate the very craftsmen he relied upon.

  • Celestial spheres

    The article's mention of 'twenty-seven celestial spheres' subverts the standard medieval cosmology of ten or eleven spheres, requiring the reader to understand the historical evolution of this model to grasp the Glaziers' fabricated theology.

Page 9

I was only a young man, yet I distinctly recall the unease I felt when the Glaziers emerged as the City’s most revered class of men. They set about pouring sand into their furnaces, devising new ways to make glass as thick as bricks yet as translucent as air. No one could have imagined before their efforts began what wonders may be conjured out of heat and silica, ingenuity and persistence.

We live beneath glass here in the City, all the time, but we are reminded of this in practice only when we attempt to walk away from it, or when, as happens several times a day, we hear the sad thunk1 of an oblivious bird, and then look up to see it sliding down the invisible slope. And of course we are constantly reminded of the dome by the Glaziers themselves, who have by now made it official doctrine in the City that there are twenty-seven celestial spheres in all,2 the closest of them being the one they themselves built, which, they insist, shares in the same nature as the most distant and empyrean of them all.

Page 10

The children of the City —can we even call them that? anyhow the Citizens generated after the construction of the dome— do not know that it was constructed at all. They believe it is as fixed and eternal as the other twenty-six spheres, and that it is paired, beneath the ground, with a complementary hemisphere invisible to us. But you cannot erase the memories of those of us old enough to have been here bedore its construction. You can kill us, which seems to have been one of the strategies Cocalus considered some years back, though in the end he understood he needed us for our expertise in sundry fields, such as mine, in botany.
