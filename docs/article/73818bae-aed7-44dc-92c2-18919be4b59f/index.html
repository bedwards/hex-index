Deep Dives

Explore these related deep dives:

  • Antipodes

    The narrator's claim that the 'old geography' recognizes the Antipodes provides the crucial historical context for the story's central conflict: the Glaziers' doctrine that the dome is a fixed sphere versus the narrator's knowledge that the world extends beyond it.

  • Cocalus

    Identifying Cocalus as the legendary King of Sicily who sheltered Daedalus explains the story's specific allusion to a ruler who 'needed us for our expertise' while simultaneously plotting to eliminate the very craftsmen he relied upon.

  • Celestial spheres

    The article's mention of 'twenty-seven celestial spheres' subverts the standard medieval cosmology of ten or eleven spheres, requiring the reader to understand the historical evolution of this model to grasp the Glaziers' fabricated theology.
