Eno is ch-ch-ch-changing

By Gary Hustwit · Gary Hustwit's Films ·Aug 29, 2024 · 4 min read

Generation 4 of “Eno” is coming…

One of most interesting aspects of using a software-based platform to dynamically create a film is that it can continue to evolve over time. We can add more footage to the film’s data set, we can continue to develop the software’s functionality, or we can change the narrative arc after receiving audience feedback. The film and the storytelling structure itself can continually develop.

“Eno” is currently in Generation 3, which was the third major update of the film and software. But starting in mid-September, it will move into Generation 4, with more new scenes and footage added into the mix, and more real-time generative actions by Anamorph’s latest Brain One software. It’s not going to be radically different from Gen 3, it’s more of an evolution into its next form, which in itself is a fascinating thing to think about: we can now make films that evolve.

Almost everything we’re doing with “Eno” is breaking ground in some way, and it’s exciting to keep experimenting with the film and this new approach to cinema. I’m looking forward to showing you all Gen 4 in September, stay tuned for more details.

Cheers!
-Gary
