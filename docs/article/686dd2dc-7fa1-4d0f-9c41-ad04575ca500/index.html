Why did NYC Build a Park in the Middle of a River?

Dave Amos · City Beautiful ·Nov 28, 2025 · 18 min read
Commentary by Brian Edwards
Original video →
Transcript excerpt on City Beautiful

This is Little Island. It's not an island exactly, but 132 concrete tulips that create a 1.5 football pitch-sized park in the Hudson River on the west side of Manhattan. I've been wanting to visit it since it opened in 2021, and I finally got the chance to go a couple months ago. I'm glad I went.

It's really, really cool. Let me show you around. There are two paths onto the island, and perhaps the most dramatic one is this one where you go right underneath some of the tulip structures. Once you're inside, there's a whole little world to explore, and the entire design seems to invite you to do just that.

Serpentine paths wind their way up and down the very topography created by the tulips, and you're always surrounded by lush native plants native to New York, not the concrete tulips. In addition to these winding paths, there are these staircases nestled into the landscape that create little shortcuts up toward the top corner of the island. It's kind of like the board game Candyland and the stairs of the Rainbow Trail and Gumdrop Pass. Perhaps even more charming than the stairs are the little rocky trails that let you scramble up and down from one part of the path to the other.

It almost looks like you aren't allowed to do it, but in fact, you are. It's the kind of place kids would have fun climbing on. Most of the paths on the western side of the island lead to a vista point at one corner of the park. The views of lower Manhattan from the Hudson River are excellent, and it was definitely a popular spot for selfies and group photos.

You can also get a nice view of New Jersey. Well, I guess I wouldn't call that view nice, but you get it. The winding path also connects to a large amphitheater complete with 687 seats and another view of the Hudson River. There are more paths to the northern corner with somewhat less interesting views than the previous corner, but still a fun walk nonetheless.

The center of Little Island is the main lawn. There's a plaza space with food kiosks and shade. It's a pretty nice vibe overall. Finally, you're allowed to hike up and over the arched entrance to the park via an extremely windy path.
