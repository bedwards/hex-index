India Just Built an Airport in a Swamp

This might just be India's answer to Changi. It's called Navim Mumbai International Airport and it opened its terminals to passengers in December last year. The place looks pretty stunning and that's just phase 1. Once the final phase is complete, Navi Mumbai will welcome an astounding 90 million passengers a year, 20 million more than Changi.

India wants this to become a hub for world travel. Now, you'd be right to raise an eyebrow to any newcomer being compared to Singapore's Pride and Joy. That is arguably the best airport in the world. But don't forget that Changi was a military air base long before it became a civilian facility.

The engineers behind Navi Mumbai could only dream of building on an air base. Their reality was entirely different. They were tasked with building an airport on a swamp covered in unstable mud flats, a giant hill, and a river flowing right through the middle of it. While the renders of Navi Mumbai International look spectacular, this airport's location has been an absolute nightmare.

India has one of the fastest growing aviation sectors on the planet behind only China and the US. The number of operational airports in the country has doubled in the last decade as Prime Minister Narendra Modi looks set to overhaul India's travel infrastructure. Mumbai, its financial powerhouse is under resourced. A number of the world's most prominent cities feature multiple major airports.

Think London, New York or Tokyo. And that's obviously crucial for visitor numbers, business trips and cargo. Let's take London for example. The city is serviced by six major airports.

All of those combined handled a whopping 177 million passengers in 2024. Mumbai, however, has historically had just one major airport. That would be like London having just Heathrow. The city would miss out on nearly 100 million passengers every year.

And given that rapidly growing aviation sector in India, the country is desperate to improve capacity to its financial capital. The answer authorities came up with was simple. a second major airport in Mumbai. But executing an idea of this scale is rarely as straightforward as having the epiphany itself.
