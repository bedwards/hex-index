Even amongst all the horrors of Ashvitz, there was one place that prisoners feared more than anywhere else. Block 11, the punishment block. This was where prisoners who were considered to have broken certain rules were taken to be punished and often murdered. But this block was also known for something else.

It was here in this basement in September 1941 that the Nazis first use Cyclone B for the purposes of mass murder. Around 850 prisoners, Soviet prisoners of war and Polish political prisoners were murdered in this block. What the experiments conducted here proved to the Nazis is that Zyclon B worked. What it also showed them is that they needed to get the quantities right because the first attempted mass execution here lasted two or three days.

The Nazis used the information that they gleaned to calculate how much Cyclon B will be necessary for mass murder. But what they also realized is that this wasn't the place to do it. There were too many small rooms and it took too long to ventilate. They knew that they needed to build a purpose-built facility and that is what led to the first gas chamber at Ashvitz.

Between 1940 and 1945, 1.1 million people were killed here. The vast majority murdered in the purpose-built gas chambers. My name is Dr. James Bulgian.
