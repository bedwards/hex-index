Like many Turk peoples throughout history, the early Ottomans initially developed as a primarily warrior nomadic society. In its earliest form, the Turk tribes of Central Asia used conventional nomadic tactics of warfare, emphasizing mobility and light cavalry.

For many centuries, the Turkish peoples employed such tactics against settled peoples from China to Eastern Europe with great success. From the mighty Gtkurks to the lethal Bulgars and the rampaging page and Kumman Kipteks, Western and Eastern monarchs alike trembled in fear behind the safety of stone walls as such nomadic groups raided their lands. However, over time, these nomadic groups adopted the military systems of the settled peoples they conquered and integrated with, thereby abandoning their ancestral traditions. An early example of such a shift can be seen with the Seljic Turks of the 11th century, who had encountered both the Islamic and Byzantine worlds during their conquests of the Middle East.
