We still don't understand the 20th century

If you time travel, speed ran from January the 1st, 1900 to January the 1st, the year 2000, what would you make of the history of the 20th century? Well, you might notice some strange new technologies, the internet, some advanced medicines and machinery and television. Sure. But what about intellectually?

What about in the realm of ideas, the big questions, the direction of history? What about how you feel about the world? Well, you might notice that on the eve of the First World War in 1914, there were just three republics in Europe. The map was dominated by empires and monarchies.

Religion had been on the decline, sure, but in 1900, it's still a dominant force. Most lives still in some way revolve around local, physical, community issues and the church. By 2000, all of this seemed as if it belonged to a strange old ancient world. You'd be struck by how the Austrian Hungarian Empire is gone entirely.

The last empires disappeared from the map. how national self-determination is the norm pretty much everywhere. Colonialism has all but disappeared. Almost everyone, including women and African-Ameans in the West, extraordinarily have the vote.

You'd certainly notice the rise and fall of the Soviet Union. You'd be struck by the extent of the welfare state in Western Europe, especially. In fact, you might very quickly buy into the idea of history as progress. The year 2000 is looking quite good for many, you might say.

Then the years tick on. 2001, 2008, 2016, 2020. Some eventful years. I think if you're that time traveler, you'd be struck by an uncanny feeling of familiarity.

Maybe things you'd think aren't as different as they first appeared. terrorist attacks, global financial crashes, a pandemic, a Russian autocrat, crises of democracy, populism, the revolt against globalism, increasing authoritarianism, echoes of the early 20th century in the 21st. History not repeating exactly, but certainly rhyming. Both centuries start with a unipolar world.

The British Empire and the American Empire being challenged by increasing multipolarity. Both see tariffs and nationalism on the rise. And here's the crucial thing we might ask our time traveler.
