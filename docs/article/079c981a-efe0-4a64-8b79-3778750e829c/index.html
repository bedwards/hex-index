She Honked at ICE. They Shot Her Five Times

by Devin Stone · LegalEagle · Read full article

On Saturday, October 4th, Chicago preschool teacher Maramar Martinez was driving to church to donate clothes to needy people. She wound up in a hail of bullets fired by Border Patrol agents, after which the FBI dragged her out of her hospital bed and arrested her for assault with a deadly weapon. A month later, the charges were dropped. Turns out the ICE agent's story about getting boxed in and rammed by a convoy of 10 cars didn't really add up.

He's now under investigation for perjury. And now that monastery preschool teacher is suing ICE for attacking her. So, how did everything go so wrong in the Windy City? How on earth did this doomed prosecution ever get filed?

And what does it reveal about the surveillance state being secretly constructed by the Department of Homeland Security to spy on American citizens? On September 8th, the Department of Homeland Security announced Operation Midway Blitz, the immigration draget in greater Chicago. DHS commemorated the occasion with hundreds of social media posts, as is their habit, and Customs and Border Patrol's commander at large, Greg Bavino, made sure to post lots of slick videos of himself preparing to invade the city. The counterrevolution will definitely be televised.

In practice, ICE CBP's attack on Chicago looked like this. And unsurprisingly, that made the city's residents very mad because while Secretary Christine Owen said they were going after the worst of the worst, >> make sure that these child pedophiles, these rapists, and these murderers are off our streets, most of the people swept up had no criminal record at all. >> Construction workers, landscapers, and nannies seem to be a target. >> And so residents banded together to warn each other about the presence of ICE in their neighborhoods.

On October 4th, Marramar Martinez saw a Chevy Tahoe carrying border patrol agents driving through her neighborhood and began following it. She honked her horn and shouted La Migra out the window, which is not illegal. It may be annoying or unpleasant for the Customs and Border Patrol agents in the car, but that's still protected by the First Amendment. At some point, another car driven by a Chicago resident named Anthony Ruiz also began to follow the agents and sound the alarm.
