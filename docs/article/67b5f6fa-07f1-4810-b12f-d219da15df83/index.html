In 1955, Walt Disney opened Disneyland in Anaheim, California, introducing the world to his vision for a new thing, a theme park. More than a mere amusement park, Disney's park would be clean, well-kept, a place for family entertainment. It would feature a main street modeled loosely on his hometown of Marceline, Missouri. Within Disneyland, every experience, view, and interaction will be carefully curated to make you feel like you're in this magical bubble.

Then you leave the park, Cross Harbor Boulevard, and the bubble bursts. You're met with wide suburban arterial streets, strip malls, and motel. Walt himself hated this juxaposition. He even anticipated the problem and tried to encourage a friend to purchase the land next to his so at least he could have some say in what would go there, but his friend declined.

Later, his friend would say that was one of the biggest business mistakes of his life. But at the time, people were betting against Disney and Disneyland. After the eventual success of Disneyland, Disney eventually went on to buy 25,000 acres in central Florida, where he could build multiple theme parks and surround them with vast swaths of natural area, shielding his guests from anything that could pierce the Disney bubble. The story I just told you is kind of the origin story of the Walt Disney Resort in Florida.

It also implies that Walt Disneyland was the first park. Walt Disney World is the ultimate park, the closest reflection of Walt Disney's true vision, the better resort, basically. And I'm here to tell you that assessment is wrong. Disneyland is better, at least from an urbanism point of view.

And the commentary about how bad Anaheim is just blown out of proportion. Now, I know apologist for suburbia, but at least there's a mix of land uses near the park entrance, unlike Walt Disney World, which is basically ex urban sprawl and a countyized gated community. But while Disneyland may be better, there are other parks out there with even better land use neighbors. The question I'm interested in is where's the best place to put a theme park?
