  • People's commune

    This historical institution explains the specific generation of rural laborers mentioned in the article who built China's early infrastructure without receiving contributory pension rights, grounding the claim of 'historical justice' in a concrete policy legacy.

  • Hukou

    Understanding this household registration framework is essential to grasp why the pension system is structurally bifurcated into 'urban employees' and 'urban and rural residents,' creating the legal barrier that prevents farmers from accessing the higher contributory tier.

  • Prodrazverstka

    This practice details the mechanism by which agricultural surplus was historically extracted to fund industrialization, providing the economic context for the argument that today's elderly farmers are undercompensated for their role in the state's early prosperity.
