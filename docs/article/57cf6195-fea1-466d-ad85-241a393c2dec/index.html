"Revenge Tax" On Investors Outside the US

by Richard Coffin · The Plain Bagel · Read full article

Hey guys, it's Richard. You're watching the Plain Bagel. We've had a pretty important update regarding investors outside the United States who hold US stocks or other income producing investments. As you may have heard, a couple of weeks ago, we saw the big beautiful bill, the Republican budget, passed through the House of Representatives in the United States, bringing about a number of headline grabbing changes.

But buried within this 10,00 plus page document is a pretty important tax proposal that's likely to hit many investors who are again based outside the United States. the so-called revenge tax that's looking to target countries like Canada, Japan, Australia, the UK, and most countries within the European Union, among others. Section 899 titled Enforcement of Remedies against unfair foreign taxes. A new levy that if approved and utilized could end up costing Canadian and other foreign investors up to 20% or potentially even more of the income they generate from their US investments.

So, today we're going to forgo some frills and hop right into the details of this bill. what it means for the US and these foreign countries and importantly how individual investors whether based in Canada or another country outside the United States should be treating this information because importantly this hasn't yet been passed into law. The update still needs to go through the Senate and we might still see changes and we also don't know if this is just a negotiating tactic, a big stick that the US might wave at other countries during trade negotiations or whether it ends up being implemented. but if this does go through, it would represent a significant change in US tax policy regarding foreign investors.

Well, it essentially looks to charge a punitive withholding tax on investment income earned by applicable persons who reside in a so-called discriminatory foreign country, meaning a country the US deems to be charging unfair taxes against the US.
