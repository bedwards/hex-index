In July 2025, a startup called Vard Space Industries, raised $187 million to make drugs in orbit. They're not the only startup trying to make stuff in space. StarCloud, formerly called Lumen Orbit, raised $21 million to try and assemble a 5 gawatt data center in space. and tech giants Elon and Bezos have been talking a great deal about putting AI data centers in space.

A few people on the internet have rifted off these trends and ideas to also ask, why not do semiconductors in space, too? Bezos himself said all the way back in 2016, we can build gigantic chip factories in space. Sure, we can, but it's not going to be practical. This video is brought to you by the Asianometry Patreon. Let's start with the reasons why people think we should be putting AI data centers in space. Part of this recent push seems to be frustration with how long the data center buildouts have been taking here on Earth due to energy shortages or local opposition. With regards to the latter blocker, there are no local residents in space that I personally know of.

So presumably we bypass that. With regards to the former energy, there is something kind of compelling. Up in space, a data center taking a specific Earth orbit can face the sun for extended periods of time. And without clouds or atmosphere, solar panels generate anywhere from 3 to 10 times more power.

There are also other cited reasons. Elon has said that space is cold, implying that we can vent the waste heat into it. Though many have noted that in a vacuum, heat dissipation can only happen via radiation. More on this later.

And data connections with space-based fabs are assumed to work faster because signals can travel faster through the vacuum of space than within optical fibers. I don't know if that's right or not. Maybe it's right, maybe it's not. I don't know.
