  • Grace Hopper College 17 min read

    The residential college where Corey Menafee worked and destroyed the controversial window. It was named after John C. Calhoun, a slavery advocate, and was later renamed Grace Hopper College in 2017 following this incident and broader debates about honoring Confederate figures.

  • John C. Calhoun 15 min read

    The 7th Vice President of the United States and ardent defender of slavery whose legacy at Yale sparked the controversy. Understanding his role in championing slavery as a 'positive good' provides essential context for why the windows and college name were so offensive.

  • Removal of Confederate monuments and memorials 11 min read

    The broader movement to remove symbols honoring slavery and the Confederacy from public spaces. Menafee's action and Yale's subsequent decisions were part of this nationwide reckoning with racist iconography on campuses and in public spaces.
