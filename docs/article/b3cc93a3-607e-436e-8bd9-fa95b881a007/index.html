What is the price of 4,322 kilmters of territory? Roughly 400,000 killed and wounded. That figure reflects Russia's total territorial gains throughout 2025 and the human losses suffered in that single year alone. In other words, for every square kilometer ceased, Russia paid the price of nearly 100 killed or wounded.

for land that amounts to less than 1% of Ukraine's pre-war territory. Since the beginning of the war, an estimated 1.2 to 1.5 million citizens of the Russian Federation, along with contracted mercenaries, have been killed or wounded on the Ukrainian steps. Maps like this help us grasp the sheer scale of the land over which the war is being fought. They also show how extraordinarily costly it has become to pry even a single kilometer from the hands of the defenders and how deeply the army directed from the Kremlin is being carved up in the process.

Seen for the length of time, we have now passed day 1400 of the war. In their selective reading of history, Russians conveniently sidestepped their collaboration with Nazi Germany. For Russia, the Second World War began on June 22nd, 1941, when the Third Reich launched Operation Barbarosa against the Soviet Union. Yet, 1400 days later, the Russian textbook proudly state the first Soviet units were already on the outskirts of Berlin.

By contrast, nearly 4 years after February 24th, 2022, Russian forces are almost as far from Kev as they were 1,400 days ago. This underscores a central truth. Although Russia has held the operational initiative for the past 2 years, it remains far from achieving the war's most basic objective. Even so, the Kremlin continues to bet that sustained pressure will eventually break Ukraine, that the front will collapse like a house of cards.

Yet, while the defender's position has undeniably deteriorated, the empirical facts do not support this thesis. All the more so because 2025, while indeed the most difficult year for Ukraine, also marked the beginning of a sharp increase in the cost of war for the Russian Federation, driven in part by a sustained campaign of long range strikes, a campaign that notably has not abated.
