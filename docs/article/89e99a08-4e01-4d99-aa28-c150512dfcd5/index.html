Explore these related deep dives:

  • Montgomery C. Meigs

    His role as Quartermaster General exemplifies the article's argument that a technically competent, non-partisan bureaucracy could achieve massive infrastructure feats like the railroad expansion and military logistics during the Civil War, predating the Pendleton Act.

  • Hatch Act

    While the article critiques the hollowing out of mission-driven agencies, this law represents the specific legal mechanism that severed the link between technical expertise and political campaigning, cementing the 'process management' over 'subject knowledge' dynamic the authors describe.

  • National Cooperative Soil Survey

    This now-defunct agency serves as the perfect case study for the 'rise of the subject-matter state,' illustrating how early 20th-century entomologists and agronomists used soil mapping to drive agricultural breakthroughs before functional reorganization diluted their specialized authority.
