Transcript excerpt on Economics Explained

Over the last few years, there has understandably been a lot of attention paid to the ballooning government debt in the USA. Interest repayments alone are now one of the largest government expenditures in the country, and it only looks to be getting worse. New legislation is projected to only further widen the deficit between government income and spending, which means the rate of debt accumulation will have to increase to accommodate this. Now, the USA is a special economy, which for now at least controls the world's reserve currency.

So, it has more wiggle room to run these kinds of deficits before getting itself into too much trouble. But it's also not magic. The US federal government is now the most nominally indebted institution in history. Eventually, through one means or another, someone is going to pay the price for this debt.

Whether that's the next group of workers through high taxes, everybody through inflation, or future generations through a complete economic collapse, the latter seemingly being the popular option at the moment. But anyway, on the other hand, China outwardly seems like the flip side of this situation. It has its debts far more under control, being just 84% of GDP compared to 123% in the USA or 250% in Japan. And on top of that, it's actually one of the biggest purchases of other people's debt.

During the back and forth trade negotiations between the USA and China, one of the People's Republic's biggest hidden weapons was the fact that they owned hundreds of billions of dollars worth of US debt. The optics were pretty clear. The USA is the borrower taking on more and more debt, and China is the lender saving for the future. Except that's not exactly true.

Just a little bit of digging under the surface reveals that China is arguably more indebted than the USA is. And depending on which numbers can be believed, its debt levels could actually be more than three times as high. But this all needs a significant amount of context. China is a country with a very different government, private sector, and overall economy.
