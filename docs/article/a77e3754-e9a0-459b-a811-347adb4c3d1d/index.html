The Voices I Have Lived In

A craft essay

The writing life does not proceed linearly—and this is both its great frustration and its great mercy. It accumulates gradually—long dry stretches, sudden storms, seasons when the light is strange and everything you write seems to shimmer, seasons when the words land lifeless and you bury them in drawers. The voice we are searching for on the page is not simply “our voice,” as though such a thing exists whole and waiting, like a room with the lights already on. The voice is something we construct from wreckage and from wonder, from the places we have written and the places we have been too afraid to write ...
