Explore these related deep dives:

  • New trade theory

    This framework explains how economies of scale and product differentiation allow nations to specialize in ways that standard comparative advantage models cannot, providing the theoretical backbone for the author's argument about human-AI labor division.

  • Great Famine of 1876–1878

    The author uses this historical catastrophe to illustrate how resource constraints (specifically land and food distribution) can lead to mass unemployment and suffering even when global production is theoretically sufficient, mirroring the potential AI-induced resource squeeze.

  • Moore's second law

    While Moore's first law predicts chip speed, this 'second law' describes the exponential rise in the cost of fabrication, offering a concrete economic mechanism that could eventually cap AI expansion and preserve human comparative advantage.
