As another summer and autumn campaign season comes to a close in Ukraine, Ukraine's leadership are very clear that the country is under immense pressure. These last months have definitely brought pain points for Moscow. Russia's ground offensives failed to generate any strategic scale breakthroughs and fell far short of their generally assessed objectives. Their near irreplaceable strategic bomber assets took a bloody nose on home turf, while Ukraine's ability to strike targets deep in the Russian rear and, shall we say, place upward pressure on insurance premiums for targets in the Russian energy sector only increased.

Coupled with the effect of new non-kinetic sanctions imposed by various countries, including the United States, the growing burden on the overt taxed Russian war economy has been plainly apparent. But in other respects, Ukraine is perhaps under more pressure than it has been at any time since 2022. The coming winter is expected to be a difficult and cold one.
