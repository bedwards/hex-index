  • Decodable text

    The article critiques the overreliance on these simplified texts, and this entry explains the specific phonics-based constraints that often strip away the complex vocabulary necessary for genuine knowledge acquisition.

  • Matthew effect

    This concept describes how students with prior knowledge gain advantages exponentially, illuminating why the article argues that 'faux cohesion' in curricula widens the achievement gap rather than closing it.

  • Cognitive load

    The author's argument that teachers should read aloud complex texts to avoid overwhelming working memory relies on this psychological framework to explain why 'decodable' texts can actually hinder learning.
