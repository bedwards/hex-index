Should Cities Ban Drive Thrus?

you may have already seen the images lines of cars spilling out onto nearby streets causing traffic jams the culprit popular drive-through fast food restaurants like Chick-fil-A all over the country Traffic Engineers are trying to figure out how to unsnarl traffic caused by drivers waiting to turn into the driveway and get to the official drive-through Lane the problem is getting so bad that the fast food restaurants are dealing with complaints from Neighbors and local governments a shopping center in Bowmont Texas is suing a Chick-fil-A for the traffic jams caused by its drive-throughs apparently the long lines block access to other businesses in the city of Santa Barbara a Chick-fil-A is so popular that it regularly blocks the rightmost lane for about 90 minutes every weekday and 2 and 1 half hours on Saturdays essentially turning State Street into a on Lane Road the restaurant owner had a hire thirdparty traffic control in an attempt to avoid the city council declaring the restaurant a public nuisance with all of these problems Chick-fil-A isn't standing still they're innovating when Chick-fil-A locations reach certain Revenue thresholds the company shuts down the restaurants and aims to improve capacity and throughput they do what's called a scrape and rebuild and completely demolish the old restaurant then they do things like add a second drive-through Lane here's the same Chick-fil-A this time with two drive-through Lanes another scrap and rebuild resulted in a restaurant without any indoor dining the parking spaces gave way to more drive-through space Chick-fil-A is going even further too they launched a new four-lane drive-through concept with no indoor dining and a kitchen that sits above the drive-through Lanes according to Chick-fil-A unique meal transport system that is some kind of conveyor belt moves the orders from the kitchen above to the server below the kitchen is twice the size of a normal Chick-fil-A and they claim they can deliver a meal every 6 seconds these drive-through problems are not limited to Chick-fil-A Taco Bell also built a four-line concept restaurant similar to the Chick-fil-A one but this one in Minnesota while some of these Innovations might solve the queuing problem they are also eliminating third spaces or places for people to hang out and building more lanes and space for cars isn't a great long-term solution for traffic and Emissions it's clear that drive-throughs are getting out ...
