#34: Second-Hand Only Malls, AI Bus Cameras, and Metro Underpasses

By Raymond Berger · Urbanism Now ·Sep 9, 2025 · 3 min read

Welcome to the 34th issue of Urbanism Now, our weekly newsletter curated with brief and insightful urban ideas from around the world to inspire action where you (c)are.

This newsletter is curated by Maria Paula Moreno Vivas and Ray Berger. Learn more on our About page.

Thank you for your interest and support. If you find this newsletter useful, please forward it to a friend!

Let's get started:

  • Despacio and Light Projects explore how Bogotá's new Metro underpasses could become "Urban Rooms of Luminous Color" through participatory lighting design, rather than sterile LED corridors that divide communities.

  • Next City examines how Philadelphia's AI-powered bus cameras detected 36,000 violations in just 70 days, but cautions that transparency and fairness are essential for public acceptance.

  • Sweden's innovative ReTuna mall, the world's first shopping center selling only secondhand items, transforms circular retail into a stylish, municipality-supported experience.

  • Los Angeles is one step closer to eliminating parking minimums for new housing after a city council committee advanced a proposal to study the policy change citywide.

Videos, podcasts, books, and socials:

  • Shreya Khandekar and Anuj Kale created a brilliant interactive comic exploring child-friendly urban design, following two siblings navigating a city where simple errands become adventures.
