Deep Dives

Explore these related deep dives:

  • Fibrinolysis

    The article claims the new clots exhibit 'impaired fibrinolysis,' a specific biological failure where the body cannot dissolve clots, which is the core mechanism distinguishing these casts from standard thrombosis.

  • Blood vessel

    While the article describes 'anomalous intravascular casts' that mimic vessel shapes, this term historically refers to rare, non-clotting molds formed by other substances, providing a crucial baseline for understanding why these new findings are considered 'non-canonical'.

  • Proteomics

    The New Zealand study cited relies on 'proteomic characterization' to identify the unique protein architecture of the clots, a technical method essential for explaining how researchers determined the clots' abnormal biochemical matrix.
