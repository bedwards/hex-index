In the Gospel of Mark, Jesus makes a striking promise. He tells his followers that those who believe in him will perform miracles by using my name. They will cast out demons. They will speak in new tongues.

They will pick up snakes. And if they drink any deadly thing, it will not hurt them. They will lay their hands on the sick and they will recover. A few of these miraculous signs have become central ritual practices for Pentecostal and charismatic Christians.

Speaking in tongues and healing and exorcistic rituals are still practiced in Pentecostal and charismatic worship services all over the world. But what about picking up snakes? Well, that one never really caught on, except for a handful of Pentecostal churches in the Appalachian Mountains of the US. They handle live venomous snakes during worship as a demonstration of faith, believing that if the Bible says true believers will pick up snakes, then doing so is a test and testimony, physical proof that the Holy Spirit truly dwells within them.

Today in the US there are only about a hundred small independent Christian churches where handling venomous snakes is a common practice. These independent churches typically identify with two denominations. The church of God and the church of God prophecy which are denominations within the broader brella of Pentecostal Christianity and charismatic Christianity. Now these are very large branches of Christianity.

Pentecostal Christianity includes big global denominations such as the Assemblies of God and the church of God in Christ. both of which have millions of members, but neither of them recognize these snake handling churches. Pentecostal leaders have disavowed snake handling for decades, and they do not consider it an acceptable form of worship. It's also illegal in every single state except for West Virginia.

Snake handling churches are spread throughout Appalachia, often located in the mountain towns of West Virginia, Kentucky, and Tennessee. There's also a few in the southeast, as far south as central Florida, Georgia, and Alabama. But in general, snake handling churches are very regional in the Appalachian Mountains. Now, these services are not all about snakes.
