The Fascist Ideal: What if Fascists Won?

by Then & Now · Then & Now · Read full article

In September 1939, the Nazis invaded Poland, starting World War II. In December, just 3 months after, a courier for the Polish underground described the Nazi occupied city of Pnan. The Polish city had been transformed, he said, in just 3 months, writing, "The city with the finest historical tradition in all of Poland was now to all appearances a typical German community. Every sign on stores and banks and institutions was in German.

The street names were in German. If a German had passed by, a pole had to step off the sidewalk. A pole could not travel by automobile or trolley and was even forbidden to own a bicycle. He had been placed completely outside the protection of the law and all his property, movable or immovable, was at the disposal of the German authorities.

To the Nazis, Slavs and Poles were seen as not being able to govern themselves. Germans, of course, were a biologically superior race. Joseph Gerbles, the Nazi propaganda minister, reported Hitler's feelings towards the polls, saying, "The Fiora's verdict on the polls is damning, more like animals than human beings, completely primitive, stupid, and amorphous, and a ruling class that is an unsatisfactory result of mingling between the lower orders and an Aryan master race. The Po's dirtiness is unimaginable.

Their capacity for intelligent judgment is absolutely nil. How far did this conviction in biological German superiority extend across the world? The German newspaper Grenb wrote, "If the good of our fatherland demands the conquest, enslavement, elimination, or destruction of other nations, we should not be restrained from doing this by any Christian or humanitarian scruples." So we all know the Nazi attitude towards Jews. We know about Italian colonial ambitions in Ethiopia.

We know about Britain in India at the same time too. And we shouldn't forget this was the period of Jim Crow in America. This kind of thinking was global. But how did the Nazis differ?

What would the world look like today if fascists had won World War II? And importantly, why does this question matter? Right now, since World War II, thousands of novels, TV series, films, and scholarly articles have tried to get at this big question. European domination.
