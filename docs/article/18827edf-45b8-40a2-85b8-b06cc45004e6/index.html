shabuya Crossing in Tokyo is the busiest intersection in the world this scramble allows pedestrians from all Corners to cross at the same time and during Peak periods this can mean 2500 or 3,000 people in the intersection you might be familiar with shuya Crossing from just about any b-roll compilation of Tokyo or this scene from Lost in Translation or if you're a person of culture this scene from Fast and the Furious Tokyo Drift a few months ago I asked everyone I knew for video topic suggestions for when I visited Tokyo a colleague of mine suggested the topic why is shabuya crossing the busiest intersection in the world and to be honest back then I didn't think much of the idea sorry Mike how interesting is that question considering that the crossing is the intersection right in front of the busiest train station in the world but when I looked into it I realized that the busiest train station in the world wasn't here but it was here and when I eventually visited shabuya Crossing I found it to be surprisingly not that busy I started to wonder if it even was the busiest intersection in the world and why was it in front of the fourth busiest train station and not the first let's get some answers after the bike Bell my first stop was to verify that shabuya Crossing had the most pedestrians so I went right to the source the international intersection certifying committee I quickly realized that no such organization exists I'm also pretty sure that nobody's done a study of all possible busiest intersections in the world and dubbed shabuya the absolute busiest I am willing to believe that a single pedestrian signal could see 3,000 people crossing at Absolute peak times though I have to admit I'm pretty sure it's a lot less during off peak periods like a random 10 a.m. in June this is when I visited and you can see that it's really not that crowded here's a view from above and you can see there are lots of people but certainly not at Tokyo Drift levels shabuya Crossing's pedestrian counts definitely vary throughout the day and year and if you want to see it packed evenings are best and Halloween is best of all the crossing turns into a massive costume party and I got to believe ...
