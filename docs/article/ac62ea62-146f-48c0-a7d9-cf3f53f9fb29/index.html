Without doubt, this is one of the biggest events on Earth. It's time for the Super Bowl. Once a year, every year, one team is crowned champion, another leaves heartbroken, and the majority of us sit at home critiquing athletes over physical feats we couldn't even dream of attempting. But while the purists may disagree, the Super Bowl is a whole lot more than just a football game.

Some people tune in purely for the halftime shows. The stadiums that host this iconic game are a glorious combination of sporting excellence and engineering mastery. But over in Nashville, since opening the Nissan Stadium in 1999, the Titans have never hosted the NFL's season culmination. And while they stay in their current arena, that's never likely to change.

Hosting the Super Bowl can earn your city crazy money. We're talking as much as a billion dollars in visitor spending and employment. So, the city of Nashville is making a hail mary play to host the showcase game. The Titans are building a brand new state-of-the-art facility with the biggest public investment for a stadium in American history.

Nissan Stadium has welcomed a lot of famous faces through its doors. Whether it's hosting Brock Lesnar and Ronda Rousey for the WWE SuperSlam or musicians like Taylor Swift and Luke Combmes, it's one of Tennessee's most iconic venues. But nothing is more synonymous with the arena than football. For the last 26 years, nearly 70,000 fans have flooded the stands each game day to watch stars like Eddie George, Bruce Matthews, and Steve McNair.

But the Super Bowl has evaded the Nashville franchise, both in playing and hosting. And that's because there's some key criteria an arena has to meet to be deemed up to the task. Media amenities, game day temperature of 10° C or a domed roof, space for the NFL game day experience, ample hotel space nearby, and general stadium quality. When you compare Nissan Stadium with newer venues like SoFi or Allegant, its age really does start to show.

But ultimately, the main factor getting in the way of the Nissan Stadium's ability to host a Super Bowl is the weather. Now, you'll notice one key difference when compared to the four most recently opened NFL grounds. It doesn't have a roof. In February 2025, Nashville dropped to a daytime low of -4° C, well below that 10° target....
