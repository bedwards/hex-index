The Absolute Best Transportation for Cities

I ride a bike pretty much every day here in Amsterdam, but to be totally honest, I don't even like bicycles that much. I do it because I'd be crazy not to when it's so convenient. Which is the case for the vast majority of people you see here. I've learned over the years that there really aren't that many car people, bike people, or train people.

The vast majority of people just want to get from point A to point B conveniently and efficiently. which is why cities need to make it easy to use transportation methods that make the city a better place to live. Now, that being said, there are still a few people who have a preferred form of transportation that goes beyond just convenience, and I happen to be one of those people because I love trams. They're my absolute favorite form of transportation within cities.

So when I visit a new city, the first thing I do is take a tram and look out the window to get a feel for the place. But the benefits of trams go beyond transportation. And I actually think that trams make urban places fundamentally better. Which is why I firmly believe that if you want to create a truly great city, you need to be building tram lines.

And then you can hear The tram network here in Amsterdam is pretty great. Certainly in and around the city center, but also in many suburban neighborhoods as well. I find that for many places I'd like to go to, there's usually a tram stop nearby, which is really convenient. The stops are simple but functional, and they have screens that let you know when the next tram is coming.

All of the trams are now the modern lowfloor kind, so there's level boarding with the platform, which makes it really easy and quick to get on board. The trams are reasonably spacious and comfortable. And though the seats aren't exactly luxurious, they're perfectly fine for short distances, and it's great to look out the window. It's one of my favorite ways to see the city because if I see something interesting, I can just get off at the next stop and walk there easily, making it a great way to find new places.
