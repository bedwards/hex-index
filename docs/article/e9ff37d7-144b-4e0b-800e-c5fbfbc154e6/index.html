Then I looked and there was the lamb and with him were 144,000 people who had his name and his father's name written on their foreheads. That is one of the most mysterious passages from the book of Revelation, which is already arguably one of the most mysterious books in the New Testament. It's packed with beasts, dragons, cosmic battles, and a lot of symbolic numbers. The number seven pops up constantly.

Seven seals, seven trumpets, seven churches. And in the Hebrew Bible, 7 often symbolizes perfection or completeness, which is why it so often gets tied to the divine. But Revelation also leans heavily on another symbolic number, 12, which also symbolizes wholeness and divine authority. Think the 12 tribes of Israel descended from the 12 sons of Jacob or the 12 disciples chosen by Jesus.

Revelation draws on the symbolism by introducing this striking figure of 144,000 people. That's 12 * 12 * 1,000. These 144,000 people show up in two key moments. Revelation 7 and chapter 14, where they're described as being sealed by God, marked for salvation.

But who exactly are these 144,000? Are we supposed to take that number literally, or is it symbolic like so much of this book? This mysterious group of 144,000 people has sparked centuries of speculation from early Christian theologians all the way up to 21st century prophecy fiction. Each offering very different interpretations of who the 144,000 really are.

Okay, so let's take a closer look at where the 144,000 actually appear in Revelation. In Revelation 7, the author describes a vision of four angels standing at the corners of the earth. Each one representing one of the four cardinal directions. These four angels are told to hold back the destructive winds from the earth until we have marked the servants of our God with a seal on their foreheads.

John then hears the number of those who are sealed. 144,000 drawn evenly from the 12 tribes of Israel, 12,000 from each tribe. These people are marked with a seal, which we'll unpack more in a moment. The 144,000 show up again in Revelation 14, which I quoted at the start.
