But first, we have to clarify what we even mean by a black hole. A black hole is a type of space-time geometry that's a solution to Einstein's field equations. Its defining property is the event horizon. That's a closed surface from within which nothing, not even light, can escape.

Such a surface will generically form if matter becomes sufficiently compressed, for example, when a star collapses after a supernova. You can understand this in terms of escape velocity. To launch a rocket off the surface of a planet, you need a certain minimum initial velocity. This is the escape velocity.

The higher the mass of the planet and the smaller the radius, the higher the velocity needs to be. Eventually, it exceeds the speed of light. This is when you have a black hole. In Einstein's theory, black holes have a singularity on the inside hidden behind the horizon.
