  • Essays (Montaigne)

    Kluge explicitly cites Montaigne as a precursor to his method of using fragmented, self-reflective storytelling to dismantle the illusion of a single, objective reality.

  • Battle of Stalingrad

    This specific historical catastrophe serves as Kluge's primary case study for how 'reality' can be both brutally rigid and a 'crude invention' that fails to capture the multiple, overlapping life spans of its victims.

  • Negative Dialectics

    As the philosophical framework developed by Adorno, this concept explains the 'antagonistic concept of reality' Kluge employs to argue that truth is found in the contradictions of experience rather than in a unified narrative.
