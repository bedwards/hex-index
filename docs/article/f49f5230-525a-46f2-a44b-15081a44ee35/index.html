Hey everyone, it's Richard. You're watching the Plain Bagel. There's been a lot of talk about Alberta separatism lately. Started off as a fringe movement that slowly picked up steam and thanks to changes made at the provincial level to make a referendum easier to have.

The Alberta Prosperity Project, a nonprofit pushing for independence, has started collecting signatures to put the question to voters as to whether Alberta should leave the rest of Canada. And as you can imagine, in the current environment, the whole thing has been pretty controversial. Canada is already facing a trade war from the United States and threats from Trump to turn the country into the 51st state via economic pressure. And news broke last month of separatist meeting with US officials to discuss a $500 billion loan to help the province transition to independence, something BC Premier David Ebie likened to treason.

Now, right from the get-go, it's worth highlighting these aren't elected officials, and only a minority of Albertans. between 19 and 29% according to recent polls actually support independence to varying degrees which while high for Canada is actually on par with some US states like California and actually less than demand for independence from states like Texas and Alaska and the Alberta Prosperity Project behind the referendum the most vocal group in pushing for independence here is very much a a questionable organization we'll call it in their own marketing material which starts with a quote of the US Declaration of Independence the group laments about Alberta's quote downhill spiral into the vortex of the WEF UN Marxist agenda that'll be exacerbated by the proposed digital ID programmable digital currency and the ESG credit score to control every aspect of your life. And there are plenty of other examples of the group's co-founder Jeffrey Wrath and CEO Mitch Sylvester saying pretty outlandish stuff. Yet, this fringe group has been given quite the megaphone, especially among some within the mega crowd.

Personalities on Twitter would have you believe that Albertans are dead set on separating or even joining the US, sharing videos of long lines and filled venues for referendum events. And Treasury Secretary Scott Essent has even recently commented on working with Alberta as an independent country.
