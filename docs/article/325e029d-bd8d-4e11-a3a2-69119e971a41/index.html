Over centuries the south caucuses and highlands of eastern Anatolia were home to the Armenian people.

These lands had long been contested by the Persians, Ottomans and Russians. And by the late 19th century, Armenians found themselves caught up in rising Turkish nationalism and the decline of imperial authority in the Ottoman Empire. Such conditions were difficult for minority groups like the Armenians. The Himeidan massacres of 1894 to 1897, for instance, saw the deaths of tens of thousands of Armenians, if not more, while various restrictions had been placed on them by Ottoman leaders in the decades prior.

Around this same time, a panarmenian nationalist faction called the Armenian Revolutionary Federation or ARF was formed. It sought to protect Armenians from the Ottomans and eventually created an independent state. However, the First World War was particularly destabilizing for the Ottomans and the authorities grew even more suspicious of non-Turkish minorities.
